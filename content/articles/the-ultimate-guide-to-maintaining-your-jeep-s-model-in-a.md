## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Inspect and Maintain the Engine
#### Step 1.1: Check the Oil and Fluid Levels
Regularly check the oil, coolant, and brake fluid levels to ensure they are at the recommended levels. Top off as needed.
#### Step 1.2: Replace the Air Filter
Replace the air filter every 15,000 to 30,000 miles to maintain optimal engine performance and fuel efficiency.
#### Step 1.3: Inspect the Spark Plugs
Inspect the spark plugs every 30,000 to 100,000 miles and replace them as needed to prevent engine misfires and decreased performance.

### Step 2: Maintain the Suspension and Steering
#### Step 2.1: Inspect the Ball Joints
Inspect the ball joints every 12,000 to 15,000 miles and replace them as needed to prevent loose or worn-out joints.
#### Step 2.2: Check the Tire Pressure
Regularly check the tire pressure to ensure it's at the recommended level. Proper tire pressure can improve handling, fuel efficiency, and tire life.
#### Step 2.3: Inspect the Steering Components
Inspect the steering components, including the tie rods and control arms, every 12,000 to 15,000 miles and replace them as needed to maintain optimal steering performance.

### Step 3: Maintain the Electrical System
#### Step 3.1: Inspect the Battery
Inspect the battery every 6 months and replace it as needed to prevent electrical system failures.
#### Step 3.2: Check the Alternator Belt
Check the alternator belt every 12,000 to 15,000 miles and replace it as needed to prevent electrical system failures.
#### Step 3.3: Inspect the Wiring and Connectors
Inspect the wiring and connectors every 12,000 to 15,000 miles and replace them as needed to prevent electrical system failures.
