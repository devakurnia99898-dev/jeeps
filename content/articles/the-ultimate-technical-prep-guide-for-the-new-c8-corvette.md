## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The new C8 Corvette Grand Sport is a high-performance vehicle that requires regular maintenance to ensure optimal performance. As a mechanic, it's essential to understand the technical background of the vehicle, including its engine, suspension, and electrical systems. Symptoms or signs to look for include decreased performance, unusual noises, or warning lights on the dashboard.

## Technical Deep Dive
The C8 Corvette Grand Sport is equipped with a 6.2L V8 engine, producing 460 horsepower and 465 lb-ft of torque. The engine features a dry-sump oil system, which requires regular checks and maintenance. The suspension system is a double-wishbone design with magnetic ride control, providing exceptional handling and stability. Common failure points include the plastic impeller in the water pump, the serpentine belt, and the spark plugs.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
The C8 Corvette Grand Sport requires regular oil changes to maintain engine performance and longevity. To perform an oil change, follow these steps:
#### Step 1.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the vehicle.
#### Step 1.2: Jack Up the Vehicle
Use a hydraulic jack to lift the vehicle, ensuring the oil pan is accessible.
#### Step 1.3: Remove the Oil Drain Plug
Use a 10mm socket to remove the oil drain plug, allowing the oil to drain into a pan.
#### Step 1.4: Replace the Oil Filter
Use a filter wrench to remove the oil filter, and replace it with a new one.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
The C8 Corvette Grand Sport features high-performance brake pads that require regular replacement. To replace the brake pads, follow these steps:
#### Step 2.1: Remove the Wheels
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheels, providing access to the brake calipers.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the Caliper
Use a 10mm socket to remove the caliper bolts, allowing the caliper to be removed.
#### Step 2.3: Remove the Old Brake Pads
Use pliers to remove the old brake pads, taking care not to damage the brake rotors.
#### Step 2.4: Install the New Brake Pads
Use pliers to install the new brake pads, ensuring they are properly seated and secured.

### Step 3: Spark Plug Replacement
The C8 Corvette Grand Sport features spark plugs that require regular replacement. To replace the spark plugs, follow these steps:
#### Step 3.1: Locate the Spark Plug Wires
The spark plug wires are located on the sides of the engine, near the cylinder heads.
#### Step 3.2: Remove the Spark Plug Wires
Use a spark plug wire puller to remove the spark plug wires, allowing access to the spark plugs.
#### Step 3.3: Remove the Old Spark Plugs
Use a spark plug socket to remove the old spark plugs, taking care not to damage the spark plug wells.
#### Step 3.4: Install the New Spark Plugs
Use a spark plug socket to install the new spark plugs, ensuring they are properly seated and secured.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type of oil and filter for the C8 Corvette Grand Sport
* Never over-tighten the oil drain plug or spark plugs, as this can cause damage to the engine
* Always wear protective gear when working with brake pads and spark plugs

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What type of oil should I use in my C8 Corvette Grand Sport?
The recommended oil type for the C8 Corvette Grand Sport is 0W-40 synthetic oil.
2. How often should I change the oil in my C8 Corvette Grand Sport?
The recommended oil change interval for the C8 Corvette Grand Sport is every 5,000-7,500 miles.
3. What are the symptoms of worn-out brake pads in the C8 Corvette Grand Sport?
Symptoms of worn-out brake pads include squealing or grinding noises, vibration when braking, and decreased stopping power.
4. How often should I replace the spark plugs in my C8 Corvette Grand Sport?
The recommended spark plug replacement interval for the C8 Corvette Grand Sport is every 30,000-50,000 miles.
5. Can I perform maintenance tasks on my C8 Corvette Grand Sport myself?
Yes, many maintenance tasks can be performed by the owner, including oil changes, brake pad replacement, and spark plug replacement. However, it's essential to consult the owner's manual and follow proper safety procedures to avoid damage to the vehicle or injury to oneself.
