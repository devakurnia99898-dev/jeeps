## Technical Deep Dive
The electromechanical torque-vectoring differential in the 2027 Audi RS5 consists of several key components, including:

* Electronic control unit (ECU): This is the brain of the system, responsible for processing data from various sensors and controlling the actuators.
* Sensors: These include wheel speed sensors, steering angle sensors, and acceleration sensors, which provide data to the ECU.
* Actuators: These are responsible for distributing torque between the wheels, and may include electric motors, hydraulic pumps, or other mechanisms.
* Differential gears: These are the mechanical components that transmit torque to the wheels.

Common failure points in this system may include:

* Faulty sensors or sensor calibration issues
* Actuator malfunctions or worn components
* Software glitches or ECU faults
* Mechanical wear or damage to the differential gears
