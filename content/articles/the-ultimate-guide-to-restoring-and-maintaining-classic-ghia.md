## Technical Deep Dive
Ghia-badged cars were known for their luxurious interiors, advanced suspension systems, and powerful engines. The suspension system, in particular, was designed to provide a smooth ride and exceptional handling. However, over time, the suspension components can wear out, causing the car to sag or lean to one side.

Common failure points in Ghia-badged cars include the suspension bushings, ball joints, and tie rod ends. The electrical system can also be prone to faults, particularly in the wiring and connectors. Engine problems can range from faulty fuel injectors to worn-out piston rings.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Suspension Restoration
#### Step 1.1: Remove the Wheels and Suspension Components
Remove the wheels and suspension components, including the control arms, ball joints, and tie rod ends. Inspect each component for wear and damage.

#### Step 1.2: Replace the Suspension Bushings
Replace the suspension bushings with new ones, ensuring they are properly lubricated and aligned.

#### Step 1.3: Reassemble the Suspension System
Reassemble the suspension system, tightening all bolts and nuts to the specified torque.

### Step 2: Electrical System Repair
#### Step 2.1: Identify the Faulty Component
Use a multimeter to identify the faulty component in the electrical system.

#### Step 2.2: Replace the Faulty Component
Replace the faulty component, ensuring all connections are secure and tight.

#### Step 2.3: Test the Electrical System
Test the electrical system to ensure it's functioning correctly.

### Step 3: Engine Overhaul
#### Step 3.1: Remove the Engine
Remove the engine from the car, taking care not to damage any surrounding components.

#### Step 3.2: Disassemble the Engine
Disassemble the engine, inspecting each component for wear and damage.

#### Step 3.3: Replace Worn-Out Components
Replace worn-out components, including piston rings, bearings, and valve seals.

#### Step 3.4: Reassemble the Engine
Reassemble the engine, ensuring all components are properly aligned and secured.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
Don't attempt to restore a classic Ghia-badged car without proper knowledge and experience. Common mistakes include using incorrect torque specs, failing to properly lubricate suspension components, and neglecting to test the electrical system after repairs.

Tricks of the trade include using specialized tools, such as a ball joint press, to simplify the restoration process. Additionally, consult with experts and join classic car communities to gain valuable insights and advice.
