## Technical Deep Dive
The GTS (Gran Turismo Sport) models are designed to be high-performance versions of Porsche's standard vehicles. They typically feature upgraded engines, suspension, and aerodynamics, making them more capable on both the road and track. The GT models, on the other hand, are designed for pure performance and are often stripped of unnecessary features to reduce weight and increase power-to-weight ratio. The GT models are usually built for racing and track use, with a focus on raw performance and handling.

Common failure points in the GTS and GT models include the engine, transmission, and suspension. The high-performance nature of these vehicles means they are often pushed to their limits, which can lead to premature wear and tear on critical components. Regular maintenance is essential to prevent these issues and ensure the vehicle continues to perform at its best.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Upgrading the Suspension
The suspension is a critical component of any high-performance vehicle. Upgrading the suspension can significantly improve handling and stability, making the vehicle more capable on both the road and track.
#### Installing Coilovers
1. Remove the wheels and suspension components to access the coilovers.
2. Install the new coilovers, ensuring they are properly aligned and secured.
3. Adjust the coilovers to the desired ride height and stiffness.

#### Installing Sway Bars
1. Remove the old sway bars and install the new ones, ensuring they are properly secured.
2. Adjust the sway bars to the desired stiffness and alignment.

### Upgrading the Engine
The engine is the heart of any high-performance vehicle. Upgrading the engine can significantly improve power and performance, making the vehicle more capable on both the road and track.
#### Installing a Performance Air Filter
1. Remove the old air filter and install the new one, ensuring it is properly secured.
2. Adjust the air filter to the desired flow rate and restriction.

#### Installing a Performance Exhaust System
1. Remove the old exhaust system and install the new one, ensuring it is properly secured.
2. Adjust the exhaust system to the desired flow rate and restriction.

### Upgrading the Brakes
The brakes are a critical component of any high-performance vehicle. Upgrading the brakes can significantly improve stopping power and safety, making the vehicle more capable on both the road and track.
#### Installing Performance Brake Pads
1. Remove the old brake pads and install the new ones, ensuring they are properly secured.
2. Adjust the brake pads to the desired friction and wear rate.

#### Installing a Performance Brake Rotors
1. Remove the old brake rotors and install the new ones, ensuring they are properly secured.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When working on high-performance vehicles like the GTS and GT models, it is essential to avoid common mistakes and follow best practices. Some tips to keep in mind include:
* Always follow the manufacturer's instructions and guidelines when upgrading or modifying the vehicle.
* Ensure all components are properly secured and aligned before starting the engine or driving the vehicle.
* Avoid over-tightening or under-tightening bolts and fasteners, as this can lead to premature wear and tear or even failure.
