## Technical Deep Dive
A brake-by-wire system replaces traditional hydraulic brake lines with electronic sensors and actuators. The system consists of:
* Electronic brake pedal
* Brake control module (BCM)
* Electronic brake actuators
* Wheel speed sensors

The electronic brake pedal sends signals to the BCM, which processes the information and sends commands to the electronic brake actuators. The actuators then apply the brakes accordingly. Common failure points in brake-by-wire systems include:
* Faulty electronic brake pedal sensors
* Corrosion or damage to the brake control module
* Worn or damaged electronic brake actuators
* Malfunctioning wheel speed sensors

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspecting the Brake-by-Wire System
#### Checking the Electronic Brake Pedal
1. Locate the electronic brake pedal and inspect for any signs of damage or wear.
2. Use a multimeter to test the pedal's electrical resistance and ensure it's within the manufacturer's specifications.
#### Checking the Brake Control Module
1. Locate the brake control module and inspect for any signs of damage or corrosion.
2. Use a scan tool to diagnose any issues with the module and check for software updates.

### Replacing the Electronic Brake Pedal
#### Removing the Old Pedal
1. Disconnect the battery to prevent any accidental starts.
2. Remove the trim panel and any other components to access the electronic brake pedal.
3. Use a socket wrench to remove the pedal's mounting bolts.
#### Installing the New Pedal
1. Mount the new pedal and secure it with the mounting bolts.
2. Connect the electrical connector to the pedal.
3. Reinstall any trim panels or components removed during the process.

### Replacing the Brake Control Module
#### Removing the Old Module
1. Disconnect the battery to prevent any accidental starts.
2. Locate the brake control module and remove any mounting bolts or clips.
3. Carefully pull the module out of its mounting location.
#### Installing the New Module
1. Mount the new module and secure it with the mounting bolts or clips.
2. Connect the electrical connectors to the module.
3. Reinstall any components removed during the process.
