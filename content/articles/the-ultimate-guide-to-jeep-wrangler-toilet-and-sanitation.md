## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspecting the System
The first step is to inspect the system for any signs of damage or wear. Check the holding tank, vents, and valves for any blockages or leaks.

#### Checking the Holding Tank
1. Locate the holding tank and inspect it for any signs of damage or corrosion.
2. Check the tank's connections and ensure they are secure.
3. Look for any signs of leaks or water damage.

#### Inspecting the Vents
1. Locate the vents and inspect them for any blockages or debris.
2. Check the vents' connections and ensure they are secure.
3. Look for any signs of corrosion or damage.

### Replacing the Toilet
If the toilet is damaged or malfunctioning, it may need to be replaced. This requires careful planning and execution.

#### Removing the Old Toilet
1. Disconnect the power and water supply to the toilet.
2. Remove the toilet's mounting hardware.
3. Carefully lift the toilet out of its mounting bracket.

#### Installing the New Toilet
1. Place the new toilet in its mounting bracket.
2. Reconnect the power and water supply.
3. Ensure the toilet is securely fastened to its mounting bracket.

### Servicing the Sanitation System
Regular servicing is essential to maintain the sanitation system's efficiency and effectiveness.

#### Checking the Valves
1. Locate the valves and inspect them for any signs of damage or wear.
2. Check the valves' connections and ensure they are secure.
3. Look for any signs of leaks or corrosion.

#### Replacing the Seals
1. Locate the seals and inspect them for any signs of damage or wear.
2. Remove the old seals and replace them with new ones.
3. Ensure the new seals are securely fastened.
