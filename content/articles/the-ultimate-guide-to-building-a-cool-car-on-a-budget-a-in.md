## Technical Deep Dive
The key to building a cool car on a budget is to understand how the various systems work and how to modify them. The engine, for example, is the heart of the car, and modifying it can significantly impact the vehicle's performance. The suspension system, on the other hand, affects the car's handling and ride quality. By understanding how these systems work and how to modify them, you can create a car that's not only cool but also fun to drive.

One of the most critical components of a car's engine is the cylinder head. The cylinder head is responsible for directing air and fuel into the engine's cylinders, and modifying it can significantly impact the engine's performance. Another critical component is the exhaust system, which affects the engine's power output and sound.

The suspension system, on the other hand, consists of several components, including the springs, shocks, and control arms. By modifying these components, you can significantly improve the car's handling and ride quality.

### Safety Warnings
When working on your car, it's essential to take safety precautions seriously. Always wear protective gear, including gloves, safety glasses, and a face mask. Make sure the car is on a level surface and apply the parking brake. Never work under a car that's supported by a jack alone.

## Step-by-Step Guide
Building a cool car on a budget requires a step-by-step approach. Here's a guide to get you started:

### Step 1: Choose the Right Car
Choosing the right car is critical to building a cool car on a budget. Look for a car that's reliable, has a strong engine, and is easy to modify. Consider factors such as the car's weight, suspension, and brakes.

#### Step 1.1: Research the Car's History
Research the car's history, including its maintenance records and any previous modifications. This will help you identify any potential issues and avoid costly repairs.

#### Step 1.2: Inspect the Car
Inspect the car thoroughly, looking for any signs of damage or wear. Check the engine, suspension, and brakes, and make sure everything is in good working condition.

### Step 2: Plan Your Modifications
Once you've chosen the right car, it's time to plan your modifications. Consider what you want to achieve with your build, including the car's performance, handling, and appearance.

#### Step 2.1: Determine Your Budget
