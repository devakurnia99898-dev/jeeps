## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspect and Replace Tires
#### Check Tire Pressure
Check the tire pressure to ensure that it is at the recommended level. The recommended tire pressure for the Caterham Seven is 35 psi for the front tires and 30 psi for the rear tires.

#### Inspect Tire Tread
Inspect the tire tread to ensure that it is in good condition. The tire tread should be at least 1/16 of an inch deep, and should not show any signs of wear or damage.

#### Replace Tires
If the tires are worn or damaged, replace them with new ones. The Caterham Seven uses 155/65R14 tires on the front and 175/60R14 tires on the rear.

### Inspect and Replace Suspension Components
#### Check Suspension Bushings
Check the suspension bushings to ensure that they are in good condition. The suspension bushings should be free of cracks or damage, and should not show any signs of wear.

#### Replace Suspension Bushings
If the suspension bushings are worn or damaged, replace them with new ones. The suspension bushings can be replaced with polyurethane or rubber bushings.

#### Check Suspension Alignment
Check the suspension alignment to ensure that it is properly aligned. The suspension alignment should be checked using a suspension alignment tool.

### Inspect and Replace Brake Pads and Rotors
#### Check Brake Pads
Check the brake pads to ensure that they are in good condition. The brake pads should be at least 1/8 of an inch thick, and should not show any signs of wear or damage.

#### Replace Brake Pads
If the brake pads are worn or damaged, replace them with new ones. The brake pads can be replaced with high-performance brake pads.

#### Check Brake Rotors
Check the brake rotors to ensure that they are in good condition. The brake rotors should be free of cracks or damage, and should not show any signs of wear.

#### Replace Brake Rotors
If the brake rotors are worn or damaged, replace them with new ones. The brake rotors can be replaced with high-performance brake rotors.
