## Step-by-Step Guide
### Oil Change
#### Step 1: Gather materials
Gather the necessary materials, including oil filter wrench, new oil filter, and enough motor oil to fill the engine.

#### Step 2: Warm up the engine
Drive the vehicle for a few minutes to warm up the engine. This will help the oil drain more easily.

#### Step 3: Locate the oil drain plug
Locate the oil drain plug, which is usually located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the vehicle.

#### Step 4: Drain the oil
Use a socket wrench to remove the oil drain plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.

#### Step 5: Replace the oil filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old oil filter and replace it with a new one.

#### Step 6: Refill the oil
Refill the engine with new oil, using the recommended type and amount.

### Tire Rotation
#### Step 1: Gather materials
Gather the necessary materials, including a tire pressure gauge and a lug wrench.

#### Step 2: Check the tire pressure
Check the tire pressure and adjust it to the recommended level.

#### Step 3: Loosen the lug nuts
Loosen the lug nuts on the first wheel using a lug wrench.

#### Step 4: Raise the vehicle
Raise the vehicle using a jack and support it with jack stands.

#### Step 5: Remove the wheel
Remove the wheel and rotate it to the next position.

#### Step 6: Repeat the process
Repeat the process for the remaining wheels.

### Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 1: Gather materials
Gather the necessary materials, including new brake pads, a brake pad wear sensor, and a torque wrench.

#### Step 2: Loosen the lug nuts
Loosen the lug nuts on the first wheel using a lug wrench.

#### Step 3: Raise the vehicle
Raise the vehicle using a jack and support it with jack stands.

#### Step 4: Remove the wheel
Remove the wheel and remove the caliper.

#### Step 5: Remove the old brake pads
Remove the old brake pads and replace them with new ones.

#### Step 6: Reinstall the caliper
Reinstall the caliper and tighten the lug nuts.

### Suspension Inspection
#### Step 1: Gather materials
Gather the necessary materials, including a torch and a socket wrench.

#### Step 2: Locate the suspension components
Locate the suspension components, including the adaptive dampers and limited-slip differential.

#### Step 3: Inspect the components
Inspect the components for any signs of wear or damage.

#### Step 4: Check the fluid levels
Check the fluid levels in the adaptive dampers and limited-slip differential.

#### Step 5: Test the suspension
Test the suspension by driving the vehicle over a rough road.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the recommended type and amount of motor oil.
* Never mix different types of brake fluid.
* Always tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern to avoid warping the brake rotor.
