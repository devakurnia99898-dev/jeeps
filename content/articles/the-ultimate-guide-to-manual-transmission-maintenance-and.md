## Technical Deep Dive
A manual transmission is a complex system that relies on a series of gears, bearings, and synchronizers to transmit power from the engine to the wheels. The system works by using a combination of hydraulic and mechanical components to engage and disengage the gears, allowing the driver to shift smoothly and efficiently.

Common failure points in manual transmissions include the clutch, synchronizers, and gear sets. The clutch is responsible for engaging and disengaging the engine from the transmission, and can wear out over time due to heavy use or improper driving techniques. Synchronizers are responsible for matching the speed of the gears during shifting, and can become worn or damaged if the transmission is not properly maintained. The gear sets themselves can also become worn or damaged, leading to grinding or crunching noises during shifting.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use proper safety precautions when working with manual transmissions, including protective gloves and eyewear.
* Make sure to properly align the clutch and gear sets during reassembly to avoid any issues with shifting or performance.
* Don't overtighten the transmission mounts or crossmember, as this can cause damage to the surrounding components.
