## The Diagnostic / The Situation
Turbulent Jet Ignition (TJI) is a revolutionary technology that has been gaining attention in the automotive industry. Originally developed for Formula 1 engines, TJI has been adapted for use in production vehicles, including some Stellantis models. But what exactly is TJI, and how does it differ from traditional spark plug technology? In this guide, we'll delve into the technical background of TJI, its symptoms, and signs to look out for.

TJI is designed to improve engine performance, particularly in smaller engines. By creating a more efficient combustion process, TJI enables engines to produce more power and torque while reducing emissions. The technology uses a combination of air and fuel to create a turbulent jet of mixture that ignites the fuel, resulting in a more complete burn. This, in turn, leads to improved engine efficiency, reduced fuel consumption, and lower emissions.

## Technical Deep Dive
So, how does TJI work? The system uses a pre-chamber to create a turbulent jet of mixture that ignites the fuel. The pre-chamber is a small, secondary combustion chamber that is connected to the main combustion chamber. When the piston reaches the top of its stroke, a small amount of fuel is injected into the pre-chamber, where it mixes with air. The mixture is then ignited, creating a turbulent jet that enters the main combustion chamber, igniting the fuel and causing a more complete burn.

One of the key advantages of TJI is its ability to improve engine performance at low engine speeds. Traditional spark plug technology can struggle to ignite the fuel at low speeds, resulting in reduced engine performance. TJI, on the other hand, can create a more efficient combustion process, even at low engine speeds, resulting in improved torque and power.

Common failure points in TJI systems include the pre-chamber, fuel injectors, and ignition system. The pre-chamber can become clogged with debris, reducing the effectiveness of the TJI system. Fuel injectors can become dirty or clogged, reducing the amount of fuel that is injected into the pre-chamber. The ignition system can also fail, preventing the TJI system from functioning properly.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Introduction to TJI System Maintenance
TJI system maintenance is essential to ensure the system functions correctly. Here's a step-by-step guide to maintaining a TJI system:

#### Step 1: Locate the TJI System
The TJI system is typically located on the side of the engine. It's essential to consult the vehicle's repair manual to locate the system.

#### Step 2: Remove the Intake Manifold
To access the TJI system, you'll need to remove the intake manifold. This requires a 10mm socket and a torque wrench.

#### Step 3: Clean the Pre-Chamber
Use a pre-chamber cleaning kit to clean the pre-chamber. This will help remove any debris that may be reducing the effectiveness of the TJI system.

#### Step 4: Clean the Fuel Injectors
Use a fuel injector cleaning kit to clean the fuel injectors. This will help remove any debris that may be reducing the amount of fuel that is injected into the pre-chamber.

#### Step 5: Inspect the Ignition System
Use an ignition system diagnostic tool to inspect the ignition system. This will help identify any issues with the system.
