## The Diagnostic / The Situation
GM's Active Fuel Management (AFM) and Dynamic Fuel Management (DFM) systems are designed to improve fuel efficiency by deactivating cylinders when they are not needed. However, these systems have been known to cause lifter failure, which can lead to costly repairs. It is essential to understand the technical background of these systems and the symptoms to look for to prevent lifter failure.

The AFM and DFM systems use a combination of sensors and solenoids to control the flow of oil to the lifters, which in turn control the movement of the valves. When a lifter fails, it can cause a range of problems, including decreased fuel efficiency, reduced power, and potentially even engine damage.

Symptoms of lifter failure include:

* A ticking or tapping noise from the engine
* Decreased fuel efficiency
* Reduced power
* Check engine light illumination

## Technical Deep Dive
The AFM and DFM systems are complex and rely on a range of components to function correctly. The system consists of:

* A control module that monitors the engine's operating conditions and determines when to deactivate cylinders
* A set of solenoids that control the flow of oil to the lifters
* A set of lifters that control the movement of the valves
* A set of sensors that monitor the engine's operating conditions, including oil pressure, engine speed, and load

The most common failure point in the AFM and DFM systems is the hydraulic lifter. The lifter is responsible for controlling the movement of the valve, and when it fails, it can cause a range of problems. The lifter is also the most difficult component to replace, as it requires specialized tools and expertise.
