## Technical Deep Dive
The Jeep's electrical system is a complex network of wires, circuits, and components. It's essential to understand how the system works to identify potential vulnerabilities. The electrical system consists of:
* Battery: The heart of the electrical system, responsible for storing and providing power.
* Alternator: Charges the battery and powers the electrical system.
* Wiring harness: A network of wires that connects the various components.
* Computer systems: The brain of the vehicle, controlling everything from the engine to the entertainment system.

Common failure points include:
* The wiring harness, which can be prone to damage or tampering.
* The computer systems, which can be vulnerable to hacking or malware.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Inspect the Wiring Harness
#### Step 1.1: Locate the Wiring Harness
The wiring harness is typically located under the dashboard or in the engine compartment. Use a wiring diagram to identify the specific location.
#### Step 1.2: Inspect for Damage or Tampering
Use a multimeter to test for any damage or tampering. Check for signs of wear, fraying, or cuts.

### Step 2: Secure the Computer Systems
#### Step 2.1: Update the Software
Ensure the computer systems are up-to-date with the latest software. This can help protect against hacking or malware.
#### Step 2.2: Install a Firewall
Consider installing a firewall to protect the computer systems from external threats.

### Step 3: Protect the Battery and Alternator
#### Step 3.1: Install a Battery Cut-Off Switch
Install a battery cut-off switch to prevent unauthorized access to the electrical system.
#### Step 3.2: Secure the Alternator
Use a Torx screwdriver to secure the alternator and prevent tampering.
