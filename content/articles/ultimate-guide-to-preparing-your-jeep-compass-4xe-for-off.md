## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation and Inspection
Before heading out on your adventure, it's crucial to inspect your vehicle's essential systems. Start by checking the oil level, tire pressure, and brake pads. Make sure all fluids are at the recommended levels, and the battery is fully charged.

#### Checking the Oil Level
1. Locate the oil dipstick under the hood.
2. Pull out the dipstick and wipe it clean with a rag.
3. Insert the dipstick back into the oil reservoir and pull it out again.
4. Check the oil level against the minimum and maximum marks on the dipstick.

#### Inspecting the Tires
1. Check the tire pressure using a tire pressure gauge.
2. Compare the pressure to the recommended level, which can be found on the tire information placard or in the owner's manual.
3. Inspect the tires for any signs of wear or damage, such as uneven tread wear or cracks in the sidewalls.

### Suspension and Steering Check
The suspension and steering systems play a critical role in your vehicle's off-road capability. Inspect the shocks, struts, and control arms for any signs of wear or damage.

#### Checking the Shocks and Struts
1. Jack up the vehicle and support it with jack stands.
2. Inspect the shocks and struts for any signs of leakage or damage.
3. Check the shock absorber's rebound and compression by bouncing the vehicle gently.

#### Checking the Control Arms
1. Inspect the control arms for any signs of wear or damage.
2. Check the ball joints and bushings for any signs of play or wear.

### Electrical System Check
The electrical system is critical for the Compass 4xe's operation, particularly the electric motor and battery management system. Inspect the wiring and connections for any signs of damage or corrosion.

#### Checking the Wiring and Connections
1. Inspect the wiring and connections for any signs of damage or corrosion.
2. Check the battery terminals and cables for any signs of wear or corrosion.
