## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Warm up the engine
Drive the car for a few minutes to warm up the engine.
#### Step 1.2: Locate the oil drain plug
Find the oil drain plug, usually located at the bottom of the engine.
#### Step 1.3: Drain the oil
Use a socket wrench to remove the oil drain plug and drain the oil into a pan.
#### Step 1.4: Replace the oil filter
Remove the old oil filter and replace it with a new one.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake calipers.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the caliper
Use a wrench to remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.
#### Step 2.3: Remove the old brake pads
Pull out the old brake pads and discard them.
#### Step 2.4: Install the new brake pads
Insert the new brake pads and reassemble the caliper.

### Step 3: Suspension Alignment
#### Step 3.1: Raise the car
Use a lift or jack stands to raise the car and access the suspension components.
#### Step 3.2: Loosen the suspension bolts
Loosen the bolts that hold the suspension components in place.
#### Step 3.3: Adjust the suspension
Use a suspension alignment tool to adjust the suspension to the recommended settings.
#### Step 3.4: Tighten the suspension bolts
Tighten the suspension bolts to secure the suspension components in place.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't over-tighten the suspension bolts, as this can damage the components.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure accurate torque settings.
* Don't neglect to check the brake fluid level after replacing the brake pads.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
The cost of maintaining and upgrading a single-seater racing car like the Dallara can vary widely, depending on the specific tasks and components involved. DIY enthusiasts can save money by performing tasks like oil changes and brake pad replacements themselves, but may need to hire a professional mechanic for more complex tasks like suspension alignment. The cost of parts can range from a few hundred dollars for oil and filters to several thousand dollars for major components like engines and transmissions.

2. **Q: How often should I replace the brake pads?**

3. **Q: Can I upgrade my Dallara's engine to increase performance?**

4. **Q: How do I know if my Dallara's suspension is out of alignment?**
