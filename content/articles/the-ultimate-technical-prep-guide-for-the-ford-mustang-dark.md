# Technical Deep Dive
The Ford Mustang Dark Horse SC features a 5.0L V8 engine, producing 500 horsepower and 418 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission and a rear-wheel-drive layout. The suspension system consists of a double-ball-joint front suspension and an integral-link rear suspension.

## Common Failure Points
Common failure points in the Dark Horse SC include:
* The plastic impeller in the water pump, which can fail due to overheating or debris
* The engine's PCV system, which can become clogged and cause oil leaks
* The suspension's ball joints, which can wear out over time and affect handling

# Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Engine Preparation
#### Step 1.1: Oil Change
Change the engine oil to a high-performance synthetic oil, such as 5W-30 or 5W-40. Use a oil filter wrench to remove the old filter and dispose of it properly.

#### Step 1.2: Spark Plug Replacement
Replace the spark plugs with high-performance plugs, such as platinum or iridium. Use a spark plug socket to remove the old plugs and install the new ones.

#### Step 1.3: Air Filter Replacement
Replace the air filter with a high-performance filter, such as a cold air intake. Use a screwdriver to remove the old filter and install the new one.

### Step 2: Suspension Preparation
#### Step 2.1: Ball Joint Inspection
Inspect the ball joints for wear or damage. Use a ball joint press to replace any damaged joints.

#### Step 2.2: Shock Absorber Replacement
Replace the shock absorbers with high-performance units, such as coilovers or adjustable shocks. Use a spring compressor to remove the old shocks and install the new ones.

#### Step 2.3: Sway Bar Replacement
Replace the sway bar with a high-performance unit, such as a adjustable sway bar. Use a wrench to remove the old sway bar and install the new one.

### Step 3: Brake Preparation
#### Step 3.1: Brake Pad Replacement
Replace the brake pads with high-performance pads, such as ceramic or carbon fiber. Use a brake pad spreader to remove the old pads and install the new ones.

#### Step 3.2: Brake Rotor Replacement
Replace the brake rotors with high-performance rotors, such as slotted or drilled rotors. Use a brake rotor puller to remove the old rotors and install the new ones.

#### Step 3.3: Brake Fluid Replacement
Replace the brake fluid with a high-performance fluid, such as DOT 4 or DOT 5.1. Use a brake fluid bleeding kit to remove the old fluid and install the new one.
