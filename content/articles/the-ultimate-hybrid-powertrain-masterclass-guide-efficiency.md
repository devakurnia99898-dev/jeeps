## Technical Deep Dive
A hybrid powertrain combines a conventional internal combustion engine with an electric motor and battery pack. The system works by using the electric motor to assist the engine during acceleration and braking, while also capturing kinetic energy and converting it into electrical energy. This energy is then stored in the battery pack and used to power the electric motor.

Common failure points in hybrid powertrains include the battery pack, electric motor, and power electronics. The battery pack is susceptible to degradation over time, which can reduce its capacity and overall system efficiency. The electric motor can also experience wear and tear, particularly in high-performance applications. Power electronics, such as the inverter and converter, can fail due to overheating or electrical surges.
