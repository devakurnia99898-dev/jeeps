## The Diagnostic / The Situation
When it comes to armoring a vehicle, it's not just about slapping on some extra metal and calling it a day. The process requires careful consideration of various factors, including the type of threat the vehicle will face, the level of protection required, and the potential impact on the vehicle's performance and handling. For luxury cars like the Mercedes S-Class, the stakes are even higher, as these vehicles are often used by high-profile individuals who require a high level of protection.

Symptoms or signs that a vehicle may require armoring include:
* High-risk occupation or lifestyle
* Frequent travel to high-risk areas
* Previous threats or attacks
* High net worth or public profile

## Technical Deep Dive
The process of armoring a vehicle involves several key components, including:
* Ballistic protection: This involves the use of armor plating to protect the vehicle from ballistic threats such as bullets and shrapnel.
* Blast protection: This involves the use of specialized materials and designs to protect the vehicle from explosive blasts.
* Reinforced doors and windows: These are designed to provide additional protection against ballistic and blast threats.
* Run-flat tires: These allow the vehicle to continue running even if the tires are damaged or deflated.

Common failure points in armored vehicles include:
* The armor plating itself, which can be compromised by high-velocity bullets or explosive blasts.
* The doors and windows, which can be vulnerable to ballistic and blast threats.
* The suspension and chassis, which can be damaged by the added weight of the armor plating.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Assessment and Planning
#### Step 1.1: Determine the level of protection required
Determine the type of threats the vehicle will face and the level of protection required. This will help determine the type and amount of armor plating needed.
#### Step 1.2: Choose the armor plating material

Design the armor plating layout to ensure maximum protection and minimal weight and space impact.

#### Step 2.1: Cut and shape the armor plating

#### Step 2.3: Test the armor plating
Test the armor plating to ensure it meets the required level of protection.
