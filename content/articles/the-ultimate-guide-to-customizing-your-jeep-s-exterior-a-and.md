## Step-by-Step Guide
### Remove Old Decals
#### Step 1: Clean the Area
Start by cleaning the area around the old decals with soap and water. This will help loosen the adhesive and make removal easier.
#### Step 2: Apply Decal Remover
Apply a decal remover to the old decals and let it sit for a few minutes. This will help break down the adhesive and make removal easier.
#### Step 3: Remove Decals
Use a credit card or similar tool to gently scrape away the old decals. Be careful not to scratch the paint.

### Apply New Decals
#### Step 1: Clean the Area
Clean the area where the new decals will be applied with soap and water. This will help ensure a strong bond between the decal and the paint.
#### Step 2: Apply Decal
Apply the new decal to the area, starting from the center and working your way outwards. Use a credit card or similar tool to smooth out any air bubbles.
#### Step 3: Seal Decal
Apply a clear coat of sealant to the decal to protect it from the elements.

### Paint Exterior Components
#### Step 1: Prepare the Area
Mask off the surrounding areas with tape and paper to prevent overspray.
#### Step 2: Sand the Area
Sand the area to be painted with sandpaper to roughen the surface.
#### Step 3: Apply Primer
Apply a coat of primer to the area to be painted. This will help the paint adhere to the surface.
#### Step 4: Apply Paint
Apply a coat of paint to the area, following the manufacturer's instructions.
#### Step 5: Apply Clear Coat
Apply a clear coat of sealant to the paint to protect it from the elements.

### Install New Bumpers
#### Step 1: Remove Old Bumpers
Remove the old bumpers by unscrewing the bolts that hold them in place.
#### Step 2: Install New Bumpers
Install the new bumpers by screwing them into place with the bolts provided.
#### Step 3: Tighten Bolts
Tighten the bolts to the specified torque to ensure a secure fit.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use a level to ensure the decals are straight and level.
* Make sure the area is clean and dry before applying decals or paint.
* Don't apply too much pressure when scraping away old decals, as this can scratch the paint.
* Use a high-quality paint and clear coat to ensure a durable finish.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What type of decals should I use?**
You can use either vinyl or magnetic decals, depending on your preference. Vinyl decals are more permanent, while magnetic decals are removable.
2. **How do I remove old decals without damaging the paint?**
Use a decal remover and a credit card or similar tool to gently scrape away the old decals. Be careful not to scratch the paint.
3. **What type of paint should I use?**
Use a high-quality paint that is specifically designed for exterior use. Make sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions for application and drying times.
4. **How do I ensure a smooth finish when painting?**
Use a roller and tray to apply the paint, and make sure to work in a well-ventilated area. Apply thin coats of paint, allowing each coat to dry before applying the next.
5. **Can I install new bumpers myself?**
Yes, you can install new bumpers yourself, but it's recommended to have a professional mechanic do the job if you're not experienced with automotive repairs.
