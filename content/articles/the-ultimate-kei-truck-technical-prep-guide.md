## Technical Deep Dive
The Kei Truck's engine is a critical component of its drifting capabilities. The typical Kei Truck engine is a small, high-revving unit that produces moderate power and torque. However, to achieve the desired level of drift performance, the engine may require upgrades such as a turbocharger or supercharger, high-flow exhaust system, and performance camshaft.

The suspension system is another critical component of the Kei Truck's drifting capabilities. The standard suspension setup is designed for on-road use, but for drifting, a more aggressive setup with increased suspension travel and damping is required. This can be achieved through the installation of coilovers, sway bars, and performance springs.

Common failure points on the Kei Truck include the drivetrain, which can be prone to wear and tear due to the high stresses imposed by drifting. The transmission, differential, and axles may require upgrading to withstand the increased stress.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Engine Upgrades
The first step in modifying the Kei Truck for drifting is to upgrade the engine. This can be achieved through the installation of a turbocharger or supercharger, high-flow exhaust system, and performance camshaft.
#### Step 1.1: Remove the standard exhaust system
Remove the standard exhaust system and replace it with a high-flow exhaust system. This will improve engine performance and reduce backpressure.

#### Step 2.3: Install performance springs
Install performance springs to improve suspension travel and damping.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When modifying a Kei Truck for drifting, it's essential to avoid common mistakes. One of the most common mistakes is over-tightening the suspension, which can lead to reduced suspension travel and increased wear and tear.
Another common mistake is underestimating the importance of proper alignment and suspension setup. Proper alignment and suspension setup are critical to achieving optimal drifting performance.

To avoid these mistakes, it's essential to follow proper installation procedures and consult with experienced mechanics or drifters.
