## Technical Deep Dive
The Oldsmobile Toronado is a rear-wheel-drive vehicle with a V8 engine, which provides ample power for traversing rough terrain. However, its age and design may make it more susceptible to damage from extreme weather conditions. Common failure points in the Toronado include:
* The plastic impeller in the water pump, which can crack and cause overheating
* The rear axle, which can be prone to damage from rough terrain and extreme weather conditions
* The electrical system, which can be vulnerable to water and moisture

To prepare the Toronado for tornado chasing, we'll need to address these common failure points and upgrade the vehicle's systems to withstand extreme weather conditions.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation and Inspection
#### Step 1: Inspect the Vehicle's Body and Chassis
Inspect the vehicle's body and chassis for signs of rust and corrosion. Use a wire brush and sandpaper to clean and prepare the surface for repairs.
#### Step 2: Replace the Water Pump and Radiator
Replace the water pump and radiator with heavy-duty components designed for extreme weather conditions. Use a torque wrench to ensure proper tightening of the water pump bolts (30 ft-lbs).
#### Step 3: Upgrade the Suspension and Steering System
Upgrade the suspension and steering system with heavy-duty components, including coil springs, shock absorbers, and a steering stabilizer. Use a torque wrench to ensure proper tightening of the suspension bolts (40 ft-lbs).

### Electrical System Upgrades
#### Step 4: Inspect and Repair the Electrical System
Inspect the electrical system for signs of damage or wear. Repair or replace damaged components, including wiring, fuses, and relays.
#### Step 5: Install a Heavy-Duty Alternator and Battery
Install a heavy-duty alternator and battery designed for extreme weather conditions. Use a torque wrench to ensure proper tightening of the alternator bolts (20 ft-lbs).

### Engine and Transmission Upgrades
#### Step 6: Inspect and Repair the Engine and Transmission
Inspect the engine and transmission for signs of damage or wear. Repair or replace damaged components, including piston rings, bearings, and seals.
#### Step 7: Install a Heavy-Duty Engine Oil Cooler
Install a heavy-duty engine oil cooler designed for extreme weather conditions. Use a torque wrench to ensure proper tightening of the oil cooler bolts (30 ft-lbs).
