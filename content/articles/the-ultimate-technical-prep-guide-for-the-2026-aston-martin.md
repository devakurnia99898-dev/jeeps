## Technical Deep Dive
The DB12 S features a 5.2-liter twin-turbo V12 engine, producing 715 horsepower and 664 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission and features an all-wheel-drive system. The suspension system consists of adaptive damping, which provides a smooth ride and exceptional handling.

Common failure points in the DB12 S include the turbochargers, which can fail due to excessive heat, and the transmission, which can experience issues with the torque converter. Regular maintenance, such as oil changes and filter replacements, can help prevent these issues.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
The oil change is a crucial maintenance task that should be performed every 5,000 miles. To start, locate the oil drain plug, which is usually located at the bottom of the engine.
#### Step 1.1: Jack Up the Vehicle
Raise the vehicle using a hydraulic jack, ensuring it is securely supported by jack stands.
#### Step 1.2: Remove the Oil Drain Plug
Use a 10mm socket to remove the oil drain plug, allowing the oil to drain into a pan.
#### Step 1.3: Replace the Oil Filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old oil filter, and replace it with a new one.
#### Step 1.4: Refill the Oil
Refill the oil using the recommended type and amount, usually 6 quarts of 5W-30.

### Step 2: Filter Replacement
The air filter and cabin filter should be replaced every 15,000 miles to ensure optimal performance and air quality.
#### Step 2.1: Locate the Air Filter
Find the air filter housing, usually located in the engine compartment.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the Air Filter
Use a 12mm socket to remove the air filter housing, and pull out the old filter.
#### Step 2.3: Install the New Air Filter
Insert the new air filter, ensuring it is properly seated and secured.
#### Step 2.4: Replace the Cabin Filter
Locate the cabin filter, usually located behind the glove box.
#### Step 2.5: Remove the Cabin Filter
Use a screwdriver to remove the cabin filter housing, and pull out the old filter.
#### Step 2.6: Install the New Cabin Filter
Insert the new cabin filter, ensuring it is properly seated and secured.

### Step 3: Tire Rotation
Tire rotation should be performed every 5,000 miles to ensure even tread wear and extend the life of the tires.
#### Step 3.1: Jack Up the Vehicle
Raise the vehicle using a hydraulic jack, ensuring it is securely supported by jack stands.
#### Step 3.2: Remove the Wheels
Use a 10mm socket to remove the lug nuts, and pull off the wheels.
#### Step 3.3: Rotate the Tires
Rotate the tires according to the recommended pattern, usually rearward cross.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
