## Technical Deep Dive
A vehicle's suspension system consists of several components, including:
* Springs (coils, leaf, or torsion bars)
* Shocks (dampers)
* Control arms
* Bushings
* Stabilizer bars

The suspension system's primary function is to absorb bumps and vibrations, maintaining tire contact with the road and providing a stable ride. Common failure points in a suspension system include:
* Worn-out bushings
* Damaged control arms
* Leaking shocks
* Weak or broken springs

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Inspect and Prepare the Vehicle
#### Step 1.1: Lift and Support the Vehicle
Raise the vehicle using a hydraulic lift or jack stands, ensuring it is securely supported.
#### Step 1.2: Remove Wheels and Brakes
Remove the wheels and brakes to access the suspension components.
#### Step 1.3: Inspect Suspension Components
Inspect the suspension components for any signs of wear or damage.

### Step 2: Upgrade the Springs
#### Step 2.1: Remove Old Springs
Remove the old springs using a spring compressor.
#### Step 2.2: Install New Springs
Install the new springs, ensuring they are properly seated and aligned.
#### Step 2.3: Tighten Spring Retainers
Tighten the spring retainers to the recommended torque specification (30-40 Nm).

### Step 3: Upgrade the Shocks
#### Step 3.1: Remove Old Shocks
Remove the old shocks using a shock absorber compressor.
#### Step 3.2: Install New Shocks
Install the new shocks, ensuring they are properly seated and aligned.
#### Step 3.3: Tighten Shock Mounts
Tighten the shock mounts to the recommended torque specification (40-50 Nm).

### Step 4: Upgrade the Control Arms
#### Step 4.1: Remove Old Control Arms
Remove the old control arms, taking note of the bushing orientation.
#### Step 4.2: Install New Control Arms
Install the new control arms, ensuring the bushings are properly oriented.
#### Step 4.3: Tighten Control Arm Mounts
Tighten the control arm mounts to the recommended torque specification (50-60 Nm).

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always follow the recommended torque specifications for suspension components
* Use a spring compressor to avoid damaging the springs or surrounding components
* Never reuse old bushings or control arms, as they may be worn out or damaged
