# The Diagnostic / The Situation
The Saturn SC is a sports car that was produced from 1991 to 2002. While it was known for its agile handling and sleek design, it was not particularly renowned for its power output. The base model came with a 1.9L inline-four engine that produced around 100 horsepower, while the higher-end models had a 1.9L turbocharged engine that produced around 180 horsepower. However, for those who wanted even more power, a factory-turbocharged SC was never an option. This is where the art of turbocharging comes in - a delicate process that requires careful planning, precision engineering, and a thorough understanding of the underlying mechanics.

## The Diagnostic / The Situation
When considering turbocharging a Saturn SC, it's essential to understand the technical background and the potential risks involved. The primary symptom of a lack of power in a Saturn SC is, of course, a lack of acceleration and responsiveness. However, other signs to look for include decreased fuel efficiency, rough idling, and a general feeling of sluggishness. To diagnose the issue, it's crucial to inspect the engine, suspension, and electrical systems to determine the root cause of the problem.

## Technical Deep Dive
The Saturn SC's engine is a 1.9L inline-four cylinder engine that uses a dual-overhead camshaft design. The engine features a cast-iron block, aluminum cylinder head, and a timing chain that drives the camshafts. The engine also features a fuel-injected system that uses a throttle body and a fuel pump to deliver fuel to the engine. When it comes to turbocharging, the key is to force more air into the engine's combustion chamber, allowing for a significant increase in power output. However, this also puts additional stress on the engine's components, particularly the pistons, rings, and cylinder walls. Common failure points to look out for include the turbocharger itself, the intercooler, and the engine's oil and cooling systems.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Prepare the Engine
The first step in turbocharging a Saturn SC is to prepare the engine for the increased stress and power output. This involves upgrading the engine's oil and cooling systems to handle the additional heat and pressure. This can be done by installing a high-performance oil pump, oil cooler, and radiator.

#### Step 1.1: Upgrade the Oil Pump
To upgrade the oil pump, you'll need to purchase a high-performance oil pump kit that includes a new pump, pickup tube, and oil filter. Install the new pump according to the manufacturer's instructions, ensuring all connections are secure and tightened to the specified torque.

#### Step 1.2: Install an Oil Cooler
An oil cooler is essential for keeping the engine's oil at a safe temperature, especially when running a turbocharger. Install an oil cooler kit that includes a new cooler, hoses, and fittings. Ensure all connections are secure and tightened to the specified torque.

### Step 2: Install the Turbocharger
With the engine prepared, the next step is to install the turbocharger. This involves installing the turbocharger itself, as well as the necessary piping and fittings.

#### Step 2.1: Install the Turbocharger
Install the turbocharger according to the manufacturer's instructions, ensuring all connections are secure and tightened to the specified torque. Connect the turbocharger to the engine's exhaust manifold and downpipe.

#### Step 2.2: Install the Intercooler
An intercooler is essential for cooling the compressed air before it enters the engine's combustion chamber. Install an intercooler kit that includes a new intercooler, hoses, and fittings. Ensure all connections are secure and tightened to the specified torque.

### Step 3: Upgrade the Fuel System
With the turbocharger installed, the next step is to upgrade the fuel system to handle the increased demand for fuel. This involves installing a high-performance fuel pump, fuel injectors, and fuel filter.

#### Step 3.1: Upgrade the Fuel Pump
To upgrade the fuel pump, you'll need to purchase a high-performance fuel pump kit that includes a new pump, fuel filter, and fuel lines. Install the new pump according to the manufacturer's instructions, ensuring all connections are secure and tightened to the specified torque.

#### Step 3.2: Install High-Performance Fuel Injectors
High-performance fuel injectors are essential for delivering the necessary amount of fuel to the engine's combustion chamber. Install a set of high-performance fuel injectors according to the manufacturer's instructions, ensuring all connections are secure and tightened to the specified torque.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When turbocharging a Saturn SC, there are several common mistakes to avoid. One of the most critical mistakes is not upgrading the engine's oil and cooling systems, which can lead to overheating and engine damage. Another common mistake is not properly sizing the turbocharger, which can result in inadequate power output or excessive boost pressure.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **Q: What is the best turbocharger for a Saturn SC?**
A: The best turbocharger for a Saturn SC depends on the specific engine and desired power output. However, a popular option is the Garrett GT28 turbocharger, which offers a good balance of power and reliability.
2. **Q: How much power can I expect from a turbocharged Saturn SC?**
A: The power output of a turbocharged Saturn SC depends on the specific turbocharger, engine modifications, and tuning. However, a well-tuned turbocharged Saturn SC can produce anywhere from 250 to 400 horsepower.
3. **Q: Do I need to upgrade the engine's internals for a turbocharged Saturn SC?**
A: Yes, it's highly recommended to upgrade the engine's internals, including the pistons, rings, and cylinder walls, to handle the increased stress and pressure of a turbocharger.
4. **Q: Can I install a turbocharger on a Saturn SC without any engine modifications?**
A: While it's technically possible to install a turbocharger on a Saturn SC without any engine modifications, it's not recommended. The engine's oil and cooling systems, as well as the fuel system, should be upgraded to handle the increased stress and demand.
5. **Q: How long does it take to install a turbocharger on a Saturn SC?**
A: The installation time for a turbocharger on a Saturn SC can vary widely, depending on the specific components and labor involved. However, a typical DIY installation can take anywhere from 20 to 30 hours, while a professional mechanic may take several days to complete the job.
