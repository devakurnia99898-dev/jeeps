## Technical Deep Dive
To comprehend the proposed EV road fee, we must first understand how EVs work and their maintenance needs. EVs use electric motors powered by batteries, which are charged through an external power source. The battery management system (BMS) plays a crucial role in maintaining the health and efficiency of the batteries. Common failure points in EVs include the battery pack, electric motor, and charging system.

The battery pack is a critical component, and its health can significantly impact the overall performance and range of the EV. The BMS monitors the battery's state of charge, voltage, and temperature to ensure optimal performance. However, the BMS can also be a common failure point, as it's a complex system that requires precise calibration.

## Step-by-Step Guide
Now that we've covered the pre-work checklist, let's dive into the step-by-step guide for EV maintenance.

### Battery Maintenance
Battery maintenance is crucial for EV owners. Here's a step-by-step guide:
#### Check the Battery State of Charge
Use a battery tester to check the state of charge. Ensure the battery is at least 50% charged before performing any maintenance tasks.
#### Check the Battery Voltage
Use a multimeter to check the battery voltage. The voltage should be within the recommended range specified by the manufacturer.
#### Check the Battery Temperature
Use a thermometer to check the battery temperature. The temperature should be within the recommended range specified by the manufacturer.

### Tire Rotation
Tire rotation is essential for maintaining even tire wear and ensuring optimal traction.
#### Jack Up the Vehicle
Use a hydraulic jack to lift the vehicle, ensuring the tires are off the ground.
#### Remove the Wheels
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheels, taking care not to damage the wheel studs.
#### Rotate the Tires
Rotate the tires according to the recommended pattern specified by the manufacturer.

### Brake Pad Replacement
Brake pad replacement is a critical maintenance task for EV owners.
#### Remove the Wheels
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheels, taking care not to damage the wheel studs.
#### Remove the Caliper
Use a socket wrench to remove the caliper, taking care not to damage the brake hose.
#### Remove the Old Brake Pads
Use a screwdriver to remove the old brake pads, taking care not to damage the brake rotor.
