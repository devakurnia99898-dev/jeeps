## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The automatic stop/start technology, also known as Auto Stop-Start or Start/Stop technology, is a feature designed to improve fuel efficiency by shutting off the engine when the vehicle comes to a stop. However, this feature can be annoying to some drivers, especially in certain driving conditions. The symptoms of this feature can include a slight delay in acceleration when taking off from a stop, a slight vibration when the engine restarts, and a potential increase in wear and tear on the starter motor and battery.

## Technical Deep Dive
The automatic stop/start system in Jeeps, such as the Wrangler and Grand Cherokee, typically consists of a combination of sensors, actuators, and control modules. The system uses a variety of inputs, including vehicle speed, engine speed, throttle position, and brake pedal position, to determine when to shut off the engine. The system also uses a high-capacity battery and a more robust starter motor to handle the increased number of engine starts. Common failure points in this system can include the starter motor, battery, and control module.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Disabling the Automatic Stop/Start System
#### Step 1: Locate the Auto Stop-Start Control Module
The Auto Stop-Start control module is usually located in the engine compartment, near the battery. It may be held in place by a bracket or clip.
#### Step 2: Disconnect the Battery
Disconnect the negative (black) cable from the battery to prevent any accidental starts or electrical shocks.
#### Step 3: Remove the Control Module
Remove the control module from its bracket or clip. This may require a 10mm socket or other tools.
#### Step 4: Identify the Auto Stop-Start Wiring
Identify the wiring harness that connects to the control module. This may include a variety of sensors and actuators.
#### Step 5: Disable the Auto Stop-Start System
Using a scan tool or other diagnostic equipment, disable the Auto Stop-Start system. This may require reprogramming the control module or replacing it with a modified version.
#### Step 6: Reinstall the Control Module
Reinstall the control module and reconnect the wiring harness.
#### Step 7: Reconnect the Battery
Reconnect the negative (black) cable to the battery.
### Alternative Method: Using a Scan Tool
If you have access to a scan tool, you can use it to disable the Auto Stop-Start system. This may require a software update or reprogramming of the control module.
#### Step 1: Connect the Scan Tool
Connect the scan tool to the vehicle's OBD-II port.
#### Step 2: Select the Auto Stop-Start Option
Select the Auto Stop-Start option from the scan tool's menu.
#### Step 3: Disable the Auto Stop-Start System
Follow the scan tool's prompts to disable the Auto Stop-Start system.
