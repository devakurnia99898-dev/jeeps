## Technical Deep Dive
The Honda Pilot's engine is a 3.5L V6, which is a reliable and powerful unit. However, like any engine, it has its common failure points. The timing belt, water pump, and oil pump are all critical components that require regular maintenance. The transmission is a 6-speed automatic, which is smooth and responsive. The suspension is a independent front and rear setup, which provides a comfortable ride and decent handling.

Common failure points on the Honda Pilot include:

* The plastic impeller in the water pump, which can fail over time
* The timing belt, which should be replaced every 100,000 miles
* The oil pump, which can fail due to low oil levels or dirty oil

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspecting the Exterior
#### Checking the Tires
Check the tire pressure and look for any signs of uneven wear. Make sure the tire tread is at least 1/16 of an inch deep.
#### Checking the Body
Look for any dents, scratches, or rust spots on the body. Check the condition of the paint and make sure it's even and glossy.
#### Checking the Lights
Check the condition of the headlights, taillights, and brake lights. Make sure they're all functioning properly.

### Inspecting the Interior
#### Checking the Dashboard
Check the dashboard for any warning lights or error messages. Make sure all the gauges are functioning properly.
#### Checking the Seats
Check the condition of the seats and make sure they're all functioning properly. Look for any signs of wear and tear.
#### Checking the Carpet and Floor Mats
Check the condition of the carpet and floor mats. Look for any signs of water damage or stains.

### Inspecting the Engine
#### Checking the Oil Level
Check the oil level and make sure it's at the recommended level. Look for any signs of oil leaks.
#### Checking the Coolant Level
Check the coolant level and make sure it's at the recommended level. Look for any signs of coolant leaks.
#### Checking the Air Filter
Check the condition of the air filter and make sure it's clean and free of debris.

### Inspecting the Transmission
#### Checking the Transmission Fluid Level
Check the transmission fluid level and make sure it's at the recommended level. Look for any signs of transmission fluid leaks.
#### Checking the Transmission Mounts
Check the condition of the transmission mounts and make sure they're secure and not damaged.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't forget to check the vehicle's history report to see if it's been in any accidents or had any major repairs.
* Make sure to test drive the vehicle to check for any unusual noises or vibrations.
* Don't skip the inspection process, as it can save you from buying a lemon.

The cost of inspecting and maintaining a pre-owned Honda Pilot can vary depending on the extent of the work. If you're doing the work yourself, you can expect to save around $500-$1000. However, if you're taking it to a mechanic, the cost can range from $1000-$2000.
