## Technical Deep Dive
The Jeep Wrangler TJ's engine, suspension, and electrical systems are all critical components that require attention during the restoration process. The 4.0L inline-six engine, for example, is known for its reliability but can suffer from issues such as blown head gaskets and faulty fuel injectors. The suspension system, which includes the coil springs, shocks, and control arms, can become worn out, causing a rough ride and decreased stability. The electrical system, which includes the wiring, battery, and alternator, can also malfunction, leading to issues with the vehicle's lights, radio, and other accessories.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Engine Overhaul
The engine overhaul is a critical step in the restoration process. It involves replacing the engine's internal components, such as the pistons, rings, and bearings.
#### Step 1.1: Remove the engine
Remove the engine from the vehicle and place it on a engine stand.
#### Step 1.2: Disassemble the engine
Disassemble the engine, removing the cylinder head, intake manifold, and oil pan.
#### Step 1.3: Inspect and replace components
Inspect the engine's internal components and replace any that are damaged or worn out.
#### Step 1.4: Reassemble the engine
Reassemble the engine, making sure to torque all bolts and fittings to the specified values.

### Step 2: Suspension Replacement
The suspension replacement involves replacing the coil springs, shocks, and control arms.
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheels
Remove the wheels and place the vehicle on a lift.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the coil springs
Remove the coil springs and shocks, taking note of the orientation and position of the components.
#### Step 2.3: Inspect and replace components
Inspect the suspension components and replace any that are damaged or worn out.
#### Step 2.4: Reassemble the suspension
Reassemble the suspension, making sure to torque all bolts and fittings to the specified values.

### Step 3: Electrical System Repair
The electrical system repair involves inspecting and replacing any faulty components, such as the wiring, battery, and alternator.
#### Step 3.1: Inspect the wiring
Inspect the wiring for any signs of damage or wear.
#### Step 3.2: Replace the battery
Replace the battery if it's old or damaged.
#### Step 3.3: Replace the alternator
Replace the alternator if it's faulty or not charging the battery properly.
#### Step 3.4: Test the electrical system
Test the electrical system to ensure that all components are working properly.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't skip the engine overhaul, as it's a critical step in the restoration process.
* Make sure to torque all bolts and fittings to the specified values to avoid damaging the engine or suspension.
* Use a multimeter to test the electrical system and ensure that all components are working properly.
* Don't forget to replace the air filter and spark plugs, as these can greatly affect the vehicle's performance.
