## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation and Inspection
The driver must begin by inspecting the car's engine, transmission, and chassis, looking for any signs of wear or damage. The fuel system must be checked for leaks and proper function, and the tires must be inflated to the correct pressure.
#### Pre-Race Checklist
1. Check the engine oil and coolant levels
2. Inspect the fuel system for leaks or damage
3. Check the tire pressure and condition
4. Test the communication device and data acquisition system (if equipped)
5. Perform a thorough safety inspection of the car and driver
### Starting the Car
The driver must start the car and allow it to warm up to the correct operating temperature. The fuel system must be primed, and the transmission must be checked for proper function.
#### Starting Procedure
1. Turn on the ignition and allow the car to start
2. Check the fuel system for proper function
3. Allow the car to warm up to the correct operating temperature
4. Test the transmission and gear sets
5. Check the brakes and steering for proper function
### Launching the Car
The driver must launch the car from a standstill, using the correct technique to avoid wheelspin and loss of traction. The car must be accelerated smoothly and consistently, using the correct gear shifts and throttle control.
#### Launch Procedure
1. Stage the car at the starting line
2. Engage the clutch and shift into first gear
3. Apply gentle pressure to the accelerator pedal
4. Monitor the car's speed and traction, making adjustments as necessary
5. Shift into higher gears as the car accelerates
### Shifting and Braking
The driver must shift the car's gears smoothly and consistently, using the correct technique to avoid wheelspin and loss of traction. The brakes must be applied gently and consistently, using the correct technique to avoid lockup and loss of control.
#### Shifting Procedure
1. Shift into the correct gear for the current speed and conditions
2. Use the clutch and accelerator pedals to control the car's speed and traction
3. Monitor the car's speed and gear position, making adjustments as necessary
4. Downshift before entering a turn or applying the brakes
#### Braking Procedure
1. Apply gentle pressure to the brake pedal
2. Monitor the car's speed and traction, making adjustments as necessary
3. Use the correct braking technique to avoid lockup and loss of control
4. Downshift before entering a turn or coming to a stop

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
