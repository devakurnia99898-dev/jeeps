## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Identify the Vehicle
Determine the make, model, and year of the vehicle to ensure the correct maintenance and repair procedures are followed.
#### Sub-Step 1.1: Check the Vehicle Identification Number (VIN)
Locate the VIN on the driver's side dashboard or on the driver's side doorjamb. Use a VIN decoder to determine the vehicle's specifications.
#### Sub-Step 1.2: Consult the Owner's Manual
Review the owner's manual to understand the vehicle's maintenance schedule and any specific recommendations for the shared model name.

### Step 2: Inspect the Vehicle
Perform a thorough inspection of the vehicle to identify any potential issues or areas of concern.
#### Sub-Step 2.1: Check the Engine
Inspect the engine for any signs of wear or damage, including oil leaks, corrosion, or excessive noise.
#### Sub-Step 2.2: Check the Electrical System
Inspect the electrical system for any signs of wear or damage, including frayed wires, corrosion, or malfunctioning components.

### Step 3: Perform Routine Maintenance
Perform routine maintenance tasks, such as oil changes, tire rotations, and brake pad replacements, to ensure the vehicle remains in good working condition.
#### Sub-Step 3.1: Change the Oil
Use a socket wrench to remove the oil drain plug and drain the oil into a pan. Replace the oil filter and refill the engine with the recommended type and amount of oil.
#### Sub-Step 3.2: Rotate the Tires
Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts and rotate the tires according to the recommended pattern.
