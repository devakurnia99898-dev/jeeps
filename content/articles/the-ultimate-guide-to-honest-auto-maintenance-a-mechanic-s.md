## Symptoms or Signs to look for
When it comes to auto maintenance, there are several signs to look out for that may indicate a problem. These include:
* Unusual noises or vibrations
* Decreased fuel efficiency
* Warning lights on the dashboard
* Leaks or unusual smells

## Technical Deep Dive
A car's engine, suspension, and electrical systems are all interconnected and rely on regular maintenance to function properly. The engine, for example, requires regular oil changes to keep the pistons and cylinders lubricated. The suspension system, on the other hand, relies on proper alignment and tire pressure to ensure a smooth ride. The electrical system, which includes the battery, alternator, and starter motor, requires regular checks to prevent corrosion and wear.

### Common failure points
Some common failure points in a car's system include:
* The plastic impeller in the water pump, which can crack and cause the engine to overheat
* The serpentine belt, which can crack and fail, causing the engine to stall
* The brake pads, which can wear down and cause the brakes to fail

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Gather materials
* Oil filter wrench
* New oil filter
* Oil drain pan
* New motor oil
#### Step 1.2: Locate the oil drain plug
* Check the car's manual to find the location of the oil drain plug
* Use a socket wrench to remove the plug
#### Step 1.3: Drain the oil
* Let the oil drain into the pan for at least 10 minutes
* Replace the oil drain plug and tighten to the specified torque

### Step 2: Tire Rotation
#### Step 2.1: Gather materials
* Tire pressure gauge
* Lug wrench
* Wheel chocks
#### Step 2.2: Check the tire pressure
* Use a tire pressure gauge to check the pressure of each tire
* Compare the pressure to the recommended pressure listed on the tire's sidewall
#### Step 2.3: Rotate the tires
* Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts on the first tire
* Raise the tire using a jack and remove the lug nuts
* Rotate the tire to the next position and repeat the process for the remaining tires

### Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Gather materials
* New brake pads
* Brake pad spreader
* C-clamps
#### Step 3.2: Remove the wheel
* Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts on the wheel
* Raise the wheel using a jack and remove the lug nuts
* Remove the wheel and set it aside
#### Step 3.3: Remove the caliper
* Use a wrench to loosen the caliper bolts
* Remove the caliper and set it aside
* Use a brake pad spreader to push the piston back into the caliper

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't over-tighten the lug nuts, as this can damage the wheel or brake rotor
* Make sure to use the correct type of oil for your car, as using the wrong type can damage the engine
* Don't neglect to check the tire pressure regularly, as under-inflated tires can lead to poor fuel efficiency and uneven tire wear

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **Q: How often should I change my oil?**
A: The frequency of oil changes depends on the type of car and driving conditions. Check the car's manual for the recommended oil change interval.
2. **Q: What type of oil should I use?**
A: The type of oil to use depends on the car's engine type and climate. Check the car's manual for the recommended oil type.
3. **Q: How do I know if my brake pads need to be replaced?**
A: If the brake pads are worn down to 1/8 inch or less, they need to be replaced. You may also notice a squealing or grinding noise when applying the brakes.
4. **Q: Can I rotate my tires myself?**
A: Yes, rotating your tires is a relatively simple process that can be done at home with the right tools.
5. **Q: How often should I check my tire pressure?**
A: Check your tire pressure at least once a month, and before long trips. Under-inflated tires can lead to poor fuel efficiency and uneven tire wear.
