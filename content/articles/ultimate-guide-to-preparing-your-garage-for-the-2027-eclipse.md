## Technical Deep Dive
The 2027 Mitsubishi Eclipse Sportback is based on the new Nissan Leaf platform, which suggests that it may share similar electrical and mechanical components. The Nissan Leaf is known for its reliable and efficient electric powertrain, which consists of an electric motor, a lithium-ion battery pack, and a power control unit. The Eclipse Sportback is likely to feature a similar powertrain, with potential upgrades and improvements to enhance performance and range.

Common failure points in EVs like the Nissan Leaf include the battery management system, electric motor, and charging system. The battery management system is responsible for monitoring and controlling the battery's state of charge, voltage, and temperature. Failure of this system can lead to reduced battery life, decreased range, and potentially even a complete system failure.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Prepare the Garage and Tools
Before starting work on the Eclipse Sportback, ensure your garage is equipped with the necessary tools and equipment. This includes a lift, jack stands, and a multimeter. Familiarize yourself with the vehicle's electrical system and components to avoid any potential risks.

#### Understand the Electrical System
The electrical system in the Eclipse Sportback is likely to be similar to that of the Nissan Leaf. Study the wiring diagrams and component locations to understand how the system works and where potential failure points may occur.

#### Inspect the Battery Management System
The battery management system is a critical component in the Eclipse Sportback's electrical system. Inspect the system for any signs of wear or damage, and test the battery's state of charge and voltage using a multimeter.

#### Test the Charging System
The charging system is responsible for replenishing the battery's energy. Test the charging system using a charging system tester to ensure it's functioning correctly.

### Perform Routine Maintenance
Regular maintenance is essential to ensure the Eclipse Sportback's electrical system and components remain in good working condition. This includes checking and replacing the air filter, inspecting the brake pads, and rotating the tires.

#### Check and Replace the Air Filter
The air filter is a critical component in the Eclipse Sportback's electrical system, as it helps to cool the battery and electrical components. Check the air filter regularly and replace it as needed to ensure optimal performance.

#### Inspect the Brake Pads
The brake pads in the Eclipse Sportback are designed to work in conjunction with the regenerative braking system. Inspect the brake pads regularly to ensure they're in good condition and functioning correctly.

#### Rotate the Tires
The tires on the Eclipse Sportback are designed to provide optimal traction and range. Rotate the tires regularly to ensure even wear and tear, and to maintain the vehicle's range and performance.
