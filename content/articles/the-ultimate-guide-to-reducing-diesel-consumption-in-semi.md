## Technical Deep Dive
The system that governs diesel consumption in semi-trucks is complex and involves various components working together in harmony. The engine, transmission, axles, and tires all play a critical role in determining the overall fuel efficiency of a semi-truck.

The engine is the heart of the semi-truck, and its efficiency is critical to reducing diesel consumption. Modern semi-truck engines are designed to provide optimal performance while minimizing fuel consumption. However, common failure points such as faulty fuel injectors, clogged air filters, and worn-out piston rings can significantly reduce engine efficiency.

The transmission and axles also play a crucial role in determining the fuel efficiency of a semi-truck. A well-maintained transmission and axles can help to reduce fuel consumption by ensuring that the engine is operating within its optimal range. However, worn-out transmission components and axles can lead to increased fuel consumption and reduced engine performance.

### Common Failure Points
Some common failure points that can lead to increased diesel consumption include:
* Faulty fuel injectors
* Clogged air filters
* Worn-out piston rings
* Worn-out transmission components
* Misaligned or worn-out axles

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Engine Tune-Up
The engine is the heart of the semi-truck, and a well-maintained engine is critical to reducing diesel consumption.

#### Check the Air Filter
The air filter plays a crucial role in ensuring that the engine receives clean air. A clogged air filter can significantly reduce engine efficiency and increase diesel consumption.

* Inspect the air filter for signs of damage or wear
* Replace the air filter if necessary
