## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparing the Garage for the ES 350e
The first step in preparing your garage for the 2026 Lexus ES 350e is to ensure you have a suitable charging station installed. This will require a 240-volt outlet and a charging cable specifically designed for the ES 350e.

#### Installing the Charging Station
1. Locate a suitable location for the charging station, ideally near the garage door or in a corner.
2. Ensure the area is clear of any obstructions or flammable materials.
3. Install the 240-volt outlet, following the manufacturer's instructions and local electrical codes.
4. Connect the charging cable to the outlet and the ES 350e's charging port.

### Maintaining the ES 350e's Battery
Regular battery maintenance is crucial to ensure the longevity and performance of the ES 350e's electric powertrain.

#### Checking the Battery State of Charge
1. Locate the battery management system (BMS) display on the ES 350e's dashboard.
2. Check the state of charge (SOC) indicator, which should show the current battery level.
3. Use a multimeter to measure the battery voltage, ensuring it's within the recommended range (typically between 300-400 volts).

#### Updating the Battery Management System
1. Connect a laptop or tablet to the ES 350e's OBD-II port using a compatible adapter.
2. Download and install the latest BMS software from the manufacturer's website.
3. Follow the on-screen instructions to update the BMS, ensuring the vehicle is in park mode and the ignition is turned off.

### Performing Routine Maintenance Tasks
Regular maintenance tasks, such as tire rotation and brake pad replacement, are essential to ensure the ES 350e runs smoothly and safely.

#### Rotating the Tires
1. Locate the tire pressure gauge and ensure the tires are inflated to the recommended pressure.
2. Use a jack and jack stands to lift the vehicle, ensuring the tires are off the ground.
3. Rotate the tires according to the manufacturer's recommended pattern (typically every 5,000-7,500 miles).

#### Replacing the Brake Pads
1. Locate the brake calipers and remove the wheel to access the brake pads.
2. Use a torque wrench to loosen the caliper bolts, then remove the old brake pads.
3. Install the new brake pads, ensuring they're properly seated and the caliper is securely tightened.
