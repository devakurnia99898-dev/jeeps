## Technical Deep Dive
The Dodge Charger EV's electric powertrain consists of a high-voltage battery pack, electric motors, and a sophisticated control system. To charge the vehicle, you'll need a Level 2 (240V) charger, which can replenish the battery to 80% in under 30 minutes. However, this requires a dedicated 240V circuit, which may not be present in every garage.

Common failure points to consider when preparing your garage include:

* Insufficient electrical infrastructure: A 240V circuit requires a minimum of 40 amps, which may necessitate an upgrade to your electrical panel.
* Inadequate charging equipment: Using a Level 1 (120V) charger can take up to 24 hours to fully charge the vehicle, making it impractical for daily use.
* Lack of ventilation: Electric vehicles produce less heat than their gasoline-powered counterparts, but they still require adequate ventilation to prevent moisture buildup and ensure safe charging.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Assess Your Garage's Electrical Infrastructure
#### Step 1.1: Check Your Electrical Panel
Verify that your electrical panel can support the additional load of a 240V charger. Check the panel's amp rating and ensure that it's not overloaded.

#### Step 1.2: Determine the Required Circuit Size
Calculate the required circuit size based on the charger's specifications. A typical Level 2 charger requires a 40-amp circuit.

#### Step 1.3: Upgrade Your Electrical Panel (If Necessary)
If your panel is not sufficient, consider hiring a licensed electrician to upgrade it. This may involve installing a new panel or adding a sub-panel.

### Step 2: Install a Dedicated 240V Circuit
#### Step 2.1: Run the Circuit
Run a dedicated 240V circuit from the electrical panel to the charging location. Ensure that the circuit is properly sized and meets local electrical codes.

#### Step 2.2: Install the Charger
Mount the Level 2 charger according to the manufacturer's instructions. Ensure that it's securely fastened and protected from the elements.

#### Step 2.3: Connect the Charger to the Circuit
Connect the charger to the dedicated 240V circuit. Verify that the connection is secure and meets local electrical codes.

### Step 3: Ensure Proper Ventilation
#### Step 3.1: Assess Your Garage's Ventilation
Evaluate your garage's ventilation system to ensure that it's adequate for electric vehicle charging. Consider installing a ventilation system or upgrading your existing one.

#### Step 3.2: Install a Ventilation System (If Necessary)
If your garage lacks adequate ventilation, consider installing a system that provides a minimum of 10 air changes per hour.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't attempt to charge your electric vehicle using a Level 1 charger for extended periods, as this can lead to reduced battery life and increased charging times.
* Ensure that your garage is well-ventilated to prevent moisture buildup and ensure safe charging.
* Consider hiring a licensed electrician if you're unsure about any aspect of the installation process.
