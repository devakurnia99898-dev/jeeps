## Technical Deep Dive
The new Infiniti Skyline features a 3.0-liter twin-turbo V6 engine, which produces 400 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission and a sophisticated all-wheel-drive system. The all-wheel-drive system features a torque vectoring system, which can send up to 50% of the engine's torque to the rear wheels.

The suspension system features a double-wishbone design in the front and a multi-link design in the rear. The brakes feature 14.7-inch rotors in the front and 13.8-inch rotors in the rear.

Common failure points in the new Infiniti Skyline include the turbochargers, which can fail due to excessive heat or oil contamination. The transmission can also be prone to issues, particularly if the fluid is not changed regularly. The all-wheel-drive system can also be complex and require specialized tools to repair.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Introduction to the Infiniti Skyline
The new Infiniti Skyline is a complex vehicle that requires specialized tools and knowledge to repair. In this guide, we will cover the basic maintenance and repair procedures for the new Infiniti Skyline.

#### Checking the Oil Level
To check the oil level in the new Infiniti Skyline, locate the oil dipstick under the hood. Pull out the dipstick and wipe it clean with a rag. Insert the dipstick back into the oil reservoir and pull it out again. The oil level should be between the minimum and maximum marks on the dipstick.

#### Changing the Oil
To change the oil in the new Infiniti Skyline, locate the oil drain plug at the bottom of the engine. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan. Once the oil has stopped dripping, replace the plug and tighten it to 30 ft-lbs.

#### Replacing the Brake Pads
To replace the brake pads in the new Infiniti Skyline, locate the brake caliper and remove the caliper bolts using a 12mm socket. Pull the caliper off the rotor and remove the old brake pads. Install the new brake pads and replace the caliper. Tighten the caliper bolts to 25 ft-lbs.

#### Changing the Transmission Fluid
To change the transmission fluid in the new Infiniti Skyline, locate the transmission fluid pan at the bottom of the transmission. Use a 10mm socket to remove the pan bolts and allow the fluid to drain into a pan. Once the fluid has stopped dripping, replace the pan bolts and tighten them to 20 ft-lbs.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type of oil and transmission fluid for the new Infiniti Skyline.
* Never over-tighten the brake caliper bolts, as this can cause damage to the brake rotor.
* Always use a torque wrench to tighten bolts and nuts to the correct specification.
