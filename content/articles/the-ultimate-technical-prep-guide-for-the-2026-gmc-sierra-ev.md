## Technical Deep Dive
The Sierra EV AT4 features a dual-electric motor powertrain, producing 754 horsepower and 785 lb-ft of torque. The vehicle's battery pack consists of 24 modules, each containing 12 cells, for a total capacity of 200 kWh. The electric motors are powered by a high-voltage electrical system, which includes a DC-DC converter, an on-board charger, and a traction battery management system.

Common failure points in electric vehicles include the battery management system, electric motors, and high-voltage electrical components. Regular maintenance and inspections are crucial to prevent these issues and ensure optimal performance.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation and Inspection
#### Checking Tire Pressure
1. Locate the tire pressure gauge in the owner's manual or on the tire information placard on the driver's side doorjamb.
2. Remove the valve cap from the tire stem and press the tire pressure gauge onto the valve stem.
3. Take a reading and compare it to the recommended pressure listed in the owner's manual.
4. Adjust the tire pressure as needed to ensure proper inflation.

#### Inspecting Brake Pads
1. Locate the brake pads on the front and rear axles.
2. Remove the wheel and caliper to access the brake pads.
3. Inspect the brake pads for wear and damage.
4. Replace the brake pads if they are worn below the minimum thickness specified in the owner's manual.

#### Checking the Battery Management System
1. Locate the battery management system (BMS) module in the vehicle's electrical system.
2. Use a multimeter to measure the voltage and current output of the BMS.
3. Compare the readings to the specifications listed in the owner's manual.
4. Consult a professional if the readings are outside the specified range.

#### Inspecting Electric Motors
1. Locate the electric motors on the front and rear axles.
2. Remove the motor covers to access the internal components.
3. Inspect the motor windings and bearings for wear and damage.
4. Replace the motor if it is damaged or worn out.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is the recommended maintenance schedule for the 2026 GMC Sierra EV AT4?**
The recommended maintenance schedule can be found in the owner's manual and includes tasks such as tire pressure checks, brake pad inspections, and battery management system checks.
2. **How often should I charge the battery pack?**
The battery pack should be charged regularly to maintain its health and lifespan. The recommended charging schedule can be found in the owner's manual.
3. **Can I perform maintenance tasks on my own, or do I need to take it to a dealership?**
Some maintenance tasks, such as tire pressure checks and brake pad inspections, can be performed by the owner. However, more complex tasks, such as electric motor inspections and repairs, should be performed by a qualified mechanic.
4. **What are the common failure points in the 2026 GMC Sierra EV AT4?**
Common failure points include the battery management system, electric motors, and high-voltage electrical components.
5. **How can I extend the lifespan of the battery pack?**
To extend the lifespan of the battery pack, avoid overcharging, keep the vehicle in a cool and dry place, and avoid deep discharging (i.e., letting the battery pack drain to 0% capacity).
