## Technical Deep Dive
The 392 Hemi V8 engine features a cast-iron block, aluminum cylinder heads, and a forged steel crankshaft. The engine is paired with an 8-speed automatic transmission and a two-speed transfer case. The drivetrain includes a heavy-duty rear axle and a limited-slip differential.

Common failure points to watch out for include:
* The water pump, which can fail due to excessive heat or wear on the impeller
* The engine oil cooler, which can leak or become clogged, leading to engine overheating
* The transmission cooler lines, which can leak or become damaged, causing transmission overheating

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change and Filter Replacement
#### Step 1.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the vehicle. Use a socket wrench to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.

#### Step 1.2: Replace the Oil Filter
The oil filter is located on the top of the engine, near the valve cover. Use a filter wrench to remove the old filter and discard it. Install a new oil filter and hand tighten it.

#### Step 1.3: Refill the Engine Oil
Use a funnel to pour in the new oil, and refer to the owner's manual for the recommended oil type and capacity.

### Step 2: Tire Rotation and Inspection
#### Step 2.1: Loosen the Lug Nuts
Before raising the vehicle, loosen the lug nuts on the tires using a lug wrench.

#### Step 2.2: Raise the Vehicle
Use a hydraulic jack to raise the vehicle, and support it with jack stands.

#### Step 2.3: Remove the Tires
Remove the tires and inspect the tread depth and wear pattern. Rotate the tires according to the recommended pattern.

### Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Remove the Wheel
Remove the wheel using a lug wrench, and set it aside.

#### Step 3.2: Remove the Caliper
Use a socket wrench to remove the caliper bolts, and slide the caliper off the rotor.

#### Step 3.3: Remove the Old Brake Pads
Remove the old brake pads and discard them. Install new brake pads and secure them with the retaining clips.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always refer to the owner's manual for specific instructions and recommendations
* Use the correct type and amount of oil for the engine
* Never over-tighten the lug nuts, as this can damage the wheel or brake rotor
* Always test the brakes after replacing the brake pads

The correct torque specification is 100 ft-lbs in a star pattern.
