## Technical Deep Dive
The Audi Quattro's engine and transmission are the most critical components when it comes to building a V8 powered restomod. The original 5-cylinder engine produces around 200 horsepower, but a V8 engine can produce significantly more power. However, this requires significant modifications to the engine and transmission.

The engine and transmission must be able to handle the increased power and torque of the V8 engine. This includes upgrading the engine mounts, transmission mounts, and driveshaft. The suspension and brakes must also be upgraded to handle the increased power and weight of the V8 engine.

Common failure points in the Audi Quattro include the engine and transmission mounts, which can fail under the increased stress of the V8 engine. The driveshaft and suspension components can also fail if they are not upgraded to handle the increased power and weight.
