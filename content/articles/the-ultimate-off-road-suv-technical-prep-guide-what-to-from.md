## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation and Inspection
Before diving into any maintenance or repair work, it's crucial to thoroughly inspect the vehicle. Check for any signs of damage or wear on the suspension, steering, and braking components. Look for leaks in the engine, transmission, and differential fluids.

#### Visual Inspection
Start by walking around the vehicle, looking for any signs of damage or wear. Check the tire condition, including tread depth and uneven wear patterns.

#### Fluid Checks
Check the engine oil, transmission fluid, and differential fluids for proper levels and condition. Look for any signs of leakage or contamination.

### Suspension and Steering System
The suspension and steering system will play a critical role in the off-road capabilities of the Ineos SUV. Regular maintenance, including inspections and replacements of worn components, will be essential.

#### Replacing Shocks and Struts
If the vehicle is equipped with coilovers or separate shocks and springs, inspect the components for wear or damage. Replace any worn or damaged parts, following the manufacturer's torque specifications.

#### Steering System Inspection
Inspect the steering system, including the tie rods, control arms, and steering gear. Look for any signs of wear or damage, and replace any components as needed.

### Electrical System
The electrical system will be critical to the operation of the Ineos SUV, including the vehicle's computer, sensors, and wiring.

#### Diagnostic Scan
Use a scan tool to check for any trouble codes or issues with the vehicle's computer system. Address any problems found, following the manufacturer's repair procedures.

#### Wiring and Connector Inspection
Inspect the wiring and connectors for any signs of damage or wear. Repair or replace any damaged components, ensuring proper connections and sealing.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
