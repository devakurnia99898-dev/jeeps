## Technical Deep Dive
The Ferrari 308 GTS's engine is a 3.0L V8, with a bore and stroke of 81mm and 71mm, respectively. The engine features a dual-overhead camshaft design, with four valves per cylinder. The engine management system is a Bosch K-Jetronic fuel injection system, which provides precise control over fuel delivery and ignition timing.

Common failure points in the Ferrari 308 GTS include:
* The water pump, which can fail due to corrosion or wear on the impeller
* The timing belt, which can fail if not replaced at the recommended interval
* The clutch, which can wear out due to excessive use or improper adjustment

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the car. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.

#### Step 1.2: Replace the Oil Filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old oil filter and discard it. Install a new oil filter and hand tighten it.

#### Step 1.3: Refill the Oil
Use a funnel to pour in the new oil. The recommended oil capacity is 6 quarts.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the Wheels
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheels and set them aside.

#### Step 2.2: Remove the Caliper
Use a 12mm socket to remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.

#### Step 2.3: Remove the Old Brake Pads
Use pliers to remove the old brake pads from the caliper.

#### Step 2.4: Install the New Brake Pads
Install the new brake pads into the caliper and slide the caliper back over the rotor.

### Step 3: Timing Belt Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Remove the Timing Belt Cover
Use a 10mm socket to remove the timing belt cover and set it aside.

#### Step 3.2: Mark the Timing Belt
Use a marker to mark the timing belt and the crankshaft sprocket.

#### Step 3.3: Remove the Timing Belt
Use a wrench to loosen the timing belt tensioner and remove the timing belt.

#### Step 3.4: Install the New Timing Belt
Install the new timing belt and tighten the tensioner.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use a torque wrench to ensure proper tightening of bolts and nuts
* Never over-tighten the timing belt, as this can cause premature wear
* Always follow the recommended maintenance schedule to prevent premature wear and tear
