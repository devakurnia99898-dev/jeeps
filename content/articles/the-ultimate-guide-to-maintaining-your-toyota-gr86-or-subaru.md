## Technical Deep Dive
The Toyota GR86 and Subaru BRZ share a similar engine and transmission, with a 2.4L naturally aspirated boxer engine producing 228 horsepower. The engine features a dual-overhead camshaft design with variable valve timing, and a 6-speed manual transmission with a Torsen limited-slip differential.

Common failure points include the engine's oil pump, which can fail due to excessive wear or debris in the oil system. The transmission's synchronizers can also wear out over time, causing grinding or hesitation when shifting gears.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Gather materials
Collect the necessary materials, including oil filter wrench, new oil filter, and enough motor oil to fill the engine.

#### Step 1.2: Warm up the engine
Drive the vehicle for a few minutes to warm up the engine. This will help the oil drain more easily.

#### Step 1.3: Locate the oil drain plug
Find the oil drain plug, usually located at the bottom of the engine. Use a socket wrench to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.

#### Step 1.4: Replace the oil filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old oil filter. Dispose of the filter responsibly and install a new one.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake calipers. Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts, then raise the vehicle using a jack.

#### Step 2.2: Remove the caliper
Use a wrench to remove the caliper bolts, then slide the caliper off the rotor.

#### Step 2.3: Remove the old brake pads
Pull out the old brake pads and discard them.

#### Step 2.4: Install the new brake pads
Insert the new brake pads into the caliper, ensuring they are properly seated.

### Step 3: Transmission Fluid Change
#### Step 3.1: Locate the transmission pan
Find the transmission pan, usually located at the bottom of the transmission. Use a socket wrench to remove the bolts and allow the fluid to drain into a pan.

#### Step 3.2: Replace the transmission filter
Use a filter wrench to remove the old transmission filter. Dispose of the filter responsibly and install a new one.

#### Step 3.3: Refill the transmission fluid
Use a funnel to pour in the new transmission fluid, ensuring the level is at the recommended height.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type of oil and transmission fluid for your vehicle.
* Never over-tighten bolts or nuts, as this can cause damage to the engine or transmission.
* Always bleed the brakes after replacing the brake pads to ensure proper brake function.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What type of oil should I use in my Toyota GR86 or Subaru BRZ?
The recommended oil type is 0W-20 synthetic oil.
2. How often should I change my brake pads?
Brake pads should be changed every 30,000 to 50,000 miles, depending on driving conditions.
3. Can I use any type of transmission fluid in my vehicle?
No, only use the recommended type of transmission fluid, as specified in the owner's manual.
4. How do I know if my engine needs a tune-up?
If your engine is running rough, or you notice a decrease in performance, it may be time for a tune-up.
5. Can I perform maintenance tasks myself, or should I take it to a mechanic?
While some maintenance tasks can be performed DIY, it's recommended to take your vehicle to a mechanic for more complex tasks, such as engine repairs or transmission overhauls.
