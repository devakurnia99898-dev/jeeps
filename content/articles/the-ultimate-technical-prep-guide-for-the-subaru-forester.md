# The Diagnostic / The Situation
The Subaru Forester Wilderness Hybrid is designed to maximize your adventure with its off-road upgrades, increased power, and fuel capacity. However, to ensure that your vehicle is always ready for the next adventure, it's essential to understand the technical background and potential issues that may arise. The Forester Wilderness Hybrid's symmetrical all-wheel drive and improved ground clearance make it an excellent choice for off-road enthusiasts. Nevertheless, the increased complexity of the hybrid powertrain and advanced all-wheel-drive system require a deeper understanding of the vehicle's systems.

Symptoms or signs to look for include decreased fuel efficiency, unusual noises from the powertrain, or vibrations while driving. These issues can be indicative of problems with the hybrid system, all-wheel-drive system, or suspension.

## Technical Deep Dive
The Subaru Forester Wilderness Hybrid features a 2.0-liter direct-injection turbocharged boxer engine, paired with an electric motor and a lithium-ion battery pack. The hybrid powertrain produces a combined 192 horsepower and 277 lb-ft of torque. The symmetrical all-wheel-drive system features a rear differential with a torque vectoring system, which improves handling and stability on various surfaces.

Common failure points in the Forester Wilderness Hybrid include the plastic impeller in the water pump, the hybrid battery pack's cooling system, and the all-wheel-drive system's electronic controls. The plastic impeller can fail due to overheating or debris in the cooling system, while the hybrid battery pack's cooling system can be prone to leaks or blockages. The all-wheel-drive system's electronic controls can be sensitive to water and dust, which can cause malfunctions.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation
The first step in preparing your Subaru Forester Wilderness Hybrid for off-road adventures is to ensure that all maintenance is up to date. This includes oil changes, tire rotations, and inspections of the hybrid battery pack and all-wheel-drive system.

#### Checking the Oil Level
1. Locate the oil dipstick under the hood
2. Pull out the dipstick and wipe it clean with a rag
3. Insert the dipstick and pull it out again to check the oil level
4. Add oil as necessary to reach the recommended level

#### Rotating the Tires
1. Locate the tire rotation pattern in the owner's manual
2. Loosen the lug nuts on the first wheel using a torque wrench
3. Raise the vehicle using a jack and remove the lug nuts
4. Rotate the tire to the next position and hand tighten the lug nuts
5. Lower the vehicle and tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern

### Hybrid Battery Pack Maintenance
The hybrid battery pack requires regular maintenance to ensure optimal performance and longevity. This includes checking the battery pack's state of charge and performing a battery pack reset if necessary.

#### Checking the Battery Pack's State of Charge
1. Locate the hybrid system's control module
2. Use a scan tool to access the hybrid system's diagnostic menu
3. Select the battery pack's state of charge option
4. Check the state of charge and perform a battery pack reset if necessary

### All-Wheel-Drive System Inspection
The all-wheel-drive system requires regular inspection to ensure that it is functioning properly. This includes checking the rear differential's fluid level and inspecting the torque vectoring system's components.

#### Checking the Rear Differential's Fluid Level
1. Locate the rear differential's fill plug
2. Remove the fill plug using a wrench
3. Check the fluid level and add fluid as necessary
4. Replace the fill plug and tighten it to the recommended torque specification

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always follow the recommended maintenance schedule to ensure optimal performance and longevity
* Use the correct type of oil and fluids for the hybrid system and all-wheel-drive system
* Avoid overloading the vehicle, as this can cause damage to the hybrid battery pack and all-wheel-drive system
* Keep the hybrid system's control module and battery pack away from water and dust
