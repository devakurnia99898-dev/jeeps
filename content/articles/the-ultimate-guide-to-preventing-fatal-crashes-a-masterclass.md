## Technical Deep Dive
The system that works to prevent fatal crashes is a complex interplay of vehicle maintenance, driver behavior, and road conditions. Common failure points that can lead to fatal crashes include:
* Worn-out tires: The tread depth of a tire is critical in maintaining traction on the road. Worn-out tires can lead to hydroplaning, skidding, and loss of control.
* Faulty brakes: Brakes are a critical safety component of any vehicle. Faulty brakes can lead to extended stopping distances, increasing the risk of a fatal crash.
* Malfunctioning headlights: Headlights are essential for visibility, especially during low-light conditions. Malfunctioning headlights can reduce a driver's ability to see the road ahead, increasing the risk of a fatal crash.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Tire Inspection
Tire inspection is a critical component of vehicle maintenance. To inspect your tires, follow these steps:
#### Step 1.1: Check tire pressure
Check the tire pressure against the recommended pressure listed on the tire information placard.
#### Step 1.2: Check tread depth
Use a tire depth gauge to check the tread depth of each tire. The minimum tread depth is 1/16 of an inch.
#### Step 1.3: Check for signs of wear
Check for signs of wear, such as uneven wear, cracks, or bulges.

### Step 2: Brake Inspection
Brake inspection is a critical component of vehicle maintenance. To inspect your brakes, follow these steps:
#### Step 2.1: Check brake pad wear
Use a brake pad wear sensor to check the thickness of the brake pads.
#### Step 2.2: Check brake fluid level
Check the brake fluid level against the recommended level listed on the brake fluid reservoir.
#### Step 2.3: Check for signs of wear
Check for signs of wear, such as squealing or grinding noises.

### Step 3: Headlight Inspection
Headlight inspection is a critical component of vehicle maintenance. To inspect your headlights, follow these steps:
#### Step 3.1: Check headlight function
Check that each headlight is functioning properly.
#### Step 3.2: Check headlight aim
Check that the headlights are aimed correctly.
#### Step 3.3: Check for signs of wear
Check for signs of wear, such as cracks or discoloration.

## Frequently Asked Questions

A: You should inspect your tires at least once a month and before long trips.
2. **Q: How do I know if my brakes need to be replaced?**

3. **Q: Can I replace my headlights myself?**
A: Yes, you can replace your headlights yourself, but it's recommended to consult a professional mechanic if you're not comfortable with the task.
