## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Engine Disassembly
#### Step 1.1: Remove the Cylinder Head
To access the engine's internals, we need to remove the cylinder head. This requires a 10mm socket and a torque wrench. Be careful not to damage the head gasket or surrounding components.
#### Step 1.2: Remove the Pistons and Crankshaft
With the cylinder head removed, we can access the pistons and crankshaft. Use a hydraulic press to remove the pistons, and a puller to remove the crankshaft.
### Step 2: Hybrid System Design
#### Step 2.1: Choose an Electric Motor
Select an electric motor that is compatible with the five-cylinder engine. Consider factors such as power output, torque, and packaging.
#### Step 2.2: Design the Power Electronics
The power electronics system is responsible for controlling the flow of energy between the engine, electric motor, and battery. Choose a suitable inverter, DC-DC converter, and battery management system.
### Step 3: Installation and Integration
#### Step 3.1: Install the Electric Motor
Mount the electric motor to the engine block, ensuring proper alignment and clearance.
#### Step 3.2: Install the Power Electronics
Install the power electronics system, including the inverter, DC-DC converter, and battery management system. Ensure proper wiring and connections.
### Step 4: Testing and Calibration
#### Step 4.1: Perform Initial Testing
Perform initial testing to ensure the hybrid system is functioning correctly. Monitor parameters such as engine speed, torque, and voltage.
#### Step 4.2: Calibrate the System
Calibrate the system to optimize performance, fuel efficiency, and emissions. Use specialized software and equipment to fine-tune the system.
