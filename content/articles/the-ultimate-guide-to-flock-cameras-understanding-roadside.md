## Technical Deep Dive
Flock cameras use a combination of cameras, sensors, and software to capture and process images of vehicles. The cameras are typically mounted on poles or other structures along the side of the road, and are equipped with high-resolution lenses that can capture clear images of license plates and other vehicle details. The cameras are also equipped with infrared sensors that can detect the presence of vehicles, even in low-light conditions.

The data collected by Flock cameras is typically transmitted to a central server, where it is stored and analyzed. The data can be used to track the movement of vehicles, identify patterns of traffic flow, and even detect potential security threats. However, the use of Flock cameras has raised concerns about privacy and data protection, as the data collected by these cameras can be used to identify and track individual vehicle owners.

One of the common failure points of Flock cameras is the plastic housing that protects the camera lens. This housing can become damaged or cracked, which can affect the camera's ability to capture clear images. Additionally, the cameras can be affected by weather conditions, such as heavy rain or snow, which can reduce their accuracy and effectiveness.
