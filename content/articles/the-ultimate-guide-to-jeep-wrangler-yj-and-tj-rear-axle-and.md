## Technical Deep Dive
The rear axle in a Jeep Wrangler YJ and TJ is a complex system that includes the axle shafts, bearings, and the differential. The axle shafts are responsible for transmitting power from the differential to the wheels, while the bearings support the axle shafts and allow for smooth rotation. The differential, on the other hand, is responsible for splitting power between the two wheels. Common failure points in the rear axle include the axle shaft seals, which can leak and cause damage to the bearings, and the differential bearings, which can wear out over time. ## Step-by-Step Guide
### Removing the Rear Axle
#### Step 1: Jack Up the Vehicle
Raise the vehicle using a hydraulic lift or jack stands to access the rear axle. Ensure that the vehicle is securely supported and on level ground.

#### Step 2: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake rotor and hub assembly. Use a 10mm socket to loosen the lug nuts, then raise the vehicle to remove the wheels.

#### Step 3: Remove the Brake Rotor
Remove the brake rotor to access the hub assembly. Use a 10mm socket to loosen the rotor screws, then pull the rotor off the hub.

#### Step 4: Remove the Hub Assembly
Remove the hub assembly to access the axle shaft. Use a hub puller to remove the hub assembly from the axle shaft.

### Inspecting and Replacing the Axle Shaft Seals
#### Step 1: Inspect the Axle Shaft Seals
Inspect the axle shaft seals for any signs of leakage or damage. If the seals are damaged, replace them with new ones.

#### Step 2: Remove the Axle Shaft
Remove the axle shaft to access the seals. Use an axle shaft puller to remove the axle shaft from the differential.

#### Step 3: Install the New Seals
Install the new seals, ensuring that they are properly seated and aligned.

### Reassembling the Rear Axle
#### Step 1: Reinstall the Axle Shaft
Reinstall the axle shaft, ensuring that it is properly seated and aligned.

#### Step 2: Reinstall the Hub Assembly
Reinstall the hub assembly, ensuring that it is properly seated and aligned.

#### Step 3: Reinstall the Brake Rotor
Reinstall the brake rotor, ensuring that it is properly seated and aligned.

#### Step 4: Reinstall the Wheels
Reinstall the wheels, ensuring that they are properly tightened and aligned.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When working with the rear axle, it's essential to ensure that all components are properly tightened and aligned to prevent any damage or accidents. A common mistake is to overtighten the axle shaft bolts, which can cause damage to the differential bearings. Additionally, always use the correct tools and follow the manufacturer's instructions to avoid any mistakes.
