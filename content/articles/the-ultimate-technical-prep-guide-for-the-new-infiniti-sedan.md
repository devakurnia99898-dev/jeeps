## Technical Deep Dive
The new Infiniti Sedan will likely feature a powerful engine, possibly a turbocharged V6, paired with a sophisticated transmission and all-wheel-drive system. The sedan's suspension will be tuned for performance, with a focus on handling and stability.

Common failure points in high-performance sedans include:
* The turbocharger's wastegate and boost control system
* The transmission's clutch packs and gear sets
* The all-wheel-drive system's transfer case and driveshafts
* The suspension's coilovers and bushings

Understanding these systems and their potential failure points will help you identify and address issues before they become major problems.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Familiarize Yourself with the Sedan's Systems
#### Step 1.1: Review the Owner's Manual
Read the owner's manual to understand the sedan's features, systems, and maintenance requirements.

#### Step 1.2: Inspect the Vehicle
Perform a visual inspection of the sedan, checking for any signs of wear or damage.

### Step 2: Check and Maintain the Engine
#### Step 2.1: Oil Change
Change the engine oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, using the recommended synthetic oil.

#### Step 2.2: Inspect the Air Filter
Check and replace the air filter every 15,000 to 30,000 miles, or as recommended by the manufacturer.

### Step 3: Inspect and Maintain the Suspension
#### Step 3.1: Visual Inspection
Inspect the suspension components, including coilovers, bushings, and control arms, for signs of wear or damage.

#### Step 3.2: Check the Ride Height
Verify the sedan's ride height is within the recommended specifications.

### Step 4: Check and Maintain the Brakes
#### Step 4.1: Inspect the Brake Pads
Check the brake pads for wear, replacing them if necessary.

#### Step 4.2: Inspect the Rotors
Inspect the brake rotors for warping or excessive wear, resurfacing or replacing them if necessary.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't neglect regular maintenance, as it can lead to premature wear and costly repairs.
* Use the correct type and amount of oil, as specified in the owner's manual.
* Avoid over-tightening suspension components, as it can cause damage and affect the sedan's handling.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. Q: What type of oil should I use in my Infiniti Sedan?
A: Use the recommended synthetic oil, as specified in the owner's manual.
2. Q: How often should I inspect the suspension?
A: Inspect the suspension every 6 months or 12,000 miles, or as recommended by the manufacturer.
3. Q: Can I replace the brake pads myself?
A: Yes, but it's recommended to have a professional mechanic perform the replacement, especially if you're not experienced with brake work.
4. Q: What are the common signs of a faulty turbocharger?
A: Signs of a faulty turbocharger include decreased performance, unusual noises, or warning lights on the dashboard.
5. Q: How often should I check the tire pressure?
A: Check the tire pressure at least once a month, and before long trips, to ensure proper inflation and optimal performance.
