## Technical Deep Dive
The body-on-frame architecture of Kia's new mid-size truck is a complex system that consists of several key components, including the frame, suspension, and steering. The frame is the backbone of the vehicle, providing a rigid foundation for the suspension and steering components. The suspension system, which includes the shocks, struts, and springs, works in conjunction with the frame to provide a smooth ride and stable handling.

Common failure points in a body-on-frame architecture include the frame mounts, suspension bushings, and steering components. The frame mounts can become loose over time, causing the frame to shift and affect the alignment of the suspension and steering components. The suspension bushings can wear out, leading to a rough ride and reduced handling performance. The steering components, including the steering gear and tie rods, can also fail if not properly maintained.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Inspect the Frame and Suspension Components
Inspect the frame and suspension components for any signs of damage or wear. Check the frame mounts for looseness and the suspension bushings for wear. Also, inspect the steering components, including the steering gear and tie rods, for any signs of damage or wear.

#### Step 1.1: Check the Frame Mounts
Check the frame mounts for looseness by using a torque wrench to check the bolt torque. If the bolts are loose, tighten them to the specified torque.

#### Step 1.2: Inspect the Suspension Bushings
Inspect the suspension bushings for wear by looking for signs of cracking or deterioration. If the bushings are worn, replace them with new ones.

#### Step 1.3: Inspect the Steering Components
Inspect the steering components, including the steering gear and tie rods, for any signs of damage or wear. If any components are damaged, replace them with new ones.

### Step 2: Replace the Suspension Bushings
Replace the suspension bushings with new ones. Use a suspension compressor to compress the suspension and remove the old bushings. Then, install the new bushings and release the suspension compressor.

#### Step 2.1: Compress the Suspension
Use a suspension compressor to compress the suspension and remove the old bushings.

#### Step 2.2: Remove the Old Bushings
Remove the old bushings and discard them.

#### Step 2.3: Install the New Bushings
Install the new bushings and make sure they are properly seated.

### Step 3: Align the Frame and Suspension Components
Align the frame and suspension components to ensure proper alignment and prevent premature wear. Use a torque wrench to tighten the frame mounts and suspension components to the specified torque.

#### Step 3.1: Tighten the Frame Mounts
Tighten the frame mounts to the specified torque using a torque wrench.

#### Step 3.2: Tighten the Suspension Components
Tighten the suspension components to the specified torque using a torque wrench.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use a torque wrench to tighten the frame mounts and suspension components to the specified torque.
* Never over-tighten the frame mounts and suspension components, as this can cause damage to the components.
* Always inspect the frame and suspension components for any signs of damage or wear before starting work.
