## Technical Deep Dive
The C8 Corvette Grand Sport features a complex system of components that work together to deliver exceptional performance. The engine, transmission, and suspension all play critical roles in the vehicle's overall performance.

The engine is a 6.2L V8 unit, featuring a cast aluminum block, forged steel crankshaft, and forged aluminum pistons. The engine is equipped with a dry-sump oil system, which provides improved oil pressure and reduced oil starvation during high-performance driving.

The transmission is an 8-speed dual-clutch unit, featuring a manual mode with paddle shifters. The transmission is designed to provide quick and seamless shifting, with a focus on performance and driver engagement.

The suspension features a short-long arm (SLA) design, with forged aluminum upper and lower control arms. The suspension is equipped with adaptive dampers, which provide real-time adjustments to the damping force to optimize ride comfort and handling.

Common failure points on the C8 Corvette Grand Sport include:

* The plastic impeller in the water pump
* The clutch pack in the transmission
* The bushings in the suspension control arms

## Step-by-Step Guide
This section will provide a detailed, step-by-step guide to preparing your C8 Corvette Grand Sport for optimal performance.

### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Gather materials
* Oil filter wrench
* New oil filter
* Oil drain pan
* New motor oil
#### Step 1.2: Warm up the engine
* Drive the vehicle for 5-10 minutes to warm up the engine
#### Step 1.3: Drain the oil
* Locate the oil drain plug and remove it using a 10mm socket
* Allow the oil to drain into the oil drain pan
#### Step 1.4: Replace the oil filter
* Locate the oil filter and remove it using an oil filter wrench
* Install a new oil filter and hand tighten
#### Step 1.5: Refill the oil
* Pour in the new motor oil and replace the oil cap

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Gather materials
* New brake pads
* Brake pad wear sensor
* C-clamps
* Tire iron
#### Step 2.2: Raise the vehicle
* Use a jack to raise the vehicle and support it with jack stands
#### Step 2.3: Remove the wheel
* Use a tire iron to loosen the lug nuts and remove the wheel
#### Step 2.4: Remove the caliper
* Use a 12mm socket to remove the caliper bolts
* Slide the caliper off the rotor
#### Step 2.5: Replace the brake pads
* Remove the old brake pads and install the new ones
* Replace the brake pad wear sensor
