Parking lots are notoriously hazardous, with a significant number of accidents occurring in these areas every year. As a Jeep owner, it's essential to understand the risks and take necessary precautions to ensure your safety and the safety of others. The low-speed environment of parking lots can be deceiving, and accidents can happen due to a combination of factors, including driver error, vehicle malfunctions, and environmental conditions.

## Technical Deep Dive
The suspension and braking systems of your Jeep play a critical role in navigating parking lots safely. The suspension system, consisting of components like coil springs, shock absorbers, and control arms, helps maintain vehicle stability and control. The braking system, comprising brake pads, rotors, and calipers, enables you to slow down or come to a stop when needed.

Common failure points in these systems include worn-out brake pads, warped rotors, and loose or damaged suspension components. It's essential to inspect and maintain these systems regularly to prevent accidents.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspecting the Suspension System
#### Checking Coil Springs
1. Locate the coil springs and inspect for any signs of damage or wear.
2. Check the spring rate and ensure it's within the manufacturer's specifications.
#### Checking Shock Absorbers
1. Locate the shock absorbers and inspect for any signs of leakage or damage.
2. Check the shock absorber's damping rate and ensure it's within the manufacturer's specifications.

### Inspecting the Braking System
#### Checking Brake Pads
1. Locate the brake pads and inspect for any signs of wear or damage.
2. Check the brake pad wear indicator and replace the pads if necessary.
#### Checking Rotors
1. Locate the rotors and inspect for any signs of warping or damage.
2. Check the rotor thickness and ensure it's within the manufacturer's specifications.

### Rotating Tires
#### Loosening Lug Nuts
1. Locate the lug nuts and loosen them using a torque wrench.
2. Turn the lug nuts counterclockwise until they're loose, but do not remove them yet.
#### Removing Lug Nuts and Rotating Tires
1. Completely remove the lug nuts and flat washers.
2. Rotate the tires according to the manufacturer's recommended pattern.
3. Reinstall the lug nuts and flat washers, and tighten them in a star pattern.
