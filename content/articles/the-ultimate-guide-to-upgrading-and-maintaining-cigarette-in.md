# **The Diagnostic / The Situation**
The cigarette lighter and ashtray were once standard features in every new vehicle, including Jeeps. However, as the culture shifted and smoking became less prevalent, automakers began to offer these features as optional packages or delete them altogether. This shift has led to a decrease in the availability of cigarette lighters and ashtrays in newer vehicles. Instead, many modern Jeeps come with 12V accessory outlets that can be used to power various devices. In this guide, we will explore the technical background of these systems, common symptoms and signs to look for, and provide a comprehensive guide on how to upgrade and maintain them.

## **Technical Deep Dive**
The cigarette lighter and 12V accessory outlet system in your Jeep is a simple yet essential feature that provides power to various devices. The system consists of a 12V outlet, a fuse, and a wiring harness that connects to the vehicle's electrical system. The outlet is typically connected to the accessory circuit, which is powered by the vehicle's battery and alternator. The fuse is designed to protect the system from overloads and short circuits. Common failure points in this system include corroded or damaged outlets, blown fuses, and faulty wiring.

## **Step-by-Step Guide**
### **Replacing a 12V Outlet**
#### Removing the Old Outlet
1. Locate the 12V outlet in your Jeep and remove any devices that may be connected to it.
2. Use a 10mm socket to remove the screws that hold the outlet in place.
3. Gently pull the outlet out of the dashboard or console.

#### Installing the New Outlet
1. Take the new outlet and connect the wiring harness to the back of it.
2. Mount the outlet in the dashboard or console using the screws provided.
3. Tighten the screws to 10Nm (7ft-lbs) to secure the outlet in place.

### **Upgrading to a USB Outlet**
#### Removing the Old Outlet
1. Follow the same steps as above to remove the old outlet.

#### Installing the New USB Outlet
1. Take the new USB outlet and connect the wiring harness to the back of it.
2. Mount the outlet in the dashboard or console using the screws provided.
3. Tighten the screws to 10Nm (7ft-lbs) to secure the outlet in place.
4. Connect the USB outlet to a 12V power source, such as the accessory circuit.

### **Repairing a Faulty Wiring Harness**
#### Locating the Fault
1. Use a multimeter to test the wiring harness for any signs of damage or corrosion.
2. Locate the faulty section of the harness and cut it out.

#### Repairing the Harness
1. Use wire strippers to strip the ends of the harness.
2. Connect the new section of harness to the old one using electrical tape or solder.
3. Test the harness to ensure it is working properly.

## **Pro Tips & Common Mistakes**
* Always use a fuse that is rated for the correct amperage to avoid overloads and short circuits.
* Avoid using too many devices on a single 12V outlet, as this can cause it to overheat and fail.
* Use a multimeter to test the wiring harness and outlets for any signs of damage or corrosion.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**
1. **Can I use a 12V outlet to power a device that requires 120V?**
No, you should not use a 12V outlet to power a device that requires 120V, as this can cause damage to the device and the outlet.
2. **How many devices can I connect to a single 12V outlet?**
It is recommended to connect no more than two devices to a single 12V outlet, as this can cause it to overheat and fail.
3. **Can I upgrade my 12V outlet to a USB outlet?**
Yes, you can upgrade your 12V outlet to a USB outlet, but you will need to ensure that the outlet is connected to a 12V power source and that the wiring harness is compatible.
4. **How do I test a 12V outlet to see if it is working properly?**
You can test a 12V outlet using a multimeter to ensure it is providing the correct voltage and amperage.
5. **Can I repair a faulty wiring harness myself?**
Yes, you can repair a faulty wiring harness yourself, but it is recommended to seek the help of a professional mechanic if you are not experienced with electrical repairs.
