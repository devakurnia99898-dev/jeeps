## Technical Deep Dive
To understand how to prevent rollover accidents, it's crucial to delve into the technical aspects of vehicle stability. The key factors that contribute to a vehicle's stability include:

* Center of gravity: A lower center of gravity reduces the likelihood of a rollover.
* Suspension design: A well-designed suspension system can help maintain stability and control.
* Tire selection: The right tires can provide the necessary traction and stability for various driving conditions.
* Electronic stability control: Modern vehicles often come equipped with electronic stability control systems that can help prevent skidding and loss of control.

Common failure points that can contribute to rollover accidents include:

* Worn or damaged suspension components
* Inadequate tire pressure or uneven tire wear
* Faulty electronic stability control systems
* Poor vehicle maintenance or design flaws

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Inspect and Maintain Your Vehicle's Suspension
#### Step 1.1: Check for worn or damaged suspension components
Inspect your vehicle's suspension for any signs of wear or damage, including loose or worn-out bushings, ball joints, or control arms.
#### Step 1.2: Replace worn or damaged components
Replace any worn or damaged components with new or refurbished parts, ensuring proper torque specs are followed (e.g., 40-50 Nm for ball joints).

### Step 2: Upgrade Your Vehicle's Tires
#### Step 2.1: Choose the right tires for your vehicle
Select tires that are specifically designed for your vehicle's make and model, taking into account factors such as load rating, speed rating, and traction requirements.
#### Step 2.2: Install the new tires
Install the new tires, ensuring proper torque specs are followed (e.g., 80-100 Nm for lug nuts).

### Step 3: Check and Maintain Your Vehicle's Electronic Stability Control System
#### Step 3.1: Consult your vehicle's repair manual
Consult your vehicle's repair manual to understand the electronic stability control system's components and functions.
#### Step 3.2: Check for any fault codes or warnings
Use a multimeter or scan tool to check for any fault codes or warnings related to the electronic stability control system.
#### Step 3.3: Perform any necessary repairs or maintenance
Perform any necessary repairs or maintenance, such as replacing faulty sensors or recalibrating the system.
