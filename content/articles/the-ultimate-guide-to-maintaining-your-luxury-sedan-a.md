## Technical Deep Dive
The BMW 7 Series, like many luxury sedans, features a complex array of systems working in harmony to provide a seamless driving experience. The engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems all play critical roles in the overall performance and comfort of the vehicle.

Common failure points in luxury sedans include the engine's water pump, timing chain, and turbochargers. The suspension system, with its complex array of links and bushings, can also be prone to wear and tear. Electrical systems, including the infotainment and driver assistance systems, can be susceptible to software glitches and hardware failures.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Routine Maintenance
#### Sub-Step 1.1: Oil Change
To perform an oil change on your BMW 7 Series, you'll need to gather the necessary materials, including oil filters, drain pans, and new oil. Locate the oil drain plug, typically found at the bottom of the engine, and remove it using a 10mm socket. Allow the oil to drain completely before replacing the plug and refilling the engine with new oil.

#### Sub-Step 1.2: Tire Pressure Check
Check the tire pressure regularly to ensure optimal performance and fuel efficiency. Use a tire pressure gauge to measure the pressure, and adjust as needed.

### Step 2: Brake System Maintenance
#### Sub-Step 2.1: Brake Pad Inspection
Inspect the brake pads for wear and tear, looking for signs of excessive wear or damage. If the pads are worn down to 1/8 inch or less, it's time to replace them.

#### Sub-Step 2.2: Brake Pad Replacement
To replace the brake pads, you'll need to remove the wheel and caliper. Use a torque wrench to loosen the caliper bolts, then remove the old brake pads and install the new ones. Be sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions for proper installation and torque specs.

### Step 3: Suspension System Maintenance
#### Sub-Step 3.1: Suspension Alignment
To perform a suspension alignment, you'll need to use a wheel alignment machine to measure the camber, toe, and ride height. Make adjustments as needed to ensure proper alignment and prevent uneven tire wear.

#### Sub-Step 3.2: Bushing Replacement
If you notice any signs of wear or damage to the suspension bushings, it's time to replace them. Use a bushing removal tool to remove the old bushings, then install the new ones. Be sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions for proper installation and torque specs.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't neglect routine maintenance, as it can lead to costly repairs down the line.
* Use the correct type of oil for your vehicle, as specified in the owner's manual.
* Avoid over-torquing bolts and fasteners, as it can cause damage to the vehicle's components.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
The cost of maintaining a luxury sedan can vary greatly depending on the task and the individual's level of expertise. DIY maintenance can save money, but it's essential to consider the cost of tools and materials. In contrast, taking your vehicle to a mechanic can provide peace of mind, but it can also come with a higher price tag.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **Q: What type of oil should I use in my BMW 7 Series?**
A: The recommended oil type for the BMW 7 Series is 0W-40 synthetic oil.
2. **Q: How often should I check my tire pressure?**
A: It's recommended to check tire pressure at least once a month, and before long trips.
3. **Q: What are the signs of worn-out brake pads?**
A: Signs of worn-out brake pads include squealing or grinding noises, vibration when braking, and decreased braking performance.
4. **Q: Can I perform a suspension alignment myself?**
A: While it's possible to perform a suspension alignment yourself, it's recommended to seek the help of a professional mechanic or alignment specialist.
5. **Q: How often should I replace my air filter?**
A: The air filter should be replaced every 15,000 to 30,000 miles, depending on driving conditions and the manufacturer's recommendations.
