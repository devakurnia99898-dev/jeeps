## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The Toyota AE86 is a highly sought-after car among enthusiasts, known for its rear-wheel drive layout and agile handling. However, restoring and upgrading one can be a daunting task, especially for those without experience. The key to a successful restoration is identifying the areas that need attention, such as the powertrain, suspension, and electrical systems. Symptoms of a neglected AE86 may include decreased performance, uneven tire wear, and faulty electrical components.

## Technical Deep Dive
The Toyota AE86's powertrain consists of a 1.6L or 1.8L inline-four engine, paired with a five-speed manual transmission. The engine features a twin-cam design, with a timing belt driving the camshafts. Common failure points include the timing belt, water pump, and oil seals. The suspension consists of MacPherson struts in the front and a live axle in the rear, with coil springs and shock absorbers. The electrical system is relatively simple, with a few quirks such as the ignition system and fuel pump circuit.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Engine Overhaul
The engine overhaul is a critical step in restoring an AE86. This involves disassembling the engine, inspecting and replacing worn components, and reassembling the engine.
#### Step 1.1: Remove the engine
Use a hoist or engine lift to remove the engine from the car. Be careful not to damage the surrounding components.
#### Step 1.2: Disassemble the engine
Remove the cylinder head, timing belt, and oil pump. Inspect the components for wear and damage.
#### Step 1.3: Replace worn components
Replace the piston rings, bearings, and seals as needed. Use a micrometer to measure the piston diameter and cylinder bore.
#### Step 1.4: Reassemble the engine
Reinstall the cylinder head, timing belt, and oil pump. Use a torque wrench to tighten the bolts to the specified torque.
### Step 2: Suspension Upgrade
The suspension upgrade involves replacing the coil springs and shock absorbers with high-performance components.
#### Step 2.1: Remove the old suspension
Use a spring compressor to remove the old coil springs. Remove the shock absorbers and inspect the mounting points.
#### Step 2.2: Install the new suspension
Install the new coil springs and shock absorbers. Use a torque wrench to tighten the bolts to the specified torque.
#### Step 2.3: Align the suspension
Use a camber gauge to align the suspension. Adjust the toe and camber to the specified settings.
### Step 3: Electrical System Repair
The electrical system repair involves inspecting and repairing the ignition system, fuel pump circuit, and other components.
#### Step 3.1: Inspect the ignition system
Use a multimeter to test the ignition coil and spark plugs. Replace the spark plugs and ignition coil if necessary.
#### Step 3.2: Repair the fuel pump circuit
Use a wiring diagram to locate the fuel pump circuit. Repair or replace the fuel pump, fuel filter, and wiring as needed.
#### Step 3.3: Test the electrical system
Use a multimeter to test the electrical system. Check for any shorts or opens in the wiring.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't neglect to replace the timing belt and water pump during the engine overhaul.
* Use a torque wrench to tighten the bolts to the specified torque.
* Don't over-tighten the suspension components, as this can cause damage to the bushings and mounts.
* Use a multimeter to test the electrical system, as this can help diagnose any issues.
