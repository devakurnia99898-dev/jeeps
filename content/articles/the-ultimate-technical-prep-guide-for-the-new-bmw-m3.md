## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation
Before starting any work on the new BMW M3, it's essential to prepare the vehicle and the workspace. This includes:
#### Gathering tools and materials
Make sure to have all the necessary tools and materials within reach.
#### Jacking up the vehicle
Use a hydraulic jack to lift the vehicle, and ensure it is properly supported on jack stands.
#### Disconnecting the battery
Disconnect the battery to prevent any accidental starts or electrical shocks.

### Oil Change
The oil change is a critical maintenance task for the new BMW M3. To perform an oil change:
#### Draining the oil
Use a socket to remove the oil drain plug, and let the oil drain into a pan.
#### Replacing the oil filter
Use a filter wrench to remove the oil filter, and discard it responsibly.
#### Refilling the oil
Use a funnel to pour in the new oil, and replace the oil cap.

### Brake Pad Replacement
The brake pads on the new BMW M3 will need to be replaced periodically. To replace the brake pads:
#### Removing the wheels
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheels, and set them aside.
#### Removing the caliper
Use a socket to remove the caliper bolts, and slide the caliper off the rotor.
#### Replacing the brake pads
Use a pair of pliers to remove the old brake pads, and install the new ones.

### Suspension Inspection
The suspension system on the new BMW M3 will need to be inspected regularly. To inspect the suspension:
#### Checking the bushings
Use a flashlight to inspect the bushings for any signs of wear or damage.
#### Checking the ball joints
Use a pair of pliers to check the ball joints for any play or looseness.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type of oil for the new BMW M3.
* Never mix different types of brake pads, as this can cause uneven braking.
* Always tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern to ensure even tightening.
