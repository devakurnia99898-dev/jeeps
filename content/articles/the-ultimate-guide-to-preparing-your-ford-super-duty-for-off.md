## Technical Deep Dive
The Ford Super Duty's 4x4 system is designed to provide excellent traction and control on various surfaces. The system consists of a two-speed transfer case, a front axle with a locking hub, and a rear axle with a limited-slip differential. However, for serious off-road adventures, it's crucial to understand the common failure points and areas that require modification or upgrade.

Common failure points include:
* The plastic impeller in the water pump
* The front axle's CV joints
* The rear axle's limited-slip differential
* The suspension's coil springs and shocks

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Lift Kit Installation
The lift kit is a crucial modification for increasing ground clearance and allowing for larger tires. To install a lift kit, follow these steps:
#### Step 1: Prepare the Vehicle
Raise the vehicle on a lift or jack stands to access the suspension components.
#### Step 2: Remove the Stock Suspension
Remove the stock coil springs and shocks to make way for the lift kit components.
#### Step 3: Install the Lift Kit Components
Install the new coil springs and shocks, ensuring proper alignment and torque specs (35 ft-lbs for the coil spring bolts).

### Suspension Upgrade
Upgrading the suspension is essential for improving the vehicle's stability and control on off-road terrain. To upgrade the suspension, follow these steps:
#### Step 1: Remove the Stock Suspension Components
Remove the stock control arms and track bar to make way for the upgraded components.
#### Step 2: Install the Upgraded Components
Install the new control arms and track bar, ensuring proper alignment and torque specs (45 ft-lbs for the control arm bolts).

### Tire Upgrade
Upgrading the tires is critical for improving traction and control on off-road terrain. To upgrade the tires, follow these steps:
#### Step 1: Choose the Right Tires
Select tires with the proper tread pattern and size for your off-road adventures.
#### Step 2: Install the New Tires
Install the new tires, ensuring proper inflation and torque specs (80 ft-lbs for the lug nuts).

### Skid Plate Installation
Installing skid plates is essential for protecting the vehicle's underside from damage on off-road terrain. To install skid plates, follow these steps:
#### Step 1: Prepare the Vehicle
Raise the vehicle on a lift or jack stands to access the underside.
#### Step 2: Remove the Stock Skid Plates
Remove the stock skid plates to make way for the new skid plates.
#### Step 3: Install the New Skid Plates
Install the new skid plates, ensuring proper alignment and torque specs (30 ft-lbs for the skid plate bolts).

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
