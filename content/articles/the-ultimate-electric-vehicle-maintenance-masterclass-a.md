## The Diagnostic / The Situation
Electric vehicles (EVs) have unique characteristics that set them apart from their internal combustion engine counterparts. One of the primary advantages of EVs is their reduced maintenance requirements. With fewer moving parts, EVs tend to have lower maintenance costs over their lifespan. However, this doesn't mean they're completely maintenance-free. Regular checks and maintenance are still crucial to ensure the vehicle operates efficiently and safely.

Symptoms or signs to look for in an electric vehicle like the Slate Truck include:
* Decreased range or efficiency
* Unusual noises or vibrations
* Warning lights on the dashboard
* Reduced performance or power output

## Technical Deep Dive
The Slate Truck, like other electric vehicles, relies on a complex system of electrical components, including the electric motor, battery pack, and power electronics. The electric motor is responsible for converting electrical energy into mechanical energy, propelling the vehicle forward. The battery pack, on the other hand, stores the electrical energy that powers the motor.

Common failure points in electric vehicles include:
* The electric motor: While electric motors are generally more reliable than internal combustion engines, they can still fail due to overheating, overloading, or manufacturing defects.
* The battery pack: Battery packs can degrade over time, reducing the vehicle's range and overall performance. Factors such as extreme temperatures, deep discharging, and improper charging habits can contribute to battery degradation.
* The power electronics: The power electronics, including the inverter and converter, play a crucial role in controlling the flow of electrical energy between the battery pack and the electric motor. Failure of these components can result in reduced performance, decreased efficiency, or even complete system failure.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Tire Rotation
#### Step 1.1: Gather necessary tools and equipment
Collect the 10mm socket, torque wrench, and tire pressure gauge.
#### Step 1.2: Loosen the lug nuts
Before raising the vehicle, loosen the lug nuts on the wheels using the 10mm socket.
#### Step 1.3: Raise the vehicle
Use a hydraulic lift or jack to raise the vehicle, ensuring it's secure and stable.
#### Step 1.4: Remove the wheels
Completely remove the lug nuts and take off the wheels.
#### Step 1.5: Rotate the tires
Rotate the tires according to the recommended pattern, usually rearward cross.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the caliper
Use a wrench to loosen the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the old brake pads
Take out the old brake pads and inspect the brake rotor for any damage or wear.
#### Step 2.3: Install the new brake pads
Insert the new brake pads, ensuring they're properly seated and aligned.
#### Step 2.4: Reinstall the caliper
Slide the caliper back over the rotor and tighten the caliper bolts.

### Step 3: Battery Maintenance
#### Step 3.1: Check the battery state of charge
Use a battery tester to determine the state of charge and overall health of the battery pack.
#### Step 3.2: Clean the battery terminals
Use a wire brush to clean the battery terminals, ensuring good contact and reducing corrosion.
#### Step 3.3: Check the battery water level
If the battery pack has a water-based cooling system, check the water level and top it off as necessary.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't overcharge the battery pack, as this can lead to reduced lifespan and efficiency.
* Avoid deep discharging, as this can cause irreversible damage to the battery cells.
* Keep the vehicle's software up to date, as updates often include improvements to efficiency, range, and overall performance.
* Use the correct type of brake pads, as incorrect pads can lead to reduced braking performance and increased wear on the brake rotor.
