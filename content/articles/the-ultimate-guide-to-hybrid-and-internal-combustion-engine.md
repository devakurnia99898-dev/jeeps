## Technical Deep Dive
The hybrid powertrain in Jeeps, such as the Wrangler 4xe, combines a traditional internal combustion engine with an electric motor. The internal combustion engine is typically a 2.0L turbocharged inline-4 cylinder, while the electric motor is powered by a 17-kWh lithium-ion battery pack. The system works by using the electric motor to assist the internal combustion engine during acceleration, and to recharge the battery pack during braking and deceleration.

Common failure points in hybrid and internal combustion engines include the battery pack, electric motor, and internal combustion engine components such as the turbocharger, fuel injectors, and spark plugs. Regular maintenance is essential to prevent these components from failing prematurely.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug
The oil drain plug is typically located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the vehicle. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.

#### Step 1.2: Replace the Oil Filter
Once the oil has drained, replace the oil filter with a new one. Be sure to dispose of the used filter responsibly.

#### Step 1.3: Refill the Oil
Use a funnel to refill the oil with the recommended type and amount. Check the owner's manual for specific instructions.

### Step 2: Battery Maintenance
#### Step 2.1: Check the Battery Voltage
Use a multimeter to check the battery voltage. A fully charged battery should read around 12.6V.

#### Step 2.2: Clean the Battery Terminals
Use a wire brush to clean the battery terminals and ensure good contact.

#### Step 2.3: Check the Battery Water Level
If your battery has removable caps, check the water level and top it off as needed.

### Step 3: Turbocharger Inspection
#### Step 3.1: Locate the Turbocharger
The turbocharger is typically located near the engine, connected to the exhaust manifold.

#### Step 3.2: Inspect the Turbocharger
Use a flashlight and mirror to inspect the turbocharger for any signs of damage or wear.

#### Step 3.3: Check the Turbocharger Boost Pressure
Use a pressure gauge to check the turbocharger boost pressure. Compare the reading to the recommended specification.

### Step 4: Spark Plug Replacement
#### Step 4.1: Locate the Spark Plugs
The spark plugs are typically located on the side of the engine, near the cylinder head.

#### Step 4.2: Remove the Spark Plug Wire
Use a spark plug wire puller to remove the spark plug wire.

#### Step 4.3: Remove the Spark Plug
Use a spark plug socket to remove the spark plug.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the recommended type and amount of oil to avoid damaging the engine.
* Never over-tighten the oil drain plug, as this can damage the threads.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure the spark plugs are tightened to the correct specification.
* Don't ignore unusual noises or decreased performance, as these can be signs of a more serious issue.
