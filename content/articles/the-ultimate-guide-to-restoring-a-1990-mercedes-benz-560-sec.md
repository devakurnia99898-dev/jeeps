## Technical Deep Dive
The 1990 Mercedes-Benz 560 SEC features a 5.6L V8 engine, producing 296 horsepower and 332 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 4-speed automatic transmission and rear-wheel drive. The suspension system consists of a double-wishbone setup in the front and a semi-trailing arm design in the rear.

Common failure points include:
* The plastic impeller in the water pump, which can crack and cause coolant leaks
* The rubber bushings in the suspension system, which can wear out and affect handling
* The electrical system's wiring and connectors, which can corrode and cause malfunctions

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Water Pump Replacement
#### Step 1: Locate the water pump
The water pump is located on the driver's side of the engine compartment. Remove the serpentine belt and the water pump pulley to access the pump.
#### Step 2: Disconnect the coolant hoses
Use a wrench to loosen the hose clamps and remove the coolant hoses from the water pump.
#### Step 3: Remove the water pump mounting bolts
Use a socket to remove the mounting bolts that hold the water pump in place.
#### Step 4: Install the new water pump
Mount the new water pump and replace the mounting bolts. Tighten the bolts to 30 ft-lbs.

### Suspension Overhaul
#### Step 1: Raise the vehicle
Use a hydraulic lift or jack stands to raise the vehicle and remove the wheels.
#### Step 2: Remove the suspension components
Use a wrench to remove the bolts that hold the suspension components in place.
#### Step 3: Inspect and replace the rubber bushings
Inspect the rubber bushings for signs of wear and replace them as needed.
#### Step 4: Reassemble the suspension system
Reinstall the suspension components and tighten the bolts to the specified torque.

### Electrical System Repair
#### Step 1: Identify the malfunctioning component
Use a multimeter to identify the malfunctioning component in the electrical system.
#### Step 2: Remove the component
Use a wrench to remove the bolts that hold the component in place.
#### Step 3: Repair or replace the component
Repair or replace the component as needed.
#### Step 4: Reinstall the component
Reinstall the component and tighten the bolts to the specified torque.
