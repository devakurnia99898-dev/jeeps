## Technical Deep Dive
The turbo-diesel engine in the AMC Eagle is a complex system that consists of several key components, including the turbocharger, fuel injection system, and engine block. The turbocharger is responsible for compressing air and forcing it into the engine's cylinders, resulting in a significant increase in power output. The fuel injection system is responsible for delivering fuel to the engine's cylinders at the correct pressure and timing. Common failure points in turbo-diesel engines include the turbocharger, fuel injectors, and engine bearings. The plastic impeller in the water pump is also a common failure point, as it can become brittle and crack over time.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Inspect the Turbocharger
The turbocharger is a critical component of the turbo-diesel engine. To inspect the turbocharger, follow these steps:
#### Step 1.1: Remove the Turbocharger
Use a 10mm socket to remove the turbocharger from the engine block. Be careful not to damage the turbocharger or surrounding components.
#### Step 1.2: Inspect the Turbocharger
Visually inspect the turbocharger for signs of damage or wear. Check the turbine and compressor wheels for excessive play or damage.
#### Step 1.3: Clean the Turbocharger
Use a soft brush and cleaning solution to clean the turbocharger. Avoid using harsh chemicals or abrasive materials that can damage the turbocharger.
### Step 2: Replace the Fuel Injectors
The fuel injectors are responsible for delivering fuel to the engine's cylinders at the correct pressure and timing. To replace the fuel injectors, follow these steps:
#### Step 2.1: Remove the Fuel Injector
Use a fuel injector tester to remove the fuel injector from the engine block. Be careful not to damage the fuel injector or surrounding components.
#### Step 2.2: Inspect the Fuel Injector
Visually inspect the fuel injector for signs of damage or wear. Check the fuel injector's spray pattern and flow rate.
#### Step 2.3: Install the New Fuel Injector
Install the new fuel injector and ensure it is properly seated and secured.
### Step 3: Rebuild the Turbocharger
The turbocharger is a complex component that requires specialized tools and expertise to rebuild. To rebuild the turbocharger, follow these steps:
#### Step 3.1: Disassemble the Turbocharger
Use a torque wrench to disassemble the turbocharger. Be careful not to damage the turbocharger or surrounding components.
#### Step 3.2: Inspect the Turbocharger Components
Visually inspect the turbocharger components for signs of damage or wear. Check the turbine and compressor wheels for excessive play or damage.
#### Step 3.3: Replace the Turbocharger Components
Replace the turbocharger components as necessary. Use a turbocharger rebuild kit to ensure all components are properly aligned and secured.
