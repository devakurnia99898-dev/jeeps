## **Technical Deep Dive**
Electric and hybrid vehicles rely on advanced electrical systems, including high-voltage batteries, electric motors, and sophisticated control units. Understanding how these systems work is essential for effective maintenance and repair. Common failure points in electric and hybrid vehicles include:

* The electric motor's bearings and windings
* The high-voltage battery's cells and management system
* The power electronics and control units

### **Tools Required**
To work on electric and hybrid vehicles, you'll need:
* A high-voltage probe and test leads
* A multimeter and oscilloscope
* A scan tool and software
* A torque wrench and socket set
* Protective gear, including gloves and safety glasses

### **Safety Warnings**
When working on electric and hybrid vehicles, it's essential to take safety precautions seriously:
* Always disconnect the high-voltage battery before starting work
* Use protective gear and follow proper lockout/tagout procedures
* Never touch electrical components with your bare hands

## **Frequently Asked Questions**
Here are five detailed FAQs relevant to the topic:
1. **Q: What's the difference between a hybrid and electric vehicle?**
A: A hybrid vehicle combines a conventional engine with an electric motor, while an electric vehicle is powered solely by electric motors and batteries.
2. **Q: How do I safely work on high-voltage systems?**
A: Always disconnect the high-voltage battery before starting work, use protective gear, and follow proper lockout/tagout procedures.
3. **Q: What tools do I need to work on electric and hybrid vehicles?**
A: You'll need a high-voltage probe and test leads, a multimeter and oscilloscope, a scan tool and software, and protective gear.
4. **Q: How often should I inspect and maintain my electric or hybrid vehicle?**
A: Schedule regular inspections every 6-12 months, and perform basic maintenance tasks as needed.
5. **Q: Can I repair my electric or hybrid vehicle myself?**
A: While some basic maintenance tasks can be performed DIY, more complex repairs may require the expertise of a professional mechanic.
