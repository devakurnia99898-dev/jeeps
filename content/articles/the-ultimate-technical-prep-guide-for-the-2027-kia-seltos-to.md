## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation and Inspection
#### Checking the Owner's Manual
 Familiarize yourself with the owner's manual to understand the recommended maintenance schedule and procedures.
#### Inspecting the Vehicle
 Walk around the vehicle to identify any signs of wear or damage, paying attention to the tires, brakes, and suspension components.

### Maintenance and Repair
#### Oil Change
1. Locate the oil drain plug and position a drain pan underneath.
2. Remove the oil drain plug using a 10mm socket and allow the oil to drain completely.
3. Replace the oil drain plug and tighten to the specified torque (30 ft-lbs).
4. Fill the engine with the recommended type and amount of oil.
#### Tire Rotation
1. Loosen the lug nuts using a torque wrench (100 ft-lbs).
2. Raise the vehicle using a jack and remove the lug nuts.
3. Rotate the tires according to the recommended pattern (rearward cross).
4. Tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern to the specified torque (100 ft-lbs).

### Suspension and Steering
#### Inspecting the Suspension
1. Jack up the vehicle and support it with jack stands.
2. Inspect the front and rear axles, looking for signs of wear or damage.
3. Check the shock absorbers and struts for leaks or damage.
#### Aligning the Wheels
1. Use a wheel alignment tool to check the camber, toe, and caster.
2. Adjust the wheel alignment as needed to ensure proper handling and tire wear.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
