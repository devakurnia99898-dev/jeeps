## Step-by-Step Guide
### Oil Change
#### Draining the Oil
1. Locate the oil drain plug and position a drain pan underneath.
2. Use a 10mm socket to remove the oil drain plug.
3. Allow the oil to drain completely before replacing the plug.

#### Replacing the Oil Filter
1. Locate the oil filter and remove it by hand or with a filter wrench.
2. Install a new oil filter and hand tighten it.

#### Refilling the Oil
1. Use a funnel to pour in the new oil.
2. Replace the oil cap and start the engine.
3. Check for any leaks around the oil drain plug and oil filter.

### Tire Rotation
#### Rotating the Tires
1. Locate the tire rotation pattern in the owner's manual.
2. Use a tire pressure gauge to check the tire pressure.
3. Rotate the tires according to the recommended pattern.

### Brake Pad Replacement
#### Removing the Wheels
1. Loosen the lug nuts with a 12mm socket.
2. Raise the vehicle using a jack and remove the wheels.

#### Removing the Brake Caliper
1. Use a 10mm socket to remove the caliper bolts.
2. Slide the caliper off the rotor.

#### Replacing the Brake Pads
1. Remove the old brake pads and install new ones.
2. Apply brake lubricant to the back of the pads.

#### Reinstalling the Brake Caliper
1. Slide the caliper back over the rotor.
2. Tighten the caliper bolts in a star pattern.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type and amount of oil for your vehicle.
* Never over-tighten the oil drain plug or oil filter.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure the correct torque specification for the brake caliper bolts.
* Avoid mixing different types of brake pads, as this can cause uneven braking performance.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is the recommended oil type for the Mazda CX-30?**
The recommended oil type for the Mazda CX-30 is 0W-20 synthetic oil.
2. **How often should I rotate my tires?**
It is recommended to rotate your tires every 5,000 to 8,000 miles.
3. **What are the symptoms of worn-out brake pads?**
Symptoms of worn-out brake pads include squealing or grinding noises, vibration when braking, and decreased braking performance.
4. **Can I use any type of brake pads for my Mazda CX-30?**
No, it is recommended to use only OEM or high-quality aftermarket brake pads specifically designed for the Mazda CX-30.
5. **How often should I check my tire pressure?**
It is recommended to check your tire pressure at least once a month, and before long trips.
