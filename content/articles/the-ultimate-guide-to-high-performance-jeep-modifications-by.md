## Technical Deep Dive
The Jeep Wrangler's engine is a critical component of its performance. The 3.6L V6 engine is a popular choice, but it can be further modified to increase power output. A cold air intake and cat-back exhaust system can improve airflow and reduce backpressure, resulting in a significant increase in horsepower.

The suspension system is also critical for off-roading. The stock suspension can be upgraded with coilovers, which provide improved articulation and stability. The electrical system must also be evaluated to ensure it can handle the increased demand of high-performance components.

Common failure points in the Jeep Wrangler include the stock axles, which can be prone to breakage under heavy stress. Upgrading to heavy-duty axles can improve the vehicle's overall strength and durability.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Cold Air Intake Installation
The cold air intake is a simple yet effective modification that can improve the vehicle's power output. To install a cold air intake, follow these steps:
#### Step 1.1: Remove the Stock Airbox
Remove the stock airbox and filter to make way for the new cold air intake.
#### Step 1.2: Install the Cold Air Intake
Install the cold air intake, ensuring it is securely fastened to the engine.
#### Step 1.3: Connect the Mass Airflow Sensor
Connect the mass airflow sensor to the cold air intake, ensuring it is securely fastened.

### Step 2: Cat-Back Exhaust Installation
The cat-back exhaust system is a more complex modification that requires careful planning and execution. To install a cat-back exhaust system, follow these steps:
#### Step 2.1: Remove the Stock Exhaust System
Remove the stock exhaust system, including the muffler and tailpipe.
#### Step 2.2: Install the Cat-Back Exhaust System
Install the cat-back exhaust system, ensuring it is securely fastened to the vehicle.
#### Step 2.3: Connect the Oxygen Sensor
Connect the oxygen sensor to the cat-back exhaust system, ensuring it is securely fastened.

### Step 3: Coilover Installation
The coilovers are a critical component of the suspension system, providing improved articulation and stability. To install coilovers, follow these steps:
#### Step 3.1: Remove the Stock Suspension
Remove the stock suspension, including the coil springs and shocks.
#### Step 3.2: Install the Coilovers
Install the coilovers, ensuring they are securely fastened to the vehicle.
#### Step 3.3: Adjust the Coilovers
