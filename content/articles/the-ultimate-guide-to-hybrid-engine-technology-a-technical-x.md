## Technical Deep Dive
The Grand Sport X's hybrid engine system combines a 6.7-liter V8 engine with an electric motor. The V8 engine provides the primary power source, while the electric motor assists during acceleration and braking. This combination allows for improved fuel efficiency, reduced emissions, and enhanced performance.

The system consists of several key components, including:
* The 6.7-liter V8 engine, which features advanced technologies such as direct fuel injection and variable valve timing
* The electric motor, which is powered by a high-voltage battery pack
* The power control unit (PCU), which manages the flow of energy between the engine, electric motor, and battery pack
* The transmission, which is designed to handle the combined power output of the engine and electric motor

Common failure points in hybrid engine systems include:
* The electric motor's bearings and seals, which can wear out over time
* The battery pack's cells, which can degrade and lose capacity
* The PCU's software and hardware, which can malfunction or fail due to overheating or electrical surges

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Introduction to Hybrid Engine Maintenance
Hybrid engine maintenance requires a comprehensive understanding of both mechanical and electrical systems. Before starting work, it's essential to familiarize yourself with the vehicle's components and systems.

#### Locating the Hybrid Engine Components
* The 6.7-liter V8 engine is located in the engine compartment, while the electric motor is usually mounted near the transmission
* The battery pack is typically located in the trunk or under the rear seat
* The PCU is usually mounted near the engine or transmission

### Step 1: Pre-Maintenance Checks
#### Checking the Battery Pack
* Use a battery tester to check the voltage and capacity of the battery pack
* Ensure the battery pack is properly secured and connected to the electrical system

#### Checking the Electric Motor
* Use a multimeter to check the resistance and voltage of the electric motor
* Ensure the electric motor is properly connected to the PCU and battery pack

### Step 2: Maintenance Procedures
#### Replacing the Electric Motor's Bearings and Seals
* Use a hydraulic press to remove the old bearings and seals
* Install new bearings and seals, ensuring proper alignment and torque specs

#### Updating the PCU's Software
* Use a scan tool to connect to the PCU and update the software
* Ensure the PCU is properly configured and calibrated

### Step 3: Post-Maintenance Checks
#### Testing the Hybrid Engine System
* Use a scan tool to monitor the system's performance and ensure proper function
* Test the vehicle's acceleration, braking, and fuel efficiency

#### Checking for Error Codes
* Use a scan tool to check for any error codes or warning messages
* Address any issues promptly to prevent further damage
