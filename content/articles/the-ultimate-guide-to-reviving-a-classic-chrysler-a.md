## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Gather materials
* Oil filter wrench
* New oil filter
* Oil drain pan
* New motor oil
#### Step 1.2: Locate the oil drain plug
* Consult the owner's manual for the location of the oil drain plug
* Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug
#### Step 1.3: Drain the oil
* Allow the oil to drain completely before replacing the plug
* Use a torque wrench to tighten the plug to the specified torque (30 ft-lbs)

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheels
* Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts
* Raise the vehicle using a jack and remove the wheels
#### Step 2.2: Remove the caliper
* Use a 10mm socket to remove the caliper bolts
* Slide the caliper off the rotor
#### Step 2.3: Remove the old brake pads
* Use pliers to remove the old brake pads
* Inspect the rotors for wear or damage

### Step 3: Suspension Overhaul
#### Step 3.1: Remove the shocks
* Use a 10mm socket to remove the shock absorber bolts
* Remove the shocks from the vehicle
#### Step 3.2: Remove the springs
* Use a spring compressor to compress the springs
* Remove the springs from the vehicle
#### Step 3.3: Inspect and replace the bushings
* Inspect the bushings for wear or damage
* Use a bushing press to remove the old bushings and install new ones

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always refer to the owner's manual for specific instructions and torque specifications
* Use the correct type of oil for your vehicle (synthetic or conventional)
* Avoid over-tightening bolts or nuts, as this can cause damage to the threads

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What type of oil should I use in my classic Chrysler?**
The type of oil to use in a classic Chrysler depends on the engine type and climate. Consult the owner's manual for specific recommendations.
2. **How often should I change my brake pads?**
Brake pads should be changed every 30,000 to 50,000 miles, depending on driving conditions.
3. **What are the symptoms of a faulty suspension system?**
Symptoms of a faulty suspension system include uneven tire wear, vibrations, and decreased handling performance.
4. **Can I replace my own shocks and springs?**
Yes, replacing shocks and springs can be done at home with the right tools and expertise. However, it is recommended to consult a professional mechanic if you are unsure.
5. **How can I prevent rust spots on my classic Chrysler?**
Rust spots can be prevented by regularly washing and waxing the vehicle, and applying a rust-inhibiting coating to the underside and suspension components.
