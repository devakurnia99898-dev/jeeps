The Chevrolet Sonic is a sub-compact car that has been revived for the South American market. In this guide, we'll focus on preparing your garage for the Chevrolet Sonic, covering the technical background, symptoms or signs to look for, and a deep dive into the system.

## Technical Deep Dive
The Chevrolet Sonic's engine, transmission, and suspension systems are critical components that require regular maintenance. The engine, for example, has a timing chain that needs to be replaced every 100,000 miles. The transmission, on the other hand, has a fluid change interval of every 30,000 miles. The suspension system, which includes shock absorbers and struts, needs to be inspected every 12,000 miles.

Common failure points in the Chevrolet Sonic include the water pump, which can fail due to a faulty thermostat or low coolant levels. The brake pads and rotors also need to be replaced every 30,000 miles, and the air filter needs to be replaced every 15,000 miles.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Prepare the Garage
#### Step 1.1: Clear the Workspace
Clear the garage floor of any clutter or obstacles to create a safe and efficient workspace.
#### Step 1.2: Gather Tools and Equipment
Gather all the necessary tools and equipment, including the owner's manual, to ensure that you have everything you need before starting any work.

### Step 2: Inspect the Chevrolet Sonic
#### Step 2.1: Check the Oil Level
Check the oil level to ensure that it is at the recommended level. If the oil level is low, add the recommended type and amount of oil.
#### Step 2.2: Inspect the Tires
Inspect the tires to ensure that they are properly inflated and have sufficient tread depth. If the tires are worn or damaged, replace them with new ones.

### Step 3: Perform Routine Maintenance
#### Step 3.1: Change the Oil
Change the oil and filter every 5,000 miles to ensure that the engine is properly lubricated.
#### Step 3.2: Replace the Air Filter
Replace the air filter every 15,000 miles to ensure that the engine is getting enough air.
