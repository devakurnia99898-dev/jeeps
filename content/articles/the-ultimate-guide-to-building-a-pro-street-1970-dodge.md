Building a Pro Street 1970 Dodge Charger is an exciting project that requires careful planning, precision execution, and a deep understanding of the vehicle's mechanics. However, transforming it into a Pro Street Daytona-style machine requires significant modifications to the engine, suspension, and body. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of building a Pro Street 1970 Dodge Charger, highlighting the key components, technical considerations, and potential challenges.

## Technical Deep Dive
The Pro Street 1970 Dodge Charger is a high-performance vehicle that requires a powerful engine, a robust suspension system, and a lightweight body. The engine is the heart of the vehicle, and for a Pro Street build, you will need a high-performance engine that can produce significant horsepower and torque. The most common engine choices for a Pro Street 1970 Dodge Charger include the 440 Magnum, the 426 Hemi, and the 512 Hemi. The suspension system is also critical, as it needs to be able to handle the high speeds and tight corners that are characteristic of Pro Street racing. The suspension system typically includes a combination of coilovers, sway bars, and control arms.
