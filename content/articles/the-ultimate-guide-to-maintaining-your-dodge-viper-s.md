## Technical Deep Dive
The Dodge Viper's engine is a 8.0L V10 powerhouse that produces 645 horsepower and 600 lb-ft of torque. The engine features a cast iron block, aluminum cylinder heads, and a forged steel crankshaft. The suspension system is a double-wishbone design with coil springs and stabilizer bars, providing exceptional handling and stability.

Common failure points on the Dodge Viper include the serpentine belt, water pump, and spark plugs. The serpentine belt can crack and break, causing the engine to overheat and potentially leading to costly repairs. The water pump can fail, causing the engine to overheat and potentially leading to engine damage. Spark plugs can become fouled, reducing engine performance and fuel efficiency.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the oil drain plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the car. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.

#### Step 1.2: Replace the oil filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the oil filter and discard it. Install a new oil filter and hand tighten it.

#### Step 1.3: Refill the oil
Use a funnel to pour in the new oil. Check the owner's manual for the recommended oil type and capacity.

### Step 2: Tire Rotation
#### Step 2.1: Loosen the lug nuts
Before raising the vehicle, loosen the lug nuts on the wheels using a 12mm socket.

#### Step 2.2: Raise the vehicle
Use a jack to raise the vehicle and support it with jack stands.

#### Step 2.3: Remove the wheels
Remove the lug nuts and wheels, and rotate them according to the recommended pattern.

### Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Remove the caliper
Use a 10mm socket to remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.

#### Step 3.2: Remove the old brake pads
Use pliers to remove the old brake pads from the caliper.

#### Step 3.3: Install the new brake pads
Install the new brake pads and reassemble the caliper.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the recommended oil type and capacity to avoid engine damage.
* Never over-tighten the lug nuts, as this can damage the wheels and brakes.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure the correct torque specification is applied to the bolts and nuts.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
The cost of maintaining your Dodge Viper can vary depending on the task and whether you choose to DIY or take it to a mechanic. Oil changes and tire rotations are relatively inexpensive and can be done DIY. However, brake pad replacement and other more complex tasks may require the expertise of a mechanic, which can increase the cost.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What type of oil should I use in my Dodge Viper?**
The recommended oil type for the Dodge Viper is 5W-40 synthetic oil.
2. **How often should I rotate my tires?**
Tires should be rotated every 5,000 to 8,000 miles to ensure even wear and extend their lifespan.
3. **What are the symptoms of a faulty serpentine belt?**
Symptoms of a faulty serpentine belt include a squealing noise, overheating engine, and loss of power steering and air conditioning.
4. **How often should I replace my spark plugs?**
Spark plugs should be replaced every 30,000 to 100,000 miles, depending on the type of spark plugs and driving conditions.
5. **Can I use a regular brake pad on my Dodge Viper?**
No, the Dodge Viper requires a high-performance brake pad that is designed to withstand the high heat and friction generated by the vehicle's high-performance capabilities.
