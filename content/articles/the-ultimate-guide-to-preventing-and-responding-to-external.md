## Symptoms or Signs to look for
If your Jeep has been damaged by an external object, you may notice a range of symptoms including dents, scratches, and broken glass. In severe cases, the damage can be more extensive, including bent or broken frame components, damaged suspension, and compromised electrical systems. It's essential to inspect your vehicle regularly and address any issues promptly to prevent further damage.

## Technical Deep Dive
### How the system works
The body and frame of your Jeep are designed to withstand a certain amount of impact, but they are not invincible. The key to preventing and responding to external object damage is to understand how the various systems work together to protect your vehicle. This includes the frame, suspension, and body panels, as well as the electrical and mechanical systems.

### Common failure points
Some common failure points to watch out for include:
* The plastic components on the front and rear bumpers, which can be prone to cracking and breaking
* The aluminum body panels, which can dent and scratch easily
* The glass components, including the windshield and side windows, which can shatter or crack on impact
* The suspension components, including the control arms and steering gear, which can bend or break if subjected to sufficient force

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspect the Vehicle for Damage
#### Step 1: Walk around the vehicle
Walk around the vehicle to inspect for any visible signs of damage, including dents, scratches, and broken glass.
#### Step 2: Check the suspension
Check the suspension components, including the control arms and steering gear, for any signs of damage or wear.
#### Step 3: Inspect the body panels
Inspect the body panels, including the hood, doors, and trunk lid, for any signs of damage or wear.

### Replace Broken Glass
#### Step 1: Remove the broken glass
Use glass removal tools to carefully remove the broken glass from the vehicle.
#### Step 2: Clean the area
Clean the area around the broken glass to remove any debris or sharp edges.
#### Step 3: Install the new glass
Install the new glass, making sure it is securely fastened and sealed.

### Repair Dented Body Panels
#### Step 1: Assess the damage
Assess the damage to the body panel to determine the best course of action.
#### Step 2: Remove the panel
Remove the panel and inspect it for any signs of further damage.
#### Step 3: Repair the panel
Use a body panel repair kit to repair the panel, following the manufacturer's instructions.

### Replace Bent Suspension Components
#### Step 1: Jack up the vehicle
Jack up the vehicle and support it with jack stands.
#### Step 2: Remove the bent component
Remove the bent suspension component and inspect it for any signs of further damage.
#### Step 3: Install the new component
Install the new suspension component, making sure it is securely fastened and aligned properly.
