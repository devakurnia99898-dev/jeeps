## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The development of autonomous vehicles has been hindered by the complexity and cost of sensor systems. Traditional autonomous vehicles rely on separate lidar and camera systems to navigate their surroundings. However, this approach has several drawbacks, including increased cost, reduced reliability, and decreased efficiency. The integration of lidar and camera systems into a single unit has the potential to revolutionize the autonomous vehicle industry.

Symptoms or signs to look for in an autonomous Jeep's sensor system include reduced accuracy, increased latency, and decreased reliability. These issues can be caused by a variety of factors, including sensor malfunction, software glitches, and hardware compatibility problems.

## Technical Deep Dive
The integrated lidar and camera system works by capturing images and lidar distance data simultaneously using the same hardware. This is achieved through the use of advanced sensors and sophisticated software algorithms. The system consists of a lidar module, a camera module, and a processing unit.

The lidar module uses laser light to measure the distance between the vehicle and surrounding objects. The camera module captures high-resolution images of the environment, which are then used to detect and classify objects. The processing unit combines the data from the lidar and camera modules to create a comprehensive 3D map of the surroundings.

Common failure points in integrated lidar and camera systems include the lidar module, camera module, and processing unit. The lidar module can be prone to malfunction due to dust, moisture, or other environmental factors. The camera module can be affected by lighting conditions, weather, or other external factors. The processing unit can be susceptible to software glitches or hardware compatibility issues.
