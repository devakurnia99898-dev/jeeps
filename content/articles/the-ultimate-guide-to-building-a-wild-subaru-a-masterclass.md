## **Technical Deep Dive**
The Subaru's all-wheel-drive system is a significant advantage for off-road driving. However, the stock suspension and chassis may not be suitable for extreme off-road use. Common failure points include:
* The stock CV axles, which can break under heavy stress
* The stock suspension components, which can be prone to damage from rocks and other obstacles
* The engine and transmission, which may not be able to handle the increased demands of off-road driving

The Subaru's engine and transmission are designed to provide a balance of power and efficiency. However, for off-road use, a more powerful engine and a transmission with a lower gear ratio may be necessary. The electrical system must also be modified to accommodate the increased demands of off-road driving, including the installation of auxiliary lights, winches, and other accessories.

## **Step-by-Step Guide**
### **Step 1: Planning and Design**
The first step in building a wild Subaru is to plan and design the build. This includes:
#### **Step 1.1: Define the Build's Purpose**
Determine the intended use of the vehicle, including the type of terrain and the level of difficulty.
#### **Step 1.2: Research and Gather Information**
Research different modification options, including suspension lifts, engine and transmission upgrades, and electrical system modifications.
#### **Step 1.3: Create a Budget and Timeline**
Establish a budget and timeline for the build, including the cost of parts and labor.

### **Step 2: Suspension Upgrades**
The suspension system is critical for off-road driving. Upgrades may include:
#### **Step 2.1: Install a Suspension Lift Kit**
Install a suspension lift kit to increase ground clearance and provide more suspension travel.
#### **Step 2.2: Upgrade the Shocks and Struts**
Upgrade the shocks and struts to provide better damping and control.
#### **Step 2.3: Install CV Axles**
Install CV axles to provide more strength and durability.

### **Step 3: Engine and Transmission Upgrades**
The engine and transmission must be upgraded to provide more power and torque. Upgrades may include:
#### **Step 3.1: Install a Performance Engine**
Install a performance engine, such as a turbocharged or supercharged engine.
#### **Step 3.2: Upgrade the Transmission**
Upgrade the transmission to provide a lower gear ratio and more strength.
#### **Step 3.3: Install a Performance Exhaust System**
Install a performance exhaust system to provide more power and torque.

### **Step 4: Electrical System Upgrades**
The electrical system must be modified to accommodate the increased demands of off-road driving. Upgrades may include:
#### **Step 4.1: Install Auxiliary Lights**
Install auxiliary lights, such as LED light bars and spotlights.
#### **Step 4.2: Install a Winch**
Install a winch to provide a means of recovery.
#### **Step 4.3: Upgrade the Battery and Alternator**
Upgrade the battery and alternator to provide more power and durability.
