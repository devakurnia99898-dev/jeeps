## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation and Planning
The first step in preparing for the 2027 Nissan Z is to research and understand the updates and features of the new model. This includes reading reviews, watching videos, and consulting with experts. It's also essential to plan and budget for any maintenance or upgrades.

#### Researching the Vehicle
Start by researching the 2027 Nissan Z, including its features, specifications, and updates. This will help you understand the vehicle's capabilities and limitations.

#### Creating a Maintenance Schedule
Create a maintenance schedule to ensure the vehicle remains in top condition. This includes regular oil changes, tire rotations, and brake pad replacements.

### Maintenance and Upgrades
The next step is to perform regular maintenance and upgrades to enhance the vehicle's performance and longevity.

#### Oil Change
Performing regular oil changes is crucial to the vehicle's health. Use a high-quality oil filter and synthetic oil to ensure the engine runs smoothly.

##### Draining the Oil
Locate the oil drain plug and use a 10mm socket to remove it. Allow the oil to drain completely before replacing the plug.

##### Replacing the Oil Filter
Use a filter wrench to remove the oil filter. Dispose of the old filter responsibly and install a new one.

#### Brake Pad Replacement
Replacing the brake pads is essential to maintaining the vehicle's safety and performance. Use high-quality brake pads and follow proper installation procedures.

##### Removing the Wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake calipers. Use a 12mm socket to loosen the caliper bolts.

##### Installing the New Brake Pads
Install the new brake pads, ensuring they are properly seated and secured.

### Performance Enhancements
The final step is to consider performance enhancements to take the vehicle to the next level.

#### Suspension Upgrade
Upgrading the suspension can significantly improve the vehicle's handling and performance. Use high-quality components and follow proper installation procedures.

##### Removing the Old Suspension
Remove the old suspension components, including the shocks and springs. Use a torque wrench to loosen the bolts.

##### Installing the New Suspension
Install the new suspension components, ensuring they are properly seated and secured. Use a torque wrench to tighten the bolts to the specified torque spec (30 Nm).
