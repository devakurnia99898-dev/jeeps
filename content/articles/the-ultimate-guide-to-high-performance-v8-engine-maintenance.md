## **Step-by-Step Guide**
### **Step 1: Oil Change**
#### **Step 1.1: Warm up the engine**
Drive the vehicle for a few minutes to warm up the engine. This will help the oil drain more easily.

#### **Step 1.2: Locate the oil drain plug**
Find the oil drain plug, usually located at the bottom of the engine.

#### **Step 1.3: Remove the oil drain plug**
Use a 10mm socket to remove the oil drain plug. Be prepared for the oil to start draining out.

#### **Step 1.4: Drain the oil into a pan**
Let the oil drain into a pan for at least 10 minutes.

#### **Step 1.5: Replace the oil filter**
Use a new oil filter and hand tighten it.

#### **Step 1.6: Refill the oil**
Use a funnel to pour in the new oil. Refer to the owner's manual for the correct type and amount of oil.

### **Step 2: Spark Plug Replacement**
#### **Step 2.1: Locate the spark plugs**
Find the spark plugs, usually located on the sides of the engine.

#### **Step 2.2: Remove the spark plug wires**
Use a spark plug wire puller to remove the spark plug wires.

#### **Step 2.3: Remove the spark plugs**
Use a spark plug socket to remove the spark plugs.

#### **Step 2.4: Install new spark plugs**
Use new spark plugs and hand tighten them.

#### **Step 2.5: Replace the spark plug wires**
Use the spark plug wire puller to replace the spark plug wires.

### **Step 3: Air Filter Replacement**
#### **Step 3.1: Locate the air filter**
Find the air filter, usually located in the engine compartment.

#### **Step 3.2: Remove the air filter housing**
Use a screwdriver to remove the air filter housing.

#### **Step 3.3: Remove the air filter**
Pull out the old air filter.

#### **Step 3.4: Install a new air filter**
Use a new air filter and replace the air filter housing.

## **Pro Tips & Common Mistakes**
* Always use the correct type and amount of oil for your vehicle.
* Never over-tighten the oil drain plug or spark plugs.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure the correct torque specs are met.
* Don't forget to replace the air filter regularly.
