## Step-by-Step Guide
### Introduction to Electric Vehicle Maintenance
The Nissan Juke EV requires regular maintenance to ensure optimal performance and longevity. This includes checking the battery state of charge, tire pressure, and brake pads.
#### Checking the Battery State of Charge
1. Locate the battery management system (BMS) display on the dashboard.
2. Note the current state of charge and any error messages.
3. Use a battery tester to verify the BMS reading.
#### Checking the Tire Pressure
1. Locate the tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) display on the dashboard.
2. Note the current tire pressure and any error messages.
3. Use a tire pressure gauge to verify the TPMS reading.
### Battery Maintenance
The Nissan Juke EV's battery requires regular maintenance to ensure optimal performance and longevity. This includes checking the battery terminals, cleaning the battery, and updating the BMS software.
#### Checking the Battery Terminals
1. Locate the battery terminals in the engine compartment.
2. Inspect the terminals for signs of corrosion or damage.
3. Clean the terminals with a wire brush and apply a thin layer of silicone-based lubricant.
#### Cleaning the Battery
1. Locate the battery in the engine compartment.
2. Inspect the battery for signs of dirt or debris.
3. Clean the battery with a soft-bristled brush and a mild detergent.
#### Updating the BMS Software
1. Locate the BMS display on the dashboard.
2. Note the current software version.
3. Use a dedicated BMS software update tool to update the software.
