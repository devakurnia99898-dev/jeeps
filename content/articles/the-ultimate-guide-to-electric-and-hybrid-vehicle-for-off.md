Symptoms or signs to look for in electric and hybrid vehicles include decreased fuel efficiency, unusual noises, and warning lights on the dashboard. Regular maintenance is crucial to prevent these issues and ensure the longevity of the vehicle.

## Technical Deep Dive
Electric and hybrid vehicles rely on complex systems to optimize fuel efficiency and performance. The electric motor, battery, and power control unit work together to provide a smooth and efficient driving experience. Common failure points in these systems include the battery management system, electric motor bearings, and power electronics.

In hybrid vehicles, the internal combustion engine and electric motor work together to provide optimal performance. The engine control unit, transmission, and battery management system must be carefully calibrated to ensure seamless transitions between electric and gasoline power.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Battery Maintenance
#### Step 1.1: Check Battery Voltage
Use a multimeter to check the battery voltage. The recommended voltage for most electric and hybrid vehicles is between 12.4 and 12.7 volts.

#### Step 1.2: Check Battery Terminal Condition
Inspect the battery terminals for signs of corrosion or damage. Clean the terminals with a wire brush and apply a battery terminal protector to prevent future corrosion.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Jack Up the Vehicle
Use a jack and jack stands to lift the vehicle and remove the wheels.

#### Step 2.2: Remove Caliper and Rotor
Remove the caliper and rotor to access the brake pads.

#### Step 2.3: Replace Brake Pads
Replace the old brake pads with new ones, making sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions.

### Step 3: Tire Rotation
#### Step 3.1: Loosen Lug Nuts
Loosen the lug nuts on the wheels using a torque wrench.

#### Step 3.2: Rotate Tires
Rotate the tires according to the manufacturer's recommended pattern.

#### Step 3.3: Tighten Lug Nuts
Tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern to ensure even tightening.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
Don't forget to check the owner's manual for specific maintenance recommendations for your vehicle. Common mistakes include over-tightening lug nuts, which can damage the wheel bearings, and using the wrong type of brake fluid, which can damage the brake system.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
The cost of maintenance for electric and hybrid vehicles can vary depending on the task and the mechanic's labor rate. However, by doing the maintenance yourself, you can save money on labor costs. For example, replacing brake pads can cost between $200 and $500 at a mechanic's shop, but can be done for around $100 if you do it yourself.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **Q: What is the recommended maintenance schedule for electric and hybrid vehicles?**
A: The recommended maintenance schedule varies depending on the manufacturer and model, but most vehicles require regular oil changes, tire rotations, and brake pad replacements.
2. **Q: Can I use regular gasoline in my hybrid vehicle?**
A: Yes, most hybrid vehicles can run on regular gasoline, but it's recommended to use premium gasoline for optimal performance and fuel efficiency.
3. **Q: How often should I charge my electric vehicle?**
A: The frequency of charging depends on your driving habits and the type of vehicle you have. Most electric vehicles have a recommended charging schedule, but it's generally recommended to charge the vehicle when the battery level falls below 20%.
4. **Q: Can I replace the battery in my electric vehicle myself?**
A: It's not recommended to replace the battery in your electric vehicle yourself, as it requires specialized tools and expertise. It's best to have a professional mechanic or the dealership replace the battery.
5. **Q: What are the benefits of regular maintenance for electric and hybrid vehicles?**
A: Regular maintenance can help extend the life of the vehicle, improve fuel efficiency, and reduce the risk of costly repairs. It can also help ensure the vehicle is running safely and efficiently.
