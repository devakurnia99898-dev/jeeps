## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the car. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and drain the oil into a pan.

#### Step 1.2: Replace the Oil Filter
The oil filter is located on top of the engine. Use a filter wrench to remove the old filter and replace it with a new one.

#### Step 1.3: Refill the Oil
Refill the oil with the recommended type and amount. Check the owner's manual for specifications.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake pads. Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts and then remove the wheels.

#### Step 2.2: Remove the Caliper
Remove the caliper to access the brake pads. Use a 10mm socket to remove the caliper bolts.

#### Step 2.3: Replace the Brake Pads
Replace the brake pads with new ones. Make sure to properly align the pads and secure them with the retaining clips.

### Step 3: Water Pump Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Locate the Water Pump
The water pump is located at the front of the engine. Use a 10mm socket to remove the water pump bolts.

#### Step 3.2: Remove the Water Pump
Remove the water pump and replace it with a new one. Make sure to properly align the pump and secure it with the retaining bolts.

#### Step 3.3: Refill the Coolant
Refill the coolant with the recommended type and amount. Check the owner's manual for specifications.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type and amount of oil and coolant.
* Make sure to properly align the brake pads and water pump.
* Don't overtighten the bolts, as this can damage the engine or other components.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure the correct torque specs.
