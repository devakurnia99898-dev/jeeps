## Technical Deep Dive
Porsche vehicles are renowned for their exceptional engineering and performance. However, like any complex machine, they require regular maintenance to function optimally. The key systems to focus on include the engine, transmission, suspension, and braking system. Common failure points to watch out for include:
* The intermediate shaft bearing in the gearbox
* The rear main seal in the engine
* The brake pads and rotors
* The suspension bushings and control arms

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Oil Change
#### Draining the Oil
1. Locate the oil drain plug underneath the vehicle and position a drip pan to catch the old oil.
2. Use a 13mm socket to loosen the plug, then allow the oil to drain completely.
3. Replace the plug and tighten to the specified torque (30 Nm).

#### Replacing the Oil Filter
1. Locate the oil filter on the top of the engine and remove it by hand or with a filter wrench.
2. Install a new oil filter and hand tighten.

#### Refilling the Oil
1. Pour in the recommended type and amount of oil (5W-40, 6 quarts).
2. Replace the oil cap and start the engine, allowing it to run for a few minutes to circulate the new oil.

### Brake Pad Replacement
#### Removing the Wheels
1. Loosen the lug nuts with a 19mm socket, then raise the vehicle and remove the wheels.
2. Remove the caliper bolts (14mm) and slide the caliper off the rotor.

#### Replacing the Brake Pads
1. Remove the old brake pads from the caliper and discard.
2. Install new brake pads, ensuring they're properly seated and aligned.

#### Reassembling the Brakes
1. Reinstall the caliper and tighten the bolts (40 Nm).
2. Replace the wheels and tighten the lug nuts (80 Nm).

### Suspension Inspection
#### Inspecting the Bushings
1. Locate the suspension bushings and inspect for signs of wear or damage.
2. Use a suspension compressor to compress the bushings and check for play.

#### Inspecting the Control Arms
1. Locate the control arms and inspect for signs of wear or damage.
2. Use a torque wrench to check the control arm bolts (80 Nm).

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type and amount of oil for your Porsche
* Never mix old and new brake fluid, as this can cause contamination and damage to the system
* When replacing brake pads, always replace the rotors as well to ensure even wear and prevent warping
* When inspecting the suspension, always use a suspension compressor to compress the bushings and check for play

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **Q: How often should I change my oil?**
A: Every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on the type of oil and driving conditions.
2. **Q: What type of brake pads should I use?**
A: Porsche-approved brake pads, which are designed to meet the specific requirements of your vehicle.
3. **Q: How do I know if my suspension bushings need to be replaced?**
A: If you notice excessive play or movement in the suspension, or if you hear unusual noises when driving over bumps.
4. **Q: Can I use any type of oil in my Porsche?**
A: No, always use the recommended type and amount of oil for your vehicle, as specified in the owner's manual.
5. **Q: How often should I inspect my brakes?**
A: Every 10,000 to 15,000 miles, or as recommended by the manufacturer.
