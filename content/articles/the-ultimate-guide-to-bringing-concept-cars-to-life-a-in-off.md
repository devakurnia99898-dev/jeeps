## Technical Deep Dive
The Jeep Hurricane's four-wheel steering system is a complex mechanism that requires a deep understanding of suspension geometry, steering dynamics, and chassis engineering. The system uses a combination of electronic and hydraulic components to steer the rear wheels in the opposite direction of the front wheels, allowing for tighter turns and improved maneuverability. However, this system also presents several challenges, including increased complexity, higher cost, and potential reliability issues.

Common failure points in such a system include the electronic control unit, hydraulic pumps, and steering gearboxes. The plastic impeller in the hydraulic pump, for example, can be prone to failure due to high pressure and temperature fluctuations. Similarly, the electronic control unit can be susceptible to software glitches and hardware failures, which can affect the overall performance of the system.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When working on complex systems like the Jeep Hurricane's four-wheel steering, it's essential to avoid common mistakes and follow best practices. Some pro tips include:

* Ensure proper hydraulic system bleeding and testing to avoid air pockets and contamination.
* Use high-quality components and materials to ensure reliability and durability.
* Follow proper installation and testing procedures to avoid damage or injury.

Common mistakes to avoid include:

* Insufficient testing and validation, which can lead to reliability issues and safety hazards.
* Poor component sourcing, which can result in substandard parts and components.
* Inadequate design and planning, which can lead to costly rework and modifications.
