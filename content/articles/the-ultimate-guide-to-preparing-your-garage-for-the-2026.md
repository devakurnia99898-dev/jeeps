## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation and Planning
#### Gathering Information
Before starting work on the Charger SIXPACK, it's essential to gather all the necessary information and resources. This includes the vehicle's owner's manual, repair manual, and any relevant technical service bulletins.

#### Inspecting the Vehicle
Next, inspect the vehicle to identify any areas that require attention. This includes checking the engine, transmission, suspension, and brakes for signs of wear or damage.

#### Creating a Work Plan
Based on the inspection results, create a work plan that outlines the necessary repairs and maintenance tasks. This should include a schedule, budget, and list of required tools and materials.

### Oil Change and Fluid Check
#### Draining the Oil
To change the oil, start by locating the oil drain plug and positioning a drain pan underneath. Use a socket wrench to loosen the plug, then allow the oil to drain completely.

#### Replacing the Oil Filter
Once the oil has drained, replace the oil filter with a new one. Make sure to dispose of the old filter responsibly.

#### Refilling the Oil
Refill the oil with the recommended type and amount, then replace the oil cap.

#### Checking the Fluids
Check the other fluids, including the coolant, transmission, and brake fluids, to ensure they are at the recommended levels.

### Brake Pad Replacement
#### Removing the Wheels
To replace the brake pads, start by removing the wheels and calipers. Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts, then raise the vehicle using a hydraulic lift.

#### Removing the Old Pads
Next, remove the old brake pads and inspect the brake rotors for signs of wear or damage.

#### Installing the New Pads
Install the new brake pads, making sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions and torque specifications.

#### Bleeding the Brakes
Finally, bleed the brakes to remove any air from the system and ensure proper function.

### Suspension Inspection
#### Inspecting the Shocks and Struts
To inspect the suspension, start by checking the shocks and struts for signs of wear or damage. Use a bounce test to check for proper function.

#### Inspecting the Springs and Bushings
Next, inspect the springs and bushings for signs of wear or damage. Use a visual inspection to check for cracks or corrosion.

#### Checking the Alignment
Finally, check the vehicle's alignment to ensure it is within the manufacturer's specifications.
