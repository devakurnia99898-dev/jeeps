## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The Scion iQ is a unique vehicle that offers a spacious interior despite its compact exterior. However, when buying a used Scion iQ, it's essential to conduct a thorough inspection to ensure you're making a smart purchase. The $4,999 asking price may seem attractive, but you need to consider the vehicle's condition, maintenance history, and potential repair costs. In this guide, we'll walk you through the technical aspects of inspecting and maintaining a used Scion iQ.

Symptoms or signs to look for when inspecting a used Scion iQ include:
* Unusual noises or vibrations from the engine or transmission
* Uneven tire wear or loose suspension components
* Water damage or signs of previous repairs
* Electrical issues or malfunctioning infotainment systems

## Technical Deep Dive
The Scion iQ is equipped with a 1.3L inline-4 cylinder engine, producing 94 horsepower and 89 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a continuously variable transmission (CVT) and features a front-wheel-drive layout. The suspension consists of MacPherson struts in the front and a torsion beam axle in the rear.

Common failure points in the Scion iQ include:
* The CVT transmission, which can be prone to slipping or hesitation
* The engine's timing chain, which may stretch or fail over time
* The brake pads and rotors, which can wear down quickly due to the vehicle's lightweight design

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspecting the Engine and Transmission
#### Checking the Oil and Fluid Levels
1. Locate the oil dipstick and check the oil level. Add oil as needed to maintain the recommended level.
2. Check the transmission fluid level and condition. The fluid should be pink or red in color and have a smooth, thick consistency.

#### Inspecting the Belts and Hoses
1. Locate the serpentine belt and inspect it for cracks, frays, or signs of wear.
2. Check the condition of the hoses, including the radiator hose, heater hose, and brake hoses.

### Inspecting the Suspension and Brakes
#### Checking the Tire Pressure and Condition
1. Check the tire pressure and adjust it to the recommended level.
2. Inspect the tires for uneven wear, cracks, or signs of damage.

#### Inspecting the Brake Pads and Rotors
1. Remove the wheel and inspect the brake pads for wear.
2. Check the condition of the brake rotors, looking for signs of warping or excessive wear.

### Inspecting the Electrical and Infotainment Systems
#### Checking the Battery and Charging System
1. Locate the battery and check its condition, including the terminals and cables.
2. Check the charging system, including the alternator and voltage regulator.

#### Inspecting the Infotainment System
1. Turn on the infotainment system and check for any error messages or malfunctioning components.
2. Check the condition of the speakers and wiring.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't neglect to check the vehicle's maintenance history, including oil change records and repair invoices.
* Be aware of common issues with the CVT transmission and engine timing chain.
* Use a scan tool to diagnose any issues with the CVT transmission or engine.
