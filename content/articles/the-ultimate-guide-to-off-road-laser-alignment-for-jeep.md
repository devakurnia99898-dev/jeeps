## Technical Deep Dive
Laser alignment systems in off-road Jeeps typically consist of a laser emitter, a receiver, and a control unit. The laser emitter sends a beam to the receiver, which calculates the vehicle's alignment and sends the data to the control unit. The control unit then adjusts the vehicle's suspension and steering to maintain optimal alignment.

Common failure points in laser alignment systems include the laser emitter's lens, the receiver's sensor, and the control unit's software. Regular maintenance and inspection of these components are essential to ensure proper function.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Prepare the Vehicle
The vehicle must be on a level surface and securely supported. Remove any loose items from the vehicle and ensure the tires are inflated to the recommended pressure.

#### Step 1.1: Raise the Vehicle
Raise the vehicle using a lift or jack stands to access the suspension and steering components.

#### Step 1.2: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels to access the hub assemblies and brake rotors.

### Step 2: Inspect the Laser Alignment System
Inspect the laser emitter, receiver, and control unit for any signs of damage or wear. Clean the lens and sensor to ensure proper function.

#### Step 2.1: Check the Laser Emitter
Check the laser emitter's lens for any scratches or damage. Replace the lens if necessary.

#### Step 2.2: Check the Receiver
Check the receiver's sensor for any signs of wear or damage. Replace the sensor if necessary.

### Step 3: Adjust the Laser Alignment
Use the laser alignment kit to adjust the vehicle's alignment. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for the specific kit being used.

#### Step 3.1: Set the Toe Angle
Set the toe angle to the recommended specification. Use a torque wrench to secure the adjustment.

#### Step 3.2: Set the Camber Angle
Set the camber angle to the recommended specification. Use a torque wrench to secure the adjustment.
