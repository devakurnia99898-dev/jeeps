## Technical Deep Dive
The Ford Mustang EcoBoost is equipped with a 2.3L turbocharged engine, producing 310 horsepower and 350 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 10-speed automatic transmission and features a number of advanced technologies, including direct fuel injection and twin-scroll turbocharging. The suspension system features a double-ball-joint front suspension and an integral-link rear suspension, providing a smooth and responsive ride.

The retro upgrade package with bronze wheels is designed to enhance the exterior design of the vehicle, providing a unique and stylish look. The package includes a number of components, including bronze wheels, a retro-style front grille, and a unique exterior trim package.

## Common failure points
When upgrading your Mustang EcoBoost with a retro package, there are a number of common failure points to watch out for. These include:

* Incorrect wheel installation: Improperly installed wheels can lead to uneven tire wear, vibrations, and other problems.
* Incompatible suspension components: Upgrading the suspension system with non-compatible components can lead to a rough ride, reduced handling, and other issues.
* Electrical system malfunctions: Upgrading the electrical system with non-compatible components can lead to malfunctions, including faulty lighting, infotainment systems, and other issues.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Prepare the Vehicle
#### Step 1.1: Jack up the vehicle and remove the wheels
Use a jack to lift the vehicle and remove the wheels. This will provide access to the brake system and suspension components.
#### Step 1.2: Remove the brake calipers and rotors
Remove the brake calipers and rotors to access the hub assembly and wheel bearings.
#### Step 1.3: Inspect the hub assembly and wheel bearings
Inspect the hub assembly and wheel bearings for wear and damage. Replace any damaged components.

### Step 2: Install the Bronze Wheels
#### Step 2.1: Clean and prepare the wheel hubs
Clean and prepare the wheel hubs to ensure proper installation of the bronze wheels.
#### Step 2.2: Install the bronze wheels
Install the bronze wheels, ensuring proper torque specs (100 ft-lbs) and wheel nut pattern.
#### Step 2.3: Tighten the wheel nuts
Tighten the wheel nuts in a star pattern to ensure even torque and prevent wheel damage.

### Step 3: Upgrade the Suspension System
#### Step 3.1: Remove the front suspension components
Remove the front suspension components, including the coil springs and shock absorbers.
#### Step 3.2: Install the new suspension components
Install the new suspension components, including coil springs and shock absorbers, ensuring proper torque specs (50 ft-lbs) and alignment.
#### Step 3.3: Align the suspension system
Align the suspension system to ensure proper ride height and handling.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always follow proper torque specs and wheel nut patterns to ensure safe and reliable operation.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure accurate torque specs and prevent over-tightening.
* Never mix and match suspension components from different manufacturers, as this can lead to compatibility issues and reduced performance.
