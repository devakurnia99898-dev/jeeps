* The twin-turbocharged engine's intercooler system, which can be prone to leaks and damage

* The rear-wheel-drive layout's differential and axles, which can be susceptible to damage from aggressive driving

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation and Inspection
Before starting any work on the 2027 Nissan Z, it's essential to inspect the vehicle and prepare the necessary tools and materials.
#### Checking the Vehicle's History
Check the vehicle's history report to ensure it has not been in any major accidents or had any significant repairs.
#### Inspecting the Vehicle's Condition
Inspect the vehicle's condition, looking for any signs of damage or wear, including dents, scratches, and worn-out tires.

### Routine Maintenance
Regular maintenance is crucial to ensure the 2027 Nissan Z runs smoothly and efficiently.
#### Oil Change
Change the engine oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, using a high-quality synthetic oil that meets the manufacturer's specifications.
#### Tire Rotation
Rotate the tires every 6,000 to 8,000 miles, to ensure even wear and extend their lifespan.

### Performance Upgrades
For enthusiasts looking to enhance the 2027 Nissan Z's performance, there are several upgrades available.
#### Suspension Upgrade
Upgrade the suspension with a sport-tuned coilover system, which can improve handling and stability.
#### Brake Upgrade
Upgrade the brakes with high-performance pads and rotors, which can improve stopping power and reduce brake fade.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't over-tighten the engine's oil drain plug, as this can damage the threads and cause leaks
* Use a torque wrench to ensure the correct torque specs are applied to the engine's bolts and fasteners
* Be aware of the potential for overheating when driving aggressively, and ensure the cooling system is functioning properly
