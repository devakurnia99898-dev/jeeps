## Technical Deep Dive
The Jeep's suspension system is a critical component that affects the vehicle's stability, handling, and overall performance. The suspension system consists of coil springs, shock absorbers, and control arms, which work together to absorb bumps and maintain tire contact with the road. Common failure points in the suspension system include worn-out coil springs, leaking shock absorbers, and loose control arms. The engine and electrical system are also critical components that require attention. The engine's air filter, oil, and coolant levels should be checked, and the electrical system's battery, alternator, and wiring should be inspected for any signs of wear or damage.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Gather materials
Collect the necessary materials, including oil filter wrench, funnel, and new oil filter.
#### Step 1.2: Warm up the engine
Drive the Jeep for a few minutes to warm up the engine.
#### Step 1.3: Drain the oil
Use a socket wrench to remove the oil drain plug and let the oil drain into a pan.
#### Step 1.4: Replace the oil filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old oil filter and install a new one.

### Step 2: Tire Rotation
#### Step 2.1: Check tire pressure
Use a tire pressure gauge to check the tire pressure and adjust it according to the manufacturer's recommendations.
#### Step 2.2: Loosen the lug nuts
Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts on the first wheel.
#### Step 2.3: Raise the vehicle
Use a jack to raise the vehicle and remove the wheel.
#### Step 2.4: Rotate the tires
Rotate the tires according to the manufacturer's recommendations.

### Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Remove the wheel
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheel and expose the brake caliper.
#### Step 3.2: Remove the caliper
Use a socket wrench to remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.
#### Step 3.3: Remove the old brake pads
Use a screwdriver to remove the old brake pads from the caliper.
#### Step 3.4: Install the new brake pads
Use a screwdriver to install the new brake pads into the caliper.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't forget to tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern to avoid warping the brake rotor.
* Use a torque wrench to tighten the caliper bolts to the recommended specification.
* Don't mix different types of brake pads, as this can affect the vehicle's braking performance.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
The cost of maintaining a Jeep can vary depending on the task and the individual's level of expertise. However, by doing the maintenance work yourself, you can save money on labor costs. The cost of an oil change, for example, can range from $50 to $100, depending on the type of oil and filter used. Tire rotation can cost between $100 to $200, depending on the type of tires and the shop's labor rate. Brake pad replacement can cost between $200 to $500, depending on the type of brake pads and the shop's labor rate.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the recommended oil change interval for a Jeep?
The recommended oil change interval for a Jeep varies depending on the model and year, but it's typically every 5,000 to 7,500 miles.
2. How often should I rotate my tires?
It's recommended to rotate your tires every 6,000 to 8,000 miles to ensure even tread wear and extend the life of the tires.
3. What are the symptoms of worn-out brake pads?
The symptoms of worn-out brake pads include squealing or grinding noises when applying the brakes, vibration when braking, and a spongy brake pedal.
4. Can I use any type of oil in my Jeep?
No, it's recommended to use the type of oil specified in the owner's manual, as using the wrong type of oil can affect the engine's performance and longevity.
5. How often should I inspect my suspension system?
It's recommended to inspect the suspension system every 12,000 to 15,000 miles to ensure that it's in good condition and functioning properly.
