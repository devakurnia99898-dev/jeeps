## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The Toyota AE86 is a highly sought-after classic car, known for its rear-wheel drive layout and agile handling. However, restoring and maintaining one can be a daunting task, especially for those without experience. Common symptoms of a neglected AE86 include a rough idle, poor acceleration, and worn-out suspension components. It's essential to identify these issues early on to prevent further damage and costly repairs.

## Technical Deep Dive
The AE86's powertrain consists of a 1.6L inline-4 engine, paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. The engine features a dual-overhead camshaft design, with a timing belt driving the camshafts. The suspension is a MacPherson strut setup in the front, with a live axle and trailing arms in the rear. Common failure points include the timing belt, water pump, and suspension bushings.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Preparation and Inspection
The first step in restoring and maintaining an AE86 is to thoroughly inspect the vehicle and prepare it for work. This includes:
#### Removing the battery and disconnecting the negative terminal
Use a 10mm socket to remove the battery hold-down clamp, then lift the battery out of the tray. Disconnect the negative terminal from the battery post.
#### Jacking up the vehicle and supporting it with jack stands
Use a hydraulic jack to lift the vehicle, then place jack stands under the frame to support it. Ensure the vehicle is level and stable before starting work.
#### Removing the wheels and inspecting the brakes
Use a 12mm socket to remove the lug nuts, then pull the wheels straight off. Inspect the brake pads and rotors for wear, and replace them if necessary.

### Step 2: Oil Change and Fluid Inspection
The next step is to change the oil and inspect the other fluids in the vehicle. This includes:
#### Draining the oil and replacing the oil filter
Use a socket to remove the oil drain plug, then let the oil drain into a pan. Replace the oil filter with a new one, and refill the oil with the recommended type and amount.
#### Inspecting the coolant and brake fluid levels
Check the coolant level in the radiator, and top it off if necessary. Inspect the brake fluid level in the master cylinder, and top it off if necessary.

### Step 3: Timing Belt Replacement
If the timing belt is due for replacement, now is the time to do it. This includes:
#### Removing the timing belt cover and water pump
Use a socket to remove the timing belt cover, then pull the water pump out of the engine block.
#### Installing the new timing belt and water pump
Use a timing belt tool to install the new timing belt, then replace the water pump with a new one.

### Step 4: Suspension Overhaul
If the suspension is due for an overhaul, now is the time to do it. This includes:
#### Removing the suspension components and inspecting them for wear
Use a socket to remove the suspension components, including the control arms and shock absorbers. Inspect them for wear, and replace them if necessary.
#### Installing the new suspension components
Use a socket to install the new suspension components, including the control arms and shock absorbers.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the recommended type and amount of oil and fluids in the vehicle.
* Never attempt to work on a vehicle while it is in motion or with the engine running.
* Use a torque wrench to tighten bolts and nuts to the recommended spec.
* Don't overtighten bolts and nuts, as this can damage the threads or strip the heads.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. What is the recommended oil type and amount for an AE86?
The recommended oil type and amount for an AE86 is 5W-30, 4.5 quarts.
2. How often should the timing belt be replaced?
The timing belt should be replaced every 60,000 miles or 5 years, whichever comes first.
3. What are the symptoms of a worn-out timing belt?
The symptoms of a worn-out timing belt include a rough idle, poor acceleration, and a squealing noise from the engine.
4. Can I replace the timing belt myself, or do I need to take it to a mechanic?
It is possible to replace the timing belt yourself, but it is a complex and time-consuming process that requires specialized tools and expertise. It is recommended to take it to a mechanic if you are not experienced with this type of work.
