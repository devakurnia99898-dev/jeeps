Symptoms or signs to look for in a Kei Truck that's not optimized for drift racing include understeer, poor acceleration, and inadequate suspension travel. These issues can be addressed by upgrading the vehicle's suspension, engine, and braking system.

## Technical Deep Dive
The Kei Truck's engine, typically a small-displacement unit, can be upgraded with high-performance parts such as camshafts, pistons, and cylinder heads. The suspension system, which includes coilovers, control arms, and bushings, can be modified to improve handling and stability. The braking system, which includes brake pads, rotors, and calipers, can be upgraded to provide better stopping power.

Common failure points in a Kei Truck include the transmission, which can be prone to overheating and wear, and the differential, which can be susceptible to damage from excessive torque. The electrical system, which includes the wiring, battery, and alternator, can also be a source of issues if not properly maintained.
