## Technical Deep Dive
The Porsche 928 has a 4.5L or 5.0L V8 engine, depending on the model year. The engine is paired with a 3-speed automatic or 5-speed manual transmission. The car's suspension system consists of a double-wishbone setup in the front and a semi-trailing arm setup in the rear.

Common failure points in the Porsche 928 include:
* The plastic impeller in the water pump
* The rear main seal in the engine
* The transmission mounts
* The brake pads and rotors
* The pop-up headlight motors

The Ruf Porsche 928, on the other hand, is a highly modified version of the 928. It features a tuned engine, upgraded suspension, and unique bodywork. The Ruf 928 has a 5.0L V8 engine that produces over 400 horsepower.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the oil drain plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the car. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug.
#### Step 1.2: Drain the oil
Allow the oil to drain completely before replacing the plug.
#### Step 1.3: Replace the oil filter
Use a new oil filter and hand tighten it in a clockwise direction.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheel
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheel and expose the brake caliper.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the caliper
Use a 13mm socket to remove the caliper bolts.
#### Step 2.3: Remove the old brake pads
Pull out the old brake pads and discard them.
#### Step 2.4: Install the new brake pads
Insert the new brake pads and make sure they are properly seated.

### Step 3: Transmission Mount Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Raise the car
Use a lift or jack to raise the car and expose the transmission mounts.
#### Step 3.2: Remove the old mounts
Use a socket set to remove the old mounts.
#### Step 3.3: Install the new mounts
Insert the new mounts and hand tighten them in a clockwise direction.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type of oil for your Porsche 928
* Never over-tighten the brake caliper bolts
* Make sure the transmission mounts are properly aligned before tightening them
* Don't forget to replace the oil filter when changing the oil
