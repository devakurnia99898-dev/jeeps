## Technical Deep Dive
The Mercedes-AMG GT 4-Door features a complex electric powertrain, consisting of multiple electric motors, a high-performance battery pack, and advanced power electronics. The system works by using the electric motors to propel the vehicle, while the battery pack provides the necessary energy storage. The power electronics control the flow of energy between the battery pack and the electric motors.

Common failure points in high-performance electric vehicles like the Mercedes-AMG GT 4-Door include:
* The electric motors' bearings and windings
* The battery pack's cells and management system
* The power electronics' components and cooling system

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Routine Maintenance
#### Checking the Battery Pack
1. Locate the battery pack and remove the protective cover
2. Inspect the battery cells for signs of damage or deterioration
3. Check the battery management system for any error codes or warnings
4. Perform a battery pack balancing procedure to ensure optimal performance

#### Inspecting the Electric Motors
1. Locate the electric motors and remove the protective covers
2. Inspect the motors' bearings and windings for signs of wear or damage
3. Check the motors' electrical connections for any signs of corrosion or damage
4. Perform a motor performance test to ensure optimal output

#### Checking the Power Electronics
1. Locate the power electronics and remove the protective covers
2. Inspect the components for signs of damage or deterioration
3. Check the cooling system for any signs of leaks or blockages
4. Perform a power electronics performance test to ensure optimal output

### Advanced Maintenance Procedures
#### Replacing the Battery Pack
1. Disconnect the high-voltage system and ensure the vehicle is properly grounded
2. Remove the battery pack and its associated components
3. Install the new battery pack and its associated components
4. Reconnect the high-voltage system and perform a battery pack balancing procedure

#### Upgrading the Electric Motors
1. Disconnect the high-voltage system and ensure the vehicle is properly grounded
2. Remove the electric motors and their associated components
3. Install the new electric motors and their associated components
4. Reconnect the high-voltage system and perform a motor performance test
