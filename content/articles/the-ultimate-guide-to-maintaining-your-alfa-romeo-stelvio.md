- The engine oil system's filter and pump

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
The Stelvio Quadrifoglio's engine oil system is critical to its performance and longevity. To change the oil:
#### Step 1.1: Gather materials
- Oil filter wrench
- New oil filter
- Oil drain pan
- New motor oil (5W-40)
#### Step 1.2: Warm up the engine
Drive the vehicle for 10-15 minutes to warm up the engine.
#### Step 1.3: Drain the oil
Locate the oil drain plug and remove it using a 10mm socket. Allow the oil to drain into the pan.
#### Step 1.4: Replace the oil filter
Use the oil filter wrench to remove the old filter. Install a new filter and hand tighten.
#### Step 1.5: Refill the oil
Pour in the new oil, following the manufacturer's recommended capacity and viscosity.

### Step 2: Tire Rotation
The Stelvio Quadrifoglio's tires are designed for high-performance handling. To rotate the tires:
#### Step 2.1: Gather materials
- Tire pressure gauge
- Wheel chocks
- Jack stands
#### Step 2.2: Loosen the lug nuts
Before raising the vehicle, loosen the lug nuts using a 10mm socket.
#### Step 2.3: Raise the vehicle
Use a lift or jack stands to raise the vehicle, ensuring the tires are off the ground.
#### Step 2.4: Remove the tires
Completely remove the lug nuts and take off the tires.
#### Step 2.5: Rotate the tires
Rotate the tires according to the manufacturer's recommended pattern.

### Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement
The Stelvio Quadrifoglio's brake system is designed for high-performance stopping power. To replace the brake pads:
#### Step 3.1: Gather materials
- Brake pad wear sensor
- New brake pads
- C-clamps
#### Step 3.2: Remove the caliper
Use a 10mm socket to remove the caliper bolts. Slide the caliper off the rotor.
#### Step 3.3: Remove the old brake pads
Use the brake pad wear sensor to determine the pad's wear. Remove the old pads.
#### Step 3.4: Install the new brake pads
Install the new brake pads, ensuring proper alignment and securement.
#### Step 3.5: Reinstall the caliper
Slide the caliper back over the rotor and tighten the caliper bolts.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
- Always refer to the manufacturer's recommended maintenance schedule.
- Use the correct type and amount of motor oil.
- Avoid over-tightening the lug nuts, which can damage the wheels or brake rotors.
- When working on the suspension, ensure the vehicle is properly supported and aligned.
