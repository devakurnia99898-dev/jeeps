## Technical Deep Dive
Jeeps are known for their off-road capabilities, but they also come equipped with advanced safety features. The Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator, for example, feature a robust frame, crumple zones, and a range of airbags to protect occupants in the event of a crash. The vehicle's safety system is designed to work in conjunction with the driver, providing alerts and interventions to prevent accidents.

Common failure points in Jeep safety systems include worn-out brake pads, faulty sensors, and damaged airbags. It's essential to address these issues promptly to ensure your vehicle's safety features are functioning correctly.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 1: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake calipers. Use a 10mm socket to loosen the lug nuts, then raise the vehicle using a jack.

#### Step 2: Remove the Caliper
Remove the caliper bolts using a 10mm socket. Slide the caliper off the rotor, taking care not to damage the brake hose.

#### Step 3: Remove the Old Brake Pads
Remove the old brake pads from the caliper. Take note of the pad wear sensor and disconnect it from the old pad.

#### Step 4: Install the New Brake Pads
Install the new brake pads, ensuring they're properly seated and the wear sensor is connected.

### Sensor Calibration
#### Step 1: Connect the Calibration Tool
Connect the sensor calibration tool to the vehicle's OBD-II port.

#### Step 2: Follow the Calibration Procedure
Follow the calibration procedure outlined in the repair manual. This may involve driving the vehicle at specific speeds or performing a series of maneuvers.

### Airbag Inspection
#### Step 1: Locate the Airbag Module
Locate the airbag module, usually found under the steering column or in the dashboard.

#### Step 2: Inspect the Airbag
Inspect the airbag for signs of damage or wear. Check the wiring and connections for any damage or corrosion.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
Don't neglect regular maintenance, as it can lead to safety-critical systems failing. Common mistakes include:
* Not following proper procedures when working with safety-critical systems
* Not addressing worn-out brake pads or faulty sensors promptly
* Not inspecting airbags regularly

Tricks of the trade:
* Use a torque wrench to ensure proper torque specs when working with brake systems
* Use a brake pad spreader to ensure even brake pad wear
* Use a sensor calibration tool to ensure accurate sensor readings
