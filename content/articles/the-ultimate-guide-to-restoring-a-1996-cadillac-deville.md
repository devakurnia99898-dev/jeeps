# The Diagnostic / The Situation
The 1996 Cadillac DeVille is a classic luxury sedan that requires regular maintenance to keep it running smoothly. As a restoration specialist, I've seen many of these vehicles come into the shop with common issues such as faulty fuel injectors, worn-out brake pads, and leaky oil seals. In this guide, we'll cover the technical background of the DeVille's engine, suspension, and electrical systems, as well as symptoms to look out for.

## Technical Deep Dive
The 1996 Cadillac DeVille comes with a 4.9L V8 engine, which is known for its reliability and performance. However, common failure points include the plastic intake manifold, which can crack and cause coolant leaks, and the fuel injectors, which can become clogged and affect engine performance. The suspension system features a coil spring design, which can wear out over time and cause uneven tire wear. The electrical system is also prone to issues, particularly with the alternator and starter motor.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the oil drain plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the car. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and let the oil drain into a pan.
#### Step 1.2: Replace the oil filter
Use a filter wrench to remove the old oil filter and discard it. Install a new oil filter and hand tighten it.
#### Step 1.3: Refill the oil
Use a funnel to pour in the new oil. Check the owner's manual for the recommended oil type and capacity.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheel
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheel and expose the brake caliper.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the caliper
Use a 10mm socket to remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.
#### Step 2.3: Remove the old brake pads
Use pliers to remove the old brake pads and discard them.
#### Step 2.4: Install the new brake pads
Install the new brake pads and make sure they are properly seated.

### Step 3: Suspension Overhaul
#### Step 3.1: Remove the coil spring
Use a coil spring compressor to remove the coil spring and expose the shock absorber.
#### Step 3.2: Remove the shock absorber
Use a 10mm socket to remove the shock absorber bolts and slide the shock absorber out.
#### Step 3.3: Inspect the suspension components
Inspect the suspension components for wear and damage. Replace any worn-out parts.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type of oil for your vehicle.
* Never mix old and new brake fluid.
* Make sure to tighten all bolts and nuts in a star pattern to avoid warping the brake rotor.
