## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Prepare the Vehicle for Maintenance
#### Step 1.1: Gather necessary tools and equipment
Ensure you have all the required tools and equipment before starting the maintenance procedure.
#### Step 1.2: Consult the owner's manual
Familiarize yourself with the vehicle's maintenance schedule and procedures.

### Step 2: Inspect and Maintain the Electric Powertrain
#### Step 2.1: Check the electric motor's bearings and seals
Use a multimeter to check the motor's electrical resistance and inspect the bearings and seals for any signs of wear or damage.
#### Step 2.2: Inspect the hybrid system's control unit and sensors
Use a scan tool to check the system's fault codes and inspect the control unit and sensors for any signs of damage or malfunction.

### Step 3: Maintain the Lithium-Ion Battery
#### Step 3.1: Check the battery's state of charge
Use a battery tester to check the battery's state of charge and ensure it's within the recommended range.
#### Step 3.2: Balance the battery cells
Use a battery balancer to ensure the cells are balanced and functioning properly.

### Step 4: Inspect and Maintain the Off-Road Capabilities
#### Step 4.1: Check the suspension and steering systems
Inspect the suspension and steering systems for any signs of wear or damage.
#### Step 4.2: Check the four-wheel-drive system
Inspect the four-wheel-drive system for any signs of wear or damage and ensure it's functioning properly.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes

## Frequently Asked Questions

It's possible to perform some maintenance procedures yourself, but it's recommended to take it to a mechanic if you're not experienced with electric vehicles and hybrid systems.
