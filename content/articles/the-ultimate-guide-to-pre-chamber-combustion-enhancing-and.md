The pre-chamber combustion system uses a small, secondary combustion chamber to ignite the fuel, which leads to a more efficient and complete burn. This results in reduced emissions, improved fuel efficiency, and enhanced engine performance.

## Technical Deep Dive
The pre-chamber combustion system works by using a small, secondary combustion chamber that is fueled by a separate injector. This chamber is designed to ignite the fuel at a much higher temperature and pressure than traditional combustion chambers, resulting in a more efficient and complete burn. The system consists of several key components, including the pre-chamber, fuel injector, and control module. Common failure points in this system include the fuel injector, which can become clogged or faulty, and the control module, which can malfunction or become corrupted.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Introduction to Pre-Chamber Combustion Maintenance
Pre-chamber combustion maintenance is crucial to ensure the system operates efficiently and effectively. This guide will walk you through the steps to maintain and troubleshoot the pre-chamber combustion system.

#### Step 1: Visual Inspection
Inspect the pre-chamber combustion system for any signs of damage or wear. Check the fuel injector for any blockages or corrosion.

#### Step 2: Fuel Injector Cleaning
Use a fuel injector cleaning kit to clean the fuel injector. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for the cleaning process.

#### Step 3: Control Module Troubleshooting
Use a scan tool to troubleshoot the control module. Check for any error codes or malfunctioning sensors.

#### Step 4: Pre-Chamber Inspection
Inspect the pre-chamber for any signs of damage or wear. Check the spark plug for any fouling or wear.

#### Step 5: Spark Plug Replacement
Replace the spark plug with a new one. Ensure the spark plug is properly gapped and installed.
