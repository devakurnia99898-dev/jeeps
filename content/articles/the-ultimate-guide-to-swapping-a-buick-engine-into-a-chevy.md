## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The Chevy Chevette is a subcompact car that was produced from 1976 to 1987. While it was known for its reliability and fuel efficiency, its stock engine left much to be desired in terms of power and performance. One popular modification among Chevette enthusiasts is swapping the stock engine with a more powerful Buick engine. This guide will walk you through the process of swapping a Buick engine into a Chevy Chevette.

## Technical Deep Dive
The Buick engine is a popular choice for Chevette engine swaps due to its compact size, lightweight design, and impressive power output. The most common Buick engines used for Chevette swaps are the 3.8L V6 and the 4.3L V6.

The 3.8L V6 engine produces 110 horsepower and 130 lb-ft of torque, while the 4.3L V6 engine produces 160 horsepower and 225 lb-ft of torque. Both engines are known for their reliability and durability, making them an excellent choice for a Chevette engine swap.

Common failure points to look out for when performing an engine swap include the engine mounts, transmission adapter, and wiring harness. It's essential to ensure that all components are properly installed and secured to avoid any issues down the road.
