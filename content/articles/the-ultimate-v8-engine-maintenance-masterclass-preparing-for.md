## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Gather materials
Collect the necessary materials, including oil filter wrench, new oil filter, and enough motor oil to fill the engine.
#### Step 1.2: Warm up the engine
Drive the car for a few minutes to warm up the engine.
#### Step 1.3: Drain the oil
Use a socket wrench to remove the oil drain plug and let the oil flow into a pan.
#### Step 1.4: Replace the oil filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old oil filter and install a new one.

### Step 2: Spark Plug Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Locate the spark plugs
Find the spark plug wires and follow them to the spark plugs.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the spark plug wires
Use a spark plug wire puller to remove the wires from the spark plugs.
#### Step 2.3: Remove the spark plugs
Use a spark plug socket to remove the spark plugs from the engine.
#### Step 2.4: Install new spark plugs
Insert the new spark plugs into the engine and tighten them to the specified torque (15-20 ft-lbs).

### Step 3: Belt Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Locate the serpentine belt
Find the serpentine belt and its tensioner.
#### Step 3.2: Release the tensioner
Use a wrench to release the tensioner and remove the belt.
#### Step 3.3: Install the new belt
Place the new belt on the engine and tighten the tensioner to the specified torque (30-40 ft-lbs).

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type of oil for your engine
* Never over-tighten the spark plugs or belt tensioner
* Use a torque wrench to ensure accurate torque specs

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What type of oil should I use in my V8 engine?**
The recommended oil type will depend on the specific engine and manufacturer. Consult your owner's manual for the correct oil type and viscosity.
2. **How often should I change my spark plugs?**
Spark plugs should be replaced every 30,000-100,000 miles, depending on the engine and driving conditions.
3. **What are the symptoms of a faulty serpentine belt?**
A faulty serpentine belt can cause the engine to overheat, the alternator to malfunction, and the power steering to fail.
4. **Can I use a regular socket to remove the spark plugs?**
No, a spark plug socket is required to remove the spark plugs without damaging the engine.
5. **How do I know if my V8 engine needs a tune-up?**
If your engine is experiencing decreased performance, rough idling, or poor fuel efficiency, it may be due for a tune-up.
