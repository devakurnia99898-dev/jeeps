## Step-by-Step Guide
In this section, we'll provide a step-by-step guide to maintaining and repairing your Jeep's license plate and lighting system.

### Inspect the License Plate Holder
#### Remove the License Plate
To inspect the license plate holder, you'll need to remove the license plate. Use a 10mm socket to loosen the screws that hold the license plate in place.

#### Inspect the Holder
Once the license plate is removed, inspect the holder for any signs of damage or corrosion. Check for any loose or missing screws, and make sure the holder is securely attached to the vehicle.

#### Replace the Holder (if necessary)
If the holder is damaged or corroded, you'll need to replace it. Use a new holder and follow the manufacturer's instructions for installation.

### Replace the Brake Lights
#### Purchase New Brake Lights
To replace the brake lights, you'll need to purchase new ones. Make sure to buy the correct type and wattage for your vehicle.

#### Remove the Old Brake Lights
Use a 10mm socket to loosen the screws that hold the old brake lights in place. Carefully pull the lights out of the socket and disconnect the wiring.

#### Install the New Brake Lights
Insert the new brake lights into the socket and connect the wiring. Make sure the lights are securely fastened with the screws.
