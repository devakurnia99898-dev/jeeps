## **Technical Deep Dive**
Bullet resistant tires are designed with multiple layers of protection, including a thick layer of rubber, a layer of Kevlar or other ballistic material, and a reinforced sidewall. The tire's tread pattern and compound are also designed to provide maximum traction and stability. Common failure points of bullet resistant tires include the tread, sidewall, and bead. The tread can become worn down over time, reducing the tire's traction and making it more vulnerable to ballistic threats. The sidewall can become damaged if the tire is subjected to extreme temperatures or physical stress. The bead can become loose or damaged if the tire is not properly mounted or maintained.

## **Step-by-Step Guide**
### **Step 1: Inspect the Tires**
Inspect the tires for any signs of damage or wear, including uneven wear, cracks, or punctures. Check the tire pressure and make sure it is at the recommended level.

Use a tire tread depth gauge to check the tread depth. If the tread is worn down to 1/16 of an inch or less, the tire should be replaced.

#### **Step 1.2: Check the Sidewall**
Inspect the sidewall for any signs of damage or wear, including cracks or bulges. Check the sidewall for any signs of uneven wear or damage.
