## Technical Deep Dive
The Mercedes-Maybach GLS680 features a powerful 6.0L V12 biturbo engine, producing 621 horsepower and 738 lb-ft of torque. This engine is paired with a 9-speed automatic transmission and a four-wheel-drive system, providing exceptional traction and control. The suspension system consists of a double-wishbone front axle and a multi-link rear axle, offering a smooth ride and responsive handling.

Common failure points in the GLS680 include the engine's turbochargers, which can be prone to overheating or damage due to excessive boost pressure. The transmission's torque converter can also be a weak point, as it may experience premature wear or failure due to the high torque output of the engine. Additionally, the vehicle's advanced electrical systems, including the infotainment and driver assistance systems, can be susceptible to software glitches or hardware faults.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparing the Garage
The first step in preparing your garage for the 2027 Mercedes-Maybach GLS680 is to ensure a clean and organized workspace. Clear any clutter or debris from the garage floor, and install a lift or jack stands to facilitate easy access to the vehicle's underside.

#### Installing a Lift
To install a lift, follow these steps:
1. Choose a suitable location for the lift, taking into account the vehicle's weight and size.
2. Assemble the lift according to the manufacturer's instructions.
3. Test the lift to ensure it's functioning correctly and safely.

#### Organizing Tools and Equipment
Next, organize your tools and equipment in a logical and accessible manner. This includes storing sockets, wrenches, and other hand tools in a designated toolbox or on a pegboard.

### Inspecting the Vehicle
Before performing any maintenance or repairs, it's essential to inspect the vehicle thoroughly. Check the engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems for any signs of wear or damage.

#### Checking the Engine Oil
To check the engine oil, follow these steps:
1. Locate the oil dipstick under the hood.
2. Pull out the dipstick and wipe it clean with a rag.
3. Insert the dipstick back into the oil reservoir and pull it out again.
4. Check the oil level and color, looking for any signs of contamination or low levels.

#### Inspecting the Brake Pads
To inspect the brake pads, follow these steps:
1. Locate the brake calipers on each wheel.
2. Remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.
3. Inspect the brake pads for wear or damage.
4. Replace the brake pads if necessary, following the manufacturer's instructions.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When working on the Mercedes-Maybach GLS680, it's essential to avoid common mistakes that can lead to costly repairs or damage to the vehicle. One common mistake is over-tightening bolts or fasteners, which can strip threads or damage components.
