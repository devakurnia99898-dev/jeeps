## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Disassembly and Inspection
The first step in restoring a classic Porsche is to disassemble the vehicle and inspect each component. This includes removing the engine, transmission, and suspension, as well as the electrical system and interior components.
#### Step 1.1: Remove the Engine
To remove the engine, you'll need to disconnect the battery, remove the air filter, and disconnect the fuel lines. Use a hoist to lift the engine out of the vehicle.
#### Step 1.2: Inspect the Engine
Once the engine is removed, inspect it for any signs of damage or wear. Check the cylinder head, pistons, and crankshaft for any damage.

### Step 2: Engine Rebuild
If the engine requires a rebuild, now is the time to do it. This includes replacing the piston rings, bearings, and seals.
#### Step 2.1: Replace the Piston Rings
To replace the piston rings, you'll need to remove the piston from the cylinder block. Use a ring compressor to remove the old rings and install the new ones.
#### Step 2.2: Replace the Bearings
To replace the bearings, you'll need to remove the crankshaft from the engine block. Use a bearing puller to remove the old bearings and install the new ones.

### Step 3: Suspension and Steering
The suspension and steering systems are critical components of the Porsche 911. Inspect the shocks, struts, and springs for any signs of wear or damage.
#### Step 3.1: Replace the Shocks
To replace the shocks, you'll need to remove the wheel and brake caliper. Use a shock absorber tool to remove the old shock and install the new one.
#### Step 3.2: Replace the Springs
To replace the springs, you'll need to remove the coil spring and shock absorber. Use a spring compressor to remove the old spring and install the new one.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When restoring a classic Porsche, it's essential to avoid common mistakes, such as:

* Not using the correct materials when repairing rust damage
