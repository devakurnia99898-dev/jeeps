## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Gather materials
Collect the necessary tools and materials, including oil filter wrench, new oil filter, and enough motor oil to fill the engine.

#### Step 1.2: Warm up the engine
Drive the vehicle for a few minutes to warm up the engine, making it easier to drain the oil.

#### Step 1.3: Locate the oil drain plug
Find the oil drain plug, usually located at the bottom of the engine, and position a drip pan to catch the oil.

#### Step 1.4: Drain the oil
Use a socket wrench to remove the oil drain plug and allow the oil to drain completely.

### Step 2: Tire Rotation
#### Step 2.1: Check the owner's manual
Consult the owner's manual to determine the recommended tire rotation pattern for your specific vehicle.

#### Step 2.2: Loosen the lug nuts
Before raising the vehicle, loosen the lug nuts on the first wheel using a lug wrench.

#### Step 2.3: Raise the vehicle
Use a car lift or jack to raise the vehicle, making sure it's stable and secure.

#### Step 2.4: Remove the wheel
Completely remove the lug nuts and take off the wheel, setting it aside to be replaced with the next wheel in the rotation pattern.

### Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Remove the caliper
Use a wrench to loosen the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.

#### Step 3.2: Remove the old brake pad
Pull out the old brake pad, taking note of the wear sensor wire and any other components that need to be transferred to the new pad.

#### Step 3.3: Install the new brake pad
Insert the new brake pad, making sure it's properly seated and the wear sensor wire is connected.

#### Step 3.4: Reinstall the caliper
Slide the caliper back over the rotor and tighten the caliper bolts to the specified torque.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always refer to the owner's manual for specific instructions and guidelines
* Use the correct type and amount of motor oil for your vehicle
* Avoid over-tightening bolts and lug nuts, as this can cause damage to the threads or strip the head

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. Q: How often should I change my oil?
A: It's recommended to change your oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on your vehicle's make and model.
2. Q: Can I rotate my tires myself?
A: Yes, rotating your tires is a relatively simple process that can be done at home with the right tools and knowledge.
3. Q: What are the signs of worn-out brake pads?
A: Signs of worn-out brake pads include squealing or grinding noises, vibration when applying the brakes, and reduced stopping power.
4. Q: How do I know if my vehicle needs a tune-up?
A: Signs that your vehicle needs a tune-up include decreased fuel efficiency, rough idling, and difficulty starting the engine.
5. Q: Can I use any type of motor oil in my vehicle?
A: No, it's essential to use the type and viscosity of motor oil recommended by your vehicle's manufacturer to ensure optimal performance and longevity.
