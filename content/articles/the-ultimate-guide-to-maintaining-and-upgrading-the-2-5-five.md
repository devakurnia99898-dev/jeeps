## Technical Deep Dive
The 2.5-liter five-cylinder engine is a turbocharged, inline-five cylinder powerplant that produces 394 horsepower and 354 lb-ft of torque. It features a compact design, with a cylinder block and head made from high-strength aluminum alloy. The engine also features a number of advanced technologies, including direct fuel injection, variable valve timing, and a twin-scroll turbocharger. Common failure points in this engine include the turbocharger, timing chain, and engine bearings. The plastic impeller in the water pump is also a known weak point, and should be replaced with a metal impeller for added durability.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Oil Change
#### Step 1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the car. It's a 10mm plug, and can be removed using a socket wrench.
#### Step 2: Drain the Oil
Once the plug is removed, the oil will start to drain out. Make sure to have a pan or container in place to catch the oil.
#### Step 3: Replace the Oil Filter
The oil filter is located on top of the engine, and can be removed using a filter wrench.
#### Step 4: Refill the Oil
Use a high-quality synthetic oil that meets the manufacturer's specifications. Fill the engine with the recommended amount of oil, and replace the oil drain plug.
### Spark Plug Replacement
#### Step 1: Locate the Spark Plugs
The spark plugs are located on the side of the engine, and can be accessed by removing the spark plug wire boots.
#### Step 2: Remove the Spark Plug Wire Boots
Use a spark plug wire boot remover to pull the boots off the spark plugs.
#### Step 3: Remove the Spark Plugs
Use a spark plug socket to remove the spark plugs from the engine.
#### Step 4: Install the New Spark Plugs
Make sure to gap the new spark plugs to the recommended specification, and install them in the engine.
### Timing Chain Replacement
#### Step 1: Remove the Timing Chain Cover
The timing chain cover is located on the side of the engine, and can be removed using a 10mm socket.
#### Step 2: Remove the Timing Chain Tensioner
The timing chain tensioner is located on the side of the engine, and can be removed using a 12mm socket.
#### Step 3: Remove the Timing Chain
The timing chain is located on the side of the engine, and can be removed using a timing chain tool.
#### Step 4: Install the New Timing Chain
Make sure to align the new timing chain with the engine's timing marks, and install it in the engine.
### Turbocharger Replacement
#### Step 1: Remove the Turbocharger Heat Shield
The turbocharger heat shield is located on the side of the engine, and can be removed using a 10mm socket.
#### Step 2: Remove the Turbocharger Mounting Bracket
The turbocharger mounting bracket is located on the side of the engine, and can be removed using a 12mm socket.
#### Step 3: Remove the Turbocharger
The turbocharger is located on the side of the engine, and can be removed using a turbocharger removal tool.
#### Step 4: Install the New Turbocharger
Make sure to align the new turbocharger with the engine's mounting bracket, and install it in the engine.
