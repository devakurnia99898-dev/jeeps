## Technical Deep Dive
Hybrid vehicles like the Toyota Prius and Camry Hybrid rely on a combination of electric motors and internal combustion engines to achieve optimal fuel efficiency. The electric motor is powered by a battery pack, which is charged by the engine and regenerative braking. The engine, on the other hand, is optimized for efficiency, with features like Atkinson cycle combustion and variable valve timing. Common failure points in hybrid vehicles include the battery pack, electric motor, and engine control module. Regular maintenance is crucial to prevent these issues and ensure the vehicle operates within optimal parameters.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Regular Oil Changes
#### Sub-step 1: Gather materials
Collect the necessary materials, including oil filter, oil drain pan, and new oil.
#### Sub-step 2: Warm up the engine
Drive the vehicle for a few minutes to warm up the engine.
#### Sub-step 3: Drain the oil
Use a socket wrench to remove the oil drain plug and drain the oil into a pan.
#### Sub-step 4: Replace the oil filter
Remove the old oil filter and install a new one.
#### Sub-step 5: Refill the oil
Pour in the new oil and replace the oil cap.

### Step 2: Tire Rotation and Pressure Check
#### Sub-step 1: Check the tire pressure
Use a tire pressure gauge to check the pressure of each tire.
#### Sub-step 2: Rotate the tires
Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts and rotate the tires according to the manufacturer's recommended pattern.
#### Sub-step 3: Tighten the lug nuts
Tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern to ensure even tightening.

### Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Sub-step 1: Remove the wheel
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheel and access the brake caliper.
#### Sub-step 2: Remove the caliper
Use a socket wrench to remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.
#### Sub-step 3: Remove the old brake pads
Pull out the old brake pads and discard them.
#### Sub-step 4: Install the new brake pads
Insert the new brake pads and secure them with the retaining clips.
#### Sub-step 5: Reinstall the caliper
Slide the caliper back over the rotor and tighten the caliper bolts.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't over-tighten the lug nuts, as this can damage the wheel bearings.
* Use the correct type of oil for your vehicle, as specified in the owner's manual.
* Avoid mixing different types of brake pads, as this can affect the braking performance.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
The cost of maintaining a hybrid vehicle can vary depending on the task and the individual's level of expertise. DIY maintenance can save owners money, but it's essential to consider the cost of tools and materials. A mechanic may charge more for labor, but they can provide expert advice and ensure the job is done correctly.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. Q: How often should I rotate my tires?
A: Tire rotation should be done every 5,000 to 8,000 miles, depending on the manufacturer's recommendations.
2. Q: What type of oil should I use in my hybrid vehicle?
A: Use the type of oil specified in the owner's manual, which is usually a synthetic blend.
3. Q: Can I replace my brake pads myself?
A: Yes, but it's recommended to have a professional mechanic do the job, especially if you're not familiar with brake systems.
4. Q: How can I improve my fuel efficiency?
A: Regular maintenance, proper tire pressure, and driving habits can all contribute to improved fuel efficiency.
5. Q: What are the common signs of a faulty battery pack?
A: Signs of a faulty battery pack include reduced fuel efficiency, decreased performance, and warning lights on the dashboard.
