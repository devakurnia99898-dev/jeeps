## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The Porsche 924 S is a unique model that combines the body of the 924 with the engine of the 944, making it a stealthy performer in the world of classic cars. When considering purchasing a used 1987 Porsche 924 S, it's essential to understand the technical background of the vehicle. The 924 S features a 2.5L inline-four engine, producing 150 horsepower, and a five-speed manual transmission. Symptoms to look for when inspecting a used 924 S include worn-out timing belt, faulty oxygen sensor, and leaky coolant system.

## Technical Deep Dive
The Porsche 924 S engine is based on the 944's powerplant, with a few key differences. The engine features a single overhead camshaft, hydraulic valve lifters, and a Bosch Motronic fuel injection system. Common failure points in the 924 S engine include the timing belt, water pump, and cylinder head gasket. The suspension system consists of MacPherson struts in the front and a semi-trailing arm setup in the rear. The electrical system is relatively simple, with a few electronic control units (ECUs) managing the fuel injection, ignition, and other systems.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspecting the Vehicle
#### Exterior Inspection
* Check for any signs of rust or corrosion on the body and chassis
* Inspect the tires for wear and damage
* Check the condition of the brakes, including the pads, rotors, and calipers
#### Interior Inspection
* Check the condition of the seats, carpets, and dashboard
* Inspect the electrical systems, including the lights, radio, and gauges
* Check the condition of the suspension and steering components

### Replacing the Timing Belt
#### Removing the Old Belt
* Disconnect the battery and remove the serpentine belt
* Remove the timing belt cover and mark the position of the belt
* Use a wrench to loosen the tensioner and remove the old belt
#### Installing the New Belt
* Install the new belt, ensuring it's properly seated and aligned
* Tighten the tensioner to the specified torque (30 Nm)
* Replace the timing belt cover and serpentine belt

### Overhauling the Suspension
#### Disassembling the Front Suspension
* Remove the wheel and caliper
* Remove the strut and spring
* Inspect and replace the bushings and bearings as needed
#### Reassembling the Front Suspension
* Install the new bushings and bearings
* Reinstall the strut and spring
* Tighten the bolts to the specified torque (40 Nm)

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't neglect to replace the water pump when replacing the timing belt, as it's a common failure point
* Use a torque wrench to ensure proper tightening of bolts and fasteners
* Don't over-tighten the suspension components, as it can lead to premature wear and damage
