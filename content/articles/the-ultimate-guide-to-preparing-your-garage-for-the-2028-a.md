## Technical Deep Dive
The V6 engine in the 2028 Honda Ridgeline is expected to be a significant improvement over its predecessor. The engine will feature a range of advanced technologies, including direct fuel injection, variable valve timing, and a high-efficiency combustion system. These technologies will work together to provide improved performance, reduced emissions, and enhanced fuel efficiency.

Common failure points in the V6 engine can include the timing chain, water pump, and engine bearings. The timing chain can stretch or break over time, causing the engine to misfire or stall. The water pump can fail due to corrosion or wear, leading to overheating and engine damage. Engine bearings can wear out over time, causing the engine to vibrate or produce excessive noise.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Prepare the Garage
#### Clear the Workspace
Clear the garage of any clutter or obstacles to create a safe and efficient workspace. This will give you room to move around the vehicle and access the engine and other components.

#### Gather Tools and Materials
Gather all the necessary tools and materials, including the ones listed in the pre-work checklist. Make sure you have a torque wrench, oil filter wrench, and spark plug gap gauge.

### Inspect the Vehicle
#### Check the Oil and Fluids
Check the oil and fluids to ensure they are at the recommended levels. This includes the engine oil, transmission fluid, brake fluid, and coolant.

#### Inspect the Tires
Inspect the tires to ensure they are properly inflated and have sufficient tread depth. Check for any signs of wear or damage, such as uneven wear or cracks in the sidewall.

### Perform Routine Maintenance
#### Change the Oil
Change the oil to ensure the engine is properly lubricated. Use a high-quality oil filter and follow the manufacturer's instructions for the oil change interval.

#### Rotate the Tires
Rotate the tires to ensure even wear and extend their lifespan. Follow the manufacturer's recommended tire rotation pattern.

#### Replace the Spark Plugs
Replace the spark plugs to ensure the engine is running efficiently. Use a spark plug gap gauge to ensure the spark plugs are gapped correctly.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't over-tighten the oil filter, as this can damage the threads.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure the spark plugs are tightened to the correct specification.
* Don't neglect to check the tire pressure, as under-inflated tires can lead to reduced fuel efficiency and increased wear.
