## **Step-by-Step Guide**
### **Step 1: Inspect and Prepare the Garage**
#### **Step 1.1: Clear the Workspace**
Clear the garage of any obstructions, including tools, equipment, and storage containers. Ensure the floor is clean and dry to prevent any accidents.

#### **Step 1.2: Install a Lift**
Install a hydraulic lift or a set of jack stands to support the vehicle during maintenance and repairs. Ensure the lift is rated for the vehicle's weight and is properly secured.

### **Step 2: Familiarize Yourself with the Vehicle's Systems**
#### **Step 2.1: Study the Owner's Manual**
Thoroughly read the owner's manual to understand the vehicle's systems, including the engine, transmission, and suspension.

#### **Step 2.2: Consult Online Resources**
Consult online forums, repair manuals, and technical guides to gain a deeper understanding of the vehicle's technical aspects.

### **Step 3: Prepare the Vehicle for Maintenance**
#### **Step 3.1: Check the Oil and Fluids**
Check the engine oil, transmission fluid, and brake fluid levels. Ensure they are at the recommended levels and are free from contamination.

#### **Step 3.2: Inspect the Tires**
Inspect the tires for wear and damage. Ensure they are properly inflated and aligned.

### **Step 4: Perform Routine Maintenance**
#### **Step 4.1: Change the Oil**
Change the engine oil and filter according to the manufacturer's schedule.

#### **Step 4.2: Replace the Air Filter**
Replace the air filter with a high-performance filter to improve engine performance.

## **Pro Tips & Common Mistakes**
* Always follow the manufacturer's recommended maintenance schedule to prevent premature wear and tear.
* Use high-quality tools and equipment to avoid damaging the vehicle's components.
* Avoid over-revving the engine, as it can cause damage to the valve train and other critical components.
