## Technical Deep Dive
The system of lights on F1 cars is designed to provide real-time information to the drivers, allowing them to make informed decisions during the race. The lights are connected to the car's onboard computer and sensors, which monitor various parameters such as speed, gear, and engine performance.

The common failure points in the lighting system include:
* The LED lights themselves, which can be prone to damage or malfunction
* The wiring and connections, which can be susceptible to wear and tear
* The onboard computer and sensors, which can experience software or hardware issues

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Understanding the Front Lights
The two lights at the front of the F1 car indicate the car's status and any potential issues. The lights can display different colors and patterns, which signify various messages to the drivers.
#### Identifying the Light Colors
* Green: The car is in a normal operating condition
* Yellow: The car is experiencing a warning or caution, such as low fuel or tire pressure
* Red: The car is experiencing a critical issue, such as engine failure or brake malfunction

### Understanding the Rear Lights
The three lights at the back of the F1 car provide information about the car's performance and any warnings. The lights can display different colors and patterns, which signify various messages to the drivers.
#### Identifying the Light Patterns
* Steady light: The car is in a normal operating condition
* Flashing light: The car is experiencing a warning or caution, such as low fuel or tire pressure
* Alternating lights: The car is experiencing a critical issue, such as engine failure or brake malfunction

### Troubleshooting the Lighting System
To troubleshoot the lighting system, follow these steps:
#### Step 1: Check the Electrical Connections
Use a multimeter to test the electrical connections and ensure they are secure and functioning properly.
#### Step 2: Check the Wiring and Connections
Use a wiring diagram to identify the connections and circuits, and check for any damage or wear and tear.
#### Step 3: Check the Onboard Computer and Sensors
Use a computer with the necessary software to access the onboard computer and sensors, and check for any software or hardware issues.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
