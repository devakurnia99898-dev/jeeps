## Technical Deep Dive
Electric vehicles rely on a complex system of components, including the electric motor, battery, and power electronics. The electric motor is responsible for propelling the vehicle, while the battery provides the necessary energy. The power electronics system controls the flow of energy between the battery and motor. Common failure points in electric vehicles include the battery management system, electric motor bearings, and power electronics components. Understanding how these components work and interact is crucial for proper maintenance and troubleshooting.

### Safety Warnings
* Always wear protective gloves and safety glasses when working with electrical components.
* Ensure the vehicle is in park and apply the parking brake before starting any maintenance work.
* Never touch electrical components with bare hands or metal objects.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Tire Rotation and Inspection
#### Step 1.1: Jack Up the Vehicle
Raise the vehicle using a hydraulic jack, ensuring it's stable and secure.
#### Step 1.2: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels and inspect the tires for any signs of wear or damage.
#### Step 1.3: Rotate the Tires
Rotate the tires according to the manufacturer's recommended pattern.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the Caliper
Remove the caliper and inspect the brake pads for wear.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the Old Brake Pads
Remove the old brake pads and clean the brake rotor.
#### Step 2.3: Install the New Brake Pads
Install the new brake pads and reassemble the caliper.

### Step 3: Battery Maintenance
#### Step 3.1: Check the Battery State of Charge
Check the battery state of charge using a multimeter.
#### Step 3.2: Balance the Battery Cells
Balance the battery cells to ensure proper charging and discharging.
#### Step 3.3: Update the Battery Management System
Update the battery management system to ensure optimal performance.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't overcharge the battery, as this can cause damage to the cells.
* Always follow the manufacturer's recommended maintenance schedule.
* Use the correct type of brake pads for your vehicle, as incorrect pads can cause vibration and noise.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
The cost of maintaining an electric vehicle can vary depending on the task and the owner's level of expertise. DIY maintenance can save owners money, but it's essential to consider the cost of tools and equipment. In contrast, taking the vehicle to a mechanic can provide peace of mind, but it may come at a higher cost.
