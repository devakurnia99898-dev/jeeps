## Technical Deep Dive
The Ford GT40 is a high-performance sports car that was designed in the 1960s. It has a sleek and aerodynamic body, with a long hood and a short rear deck. The GT40 has a distinctive shape, with a curved roofline and a flat nose. To draw a GT40, you will need to capture these features and proportions accurately. The key to drawing a GT40 is to break it down into simple shapes and lines, and then build up the details gradually.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Sketch the Basic Shape
#### Step 1.1: Draw a rectangle for the body
Start by drawing a rectangle for the body of the GT40. The rectangle should be longer than it is wide, with a ratio of about 2:1.
#### Step 1.2: Add the wheels and axles
Add the wheels and axles to the rectangle, making sure they are evenly spaced and proportional to the body.
#### Step 1.3: Sketch the roofline and nose
Sketch the curved roofline and flat nose of the GT40, using a French curve or a pencil to get the shape right.

### Step 2: Add the Details
#### Step 2.1: Draw the doors and windows
Add the doors and windows to the body, making sure they are proportional and evenly spaced.
#### Step 2.2: Sketch the headlights and grille
Sketch the headlights and grille, using a pencil to get the shape and details right.
#### Step 2.3: Add the rear deck and spoiler
Add the rear deck and spoiler to the body, making sure they are proportional and evenly spaced.

### Step 3: Refine the Drawing
#### Step 3.1: Use markers or colored pencils to add color and shading
Use markers or colored pencils to add color and shading to the drawing, making sure to capture the nuances of the GT40's shape and design.
#### Step 3.2: Add final details and textures
Add final details and textures to the drawing, such as the pattern on the seats or the texture of the tires.
