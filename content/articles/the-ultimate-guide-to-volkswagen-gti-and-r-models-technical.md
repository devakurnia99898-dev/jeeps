The GTI and R models are equipped with advanced engine and suspension systems that are designed to provide high-performance capabilities. The suspension system is a complex system that includes multiple components, including coil springs, shock absorbers, and stabilizer bars.

One of the most critical components of the suspension system is the coil spring, which provides the primary suspension force for the vehicle. The coil spring is designed to compress and expand as the vehicle moves, providing a smooth ride and stable handling. However, the coil spring can become worn or damaged over time, leading to decreased performance and potentially more serious problems.

Common failure points on the GTI and R models include the engine oil pump, which can fail if the oil level is not maintained at the correct level. ## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the vehicle. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.

#### Step 1.2: Replace the Oil Filter
The oil filter is located on the top of the engine, near the oil filler cap. Use an oil filter wrench to remove the filter and replace it with a new one.

#### Step 1.3: Refill the Oil
Refill the oil with the correct type and amount of oil for your vehicle. Use a funnel to pour the oil into the filler cap and replace the cap when finished.

### Step 2: Suspension Inspection
#### Step 2.1: Locate the Suspension Components
The suspension components are located near the wheels, including the coil springs, shock absorbers, and stabilizer bars. Use a suspension inspection tool to inspect each component for wear or damage.

#### Step 2.2: Inspect the Coil Springs
The coil springs are the primary suspension force for the vehicle. Inspect each coil spring for wear or damage, including rust or corrosion.

#### Step 2.3: Inspect the Shock Absorbers
The shock absorbers are designed to absorb bumps and vibrations. Inspect each shock absorber for wear or damage, including leaks or corrosion.

### Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Locate the Brake Pads
The brake pads are located near the wheels, inside the brake calipers. Use a 12mm socket to remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.

#### Step 3.2: Remove the Old Brake Pads
The old brake pads are located inside the caliper. Use a screwdriver to remove the old pads and discard them.

#### Step 3.3: Install the New Brake Pads
The new brake pads are installed in the same location as the old pads. Use a screwdriver to secure the new pads in place.
