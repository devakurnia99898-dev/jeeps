## Technical Deep Dive
Electric vehicles, including Jeeps, rely on a complex system of electrical components, including batteries, electric motors, and power electronics. The battery is the heart of the electric vehicle, providing the energy needed to power the motors. The electric motors, on the other hand, convert the electrical energy into mechanical energy, propelling the vehicle forward.

Common failure points in electric vehicles include the battery management system, the electric motor, and the power electronics. The battery management system is responsible for monitoring and controlling the battery's state of charge, voltage, and temperature. Failure of this system can lead to reduced battery life, decreased performance, and even safety issues.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is the difference between a hybrid and an electric vehicle?**
A hybrid vehicle uses a combination of electric and gasoline power, while an electric vehicle uses only electric power.

2. **How do I charge my electric vehicle?**
Electric vehicles can be charged using a variety of methods, including Level 1 (120V), Level 2 (240V), and DC Fast Charging.

3. **What is the range of my electric vehicle?**
The range of an electric vehicle depends on a variety of factors, including the vehicle's battery size, efficiency, and driving habits.

4. **How do I diagnose issues with my electric vehicle's electrical system?**
Diagnosing issues with an electric vehicle's electrical system requires a deep understanding of electrical theory and practice, as well as the necessary tools and training.
