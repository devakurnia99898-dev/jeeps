When working with electric vehicles, it's crucial to take necessary safety precautions to avoid electrical shock or injury. Always wear protective gear, including gloves, safety glasses, and a face mask. Ensure the vehicle is properly grounded and disconnected from the power source before starting work.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Prepare Your Garage
#### Step 1.1: Clear the workspace
Clear a dedicated area in your garage for the Mercedes-AMG GT 4-Door, ensuring a safe distance from any flammable materials or ignition sources.
#### Step 1.2: Install a high-voltage charging station
If you plan to charge the vehicle at home, install a high-voltage charging station that meets the manufacturer's specifications.
#### Step 1.3: Ensure proper ventilation
Verify that your garage has adequate ventilation to prevent the accumulation of toxic fumes or gases.

### Step 2: Acquire Necessary Tools and Equipment
#### Step 2.1: Purchase a multimeter
Invest in a high-quality multimeter to measure electrical parameters, such as voltage, current, and resistance.
#### Step 2.2: Obtain a torque wrench
Acquire a torque wrench with a range suitable for the Mercedes-AMG GT 4-Door's specific fasteners.
#### Step 2.3: Invest in high-voltage test equipment (optional)
If you plan to perform advanced electrical diagnostics, consider investing in high-voltage test equipment, such as a high-voltage probe or insulation tester.

### Step 3: Complete Safety Training
#### Step 3.1: Enroll in an electric vehicle safety course
Take an online or in-person course to learn about electric vehicle safety procedures, including high-voltage system handling and emergency response.
#### Step 3.2: Familiarize yourself with the vehicle's safety features
Study the Mercedes-AMG GT 4-Door's safety features, such as its high-voltage warning system and emergency shutdown procedure.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always follow the manufacturer's recommended maintenance schedule to prevent premature wear and tear.
* Avoid using generic or low-quality tools, as they may damage the vehicle's electrical systems.
* Never attempt to repair or maintain the high-voltage system without proper training and equipment.
