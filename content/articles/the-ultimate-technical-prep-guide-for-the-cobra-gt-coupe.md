## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation
Before starting any maintenance or repair work, it is essential to prepare the vehicle and the workspace. This includes:
#### Gathering tools and materials
Make sure you have all the necessary tools and materials before starting the job.
#### Jacking up the vehicle
Use a hydraulic jack to lift the vehicle, and support it with jack stands for safety.
#### Disconnecting the battery
Disconnect the battery to prevent any accidental starts or electrical shocks.

### Oil Change
#### Draining the oil
Locate the oil drain plug and remove it using a 10mm socket. Allow the oil to drain completely before replacing the plug.
#### Replacing the oil filter
Remove the oil filter using a filter wrench, and replace it with a new one.
#### Refilling the oil
Refill the oil using the recommended type and amount.

### Brake Pad Replacement
#### Removing the wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake calipers.
#### Removing the calipers
Remove the calipers using a 10mm socket, and suspend them using a wire to prevent damage to the brake hoses.
#### Replacing the brake pads
Remove the old brake pads and replace them with new ones.

### Suspension Tuning
#### Adjusting the coilovers
Adjust the coilovers to the recommended settings, using a torque wrench to ensure the correct torque specs.
#### Checking the shocks
Check the shocks for any signs of wear or damage, and replace them if necessary.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When working on the Cobra GT Coupe, it is essential to avoid common mistakes that can lead to damage or injury. Some pro tips include:
* Always follow the recommended torque specs to avoid damaging the engine or suspension components.
* Use the correct type and amount of oil to prevent engine damage.
* Avoid over-tightening the brake calipers, as this can cause damage to the brake rotors.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
The cost of maintaining and repairing the Cobra GT Coupe can vary depending on the task and the level of expertise. A DIY oil change, for example, can cost around $100, while a mechanic may charge $200-$300. However, for more complex tasks such as suspension tuning, it is recommended to seek the services of a professional mechanic, as the cost of repairs can be much higher if done incorrectly.
