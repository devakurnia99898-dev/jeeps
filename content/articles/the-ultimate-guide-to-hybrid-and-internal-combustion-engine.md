## Technical Deep Dive
The internal combustion engine is a complex system, comprising various components, including the engine block, cylinder head, pistons, and crankshaft. In a hybrid system, an electric motor is integrated with the internal combustion engine, allowing for improved fuel efficiency and reduced emissions. The electric motor is powered by a battery pack, which is charged through regenerative braking and the internal combustion engine.

Common failure points in internal combustion engines include the timing belt, water pump, and oil pump. In hybrid systems, the electric motor, battery pack, and power electronics are additional potential failure points. Understanding these components and their interactions is vital for effective maintenance and repair.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Introduction to Hybrid System Maintenance
Hybrid systems require specialized maintenance, including the inspection and replacement of key components. In this section, we'll guide you through the process of maintaining your Jeep's hybrid system.

#### Step 1: Inspect the Battery Pack
The battery pack is a critical component of the hybrid system, providing power to the electric motor. To inspect the battery pack, follow these steps:

1. Locate the battery pack, usually situated in the rear of the vehicle.
2. Check the battery pack for signs of damage, such as cracks or dents.
3. Use a multimeter to measure the battery pack's voltage and ensure it's within the recommended range.

#### Step 2: Check the Electric Motor
The electric motor is responsible for propelling the vehicle, and regular maintenance is essential to ensure optimal performance.

1. Locate the electric motor, usually situated near the internal combustion engine.
2. Check the electric motor for signs of wear, such as excessive noise or vibration.
3. Use a multimeter to measure the electric motor's voltage and ensure it's within the recommended range.

#### Step 3: Inspect the Power Electronics
The power electronics control the flow of energy between the battery pack, electric motor, and internal combustion engine. To inspect the power electronics, follow these steps:

1. Locate the power electronics, usually situated near the electric motor.
2. Check the power electronics for signs of damage, such as overheating or corrosion.
3. Use a multimeter to measure the power electronics' voltage and ensure it's within the recommended range.

### Internal Combustion Engine Maintenance
Regular maintenance is essential to extend the life of your Jeep's internal combustion engine. In this section, we'll guide you through the process of maintaining your engine.

#### Step 1: Change the Oil
Regular oil changes are crucial to keep your engine running smoothly. To change the oil, follow these steps:

1. Locate the oil drain plug, usually situated at the bottom of the engine.
2. Use a socket wrench to remove the oil drain plug.
3. Allow the oil to drain into a pan.
4. Replace the oil filter and add new oil.

#### Step 2: Inspect the Belts and Hoses
The belts and hoses play a critical role in the engine's operation, and regular inspection is essential to prevent damage.

1. Locate the serpentine belt, usually situated at the front of the engine.
2. Check the belt for signs of wear, such as cracks or frays.
3. Use a belt wear gauge to measure the belt's thickness and ensure it's within the recommended range.
