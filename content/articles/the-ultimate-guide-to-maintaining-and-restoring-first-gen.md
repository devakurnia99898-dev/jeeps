## Technical Deep Dive
The first-gen Ford Broncos were equipped with a range of engines, including the 170cid inline-six and the 289cid V8. The suspension system consisted of a coil-sprung front axle and a leaf-sprung rear axle. The electrical system was relatively simple, with a basic ignition system and minimal accessories. Common failure points include the plastic impeller in the water pump, the rubber seals in the differential, and the electrical connections in the ignition system.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Oil Change
#### Draining the Oil
1. Locate the oil drain plug, usually found at the bottom of the engine.
2. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.
3. Replace the plug and tighten to the specified torque (30 ft-lbs).

#### Refilling the Oil
1. Locate the oil filler cap, usually found on top of the engine.
2. Use a funnel to pour in the new oil (5W-30 or 10W-40).
3. Replace the cap and start the engine to circulate the new oil.

### Brake Pad Replacement
#### Removing the Wheels
1. Loosen the lug nuts using a torque wrench (100 ft-lbs).
2. Raise the vehicle using a jack and remove the wheels.
3. Use pliers to remove the caliper bolts (30 ft-lbs).

#### Installing the New Pads
1. Use a screwdriver to remove the old pads from the caliper.
2. Install the new pads, ensuring they are properly seated and aligned.
3. Replace the caliper bolts and tighten to the specified torque (30 ft-lbs).

### Suspension Overhaul
#### Disassembling the Front Axle
1. Use a wrench to remove the coil spring bolts (50 ft-lbs).
2. Use a socket to remove the shock absorber bolts (30 ft-lbs).
3. Use pliers to remove the steering linkage bolts (20 ft-lbs).

#### Reassembling the Front Axle
1. Use a socket to replace the shock absorber bolts (30 ft-lbs).
2. Use a wrench to replace the coil spring bolts (50 ft-lbs).
3. Use pliers to replace the steering linkage bolts (20 ft-lbs).

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct torque specs to avoid damaging components.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure accurate tightening.
* Never mix and match parts from different vehicles, as this can lead to compatibility issues.
