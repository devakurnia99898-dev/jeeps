## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Gather materials
Collect the necessary materials, including oil filter wrench, new oil filter, and enough motor oil to fill the engine.
#### Step 1.2: Warm up the engine
Drive the Jeep for a few minutes to warm up the engine.
#### Step 1.3: Locate the oil drain plug
Find the oil drain plug located at the bottom of the engine.
#### Step 1.4: Drain the oil
Use a socket wrench to remove the oil drain plug and let the oil drain into a pan.
#### Step 1.5: Replace the oil filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old oil filter and install a new one.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheels
Take off the wheels to access the brake calipers.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the caliper
Use a socket wrench to remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.
#### Step 2.3: Remove the old brake pads
Pull out the old brake pads from the caliper.
#### Step 2.4: Install the new brake pads
Insert the new brake pads into the caliper.
#### Step 2.5: Reinstall the caliper
Slide the caliper back over the rotor and tighten the caliper bolts.

### Step 3: Spark Plug Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Locate the spark plugs
Find the spark plugs located on the side of the engine.
#### Step 3.2: Remove the spark plug wire
Pull off the spark plug wire from the spark plug.
#### Step 3.3: Remove the spark plug
Use a spark plug socket to remove the spark plug from the engine.
#### Step 3.4: Install the new spark plug
Insert the new spark plug into the engine and tighten it with the spark plug socket.
#### Step 3.5: Reinstall the spark plug wire
Put the spark plug wire back onto the spark plug.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always refer to the owner's manual for specific maintenance recommendations
* Use the correct type of oil and filter for your Jeep's engine
* Avoid over-tightening brake caliper bolts, as this can damage the brake rotor

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **Q: How often should I change my Jeep's oil?**
A: The recommended oil change interval for Jeeps is every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on the model and driving conditions.
2. **Q: What type of oil should I use in my Jeep?**
A: The type of oil to use in your Jeep depends on the engine type and climate. Consult the owner's manual for specific recommendations.
3. **Q: Can I replace my Jeep's brake pads myself?**
A: Yes, replacing brake pads is a relatively straightforward task that can be done with basic tools and mechanical knowledge.
4. **Q: How do I know if my Jeep's spark plugs need to be replaced?**
A: Signs of worn-out spark plugs include decreased engine performance, rough idling, and increased fuel consumption.
5. **Q: What are the benefits of regular maintenance for my Jeep?**
A: Regular maintenance can help extend the life of your Jeep, improve fuel efficiency, and prevent costly repairs down the road.
