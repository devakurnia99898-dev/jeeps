## Technical Deep Dive
Ford vehicles, like any other car, have complex systems that work together to provide a smooth and safe driving experience. The engine, transmission, suspension, and electrical systems are all interconnected and require regular maintenance to function properly. Common failure points in Ford vehicles include the engine's water pump, timing belt, and spark plugs. The suspension system can also be prone to wear and tear, especially if the vehicle is driven in harsh conditions.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Routine Maintenance Checks
#### Check the oil level
Check the oil level regularly to ensure it is at the recommended level. Low oil levels can cause engine damage and decreased performance.
#### Check the tire pressure
Check the tire pressure monthly, and before long trips, to ensure it is at the recommended level. Proper tire pressure can improve fuel efficiency and handling.
#### Check the brakes
Check the brake pads and rotors regularly to ensure they are in good condition. Worn-out brake pads can cause decreased stopping power and increased risk of accidents.

### Step 2: Advanced Maintenance Tasks
#### Replace the air filter
Replace the air filter every 15,000 to 30,000 miles to improve engine performance and fuel efficiency.
#### Replace the spark plugs
Replace the spark plugs every 30,000 to 100,000 miles to improve engine performance and fuel efficiency.
#### Inspect the suspension
Inspect the suspension system regularly to ensure it is in good condition. Worn-out suspension components can cause decreased handling and increased risk of accidents.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't neglect routine maintenance tasks, as they can help prevent costly repairs and improve vehicle performance.
* Use the correct type of oil and fluids for your vehicle, as specified in the owner's manual.
* Don't over-tighten bolts and screws, as this can cause damage to the vehicle's components.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **Q: How often should I change my oil?**
A: The recommended oil change interval varies depending on the vehicle and driving conditions. Check your owner's manual for specific recommendations.
2. **Q: What type of oil should I use?**
A: Use the type of oil specified in your owner's manual. Using the wrong type of oil can cause engine damage and decreased performance.
3. **Q: How often should I rotate my tires?**
A: Rotate your tires every 5,000 to 8,000 miles to ensure even wear and improve handling.
4. **Q: What are the symptoms of worn-out brake pads?**
A: Symptoms of worn-out brake pads include decreased stopping power, squealing or grinding noises, and vibrations when braking.
5. **Q: How often should I inspect my suspension?**
A: Inspect your suspension system regularly, or every 12,000 to 15,000 miles, to ensure it is in good condition and to prevent decreased handling and increased risk of accidents.
