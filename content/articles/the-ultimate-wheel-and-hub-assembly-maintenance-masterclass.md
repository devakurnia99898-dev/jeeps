The recent recall of the Tesla Cybertruck due to wheels that could fall off highlights the importance of proper wheel and hub assembly maintenance. A loose or faulty wheel and hub assembly can lead to a loss of control, accidents, and even fatalities. It is crucial to identify the symptoms of a faulty wheel and hub assembly and take corrective action to prevent such incidents.

Symptoms of a faulty wheel and hub assembly include:
* Vibration or shaking of the vehicle while driving
* Uneven tire wear
* Grinding or scraping noises from the wheels
* Loose or missing wheel nuts or bolts
* Visible damage to the wheel or hub assembly

## **Technical Deep Dive**
The wheel and hub assembly is a critical component of a vehicle's suspension and steering system. It consists of the wheel, hub, bearings, and other components that work together to support the vehicle's weight and provide a smooth ride. The wheel and hub assembly is subject to various stresses and loads, including radial, axial, and torsional forces, which can cause wear and tear over time.

Common failure points in the wheel and hub assembly include:
* Worn or damaged wheel bearings
* Loose or corroded wheel nuts or bolts
* Damaged or bent wheel hubs
* Worn or damaged brake rotors or drums
* Uneven or excessive wear on the wheel or tire

## **Step-by-Step Guide**
### **Inspecting the Wheel and Hub Assembly**
#### **Step 1: Raise the Vehicle**
Raise the vehicle using a jack and support it with jack stands. Ensure the vehicle is on a level surface and apply the parking brake.

#### **Step 2: Remove the Wheel**
Remove the wheel using a wheel nut wrench. Set the wheel aside and inspect the hub assembly.

#### **Step 3: Inspect the Hub Assembly**
Inspect the hub assembly for signs of wear or damage. Check the wheel bearings for play or roughness. Check the brake rotors or drums for excessive wear or damage.

#### **Step 4: Check the Wheel Nuts or Bolts**
Check the wheel nuts or bolts for looseness or corrosion. Tighten any loose nuts or bolts to the specified torque.

#### **Step 5: Reassemble the Wheel and Hub Assembly**
Reassemble the wheel and hub assembly in the reverse order of disassembly. Ensure all components are properly secured and tightened to the specified torque.

### **Replacing the Wheel Bearings**
#### **Step 1: Remove the Hub Assembly**
Remove the hub assembly from the vehicle. Set the hub assembly aside and inspect the wheel bearings.

#### **Step 2: Remove the Wheel Bearings**
Remove the wheel bearings from the hub assembly. Set the wheel bearings aside and clean the hub assembly.

#### **Step 3: Install the New Wheel Bearings**
Install the new wheel bearings into the hub assembly. Ensure the wheel bearings are properly seated and secured.

#### **Step 4: Reassemble the Hub Assembly**
Reassemble the hub assembly in the reverse order of disassembly. Ensure all components are properly secured and tightened to the specified torque.

## **Pro Tips & Common Mistakes**
* Always follow proper lifting and handling procedures to avoid injury.
* Never over-tighten the wheel nuts or bolts, as this can damage the wheel or hub assembly.
* Always use a torque wrench to ensure the proper torque is applied to the wheel nuts or bolts.
* Don't ignore signs of wear or damage to the wheel and hub assembly, as this can lead to more costly repairs down the road.
