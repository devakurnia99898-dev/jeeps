## Technical Deep Dive
High-performance vehicles have complex systems that require specialized knowledge to maintain. The engine, transmission, suspension, and braking systems all work together to provide optimal performance. Common failure points in high-performance vehicles include the engine's piston rings, cylinder walls, and valve train. The transmission's clutch packs, bearings, and gears can also fail if not properly maintained.

The suspension system, including the shocks, struts, and springs, can also be prone to failure if not regularly inspected and maintained. The braking system, including the pads, rotors, and calipers, requires regular maintenance to ensure optimal stopping power.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Regular Oil Changes
#### Step 1.1: Gather materials
Gather the necessary materials, including oil filter wrench, new oil filter, and enough motor oil to fill the engine.
#### Step 1.2: Warm up the engine
Warm up the engine by driving the vehicle for a few minutes.
#### Step 1.3: Drain the oil
Drain the oil into a pan and replace the oil filter with a new one.
#### Step 1.4: Refill the oil
Refill the oil with the recommended type and amount.

### Step 2: Tire Rotation
#### Step 2.1: Gather materials
Gather the necessary materials, including a lug wrench and a car jack.
#### Step 2.2: Loosen the lug nuts
Loosen the lug nuts on the first wheel using the lug wrench.
#### Step 2.3: Raise the vehicle
Raise the vehicle using the car jack and remove the lug nuts.
#### Step 2.4: Rotate the tires
Rotate the tires according to the recommended pattern.

### Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Gather materials
Gather the necessary materials, including new brake pads and a C-clamp.
#### Step 3.2: Remove the caliper
Remove the caliper using a wrench and puller.
#### Step 3.3: Remove the old brake pads
Remove the old brake pads and replace them with new ones.
#### Step 3.4: Reinstall the caliper
Reinstall the caliper and tighten the bolts.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always refer to the owner's manual for specific maintenance recommendations.
* Use the correct type and amount of motor oil to avoid engine damage.
* Never neglect regular tire rotations to avoid uneven tire wear.
* Always inspect the brake pads and rotors for wear and damage.
