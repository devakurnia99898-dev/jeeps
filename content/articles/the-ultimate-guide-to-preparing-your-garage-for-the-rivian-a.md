## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparing Your Garage for the R2 and R2X
The first step in preparing your garage for the R2 and R2X is to ensure you have the necessary tools and equipment. This includes a 10mm socket, torque wrench, multimeter, and battery tester.

#### Installing a Level 2 Charger
To charge the R2 and R2X, you'll need to install a Level 2 charger in your garage. This will require a 240-volt electrical connection and a charger specifically designed for electric vehicles.

##### Step 1: Determine the Location of the Charger
Choose a location for the charger that is convenient and safe. Make sure it's away from any flammable materials and has a clear path for the charging cable.

##### Step 2: Install the Electrical Connection
Install a 240-volt electrical connection at the location of the charger. This will require a licensed electrician and may involve upgrading your electrical panel.

##### Step 3: Install the Charger
Install the Level 2 charger according to the manufacturer's instructions. This will typically involve mounting the charger to the wall and connecting it to the electrical connection.

### Maintaining the Electric Motors
To maintain the electric motors in the R2 and R2X, you'll need to follow a regular maintenance schedule. This will include checking the motor bearings, lubricating the motors, and inspecting the electrical connections.

#### Checking the Motor Bearings
Check the motor bearings for wear or damage. This will require a multimeter and a bearing puller.

##### Step 1: Remove the Motor Cover
Remove the motor cover to access the bearings. This will require a 10mm socket and a torque wrench.

##### Step 2: Inspect the Bearings
Inspect the bearings for wear or damage. If necessary, replace the bearings with new ones.

### Maintaining the Suspension System
To maintain the suspension system in the R2 and R2X, you'll need to follow a regular maintenance schedule. This will include checking the shock absorbers, lubricating the suspension components, and inspecting the suspension bushings.

#### Checking the Shock Absorbers
Check the shock absorbers for wear or damage. This will require a multimeter and a shock absorber tester.

##### Step 1: Remove the Wheel
Remove the wheel to access the shock absorber. This will require a lug wrench and a jack.

##### Step 2: Inspect the Shock Absorber
Inspect the shock absorber for wear or damage. If necessary, replace the shock absorber with a new one.
