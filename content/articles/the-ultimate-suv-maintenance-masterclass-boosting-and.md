## Step-by-Step Guide
Maintaining your SUV requires a thorough understanding of the various systems involved. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

### Oil Change
#### Step 1: Gather Materials
Collect the necessary tools and materials, including oil filters, oil filler cap, and a drain pan.

#### Step 2: Warm Up the Engine
Drive the vehicle for a few minutes to warm up the engine. This will help the oil drain more easily.

#### Step 3: Drain the Oil
Locate the oil drain plug and use a socket wrench to remove it. Allow the oil to drain into the pan.

#### Step 4: Replace the Oil Filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old filter. Install a new filter and hand tighten it.

### Tire Rotation
#### Step 1: Check Tire Pressure
Use a tire pressure gauge to check the pressure of each tire. Compare it to the recommended pressure listed in the owner's manual.

#### Step 2: Loosen the Lug Nuts
Before raising the vehicle, loosen the lug nuts on the first wheel using a lug wrench.

#### Step 3: Raise the Vehicle
Use a car lift or jack stands to raise the vehicle. Remove the lug nuts and flat washers.

#### Step 4: Rotate the Tires
Rotate the tires according to the recommended pattern. Tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern.

### Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 1: Remove the Caliper
Use a wrench to loosen the caliper bolts. Remove the caliper and suspend it using a wire.

#### Step 2: Remove the Old Brake Pads
Pull out the old brake pads from the caliper.

#### Step 3: Install the New Brake Pads
Insert the new brake pads into the caliper. Make sure they're properly seated.

#### Step 4: Reinstall the Caliper
Put the caliper back in place and tighten the caliper bolts.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When maintaining your SUV, it's essential to avoid common mistakes that can lead to further damage.

* Don't over-tighten the lug nuts, as this can damage the wheel bearings.
* Use the correct type of oil for your vehicle, as specified in the owner's manual.
* Don't neglect to check the brake fluid level, as low levels can cause brake failure.

## Frequently Asked Questions
Here are some common questions and answers related to SUV maintenance:

1. **Q: How often should I change my oil?**
A: It's recommended to change your oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on the type of oil used.

2. **Q: What type of oil should I use?**
A: Use the type of oil specified in your owner's manual. Using the wrong type of oil can damage your engine.

3. **Q: How often should I rotate my tires?**
A: Rotate your tires every 6,000 to 8,000 miles to ensure even wear and extend their lifespan.

4. **Q: What are the symptoms of worn-out brake pads?**
A: Symptoms include squealing noises, vibration, and decreased braking performance.

5. **Q: Can I replace my brake pads myself?**
A: Yes, but it's recommended to have a professional mechanic do it, especially if you're not experienced with brake repairs.
