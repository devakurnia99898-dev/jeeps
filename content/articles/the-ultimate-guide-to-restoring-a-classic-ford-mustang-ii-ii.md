## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The Ford Mustang II Cobra II is a classic car that has been overlooked by many enthusiasts due to its lack of horsepower and respect. However, with the right restoration, this car can be a gem. The 1978 model, in particular, is a great candidate for restoration, with its sleek design and powerful engine. But before you start, it's essential to diagnose the situation and understand what you're getting into. Look for signs of wear and tear, such as rust, dents, and faded paint. Also, check the engine, transmission, and suspension for any signs of damage or neglect.

## Technical Deep Dive
The Ford Mustang II Cobra II is equipped with a 5.0L V8 engine, which produces 139 horsepower. The engine is paired with a 3-speed automatic transmission and a rear-wheel drive system. The suspension is a coil spring front suspension with a live rear axle. The car also features power steering and power brakes. Common failure points on this model include the plastic impeller in the water pump, the alternator, and the transmission seals. Understanding how these systems work and where they tend to fail is crucial for a successful restoration.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Disassembly and Inspection
#### Step 1.1: Remove the exterior trim and moldings
Use a screwdriver to remove the screws that hold the trim and moldings in place. Gently pull the trim away from the car to avoid damaging the paint or the trim itself.
#### Step 1.2: Remove the interior components
Remove the seats, carpet, and dashboard to access the underlying components. Use a torque wrench to remove the bolts that hold the seats in place.
#### Step 1.3: Inspect the frame and suspension
Use a flashlight to inspect the frame and suspension for any signs of damage or rust. Make a note of any areas that need repair or replacement.

### Step 2: Engine and Transmission Restoration
#### Step 2.1: Remove the engine and transmission
Use a hoist to lift the engine and transmission out of the car. Be careful not to damage any of the surrounding components.
#### Step 2.2: Disassemble the engine and transmission
Use a socket set to remove the bolts that hold the engine and transmission together. Inspect the components for any signs of wear or damage.
#### Step 2.3: Replace the water pump and alternator
Use a new water pump and alternator to replace the old ones. Make sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions for installation.

### Step 3: Body and Paint Restoration
#### Step 3.1: Sand the entire body
Use sandpaper to sand the entire body, removing any rust or old paint. Be careful not to scratch the metal.
#### Step 3.2: Apply a coat of primer
Use a paintbrush to apply a coat of primer to the entire body. Make sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions for application.
#### Step 3.3: Apply a coat of paint
Use a paintbrush to apply a coat of paint to the entire body. Make sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions for application.

The cost of restoring a classic Ford Mustang II Cobra II can vary depending on the extent of the restoration and the materials used. However, the cost of a professional restoration may be worth it if you don't have the experience or time to do it yourself.
