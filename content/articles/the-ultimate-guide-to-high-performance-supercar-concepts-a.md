## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Preparation and Inspection
Before beginning any work on the Lynk & Co GT concept, it is essential to thoroughly inspect the vehicle and prepare the necessary tools and equipment. This includes checking the owner's manual, reviewing the vehicle's maintenance history, and inspecting the engine, transmission, and suspension systems for any signs of wear or damage.

#### Step 1.1: Check the Owner's Manual
Review the owner's manual to understand the recommended maintenance schedule, as well as any specific procedures or precautions for working on the vehicle.

#### Step 1.2: Review the Maintenance History
Review the vehicle's maintenance history to identify any potential issues or areas of concern.

#### Step 1.3: Inspect the Engine
Inspect the engine for any signs of wear or damage, including oil leaks, coolant leaks, or damaged belts and hoses.

### Step 2: Oil Change and Fluid Check
Performing regular oil changes and fluid checks is essential to maintaining the health and performance of the Lynk & Co GT concept.

#### Step 2.1: Drain the Oil
Drain the oil from the engine, using a socket wrench to remove the oil drain plug.

#### Step 2.2: Replace the Oil Filter
Replace the oil filter, using a new filter and hand tightening the filter in a clockwise direction.

#### Step 2.3: Refill the Oil
Refill the oil, using the recommended type and amount of oil.

### Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement
Replacing the brake pads is a critical maintenance task, as worn or damaged brake pads can compromise the safety and performance of the vehicle.

#### Step 3.1: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels, using a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts.

#### Step 3.2: Remove the Calipers
Remove the calipers, using a socket wrench to remove the caliper bolts.

#### Step 3.3: Remove the Old Brake Pads
Remove the old brake pads, using a screwdriver to remove the pad retaining clips.

### Step 4: Suspension Upgrade
Upgrading the suspension system can significantly improve the handling and performance of the Lynk & Co GT concept.

#### Step 4.1: Remove the Old Suspension Components
Remove the old suspension components, including the shocks, springs, and control arms.

#### Step 4.2: Install the New Suspension Components
Install the new suspension components, including the shocks, springs, and control arms.

#### Step 4.3: Adjust the Suspension
Adjust the suspension, using a wrench to tighten the suspension bolts.
