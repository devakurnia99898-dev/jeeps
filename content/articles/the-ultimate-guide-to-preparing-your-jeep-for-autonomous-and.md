## Technical Deep Dive
Autonomous systems in vehicles work by utilizing a combination of sensors, including cameras, radar, lidar, and ultrasonic sensors. These sensors feed data into a sophisticated software system that interprets the environment and makes decisions in real-time. Common failure points in these systems include faulty sensors, software glitches, or hardware compatibility issues. For example, a faulty camera sensor can cause the autonomous system to malfunction, leading to accidents or system failures.

## Step-by-Step Guide

Before starting any work, research the autonomous system and its components. Understand how the system works, including the sensors, software, and hardware.

Select compatible upgrades and ensure they meet the vehicle's specifications. Consult with experts or manufacturers to determine the best course of action.

Install the radar and lidar sensors, following the manufacturer's guidelines. Calibrate the sensors to ensure accurate data feed into the system.
#### Ultrasonic Sensor Installation
Install the ultrasonic sensors, ensuring proper wiring and connections. Calibrate the sensors to ensure accurate data feed into the system.
