## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Sketch the Basic Shape
Start by sketching the basic shape of the GT40, including the long hood, the curved roofline, and the rear spoiler. Use a pencil or a pen to draw a rough outline of the car, paying attention to its proportions and overall shape.
#### Sub-Step 1.1: Draw the Hood and Front Bumper
Draw the hood and front bumper, using a gentle curve to define the shape of the hood and a straight line to define the front bumper.
#### Sub-Step 1.2: Draw the Roofline and Rear Spoiler
Draw the roofline, using a smooth curve to define its shape, and the rear spoiler, using a straight line to define its edge.
### Step 2: Add the Wheels and Tires
Add the wheels and tires to your sketch, using a circle to define the shape of the wheels and a curved line to define the shape of the tires.
#### Sub-Step 2.1: Draw the Front Wheels
Draw the front wheels, making sure they are evenly spaced and aligned with the front bumper.
#### Sub-Step 2.2: Draw the Rear Wheels
Draw the rear wheels, making sure they are evenly spaced and aligned with the rear spoiler.
### Step 3: Add the Details
Add the details to your sketch, including the headlights, taillights, and exhaust pipes.
#### Sub-Step 3.1: Draw the Headlights
Draw the headlights, using a circle to define their shape and a curved line to define their edges.
#### Sub-Step 3.2: Draw the Taillights
Draw the taillights, using a rectangle to define their shape and a curved line to define their edges.
#### Sub-Step 3.3: Draw the Exhaust Pipes
Draw the exhaust pipes, using a straight line to define their shape and a curved line to define their edges.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Use a grid to help you draw the GT40's proportions accurately
* Pay attention to the car's lines and curves, as these define its shape and character
* Avoid drawing the GT40 with a too-boxy or too-rounded shape, as this can make it look unnatural
* Don't be afraid to experiment and try different drawing techniques to achieve the desired effect
