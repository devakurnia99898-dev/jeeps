## Technical Deep Dive
The Miata's DNA is rooted in its engineering and design. The vehicle's lightweight body, rear-wheel drive layout, and precise steering make it a handling enthusiast's dream. However, the Miata's components can wear out over time, affecting its performance and handling. Common failure points include the clutch, gearbox, and suspension components. The clutch, for example, can wear out due to excessive use, causing the vehicle to slip or hesitate when shifting gears. The gearbox can also develop issues, such as worn-out synchros or bearings, which can cause grinding or difficulty shifting gears. The suspension components, including the shocks, struts, and bushings, can also wear out, affecting the vehicle's handling and stability.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Regular Maintenance
Regular maintenance is key to preserving the Miata's DNA. This includes regular oil changes, tire rotations, and brake pad replacements. The oil change, for example, should be done every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on the type of oil used. The tire rotation should be done every 6,000 to 8,000 miles, to ensure even tread wear and prevent uneven handling.
#### Sub-step 1.1: Oil Change
To change the oil, you will need to locate the oil drain plug, which is usually located at the bottom of the engine. Use a socket wrench to remove the plug and let the oil drain into a pan. Once the oil has stopped dripping, replace the plug and tighten it to the specified torque.
#### Sub-step 1.2: Tire Rotation
To rotate the tires, you will need to loosen the lug nuts on each wheel using a lug wrench. Then, raise the vehicle using a jack and remove the lug nuts. Rotate the tires according to the recommended pattern, which is usually a rearward cross pattern.

### Step 2: Suspension Overhaul
The suspension is a critical component of the Miata's DNA, and it requires regular maintenance to keep it in top condition. This includes replacing the shocks, struts, and bushings, as well as aligning the vehicle's wheels.
#### Sub-step 2.1: Shock Replacement
To replace the shocks, you will need to locate the shock absorbers, which are usually located at the rear of the vehicle. Use a socket wrench to remove the nut that holds the shock in place, then pull the shock out of its mounting bracket. Install the new shock and tighten the nut to the specified torque.
#### Sub-step 2.2: Strut Replacement
To replace the struts, you will need to locate the strut assembly, which is usually located at the front of the vehicle. Use a socket wrench to remove the nut that holds the strut in place, then pull the strut out of its mounting bracket. Install the new strut and tighten the nut to the specified torque.

### Step 3: Brake System Overhaul
The brake system is another critical component of the Miata's DNA, and it requires regular maintenance to keep it in top condition. This includes replacing the brake pads, rotors, and calipers, as well as bleeding the brake system.
#### Sub-step 3.1: Brake Pad Replacement
To replace the brake pads, you will need to locate the brake caliper, which is usually located at the wheel hub. Use a socket wrench to remove the caliper bolts, then pull the caliper off the rotor. Remove the old brake pads and install the new ones, then reassemble the caliper and tighten the bolts to the specified torque.
#### Sub-step 3.2: Rotor Replacement
To replace the rotors, you will need to locate the rotor, which is usually located at the wheel hub. Use a socket wrench to remove the hub nuts, then pull the rotor off the hub. Install the new rotor and tighten the hub nuts to the specified torque.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type of oil and fluids for your vehicle, as specified in the owner's manual.
* Never neglect regular maintenance, as it can cause premature wear and tear on your vehicle's components.
* Always follow the recommended torque specs when tightening bolts and nuts, to avoid damaging your vehicle's components.
* Never use aftermarket parts that are not compatible with your vehicle, as they can cause performance and handling issues.
