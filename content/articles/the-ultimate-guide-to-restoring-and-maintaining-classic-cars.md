The symptoms of a classic muscle car in need of restoration can be subtle, but some common signs include:
* Decreased performance and power output
* Leaks and corrosion in the engine and suspension
* Worn-out interior and exterior components
* Electrical system malfunctions

## Technical Deep Dive
Classic muscle cars typically feature large V8 engines, rear-wheel drive, and a robust suspension system. The engine is the heart of the car, and common failure points include:
* The intake manifold and carburetor
* The ignition system, including spark plugs and wires
* The oil pump and lubrication system
* The transmission and drivetrain components

The suspension system is also critical, with common failure points including:
* The coil springs and shock absorbers
* The control arms and bushings
* The steering system, including the power steering pump and gear box

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Engine Overhaul
#### Disassembly and Inspection
1. Remove the engine from the car and disassemble the components
2. Inspect the engine block, cylinder head, and crankshaft for damage or wear
3. Clean and deburr the components as necessary

#### Rebuilding the Engine
1. Rebuild the engine using new or refurbished components
2. Install new piston rings, bearings, and seals
3. Assemble the engine and torque the components to spec (e.g., 30 ft-lbs for the cylinder head)

#### Reinstallation and Testing
1. Reinstall the engine in the car and connect the electrical and fuel systems
2. Test the engine for leaks and proper function
3. Adjust the ignition timing and carburetor as necessary

### Suspension Rebuild
#### Disassembly and Inspection
1. Remove the suspension components from the car and disassemble the parts
2. Inspect the coil springs, shock absorbers, and control arms for damage or wear
3. Clean and deburr the components as necessary

#### Rebuilding the Suspension
1. Rebuild the suspension using new or refurbished components
2. Install new bushings and seals
3. Assemble the suspension and torque the components to spec (e.g., 50 ft-lbs for the control arms)

#### Reinstallation and Testing
1. Reinstall the suspension in the car and connect the steering and brake systems
2. Test the suspension for proper function and alignment
3. Adjust the ride height and alignment as necessary

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't neglect to properly clean and deburr the components before reassembly
* Use the correct torque specs and procedures to avoid damage or injury
* Don't skip the testing and inspection steps to ensure the car is safe and functional

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is the best way to store a classic muscle car?**
Store the car in a dry, climate-controlled environment, and consider using a car cover and desiccant to prevent moisture buildup.
2. **How often should I change the oil in my classic muscle car?**
Change the oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on the type of oil and driving conditions.
3. **What is the best way to clean and maintain the interior of my classic muscle car?**
Use a gentle cleaning product and avoid harsh chemicals or abrasive materials. Regularly vacuum and condition the upholstery and carpets.
4. **Can I use modern parts and components in my classic muscle car?**
Yes, but be sure to research and select parts that are compatible with the original components and will not compromise the car's performance or safety.
5. **How do I troubleshoot common issues with my classic muscle car?**
Start by consulting the owner's manual and troubleshooting guides, and consider seeking the advice of a professional mechanic or restoration specialist if necessary.
