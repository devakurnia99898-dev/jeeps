## **Step-by-Step Guide**
### **Oil Change and Filter Replacement**
#### **Drain the Oil**
Locate the oil drain plug and position a drain pan underneath. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and let the oil drain completely.

#### **Replace the Oil Filter**
Use a filter wrench to remove the old oil filter. Install a new filter by hand tightening it in a clockwise direction.

#### **Refill the Oil**
Pour in the recommended type and amount of oil. Replace the oil cap and start the engine to circulate the new oil.

### **Brake Pad Replacement**
#### **Remove the Wheels**
Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts. Raise the vehicle using a jack and remove the wheels.

#### **Remove the Caliper**
Use a 10mm socket to remove the caliper bolts. Slide the caliper off the rotor and suspend it using a wire.

#### **Replace the Brake Pads**
Remove the old brake pads and install new ones. Make sure they're properly seated and secured.

### **Suspension Inspection**
#### **Check the Bushings**
Inspect the suspension bushings for signs of wear or damage. Replace them if necessary.

#### **Check the Shocks**
Inspect the shocks for leaks or damage. Replace them if necessary.

## **Pro Tips & Common Mistakes**
* Don't neglect regular oil changes, as this can lead to engine damage.
* Use the correct type of oil for your SUV to ensure optimal performance.
* Avoid over-tightening brake pads, as this can cause damage to the rotor.

## **Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic**
The cost of maintaining an SUV can vary greatly depending on the task. DIY oil changes can cost as little as $50, while brake pad replacement can cost up to $200. However, if you take your SUV to a mechanic, labor costs can add up quickly. It's essential to weigh the costs and benefits of DIY maintenance versus professional repair.

## **Frequently Asked Questions**
1. **Q: How often should I change my SUV's oil?**
A: It's recommended to change your SUV's oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on the manufacturer's guidelines.
2. **Q: What type of oil should I use in my SUV?**
A: Check your owner's manual to find the recommended type of oil for your SUV.
3. **Q: Can I replace my SUV's brake pads myself?**
A: Yes, but it's recommended to have a professional mechanic do it if you're not experienced with brake repairs.
4. **Q: How do I know if my SUV's suspension needs to be replaced?**
A: Look for signs of wear or damage, such as uneven tire wear or a bouncy ride.
5. **Q: Can I use any type of fuel in my SUV?**
A: No, it's essential to use the recommended type of fuel for your SUV to ensure optimal performance and prevent damage to the engine.
