## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Preparing the Vehicle
#### Step 1.1: Locate the Powertrain Components
Locate the engine, transmission, and other powertrain components. Identify the spark plugs, fuel injectors, and turbocharger.

#### Step 1.2: Disconnect the Battery
Disconnect the negative battery cable to prevent any accidental starts or electrical shocks.

### Step 2: Replacing the Spark Plugs
#### Step 2.1: Remove the Spark Plug Wires
Remove the spark plug wires and boots. Use a spark plug wire puller to avoid damaging the wires.

#### Step 2.2: Remove the Spark Plugs
Use a spark plug socket to remove the spark plugs. Be careful not to round the spark plug holes.

#### Step 2.3: Install the New Spark Plugs
Install the new spark plugs and tighten them to the specified torque (e.g., 10 Nm).

### Step 3: Cleaning the Fuel Injectors
#### Step 3.1: Remove the Fuel Injector Electrical Connectors
Remove the electrical connectors from the fuel injectors.

#### Step 3.2: Remove the Fuel Injector Hold-Down Clips
Remove the hold-down clips and gaskets from the fuel injectors.

#### Step 3.3: Clean the Fuel Injectors
Use a fuel injector cleaning kit to clean the fuel injectors. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for the cleaning process.

### Step 4: Replacing the Air Filter
#### Step 4.1: Locate the Air Filter Housing
Locate the air filter housing and remove the screws or clips.

#### Step 4.2: Remove the Old Air Filter
Remove the old air filter and discard it.

#### Step 4.3: Install the New Air Filter
Install the new air filter and replace the air filter housing.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
