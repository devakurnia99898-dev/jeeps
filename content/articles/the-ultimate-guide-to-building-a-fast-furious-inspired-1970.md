## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Engine Rebuild
The first step in building a Fast & Furious inspired 1970 Dodge Charger is to rebuild the engine. This involves disassembling the engine, inspecting the components, and replacing any worn or damaged parts.

#### Step 1.1: Remove the Engine
To remove the engine, you will need to disconnect the battery, remove the air cleaner, and disconnect the spark plug wires. You will also need to remove the engine mounts and transmission crossmember.

#### Step 1.2: Disassemble the Engine
Once the engine is removed, you can begin to disassemble it. This involves removing the cylinder heads, intake manifold, and exhaust manifold. You will also need to remove the oil pan and timing cover.

#### Step 1.3: Inspect the Components
Once the engine is disassembled, you can inspect the components for wear and tear. Look for signs of damage, such as cracks, scoring, and excessive wear.

#### Step 1.4: Replace Worn or Damaged Parts
If you find any worn or damaged parts, you will need to replace them. This may involve purchasing new components, such as piston rings, bearings, and gaskets.

### Step 2: Suspension Upgrade
The next step in building a Fast & Furious inspired 1970 Dodge Charger is to upgrade the suspension system. This involves replacing the coil springs and shock absorbers with high-performance components.

#### Step 2.1: Remove the Old Suspension Components
To remove the old suspension components, you will need to raise the car and support it with jack stands. You can then remove the coil springs and shock absorbers.

#### Step 2.2: Install the New Suspension Components
Once the old suspension components are removed, you can install the new components. This involves bolting the new coil springs and shock absorbers into place.

#### Step 2.3: Adjust the Suspension
Once the new suspension components are installed, you will need to adjust the suspension to ensure proper alignment and ride height.

### Step 3: Braking System Upgrade
The final step in building a Fast & Furious inspired 1970 Dodge Charger is to upgrade the braking system. This involves replacing the drum brakes with disc brakes and installing a high-performance brake kit.

#### Step 3.1: Remove the Old Braking Components
To remove the old braking components, you will need to raise the car and support it with jack stands. You can then remove the drum brakes and brake lines.

#### Step 3.2: Install the New Braking Components
Once the old braking components are removed, you can install the new components. This involves bolting the new disc brakes and brake lines into place.

#### Step 3.3: Bleed the Braking System
Once the new braking components are installed, you will need to bleed the braking system to ensure proper function.
