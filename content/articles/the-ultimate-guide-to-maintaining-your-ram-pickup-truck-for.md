## Technical Deep Dive
Ram pickup trucks are equipped with advanced suspension, engine, and electrical systems. The suspension system, for example, features a five-link coil suspension with a stabilizer bar, providing a smooth ride and excellent handling. The engine, on the other hand, is a powerful 3.6L V6 or 5.7L V8, delivering exceptional towing capacity and performance. However, common failure points include the water pump, alternator, and spark plugs. Regular maintenance is crucial to prevent these components from failing, which can lead to costly repairs.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Gather materials
Collect the necessary materials, including oil filter wrench, socket set, and new oil filter.
#### Step 1.2: Warm up the engine
Drive the vehicle for a few minutes to warm up the engine.
#### Step 1.3: Locate the oil drain plug
Find the oil drain plug, usually located at the bottom of the engine.
#### Step 1.4: Drain the oil
Use a socket wrench to remove the oil drain plug and drain the oil into a pan.
#### Step 1.5: Replace the oil filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old oil filter and install a new one.
### Step 2: Tire Rotation
#### Step 2.1: Check the tire pressure
Use a tire pressure gauge to check the pressure of each tire.
#### Step 2.2: Loosen the lug nuts
Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts on each wheel.
#### Step 2.3: Raise the vehicle
Use a car jack to raise the vehicle and remove the wheels.
#### Step 2.4: Rotate the tires
Rotate the tires according to the manufacturer's recommendations.
### Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Remove the wheels
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheels and access the brake pads.
#### Step 3.2: Remove the caliper
Use a socket wrench to remove the caliper and access the brake pads.
#### Step 3.3: Remove the old brake pads
Use a pair of pliers to remove the old brake pads.
#### Step 3.4: Install the new brake pads
Use a pair of pliers to install the new brake pads.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always refer to the owner's manual for specific maintenance recommendations.
* Use the correct type of oil for your vehicle.
* Avoid over-tightening the lug nuts, as this can damage the wheel bearings.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure the correct torque specification for each bolt and nut.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What type of oil should I use in my Ram pickup truck?**
The recommended oil type for Ram pickup trucks is 5W-30 or 5W-20, depending on the engine type and climate.
2. **How often should I rotate my tires?**
Tires should be rotated every 5,000 to 8,000 miles, depending on the manufacturer's recommendations.
3. **What are the symptoms of worn-out brake pads?**
Symptoms of worn-out brake pads include squealing or grinding noises, vibration, and reduced braking performance.
4. **Can I replace the brake pads myself?**
Yes, you can replace the brake pads yourself, but it's recommended to have a professional mechanic do the job if you're not experienced with brake repairs.
5. **How often should I check my tire pressure?**
Tire pressure should be checked at least once a month, and before long trips, to ensure optimal tire performance and safety.
