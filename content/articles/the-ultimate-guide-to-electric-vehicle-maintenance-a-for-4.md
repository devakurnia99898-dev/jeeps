Electric vehicles like the Polestar 4 rely on advanced technology, including regenerative braking, electric motors, and high-voltage batteries. Regular maintenance is crucial to prevent issues such as reduced battery life, decreased performance, and increased risk of electrical faults. Common symptoms to look out for include unusual noises, decreased range, or warning lights on the dashboard.

## Technical Deep Dive
To understand how to maintain your Polestar 4, it's essential to know how its systems work. The electric motor, powered by a high-voltage battery, drives the vehicle. The battery management system (BMS) monitors and controls the battery's state of charge, voltage, and temperature. The regenerative braking system captures kinetic energy and converts it back into electrical energy, feeding it back into the battery.

Common failure points in electric vehicles include the battery cells, electric motor, and power electronics. The battery cells can degrade over time, reducing the vehicle's range and overall performance. The electric motor can experience wear and tear, leading to decreased efficiency and increased risk of failure. Power electronics, such as the inverter and converter, can also fail due to overheating, overvoltage, or manufacturing defects.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Checking and Maintaining the Battery
#### Step 1: Check the Battery State of Charge
Use the vehicle's dashboard display to check the battery's state of charge. Ensure the battery is at least 20% charged before starting any maintenance work.

#### Step 2: Check the Battery Voltage
Use a multimeter to check the battery voltage. The recommended voltage range for the Polestar 4 battery is between 300V and 400V.

#### Step 3: Check the Battery Temperature
Use a thermometer to check the battery temperature. The recommended temperature range for the Polestar 4 battery is between 15°C and 35°C.

### Rotating the Tires
#### Step 1: Loosen the Wheel Nuts
Before jacking up the vehicle, loosen the wheel nuts using a 10mm socket.

#### Step 2: Jack Up the Vehicle
Use a hydraulic jack to lift the vehicle, ensuring it's stable and secure.

#### Step 3: Remove the Wheel
Remove the wheel and rotate it to the recommended position, as specified in the vehicle's manual.

### Updating the Software
#### Step 1: Connect to the Internet
Connect your computer to the internet and navigate to the Polestar website.

#### Step 2: Download the Software Update
Download the latest software update for your Polestar 4, following the on-screen instructions.

#### Step 3: Install the Software Update
Install the software update, ensuring the vehicle is in park and the parking brake is applied.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When maintaining your Polestar 4, it's essential to avoid common mistakes, such as:
* Overcharging the battery, which can reduce its lifespan
* Ignoring software updates, which can lead to decreased performance and increased risk of faults
* Not checking the tire pressure regularly, which can reduce fuel efficiency and increase wear on the tires

Pro tips include:
* Regularly cleaning the vehicle's electrical components to prevent corrosion and damage
* Using a tire pressure gauge to ensure accurate readings
* Keeping the vehicle's software up-to-date to ensure optimal performance and safety
