## Technical Deep Dive
The crash testing process involves a series of evaluations designed to assess a vehicle's ability to withstand various types of collisions. These tests include front, side, and rear impact tests, as well as rollover tests. The vehicle's safety features, such as airbags, seatbelts, and crumple zones, are also evaluated.

Common failure points in a vehicle's safety system include:

* Worn-out brake pads or rotors
* Faulty airbag sensors or inflators
* Damaged or corroded frame components
* Inadequate seatbelt or seat mounting systems

Understanding how these systems work and identifying potential failure points is crucial in preparing your Jeep for a crash test.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Inspect Brake Pads and Rotors
#### Step 1.1: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake pads and rotors. Use a 10mm socket to loosen the lug nuts, then raise the vehicle using a jack and support it with jack stands.

#### Step 1.2: Inspect the Brake Pads
Use a brake pad wear gauge to measure the thickness of the brake pads. If the pads are worn out, replace them with new ones.

#### Step 1.3: Inspect the Rotors
Use a multimeter to check the rotor's surface for excessive wear or warping. If the rotors are damaged, replace them with new ones.

### Step 2: Check Airbag Sensors and Inflators
#### Step 2.1: Locate the Airbag Sensors
Locate the airbag sensors, usually found in the steering wheel, dashboard, or seatbelt mounts.

#### Step 2.2: Inspect the Sensors
Use a multimeter to check the sensor's electrical connections and ensure they are functioning correctly.

#### Step 2.3: Check the Inflators
Inspect the airbag inflators for signs of damage or wear. If the inflators are faulty, replace them with new ones.

### Step 3: Inspect Frame Components
#### Step 3.1: Locate the Frame Components
Locate the frame components, including the front and rear axles, suspension mounts, and body mounts.

#### Step 3.2: Inspect for Damage or Corrosion
Use a visual inspection to check for signs of damage or corrosion on the frame components. If any damage is found, repair or replace the affected components.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't neglect regular maintenance, as it can help identify potential safety issues before they become major problems.
* Use high-quality replacement parts to ensure optimal safety performance.
* Avoid over-tightening brake pads or rotors, as this can cause premature wear or damage.
