## Technical Deep Dive
A vehicle's suspension system is designed to absorb shocks from the road, maintain tire contact with the ground, and provide stability and control. It consists of several key components, including springs, shock absorbers, and struts. The type of suspension used can vary, with common configurations including coilovers, leaf springs, and independent suspension systems.

Common failure points in a suspension system include worn-out bushings, leaking shock absorbers, and broken springs. When upgrading, it's essential to consider the entire system, including the type of springs, shocks, and control arms, to ensure that all components work harmoniously together.

### Safety Warnings
When working with suspension systems, it's crucial to follow proper safety procedures to avoid injury. Always work on a level surface, use jack stands for support, and never get under a vehicle that's only supported by a jack. Wear protective gear, including gloves and safety glasses, and ensure the vehicle is securely braked and in neutral gear.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Planning and Preparation
Before starting the upgrade, research and plan your approach. Decide on the type of suspension system you want to install, considering factors such as your vehicle's make and model, your driving style, and the terrain you'll be driving on. Purchase a coilover suspension kit that's specifically designed for your vehicle, and gather all the necessary tools and materials.

#### Sub-Step 1.1: Raise the Vehicle
Raise the vehicle using a lift or jack stands to access the suspension components. Ensure the vehicle is securely supported and on level ground.

#### Sub-Step 1.2: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels to gain better access to the suspension components. This will also make it easier to work on the brakes and other related systems.

### Step 2: Disassemble the Old Suspension
Begin disassembling the old suspension system, starting with the removal of the shock absorbers and springs. Use a spring compressor to safely remove the springs, and a shock absorber spanner to remove the shocks.

#### Sub-Step 2.1: Remove the Control Arms
Remove the control arms, taking note of the bushing locations and the condition of the arms. Inspect the bushings for wear and damage, and replace them if necessary.

#### Sub-Step 2.2: Remove the Struts
Remove the struts, if applicable, and set them aside for inspection and potential replacement.

### Step 3: Install the New Suspension
Install the new coilover suspension kit, following the manufacturer's instructions. Start by installing the new springs and shocks, ensuring they're properly seated and aligned.

#### Sub-Step 3.1: Install the New Control Arms
Install the new control arms, if necessary, and ensure the bushings are properly seated and lubricated.

#### Sub-Step 3.2: Install the New Struts
Install the new struts, if applicable, and ensure they're properly aligned and secured.

### Step 4: Reassemble and Align
Reassemble the suspension system, ensuring all components are properly aligned and secured. Use a torque wrench to tighten all bolts and nuts to the specified torque specs.

#### Sub-Step 4.1: Align the Wheels
Align the wheels, ensuring proper camber, toe, and ride height. This may require the use of specialized tools and equipment.
