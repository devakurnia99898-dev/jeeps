### Safety Warnings
When working with high-voltage electrical systems, it is essential to take necessary safety precautions to avoid injury or death. Always:
* Wear protective gear, including gloves and safety glasses
* Ensure the vehicle is in a well-ventilated area
* Disconnect the high-voltage battery before starting work

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Tire Rotation and Inspection
#### Step 1.1: Jack up the vehicle and remove the wheels
Use a hydraulic jack to lift the vehicle, and remove the wheels using a lug wrench.
#### Step 1.2: Inspect the tires and rotate them if necessary
Inspect the tires for wear and damage, and rotate them to ensure even wear.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the calipers and rotors
Use a wrench to remove the calipers and rotors, taking care not to damage the brake lines.
#### Step 2.2: Inspect and replace the brake pads
Inspect the brake pads for wear, and replace them if necessary.

### Step 3: Electrical Component Inspection
#### Step 3.1: Disconnect the high-voltage battery
Use a multimeter to verify the battery is disconnected, and ensure the vehicle is in a safe state.
#### Step 3.2: Inspect the electrical components
Use high-voltage electrical test equipment to inspect the electrical components, including the electric motor and high-voltage battery.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always follow the manufacturer's instructions when working with high-voltage electrical systems.
* Use the correct tools and equipment to avoid damaging the vehicle's components.
* Never attempt to repair or replace high-voltage electrical components without proper training and experience.
