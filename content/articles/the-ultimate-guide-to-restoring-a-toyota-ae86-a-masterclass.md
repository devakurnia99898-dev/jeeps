## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The Toyota AE86 is a highly sought-after classic car, known for its rear-wheel drive layout and agile handling. However, restoring one can be a daunting task, especially for those without experience. The key to a successful restoration is to identify the areas that need attention, such as the engine, transmission, suspension, and bodywork. In this guide, we will walk you through the process of restoring a Toyota AE86, including technical prep, maintenance, and upgrades.

## Technical Deep Dive
The Toyota AE86 is powered by a 1.6L inline-4 engine, paired with a 5-speed manual transmission. The suspension consists of MacPherson struts in the front and a live axle in the rear. The AE86 is also equipped with a limited-slip differential, which helps to improve traction and handling. Common failure points on the AE86 include the engine's oil pump, the transmission's synchros, and the suspension's bushings.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Engine Rebuild
The engine rebuild is a critical part of the restoration process. It involves disassembling the engine, inspecting and replacing worn-out components, and reassembling the engine.
#### Disassembling the Engine
1. Remove the engine from the vehicle using an engine hoist.
2. Disconnect the battery and remove the spark plugs.
3. Remove the valve cover and timing belt.
4. Remove the cylinder head and pistons.
#### Inspecting and Replacing Components
1. Inspect the engine block and cylinder head for any damage or wear.
2. Replace the piston rings and bearings.
3. Replace the valve stem seals and guides.
#### Reassembling the Engine
1. Reinstall the cylinder head and pistons.
2. Reinstall the timing belt and valve cover.
3. Reconnect the spark plugs and battery.
### Transmission Rebuild
The transmission rebuild involves disassembling the transmission, inspecting and replacing worn-out components, and reassembling the transmission.
#### Disassembling the Transmission
1. Remove the transmission from the vehicle using a transmission jack.
2. Disconnect the shift linkage and remove the transmission pan.
3. Remove the gearset and bearings.
#### Inspecting and Replacing Components
1. Inspect the transmission case and gearset for any damage or wear.
2. Replace the synchros and bearings.
3. Replace the gear oil and filter.
#### Reassembling the Transmission
1. Reinstall the gearset and bearings.
2. Reinstall the transmission pan and shift linkage.
3. Reconnect the transmission to the engine.
### Suspension Overhaul
The suspension overhaul involves inspecting and replacing worn-out components, such as bushings and shocks.
#### Inspecting the Suspension
1. Inspect the suspension for any damage or wear.
2. Check the bushings and shocks for any signs of wear.
#### Replacing Components
1. Replace the bushings and shocks.
2. Replace the sway bar links and end links.
3. Replace the coil springs and strut mounts.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct torque specs when tightening bolts and nuts.
* Make sure to properly align the engine and transmission before reinstalling them.
* Don't forget to replace the engine and transmission mounts, as they can cause vibration and noise.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is the best way to inspect the engine and transmission for damage or wear?**
The best way to inspect the engine and transmission is to disassemble them and visually inspect the components. You can also use specialized tools, such as a bore scope, to inspect the engine block and cylinder head.
2. **How do I know if I need to replace the piston rings and bearings?**
You can inspect the piston rings and bearings for any signs of wear or damage. If they are worn out, you will need to replace them to prevent further damage to the engine.
3. **What is the best way to replace the valve stem seals and guides?**
The best way to replace the valve stem seals and guides is to use a valve stem seal installer and a guide installer. These tools will help you to properly align and install the new seals and guides.
4. **How do I know if I need to replace the synchros and bearings in the transmission?**
You can inspect the synchros and bearings for any signs of wear or damage. If they are worn out, you will need to replace them to prevent further damage to the transmission.
5. **What is the best way to replace the bushings and shocks in the suspension?**
The best way to replace the bushings and shocks is to use a bushing installer and a shock installer. These tools will help you to properly align and install the new bushings and shocks.
