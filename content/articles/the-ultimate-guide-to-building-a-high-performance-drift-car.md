## The Diagnostic / The Situation
When it comes to building a high-performance drift car, there are several key factors to consider. The first is the vehicle's power output, which must be sufficient to overcome the forces of gravity and friction. The second is the suspension system, which must be capable of withstanding the stresses of high-speed drifting. The third is the drivetrain, which must be able to transmit the power to the wheels efficiently.

Symptoms of a poorly built drift car can include a lack of traction, instability, and poor handling. In order to avoid these issues, it is essential to carefully plan and execute the build process.

## Technical Deep Dive
A high-performance drift car requires a number of specialized components, including a high-output engine, a performance-tuned suspension system, and a heavy-duty drivetrain. The engine must be capable of producing a significant amount of power, typically in the range of 500-1000 horsepower. The suspension system must be designed to handle the stresses of high-speed drifting, with components such as coilovers, sway bars, and camber kits.

The drivetrain must be capable of transmitting the power to the wheels efficiently, with components such as a performance-tuned transmission, a heavy-duty driveshaft, and high-performance axles.

Common failure points in a drift car build can include the engine, transmission, and drivetrain components. The engine can be prone to overheating, oil leaks, and blown head gaskets. The transmission can be prone to slipping, grinding, and failure. The drivetrain components can be prone to breakage, particularly the axles and driveshaft.
