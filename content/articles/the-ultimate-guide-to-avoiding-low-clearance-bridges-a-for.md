## Technical Deep Dive

The Jeep Wrangler, for example, has a ground clearance of around 10.8 inches, while the Jeep Gladiator has a ground clearance of approximately 11.1 inches. However, these measurements can vary depending on the trim level, tire size, and suspension modifications.

Common failure points to be aware of include:
* The roof of your Jeep, which can be vulnerable to damage from low-hanging structures
* The suspension and axles, which can be damaged if you attempt to drive under a low clearance bridge

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Determine Your Jeep's Clearance
To determine your Jeep's clearance, you'll need to measure the distance from the ground to the highest point on your vehicle. This can be done using a tape measure or height gauge.

#### Step 1.1: Measure the Distance from the Ground to the Highest Point
Use a tape measure or height gauge to measure the distance from the ground to the highest point on your Jeep. Take note of this measurement, as it will be used to determine whether you can safely navigate a low clearance bridge.
