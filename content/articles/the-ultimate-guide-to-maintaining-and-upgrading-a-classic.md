## Technical Deep Dive
The Pontiac Sunbird's engine is a 2.5L inline-four cylinder engine that uses a carburetor for fuel delivery. The engine has a cast iron block and aluminum cylinder head, with a compression ratio of 8.0:1. The engine is equipped with a timing chain and a hydraulic valve lifter system. The transmission is a four-speed manual with a non-synchromesh first gear and a synchronized second, third, and fourth gear.

The Pontiac Sunbird's suspension system consists of a coil spring and shock absorber at each corner, with a stabilizer bar at the front and rear. The braking system consists of disc brakes at the front and drum brakes at the rear. The electrical system is a 12-volt system with a alternator, voltage regulator, and a battery.

Common failure points in the Pontiac Sunbird include the carburetor, which can become clogged with debris and cause poor engine performance. The timing chain can also wear out over time, causing the engine to misfire and lose power. The brake system can also fail, causing the car to pull to one side or lose braking power.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Introduction to Maintenance
The key to keeping a classic car like the Pontiac Sunbird running smoothly is regular maintenance. This includes regular oil changes, brake pad replacements, and timing chain replacements.

#### Oil Change
1. Locate the oil drain plug and position a drain pan underneath.
2. Remove the oil drain plug and allow the oil to drain completely.
3. Replace the oil drain plug and fill the engine with new oil.
4. Check the oil level and filter to ensure they are at the recommended level.

#### Brake Pad Replacement
1. Locate the brake caliper and remove the caliper bolts.
2. Remove the caliper and rotor, and inspect the brake pads for wear.
3. Replace the brake pads and reassemble the caliper and rotor.
4. Bleed the brake system to remove any air.

#### Timing Chain Replacement
1. Locate the timing chain cover and remove the cover bolts.
2. Remove the timing chain cover and inspect the timing chain for wear.
3. Replace the timing chain and reassemble the timing chain cover.
4. Check the engine timing to ensure it is at the recommended setting.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct type of oil for your car, as using the wrong type can cause engine damage.
* Never over-tighten the oil drain plug, as this can cause the threads to strip.
* Always check the brake system for leaks after replacing the brake pads.
* Never attempt to replace the timing chain without consulting a repair manual.
