## Technical Deep Dive
The Miata's engine, suspension, and transmission are designed for on-road performance, but they can be modified to handle off-road terrain and increased power output. The engine can be upgraded with a turbocharger or supercharger to increase horsepower and torque. The suspension can be modified with lifted springs, shocks, and sway bars to improve ground clearance and stability. The transmission can be upgraded to a heavy-duty unit to handle the increased power and torque.

Common failure points in a modified Miata include the engine's cooling system, the transmission's gearing, and the suspension's components. The cooling system may need to be upgraded to handle the increased heat generated by the engine modifications. The transmission's gearing may need to be adjusted to handle the increased power and torque. The suspension's components may need to be reinforced to handle the increased stress and strain of off-road driving.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Engine Upgrade
The engine upgrade involves installing a turbocharger or supercharger to increase horsepower and torque. This requires modifying the engine's intake and exhaust systems, as well as upgrading the fuel system.

#### Step 1: Remove the Intake Manifold
Remove the intake manifold to access the engine's intake system. This requires disconnecting the throttle body, fuel injectors, and intake manifold sensors.

#### Step 2: Install the Turbocharger or Supercharger
Install the turbocharger or supercharger, making sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions. This requires connecting the turbocharger or supercharger to the engine's intake and exhaust systems.

#### Step 3: Upgrade the Fuel System
Upgrade the fuel system to handle the increased power and torque. This requires installing a high-flow fuel pump, fuel injectors, and fuel lines.

### Suspension Upgrade
The suspension upgrade involves installing lifted springs, shocks, and sway bars to improve ground clearance and stability. This requires modifying the suspension's components and adjusting the vehicle's alignment.

#### Step 1: Remove the Old Suspension Components
Remove the old suspension components, including the springs, shocks, and sway bars. This requires disconnecting the suspension's components from the vehicle's frame and axles.

#### Step 2: Install the New Suspension Components
Install the new suspension components, making sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions. This requires connecting the new suspension components to the vehicle's frame and axles.

#### Step 3: Adjust the Vehicle's Alignment
Adjust the vehicle's alignment to ensure proper tracking and stability. This requires using a alignment machine to adjust the vehicle's camber, toe, and ride height.

### Transmission Upgrade
The transmission upgrade involves installing a heavy-duty transmission to handle the increased power and torque. This requires modifying the transmission's gearing and torque converter.

#### Step 1: Remove the Old Transmission
Remove the old transmission, making sure to disconnect the transmission's components from the engine and drivetrain.

#### Step 2: Install the New Transmission
Install the new transmission, making sure to follow the manufacturer's instructions. This requires connecting the new transmission to the engine and drivetrain.

#### Step 3: Adjust the Transmission's Gearing
Adjust the transmission's gearing to handle the increased power and torque. This requires using a transmission tuning software to adjust the transmission's shift points and torque converter lockup.
