## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona is a highly sought-after classic American muscle car, known for its unique design and powerful engine. However, restoring one of these cars can be a daunting task, especially for those without experience. The process requires a deep understanding of the car's mechanics, as well as the ability to source rare and hard-to-find parts.

Symptoms or signs to look for when considering a restoration project include rust, worn-out engine components, and damaged or missing interior and exterior trim pieces. It's also important to research the car's history, including any previous owners and accidents, to ensure that the car is worth restoring.

## Technical Deep Dive
The 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona is powered by a 440 cubic-inch V8 engine, which produces 375 horsepower and 480 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a four-speed manual transmission and features a number of innovative technologies, including a hydraulic camshaft and a high-performance intake manifold.

Common failure points on the 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona include the engine's hydraulic camshaft, which can leak oil and cause damage to the engine's bearings and lifters. The car's suspension system is also prone to wear and tear, particularly the rear axle, which can cause the car to pull to one side.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Disassembly and Inspection
The first step in restoring a 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona is to disassemble the car and inspect its components. This includes removing the engine, transmission, and suspension, as well as the interior and exterior trim pieces.

#### Step 1.1: Remove the Engine and Transmission
To remove the engine and transmission, start by disconnecting the battery and draining the cooling system. Next, remove the engine mounts and transmission crossmember, and use a hoist to lift the engine and transmission out of the car.

#### Step 1.2: Inspect the Engine and Transmission
Once the engine and transmission are removed, inspect them for any signs of wear or damage. Check the engine's bearings and lifters for any signs of leakage, and inspect the transmission's gears and bearings for any signs of wear.

### Step 2: Engine Rebuild
If the engine is damaged or worn out, it may need to be rebuilt. This involves replacing the engine's bearings and lifters, as well as its pistons and rings.

#### Step 2.1: Replace the Engine's Bearings and Lifters
To replace the engine's bearings and lifters, start by removing the engine's cylinder head and crankshaft. Next, use a bearing puller to remove the old bearings, and install new ones in their place.

#### Step 2.2: Replace the Engine's Pistons and Rings
To replace the engine's pistons and rings, start by removing the engine's piston rods and connecting rods. Next, use a piston puller to remove the old pistons, and install new ones in their place.

### Step 3: Suspension and Steering
The 1969 Dodge Charger Daytona's suspension and steering system is also prone to wear and tear. To repair or replace these components, start by removing the car's wheels and suspension components.

#### Step 3.1: Replace the Rear Axle
To replace the rear axle, start by removing the car's rear wheels and suspension components. Next, use a hydraulic press to remove the old axle, and install a new one in its place.

#### Step 3.2: Replace the Steering Components
To replace the steering components, start by removing the car's steering wheel and column. Next, use a steering wheel puller to remove the old steering wheel, and install a new one in its place.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use a torque wrench when tightening bolts and nuts to avoid stripping threads.
* Make sure to use the correct type of lubricant for each component, such as engine oil for the engine and transmission fluid for the transmission.
* Don't forget to replace the car's brake pads and rotors, as these can wear out quickly.
