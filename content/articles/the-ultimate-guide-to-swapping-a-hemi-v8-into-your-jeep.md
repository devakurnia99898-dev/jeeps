## Technical Deep Dive
The Hemi V8 engine is a complex piece of machinery that requires careful consideration before attempting a swap. The engine itself is a 5.7L or 6.4L V8, depending on the application, and produces anywhere from 375 to 485 horsepower. The engine is paired with a variety of transmissions, including the 545RFE and 65RFE automatics, as well as the TREMEC TR-6060 manual.

Common failure points to look out for when swapping a Hemi V8 include the engine mounts, transmission crossmember, and driveshaft. These components must be upgraded to handle the increased power and torque of the Hemi engine.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Prepare the Vehicle
The first step in swapping a Hemi V8 into your Jeep is to prepare the vehicle. This includes removing the old engine, transmission, and driveshaft, as well as any other components that will be affected by the swap.

#### Step 1.1: Remove the Old Engine
To remove the old engine, start by disconnecting the battery and removing the air intake duct. Next, remove the engine mounts and transmission crossmember, and then pull the engine out of the vehicle using an engine hoist.

#### Step 1.2: Remove the Old Transmission
To remove the old transmission, start by disconnecting the transmission cooler lines and removing the transmission crossmember. Next, remove the transmission mounts and pull the transmission out of the vehicle using a transmission jack.

#### Step 1.3: Remove the Old Driveshaft
To remove the old driveshaft, start by disconnecting the driveshaft from the transmission and then pulling it out of the vehicle.

### Step 2: Install the New Engine
The next step is to install the new Hemi V8 engine. This includes installing the engine mounts, transmission crossmember, and driveshaft, as well as any other components that are required for the swap.

#### Step 2.1: Install the Engine Mounts
To install the engine mounts, start by bolting them to the engine block and then attaching them to the vehicle's frame.

#### Step 2.2: Install the Transmission Crossmember
To install the transmission crossmember, start by bolting it to the vehicle's frame and then attaching it to the transmission.

#### Step 2.3: Install the Driveshaft
To install the driveshaft, start by attaching it to the transmission and then connecting it to the axle.

### Step 3: Install the New Transmission
The final step is to install the new transmission. This includes installing the transmission mounts, transmission cooler lines, and any other components that are required for the swap.

#### Step 3.1: Install the Transmission Mounts
To install the transmission mounts, start by bolting them to the transmission and then attaching them to the vehicle's frame.

#### Step 3.2: Install the Transmission Cooler Lines
To install the transmission cooler lines, start by connecting them to the transmission and then routing them to the transmission cooler.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use a torque wrench when tightening bolts and fasteners to avoid stripping threads.
* Make sure to properly align the driveshaft to avoid vibration and damage to the vehicle.
* Don't forget to upgrade the engine mounts, transmission crossmember, and driveshaft to handle the increased power and torque of the Hemi engine.
