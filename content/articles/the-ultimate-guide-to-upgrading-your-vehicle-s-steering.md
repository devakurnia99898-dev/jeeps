## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The touch-capacitive steering wheel controls have become a standard feature in many modern vehicles, including the Ferrari Purosangue and the 12Cilindri. However, these controls can be prone to malfunctions and may not provide the same level of tactile feedback as traditional buttons. This can lead to driver distraction and decreased safety on the road. In this guide, we will explore the process of replacing touch-capacitive steering wheel controls with real buttons, providing a more intuitive and safe driving experience.

## Technical Deep Dive
The touch-capacitive steering wheel controls use a capacitive sensing technology to detect the presence of a finger or other conductive object. This technology relies on the principles of capacitance and inductance to detect changes in the electrical field around the control surface. However, this technology can be affected by various factors such as humidity, temperature, and electrical interference, leading to malfunctions and decreased reliability.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Remove the Steering Wheel
#### Step 1.1: Disconnect the Battery
Locate the battery and disconnect the negative terminal to prevent any accidental start-ups or electrical shocks.

#### Step 1.2: Remove the Steering Wheel Trim
Use a 10mm socket to remove the screws holding the steering wheel trim in place. Gently pull the trim away from the steering wheel to access the electrical connections.

#### Step 1.3: Disconnect the Electrical Connections
Use wire strippers to disconnect the electrical connections to the touch-capacitive steering wheel controls. Take note of the wiring diagram to ensure correct reconnection later.

#### Step 1.4: Remove the Steering Wheel
Use a puller to remove the steering wheel from the steering column. Take care not to damage the electrical connections or the steering column.

### Step 2: Install the New Button Switches
#### Step 2.1: Mount the Button Switches
Mount the new button switches to the steering wheel using the provided screws or adhesive. Ensure the switches are securely fastened and aligned with the original control layout.

#### Step 2.2: Connect the Electrical Connections
Use the wiring diagram to connect the electrical connections to the new button switches. Ensure all connections are secure and not loose.

#### Step 2.3: Test the Button Switches
Test the button switches to ensure they are functioning correctly and providing the expected tactile feedback.

### Step 3: Reinstall the Steering Wheel
#### Step 3.1: Reconnect the Electrical Connections
Reconnect the electrical connections to the steering wheel controls, ensuring all connections are secure and not loose.

#### Step 3.2: Reinstall the Steering Wheel Trim
Reinstall the steering wheel trim, ensuring all screws are securely fastened.

#### Step 3.3: Reconnect the Battery
Reconnect the battery and test the steering wheel controls to ensure they are functioning correctly.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Use a wiring diagram to ensure correct electrical connections
* Test the button switches before reinstalling the steering wheel to ensure correct function
* Avoid over-tightening the screws or bolts, which can damage the steering wheel or electrical components
* Use protective gloves and safety glasses when working with electrical components to prevent damage or injury
