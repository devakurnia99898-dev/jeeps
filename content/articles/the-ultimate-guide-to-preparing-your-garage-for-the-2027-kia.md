## Technical Deep Dive
The Kia Niro Hybrid's powertrain is a complex system, comprising multiple components that work together to provide optimal performance and efficiency. The system includes:

* A 1.6L gasoline engine, which provides the primary source of power
* An electric motor, which assists the engine and provides additional torque
* A lithium-ion battery pack, which stores energy captured during regenerative braking and deceleration
* A power control unit (PCU), which manages the flow of energy between the engine, electric motor, and battery pack

Common failure points in the Kia Niro Hybrid's powertrain include the battery pack, electric motor, and PCU. The battery pack is susceptible to degradation over time, which can reduce its overall capacity and affect the vehicle's performance. The electric motor can also experience wear and tear, particularly if it is subjected to excessive heat or stress. The PCU is a critical component, as it manages the complex interactions between the engine, electric motor, and battery pack.
