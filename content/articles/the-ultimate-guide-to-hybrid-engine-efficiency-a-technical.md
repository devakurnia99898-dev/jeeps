## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Prepare the Vehicle
#### Step 1.1: Lift the Vehicle
Lift the vehicle using a hydraulic lift or jack stands to access the engine compartment. Ensure the vehicle is secure and on level ground.

#### Step 1.2: Disconnect the Battery
Disconnect the battery to prevent any accidental starts or electrical shocks. Remove the negative cable first, followed by the positive cable.

### Step 2: Remove the Engine Cover
#### Step 2.1: Remove the Engine Cover Bolts
Remove the engine cover bolts using a 10mm socket. Be careful not to strip the threads.

#### Step 2.2: Remove the Engine Cover
Gently pull the engine cover away from the engine. Take care not to damage any surrounding components.

### Step 3: Inspect the Engine
#### Step 3.1: Inspect the Engine Components
Inspect the engine components, including the cylinder head, piston, and valves. Look for any signs of wear or damage.

#### Step 3.2: Check the Engine Oil
Check the engine oil level and condition. Ensure the oil is at the recommended level and is in good condition.

### Step 4: Perform Maintenance Tasks
#### Step 4.1: Replace the Air Filter
Replace the air filter with a new one. Ensure the new filter is properly seated and secured.

#### Step 4.2: Replace the Spark Plugs
Replace the spark plugs with new ones. Ensure the new spark plugs are properly gapped and secured.

### Step 5: Reassemble the Engine
#### Step 5.1: Reinstall the Engine Cover
Reinstall the engine cover, ensuring it is properly secured with the engine cover bolts.

#### Step 5.2: Reconnect the Battery
Reconnect the battery, ensuring the positive cable is connected first, followed by the negative cable.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When working with hybrid engines, it's essential to avoid common mistakes that can lead to decreased performance, fuel economy, and increased emissions. 

* Don't over-tighten the engine cover bolts, as this can damage the threads.
* Don't use the wrong type of engine oil, as this can damage the engine components.
* Don't neglect regular maintenance tasks, such as replacing the air filter and spark plugs.
