These issues can cause problems such as difficulty shifting gears, grinding noises, and premature wear on the transmission components.

## Technical Deep Dive
The manual transmission in the Juke Nismo RS uses a combination of hydraulic and mechanical components to engage and disengage the clutch and shift gears. The transmission consists of a clutch pack, synchronizers, gear sets, and a final drive. The clutch pack is responsible for engaging and disengaging the engine from the transmission, while the synchronizers ensure smooth shifting between gears.

Common failure points in the manual transmission include the clutch pack, synchronizers, and gear teeth. The clutch pack can wear out over time, causing the clutch to slip or fail to engage properly. The synchronizers can also wear out, causing grinding noises and difficulty shifting gears. The gear teeth can become damaged, causing premature wear on the transmission components.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Inspect the Transmission
Inspect the transmission for any signs of damage or wear. Check the clutch pack, synchronizers, and gear teeth for any signs of wear or damage.

#### Step 1.1: Remove the Transmission Pan
Remove the transmission pan to inspect the clutch pack and synchronizers. Use a 10mm socket to remove the bolts and a torque wrench to loosen the pan.

#### Step 1.2: Inspect the Clutch Pack
Inspect the clutch pack for any signs of wear or damage. Check the clutch discs and pressure plate for any signs of wear or damage.

### Step 2: Replace the Clutch Pack
Replace the clutch pack if it is worn out or damaged. Use a clutch pack replacement kit and follow the manufacturer's instructions.

#### Step 2.1: Remove the Old Clutch Pack
Remove the old clutch pack and inspect the transmission case for any signs of damage or wear.

#### Step 2.2: Install the New Clutch Pack
Install the new clutch pack and ensure it is properly seated and aligned.

### Step 3: Replace the Synchronizers
Replace the synchronizers if they are worn out or damaged. Use a synchronizer replacement kit and follow the manufacturer's instructions.

#### Step 3.1: Remove the Old Synchronizers
Remove the old synchronizers and inspect the transmission case for any signs of damage or wear.

#### Step 3.2: Install the New Synchronizers
Install the new synchronizers and ensure they are properly seated and aligned.
