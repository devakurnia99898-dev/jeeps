## Technical Deep Dive
The system we'll be focusing on is the overall vehicle maintenance and safety features. A well-maintained vehicle is less likely to experience mechanical failures that can lead to accidents. Common failure points in vehicles include worn-out brake pads, faulty suspension components, and malfunctioning electrical systems.

The electrical system, in particular, plays a critical role in ensuring the safe operation of your vehicle. A faulty alternator, for example, can cause your battery to drain, leading to a breakdown. Similarly, a malfunctioning ABS system can increase stopping distances, making it more challenging to avoid accidents.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Regular Maintenance Checks
#### Step 1.1: Oil Change
Check your owner's manual for the recommended oil change interval, typically between 5,000 to 7,500 miles. Use a socket to drain the oil, and replace the filter with a new one.

#### Step 1.2: Tire Pressure Check
Check your tire pressure at least once a month, and before long trips. Use a tire pressure gauge to ensure the pressure matches the recommended level, as specified in your owner's manual.

### Step 2: Safety Features Inspection
#### Step 2.1: Airbag Inspection
Check your airbag system for any warning lights on your dashboard. If a light is illuminated, have your vehicle inspected by a professional mechanic.

#### Step 2.2: ABS System Check
Check your ABS system by looking for any warning lights on your dashboard. If a light is illuminated, have your vehicle inspected by a professional mechanic.

### Step 3: Driver Safety Tips
#### Step 3.1: Avoid Distractions
Avoid using your phone or other electronic devices while driving. Keep your eyes on the road and your hands on the wheel at all times.

#### Step 3.2: Maintain a Safe Following Distance
Leave a safe following distance between you and the vehicle in front of you. This will give you time to react to any unexpected situations.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
Don't neglect regular maintenance checks, as this can lead to mechanical failures and increase the risk of accidents. Additionally, avoid using your phone or other electronic devices while driving, as this can significantly increase your reaction time.

Tricks of the trade include keeping a maintenance log to track your vehicle's maintenance history, and using a torque wrench to ensure proper tightening of bolts and nuts.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
The cost of maintaining your vehicle can vary significantly depending on whether you choose to DIY or take it to a mechanic. A typical oil change, for example, can cost between $25 to $75 if you DIY, compared to $50 to $150 if you take it to a mechanic.

However, some tasks, such as brake pad replacement, may require specialized tools and expertise, making it more cost-effective to take it to a mechanic.
