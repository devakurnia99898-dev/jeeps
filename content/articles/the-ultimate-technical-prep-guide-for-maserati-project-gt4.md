## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Warm up the engine
Drive the vehicle for a few minutes to warm up the engine and circulate the oil.

#### Step 1.2: Locate the oil drain plug
Find the oil drain plug, usually located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the car.

#### Step 1.3: Remove the oil drain plug
Use a 10mm socket to remove the oil drain plug, being prepared for the oil to start draining out.

#### Step 1.4: Replace the oil filter
Remove the old oil filter and install a new one, making sure not to overtighten.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheels
Take off the wheels to access the brake calipers and pads.

#### Step 2.2: Remove the caliper
Use a 12mm socket to remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.

#### Step 2.3: Remove the old brake pads
Pull out the old brake pads and discard them.

#### Step 2.4: Install the new brake pads
Insert the new brake pads, making sure they are properly seated and aligned.

### Step 3: Suspension Alignment
#### Step 3.1: Raise the vehicle
Lift the vehicle using a hydraulic lift or jack stands, making sure it is securely supported.

#### Step 3.2: Remove the wheels
Take off the wheels to access the suspension components.

#### Step 3.3: Adjust the camber
Use a suspension alignment tool to adjust the camber, ensuring it is within the manufacturer's specifications.

#### Step 3.4: Adjust the toe
Use the suspension alignment tool to adjust the toe, ensuring it is within the manufacturer's specifications.
