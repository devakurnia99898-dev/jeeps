## Technical Deep Dive
The M120 Mercedes-Benz V12 engine is a 5.8-liter powerplant, known for its smooth operation and substantial torque output. This engine features a 90-degree V12 configuration, with dual overhead camshafts and 24 valves. The engine's compact size, despite its V12 configuration, makes it an attractive choice for engine swaps, as it can fit into engine bays designed for smaller engines.

Common failure points in V12 engine swaps include inadequate cooling systems, insufficient engine mounts, and poorly designed wiring harnesses. The plastic impeller in the water pump can also be a weak point, especially when the engine is subjected to high-performance driving. Additionally, the engine's electronic control unit (ECU) must be properly calibrated to ensure smooth operation and optimal performance.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation and Planning
The first step in a successful V12 engine swap is thorough planning and preparation. This includes researching the donor engine, assessing the recipient vehicle, and creating a detailed project plan.

#### Research and Assessment
* Research the M120 Mercedes-Benz V12 engine, including its specifications, common failure points, and required maintenance.
* Assess the condition of your Datsun 240Z, ensuring that the chassis, suspension, and brakes can handle the additional power and stress.

#### Project Planning
* Create a detailed project plan, including timelines, budgets, and required tools and materials.
* Identify potential challenges and develop contingency plans to address them.

### Engine Removal and Preparation
The next step is to remove the original engine from the Datsun 240Z and prepare the engine bay for the V12 engine swap.

#### Engine Removal
* Disconnect the battery and remove the negative cable to prevent any accidental starts.
* Drain the cooling system and remove the radiator.
* Disconnect the wiring harness and remove the engine mounts.

#### Engine Bay Preparation
* Clean and inspect the engine bay, ensuring that all surfaces are free from debris and corrosion.
* Install custom engine mounts, designed to accommodate the V12 engine.
* Modify the wiring harness to connect with the V12 engine's ECU.

### V12 Engine Installation
With the engine bay prepared, the next step is to install the M120 Mercedes-Benz V12 engine.

#### Engine Installation
* Carefully lower the V12 engine into the engine bay, ensuring that it is properly aligned with the custom engine mounts.
* Connect the wiring harness to the ECU, ensuring that all connections are secure and insulated.
* Install the high-performance cooling system components, including a custom radiator and cooling fans.

#### Transmission and Drivetrain Modifications
* Modify the transmission to accommodate the V12 engine's power output, potentially requiring a high-performance clutch and gearset.
* Upgrade the drivetrain components, including the driveshaft and axles, to handle the increased stress and power.

### ECU Tuning and Calibration
The final step is to tune and calibrate the V12 engine's ECU, ensuring that it operates smoothly and efficiently.

#### ECU Tuning
* Use specialized software to tune the ECU, adjusting parameters such as fuel injection, ignition timing, and boost pressure.
* Monitor the engine's performance, making adjustments as necessary to optimize power output and efficiency.

#### Calibration and Testing
* Calibrate the engine's systems, including the cooling and fuel systems, to ensure proper operation.
* Test the engine, monitoring its performance and making any necessary adjustments to ensure smooth and efficient operation.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When embarking on a V12 engine swap, it's essential to avoid common mistakes and follow proven techniques.

* Use high-quality components, including a high-performance cooling system and custom drivetrain components.
