## Technical Deep Dive
To appreciate the complexity of a diesel swap, it's crucial to understand how a diesel engine works and how it differs from a gasoline engine. A diesel engine uses compression ignition, where the fuel is injected into the cylinder and ignited by the heat generated during compression. This process requires a higher compression ratio, which is achieved through the use of a different cylinder head design, pistons, and connecting rods.

Common failure points in a diesel engine include the fuel injectors, fuel pump, and turbocharger. The fuel injectors are responsible for delivering the precise amount of fuel into the cylinder, while the fuel pump provides the necessary pressure to operate the injectors. The turbocharger, on the other hand, compresses the air entering the engine, allowing for a significant increase in power and efficiency.

In the context of a Jeep, a diesel swap typically involves replacing the existing gasoline engine with a diesel engine, such as the Cummins 4BT or the VM Motori 2.8L. This requires modifications to the engine mounts, transmission, and fuel system, as well as upgrades to the cooling and exhaust systems.

### Tools Required
To complete a diesel swap, you'll need the following tools:
* Engine hoist
* Transmission jack
* Fuel system components (fuel tank, fuel lines, fuel injectors)
* Engine mounts and adapters
* Cooling system components (radiator, hoses, water pump)
* Exhaust system components (exhaust manifold, turbocharger, exhaust pipes)
* Wiring and electrical components (wiring harness, sensors, relays)

### Safety Warnings
When working with diesel engines, it's essential to take necessary safety precautions to avoid injury or damage. Some critical safety warnings include:
* Always wear protective gear, such as gloves and safety glasses, when working with power tools and electrical components.
* Ensure the vehicle is securely supported on jack stands and the engine is properly disconnected from the transmission and fuel system.
* Never smoke or use open flames near diesel fuel or vapors.
