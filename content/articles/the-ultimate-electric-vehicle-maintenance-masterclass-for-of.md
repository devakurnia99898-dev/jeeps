# The Diagnostic / The Situation
As the world shifts towards electric vehicles, it's essential to stay ahead of the curve when it comes to maintenance. With the delay of the Volkswagen Golf EV, it's clear that the automotive industry is still navigating the transition to electric. However, this doesn't mean that current EV owners should wait to learn about maintaining their vehicles. In this guide, we'll cover the technical background of electric vehicles, symptoms to look for, and how to prepare for the future of EVs.

The technical background of electric vehicles is rooted in their unique powertrain and battery systems. Unlike traditional internal combustion engines, electric vehicles rely on electric motors and batteries to propel the vehicle. This means that maintenance needs to focus on the health of the battery, the condition of the electric motor, and the overall electrical system.

Symptoms to look for in electric vehicles include decreased range, slower charging times, and unusual noises from the electric motor. These symptoms can indicate a range of issues, from battery degradation to electrical system faults.

## Technical Deep Dive
The system that powers an electric vehicle is complex and multifaceted. At its core is the battery pack, which stores the energy that powers the vehicle. The battery pack is made up of individual cells, which are grouped together to form modules. These modules are then connected to form the overall battery pack.

The electric motor is responsible for converting the energy stored in the battery pack into motion. This is done through a series of complex electrical and mechanical systems, including the inverter, the motor controller, and the gearbox.

Common failure points in electric vehicles include the battery management system, the electric motor, and the charging system. The battery management system is responsible for monitoring the health of the battery pack and ensuring that it operates within safe parameters. The electric motor is susceptible to overheating and electrical faults, while the charging system can be prone to issues with the charging port and the onboard charger.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Battery Maintenance
#### Step 1.1: Check the Battery State of Charge
Check the battery state of charge to ensure that it's at a safe level for maintenance. This can be done using a multimeter or a battery tester.

#### Step 1.2: Inspect the Battery Pack
Inspect the battery pack for signs of damage or wear. This includes checking for cracks, dents, or corrosion on the battery modules.

#### Step 1.3: Clean the Battery Pack
Clean the battery pack to ensure that it's free from debris and corrosion. This can be done using a soft brush and a mild cleaning solution.

### Step 2: Electric Motor Inspection
#### Step 2.1: Check the Motor Temperature
Check the motor temperature to ensure that it's within safe parameters. This can be done using a thermometer or a multimeter.

#### Step 2.2: Inspect the Motor Windings
Inspect the motor windings for signs of damage or wear. This includes checking for cracks, dents, or corrosion on the windings.

#### Step 2.3: Check the Motor Bearings
Check the motor bearings for signs of wear or damage. This includes checking for unusual noises or vibrations from the motor.

### Step 3: Charging System Check
#### Step 3.1: Check the Charging Port
Check the charging port for signs of damage or wear. This includes checking for cracks, dents, or corrosion on the port.

#### Step 3.2: Inspect the Onboard Charger
Inspect the onboard charger for signs of damage or wear. This includes checking for unusual noises or vibrations from the charger.

#### Step 3.3: Check the Charging Cable
Check the charging cable for signs of damage or wear. This includes checking for cracks, dents, or corrosion on the cable.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
Don't attempt to repair or replace the battery pack or electric motor without proper training and equipment. This can be dangerous and can cause further damage to the vehicle.

Tricks of the trade include using a multimeter to check the battery state of charge and inspecting the motor windings for signs of damage or wear.
