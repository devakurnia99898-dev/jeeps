## Technical Deep Dive
The Porsche 935 is a highly specialized racing vehicle, with a number of unique systems and components designed to optimize its performance on the track. The engine, for example, is a 3.0-liter flat-six cylinder unit, producing over 700 horsepower. The transmission is a 4-speed manual unit, with a number of available gear ratios to suit different racing applications.

Common failure points on the Porsche 935 include the engine's oil pump, which can fail due to excessive heat and stress, and the gearbox, which can be prone to wear and tear on the synchros and bearings. The brakes are also a critical system, with the ability to withstand high temperatures and repeated heavy use.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Engine Preparation
The engine is the heart of the Porsche 935, and proper preparation is essential for optimal performance. This includes:
#### 1.1 Oil change and filter replacement
Use a high-quality synthetic oil and replace the filter with a new one.
#### 1.2 Spark plug replacement
Use platinum-tipped spark plugs to ensure reliable ignition and optimal engine performance.
#### 1.3 Fuel system inspection
Check the fuel injectors, fuel pump, and fuel lines for any signs of wear or damage.

### Step 2: Transmission and Drivetrain Preparation
The transmission and drivetrain are critical systems that require careful preparation for endurance racing.
#### 2.1 Gearbox inspection
Check the gearbox for any signs of wear or damage, including the synchros and bearings.
#### 2.2 Differential inspection
Check the differential for any signs of wear or damage, including the bearings and seals.
#### 2.3 Driveshaft inspection
Check the driveshaft for any signs of wear or damage, including the universal joints and center bearing.

### Step 3: Brake System Preparation
The brake system is a critical safety component that requires careful preparation for endurance racing.
#### 3.1 Brake pad replacement
Use high-performance brake pads designed for endurance racing.
#### 3.2 Brake fluid change
Use a high-quality brake fluid designed for high-temperature applications.
#### 3.3 Brake bleeding
Bleed the brake system to ensure proper function and prevent air from entering the lines.

### Step 4: Suspension and Alignment
The suspension and alignment are critical systems that require careful preparation for endurance racing.
#### 4.1 Suspension inspection
Check the suspension for any signs of wear or damage, including the shocks, springs, and bushings.
#### 4.2 Alignment check
Check the alignment of the vehicle to ensure proper handling and tire wear.
