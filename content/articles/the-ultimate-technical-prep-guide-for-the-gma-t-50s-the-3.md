## Technical Deep Dive
The GMA T.50s features a 4.0-liter, naturally aspirated V12 engine, producing 725 horsepower and 541 lb-ft of torque. The engine is mated to a 6-speed manual transmission, with a rear-wheel-drive layout and a limited-slip differential. The T.50s also boasts advanced aerodynamics, including a rear spoiler and diffuser, generating significant downforce.

Common failure points to watch out for include:

* The engine's valve train and timing system, which require regular adjustment and maintenance
* The transmission's synchros and gearsets, which can wear out due to aggressive driving
* The suspension's bushings and dampers, which can degrade over time, affecting handling and stability

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation and Inspection
#### Step 1: Visual Inspection
Inspect the T.50s for any signs of damage or wear, including scratches, dents, or broken components. Check the tire pressure and tread depth, and ensure the wheels are properly torqued.

#### Step 2: Fluid Check
Check the engine oil, transmission fluid, and brake fluid levels, and top them off as necessary. Use a multimeter to test the battery voltage and ensure the electrical system is functioning correctly.

### Maintenance and Repairs
#### Step 3: Oil Change
Drain the engine oil and replace the filter, using a high-quality synthetic oil and a new filter. Torque the oil drain plug to 30 Nm.

#### Step 4: Brake Pad Replacement
Remove the wheels and calipers, and inspect the brake pads for wear. Replace the pads with new ones, and torque the caliper bolts to 40 Nm.

#### Step 5: Suspension Alignment
Raise the T.50s on a lift and remove the wheels, then adjust the suspension's camber, toe, and ride height to the manufacturer's specifications. Use a torque wrench to tighten the suspension bolts to 60 Nm.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't over-tighten the engine's valve train, as this can cause damage to the valves and cylinder head
* Use a high-quality synthetic oil to ensure optimal engine performance and longevity
* Avoid aggressive driving, as this can cause excessive wear on the transmission and suspension

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
The cost of maintaining and repairing the GMA T.50s can be significant, with DIY tasks such as oil changes and brake pad replacement saving owners around $1,000 to $2,000 per year.
