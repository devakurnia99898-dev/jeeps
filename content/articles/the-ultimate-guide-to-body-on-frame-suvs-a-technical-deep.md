## Technical Deep Dive
The body-on-frame design uses a separate frame and body, which provides a number of benefits, including improved durability and towing capacity. The frame is typically made of steel or aluminum and is designed to absorb and distribute the stresses and loads placed on the vehicle. The body, on the other hand, is attached to the frame using a variety of methods, including bolts, welds, and adhesives.

Common failure points in body-on-frame SUVs include the frame itself, which can be prone to rust and corrosion, as well as the suspension and steering components, which can wear out over time. The engine and transmission are also critical components that require regular maintenance to ensure optimal performance.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspecting the Frame
The frame is a critical component of the body-on-frame SUV, and regular inspections are necessary to ensure it remains in good condition. Start by visually inspecting the frame for any signs of rust or corrosion, paying particular attention to areas that are prone to moisture accumulation.

#### Checking for Rust and Corrosion
Use a wire brush or sandpaper to clean any rusty or corroded areas, and apply a rust-inhibiting coating to protect the metal.

#### Inspecting the Suspension and Steering
The suspension and steering components are also critical to the vehicle's performance and safety. Inspect the shocks, struts, and springs for any signs of wear or damage, and replace them as necessary.

#### Replacing the Suspension and Steering Components
Use a torque wrench to tighten the suspension and steering components to the specified torque, and ensure all bolts and nuts are properly secured.

### Maintaining the Engine and Transmission
The engine and transmission are critical components that require regular maintenance to ensure optimal performance. Check the oil and fluid levels regularly, and change them as recommended by the manufacturer.

#### Checking the Oil and Fluid Levels
Use a dipstick to check the oil level, and top it off as necessary. Check the transmission fluid level by locating the dipstick and adding fluid as needed.

#### Replacing the Engine and Transmission Filters
Use a filter wrench to remove the old filter, and discard it properly. Install a new filter, and hand tighten it to ensure it is properly secured.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always follow the manufacturer's recommended maintenance schedule to ensure the vehicle remains in good condition.
* Never neglect to check the oil and fluid levels, as this can lead to premature wear and damage to the engine and transmission.
* Use the correct tools and equipment when working on the vehicle, and never use makeshift or improvised tools.
