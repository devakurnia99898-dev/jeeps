## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Prepare the Garage and Tools
#### Step 1.1: Ensure a Safe Working Environment
Clear the garage of any flammable materials and ensure good ventilation. Designate a specific area for working with high-voltage electrical systems.

#### Step 1.2: Gather Required Tools and Equipment
Collect the necessary tools and equipment, including high-voltage electrical test equipment, battery management system diagnostic tools, and electric motor inspection cameras.

### Step 2: Inspect the Electric Motor and Battery System
#### Step 2.1: Visual Inspection of the Electric Motor
Use an electric motor inspection camera to visually inspect the motor for signs of wear or damage. Check for any unusual noises or vibrations.

#### Step 2.2: Battery Management System Diagnostic Check
Use a battery management system diagnostic tool to check the battery's state of charge, voltage, and temperature. Look for any error codes or warning messages.

### Step 3: Perform High-Voltage Electrical System Check
#### Step 3.1: Disable the High-Voltage System
Follow the manufacturer's instructions to disable the high-voltage system. Ensure the vehicle is properly grounded and the high-voltage system is isolated.

#### Step 3.2: Inspect High-Voltage Electrical Connectors
Use high-voltage electrical test equipment to inspect the high-voltage electrical connectors for signs of wear or damage. Check for any corrosion or moisture.
