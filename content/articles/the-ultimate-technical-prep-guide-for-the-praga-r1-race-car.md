Common failure points in the Praga R1 include the engine's timing chain, the gearbox's synchros, and the brake system's pads and rotors.

## **Step-by-Step Guide**
### **Step 1: Oil Change**
#### **Step 1.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug**
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the car. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.
#### **Step 1.2: Replace the Oil Filter**
The oil filter is located on the top of the engine, near the oil filler cap. Use a filter wrench to remove the old filter and install a new one.
#### **Step 1.3: Refill the Oil**
Refill the oil with the recommended synthetic oil, making sure not to overfill.
### **Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement**
#### **Step 2.1: Remove the Wheels**
Remove the wheels to access the brake calipers and pads.
#### **Step 2.2: Remove the Caliper**
Use a 12mm socket to remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.
#### **Step 2.3: Remove the Old Pads**
Remove the old brake pads and inspect the rotors for wear.
#### **Step 2.4: Install the New Pads**
Install the new brake pads, making sure they are properly seated and the caliper is securely bolted back in place.
### **Step 3: Gearbox Inspection**
#### **Step 3.1: Remove the Gearbox Cover**
Remove the gearbox cover to access the internals.
#### **Step 3.2: Inspect the Synchros**
Inspect the synchros for wear and damage, replacing them if necessary.
#### **Step 3.3: Inspect the Bearings**
Inspect the bearings for wear and damage, replacing them if necessary.

## **Pro Tips & Common Mistakes**
* Always use the recommended synthetic oil to ensure optimal engine performance and longevity.
* Avoid over-tightening the brake caliper bolts, as this can damage the caliper and rotor.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure proper bolt torque, as over-torquing can damage the engine and gearbox.
