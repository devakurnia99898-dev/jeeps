## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The XJ Cherokee is a classic Jeep model that has gained a cult following over the years. With its rugged design and capable off-road performance, it's no wonder why many enthusiasts want to upgrade and modify their XJ Cherokees to make them even more capable and comfortable. However, when it comes to restomoding an XJ Cherokee, it's essential to understand the technical background and symptoms to look out for. A restomod is a subtle upgrade that aims to improve the vehicle's performance, comfort, and overall driving experience without compromising its original character.

## Technical Deep Dive
The XJ Cherokee's engine, suspension, and electrical systems are all areas that can be improved upon with a restomod. The engine, for example, can be upgraded with a performance camshaft, headers, and a cold air intake to increase power and torque. The suspension can be improved with coilovers, sway bars, and bushings to enhance handling and stability. The electrical system can be upgraded with a modern engine management system, wiring harness, and accessories to improve reliability and functionality.

Common failure points to look out for when restomoding an XJ Cherokee include the plastic impeller in the water pump, the rear main seal, and the ball joints. These components can be prone to failure, especially if the vehicle is used for off-road driving or towing. By addressing these potential issues, you can ensure that your restomod is both reliable and durable.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Engine Upgrade
The engine upgrade is a critical component of the restomod process. To upgrade the engine, follow these steps:

#### Step 1: Remove the Intake Manifold
Remove the intake manifold to access the engine's internal components. This will require a 10mm socket and a torque wrench.

#### Step 2: Install the Performance Camshaft
Install the performance camshaft, which will increase power and torque. This will require a camshaft installer and a torque wrench.

#### Step 3: Install the Headers and Cold Air Intake
Install the headers and cold air intake, which will improve engine breathing and performance. This will require a welder and a torque wrench.

### Suspension Upgrade
The suspension upgrade is another critical component of the restomod process. To upgrade the suspension, follow these steps:

#### Step 1: Remove the Coil Springs
Remove the coil springs to access the suspension's internal components. This will require a coil spring compressor and a torque wrench.

#### Step 2: Install the Coilovers
Install the coilovers, which will improve handling and stability. This will require a coilover installer and a torque wrench.

#### Step 3: Install the Sway Bars and Bushings
Install the sway bars and bushings, which will enhance handling and stability. This will require a sway bar installer and a torque wrench.

### Electrical Upgrade

#### Step 2: Install the New Wiring Harness

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When restomoding an XJ Cherokee, it's essential to avoid common mistakes that can compromise the vehicle's performance and reliability. Here are some pro tips and common mistakes to avoid:

* Don't over-tighten the suspension components, as this can cause damage to the vehicle's frame and suspension.
* Don't under-tighten the engine components, as this can cause engine failure and damage to the vehicle's internal components.
* Use high-quality components and materials to ensure durability and reliability.
* Consult with a professional mechanic or engineer if you're unsure about any aspect of the restomod process.
