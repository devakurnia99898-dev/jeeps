## Symptoms or Signs to look for
Before starting the modification process, it's essential to inspect the vehicle for any signs of wear or damage. Look for:
* Worn-out suspension components, such as bushings and shocks
* Engine oil leaks or excessive engine noise
* Electrical system malfunctions, such as faulty wiring or corroded connections

# Technical Deep Dive
The 1994 Toyota Corolla Wagon is equipped with a 1.8L inline-4 engine, which produces 115 horsepower and 110 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 5-speed manual transmission and a rear-wheel drive system. The suspension consists of MacPherson struts in the front and a torsion beam axle in the rear.

## How the system works
The suspension system is designed to provide a comfortable ride on paved roads, but it can be improved for off-road use by upgrading the shocks, springs, and bushings. The engine can also be modified to produce more power and torque, which is essential for off-road driving.

## Common failure points
The plastic impeller in the water pump is a common failure point, as it can crack or break under high temperatures or stress. The engine's timing belt is also prone to failure, especially if it's not replaced at the recommended interval.

# Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Suspension Upgrade
#### Step 1.1: Remove the old suspension components
Remove the wheels and shocks to access the suspension components. Use a 10mm socket to remove the bolts that hold the shocks in place.

#### Step 1.2: Install the new suspension components
Install the new shocks, springs, and bushings. Use a torque wrench to tighten the bolts to the recommended specification (30 ft-lbs).

### Step 2: Engine Modification
#### Step 2.1: Remove the old engine components
Remove the intake manifold and exhaust system to access the engine components. Use a 12mm socket to remove the bolts that hold the intake manifold in place.

#### Step 2.2: Install the new engine components
Install the new camshaft, pistons, and crankshaft. Use a torque wrench to tighten the bolts to the recommended specification (40 ft-lbs).

### Step 3: Electrical System Upgrade
#### Step 3.1: Remove the old electrical components
Remove the old wiring and electrical components to access the electrical system. Use wire cutters to cut the old wiring.

#### Step 3.2: Install the new electrical components
Install the new wiring, fuse box, and electrical connectors. Use a multimeter to test the electrical system for any faults or malfunctions.

# Pro Tips & Common Mistakes

* Make sure to follow the recommended torque specifications for the engine and suspension components.
