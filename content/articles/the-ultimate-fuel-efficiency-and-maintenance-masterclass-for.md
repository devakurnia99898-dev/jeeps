## Step-by-Step Guide
### Fuel Injector Cleaning
#### Step 1: Locate the Fuel Injectors
Identify the fuel injectors in your Jeep's engine compartment. They are usually located near the intake manifold.
#### Step 2: Remove the Fuel Injector Electrical Connectors
Disconnect the electrical connectors from the fuel injectors. This will prevent any accidental fuel injection during the cleaning process.
#### Step 3: Remove the Fuel Injector Hold-Down Clips
Remove the hold-down clips that secure the fuel injectors in place. This will allow you to access the fuel injectors for cleaning.
#### Step 4: Clean the Fuel Injectors
Use a fuel injector cleaning kit to clean the fuel injectors. Follow the manufacturer's instructions for the cleaning solution and procedure.
### Fuel Filter Replacement
#### Step 1: Locate the Fuel Filter
Identify the fuel filter in your Jeep's engine compartment. It is usually located near the fuel tank or fuel pump.
#### Step 2: Remove the Fuel Filter
Use a fuel filter wrench to remove the fuel filter from the fuel line. Be prepared for any fuel spillage.
#### Step 3: Install the New Fuel Filter
Install a new fuel filter and hand tighten it in a clockwise direction.
#### Step 4: Tighten the Fuel Filter
Use the fuel filter wrench to tighten the fuel filter in a clockwise direction. Refer to your Jeep's owner's manual for the recommended torque specification.
### Spark Plug Replacement
#### Step 1: Locate the Spark Plugs
Identify the spark plugs in your Jeep's engine compartment. They are usually located near the cylinder head.
#### Step 2: Remove the Spark Plug Wire
Pull the spark plug wire off the spark plug. This will give you access to the spark plug for removal.
#### Step 3: Remove the Spark Plug
Use a spark plug socket to remove the spark plug from the cylinder head. Be careful not to round the spark plug hole.
#### Step 4: Install the New Spark Plug
Install a new spark plug and hand tighten it in a clockwise direction.
#### Step 5: Tighten the Spark Plug
Use the spark plug socket to tighten the spark plug in a clockwise direction. Refer to your Jeep's owner's manual for the recommended torque specification (usually around 10-15 ft-lbs).

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use a torque wrench when tightening fuel system components to avoid over-tightening.
* Don't forget to replace the fuel filter regularly to maintain optimal fuel efficiency.
* Avoid using low-quality fuel, as it can clog fuel injectors and reduce fuel efficiency.
