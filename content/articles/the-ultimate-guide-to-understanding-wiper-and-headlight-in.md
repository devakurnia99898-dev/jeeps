## Technical Deep Dive
The system of windshield wipers and headlights in a vehicle is designed to work together to provide maximum visibility in bad weather conditions. The windshield wipers are designed to clear the windshield of any water, snow, or debris, while the headlights are designed to illuminate the road ahead and increase the visibility of the vehicle to other road users.

The common failure points in the system of windshield wipers and headlights include the wiper motor, the wiper blades, the headlight bulbs, and the headlight switches. The wiper motor is responsible for powering the wiper blades, and any failure in this component can result in the wipers not working properly. The wiper blades are responsible for clearing the windshield, and any damage or wear to these blades can reduce their effectiveness. The headlight bulbs are responsible for providing the light, and any failure in these bulbs can result in reduced visibility. The headlight switches are responsible for controlling the headlights, and any failure in these switches can result in the headlights not working properly.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Checking the Wiper System
The first step is to check the wiper system to ensure that it is working properly. This includes checking the wiper motor, the wiper blades, and the wiper switches.
#### Step 1.1: Checking the Wiper Motor
The wiper motor is responsible for powering the wiper blades. To check the wiper motor, use a multimeter to measure the voltage at the motor terminals. If the voltage is not within the specified range, the motor may be faulty.
#### Step 1.2: Checking the Wiper Blades
The wiper blades are responsible for clearing the windshield. To check the wiper blades, inspect them for any signs of wear or damage. If the blades are damaged or worn, they should be replaced.
#### Step 1.3: Checking the Wiper Switches
The wiper switches are responsible for controlling the wipers. To check the wiper switches, use a multimeter to measure the voltage at the switch terminals. If the voltage is not within the specified range, the switch may be faulty.

### Step 2: Checking the Headlight System
The second step is to check the headlight system to ensure that it is working properly. This includes checking the headlight bulbs, the headlight switches, and the headlight wiring.
#### Step 2.1: Checking the Headlight Bulbs
The headlight bulbs are responsible for providing the light. To check the headlight bulbs, inspect them for any signs of wear or damage. If the bulbs are damaged or worn, they should be replaced.
#### Step 2.2: Checking the Headlight Switches
The headlight switches are responsible for controlling the headlights. To check the headlight switches, use a multimeter to measure the voltage at the switch terminals. If the voltage is not within the specified range, the switch may be faulty.
#### Step 2.3: Checking the Headlight Wiring
The headlight wiring is responsible for carrying the electrical signal to the headlight bulbs. To check the headlight wiring, inspect it for any signs of damage or wear. If the wiring is damaged or worn, it should be replaced.

### Step 3: Replacing the Wiper Blades
If the wiper blades are damaged or worn, they should be replaced. To replace the wiper blades, use a wrench to loosen the nut that holds the blade in place. Once the nut is loose, the blade can be removed and replaced with a new one.
#### Step 3.1: Removing the Old Blade
To remove the old blade, use a wrench to loosen the nut that holds the blade in place. Once the nut is loose, the blade can be removed.
#### Step 3.2: Installing the New Blade
To install the new blade, place it in the correct position and tighten the nut that holds it in place. Make sure the nut is tightened securely to ensure the blade stays in place.

### Step 4: Replacing the Headlight Bulbs
If the headlight bulbs are damaged or worn, they should be replaced. To replace the headlight bulbs, use a Torx screwdriver to remove the screws that hold the bulb in place. Once the screws are removed, the bulb can be removed and replaced with a new one.
#### Step 4.1: Removing the Old Bulb
To remove the old bulb, use a Torx screwdriver to remove the screws that hold the bulb in place. Once the screws are removed, the bulb can be removed.
#### Step 4.2: Installing the New Bulb
To install the new bulb, place it in the correct position and replace the screws that hold it in place. Make sure the screws are tightened securely to ensure the bulb stays in place.
