## Technical Deep Dive
The Dodge Viper GTS-R is equipped with a 8.0L V10 engine, producing 645 horsepower and 600 lb-ft of torque. The engine features a cast iron block, aluminum heads, and a forged steel crankshaft. The transmission is a 6-speed manual, with a rear-mounted transaxle.

Common failure points on the Viper GTS-R include the clutch, brakes, and suspension components. The clutch can wear out quickly if not properly maintained, while the brakes can overheat if not adequately cooled. Suspension components can fail if not properly aligned or if the vehicle is driven too aggressively.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the oil drain plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the car. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug.
#### Step 1.2: Drain the oil
Allow the oil to drain completely before replacing the plug. Use a new gasket to ensure a proper seal.
#### Step 1.3: Refill the oil
Refill the oil with the recommended synthetic oil. Use a torque wrench to tighten the oil drain plug to 30 ft-lbs.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake calipers. Use a 12mm socket to loosen the lug nuts.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the caliper
Use a torque wrench to loosen the caliper bolts. Remove the caliper and set it aside.
#### Step 2.3: Remove the old brake pads
Use a brake pad wear gauge to determine the remaining life of the brake pads. Remove the old brake pads and discard them.
#### Step 2.4: Install the new brake pads
Install the new brake pads, making sure they are properly seated and aligned.

### Step 3: Suspension Alignment
#### Step 3.1: Raise the vehicle
Raise the vehicle using a lift or jack stands. Make sure the vehicle is level and secure.
#### Step 3.2: Loosen the suspension components
Use a torque wrench to loosen the suspension components, including the control arms and toe links.
#### Step 3.3: Align the suspension
Use a suspension alignment tool to align the suspension components. Make sure the camber, caster, and toe are within the recommended specifications.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't over-tighten the suspension components, as this can cause premature wear and damage.
* Make sure to use the correct type of brake pads for your Viper GTS-R. Using the wrong type can lead to decreased performance and increased wear.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure proper torque specs are met. Over-torquing can damage the engine or suspension components.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. Q: What type of oil should I use in my Viper GTS-R?
A: The recommended oil for the Viper GTS-R is synthetic 5W-30.
2. Q: How often should I change my brake pads?
A: Brake pads should be changed every 10,000 to 15,000 miles, depending on driving conditions.
3. Q: Can I align my suspension myself?
A: While it's possible to align your suspension yourself, it's recommended to have a professional mechanic perform the task to ensure proper alignment and prevent damage to the vehicle.
4. Q: What are the recommended torque specs for the Viper GTS-R?
A: The recommended torque specs for the Viper GTS-R can be found in the owner's manual or on the manufacturer's website.
5. Q: How often should I inspect my Viper GTS-R's suspension components?
A: Suspension components should be inspected every 5,000 to 10,000 miles, depending on driving conditions.
