## The Diagnostic / The Situation
When it comes to SUVs, the terms 'full-size' and 'mid-size' are often thrown around, but what do they really mean? In this guide, we'll delve into the world of full-size SUVs, exploring their defining characteristics, features, and maintenance requirements.

Symptoms or signs that your SUV may be due for maintenance include decreased fuel efficiency, unusual noises, or vibrations while driving. Regular maintenance is essential to prevent these issues and ensure your vehicle runs smoothly.

## Technical Deep Dive
Full-size SUVs are typically defined by their size, weight, and capabilities. They usually have a larger wheelbase, longer overall length, and higher ground clearance compared to mid-size SUVs. These vehicles often feature more powerful engines, advanced four-wheel-drive systems, and increased towing capacities.

Common features of full-size SUVs include:
* Larger engines (V8 or V6)
* Advanced four-wheel-drive systems
* Higher ground clearance
* Increased towing capacity
* Luxurious interior amenities

The Jeep Grand Cherokee is a prime example of a full-size SUV, offering a range of engine options, including the powerful 5.7L V8, and advanced four-wheel-drive systems like the Quadra-Trac II.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug
Find the oil drain plug located at the bottom of the engine. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug.

#### Step 1.2: Drain the Oil
Allow the oil to drain into a pan. Be careful not to spill any oil on the ground or surrounding components.

#### Step 1.3: Replace the Oil Filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old oil filter. Dispose of the filter responsibly.

#### Step 1.4: Install the New Oil Filter
Place a new oil filter on the engine and hand tighten it.

### Step 2: Tire Rotation
#### Step 2.1: Loosen the Lug Nuts
Before raising the vehicle, loosen the lug nuts on the wheels using a torque wrench.

#### Step 2.2: Raise the Vehicle
Use a jack to raise the vehicle, ensuring it is stable and secure.

#### Step 2.3: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels and rotate them according to the recommended pattern.

### Step 3: Brake Pad Inspection
#### Step 3.1: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake pads.

#### Step 3.2: Inspect the Brake Pads
Use a flashlight and mirror to inspect the brake pads. Check for wear and damage.

#### Step 3.3: Replace the Brake Pads (if necessary)
If the brake pads are worn or damaged, replace them with new ones.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always refer to your vehicle's owner's manual for specific maintenance instructions
* Use the correct type of oil for your vehicle
* Don't over-tighten the oil filter or lug nuts
* Regularly inspect and maintain your vehicle's brakes to ensure safety
