## Technical Deep Dive
The Miata's DNA is deeply rooted in its lightweight chassis, rear-wheel drive layout, and high-revving engine. The current generation Miata (ND) features a 2.0L inline-four engine, producing 181 horsepower and 151 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a six-speed manual transmission and a limited-slip differential. The Miata's suspension consists of a double-wishbone setup in the front and a multi-link setup in the rear, providing exceptional handling and stability. Common failure points in the Miata include the clutch, brake pads, and suspension bushings.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Regular Maintenance
Regular maintenance is crucial to preserving your Miata's DNA. This includes regular oil changes, tire rotations, and brake pad replacements.
#### Sub-step 1.1: Oil Change
1. Locate the oil drain plug and position a drain pan underneath
2. Remove the oil drain plug and allow the oil to drain completely
3. Replace the oil filter and refill the engine with the recommended oil type and quantity
#### Sub-step 1.2: Tire Rotation
1. Refer to your owner's manual for the recommended tire rotation pattern
2. Loosen the lug nuts on the first wheel using a wrench
3. Raise the vehicle using a jack and remove the lug nuts
4. Rotate the tire to the next position and hand tighten the lug nuts
5. Lower the vehicle and tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern
### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
Brake pad replacement is essential to maintaining your Miata's braking performance.
#### Sub-step 2.1: Remove the Wheel
1. Loosen the lug nuts on the wheel using a wrench
2. Raise the vehicle using a jack and remove the lug nuts
3. Remove the wheel and set it aside
#### Sub-step 2.2: Remove the Caliper
1. Remove the caliper bolts using a wrench
2. Slide the caliper off the rotor and set it aside
#### Sub-step 2.3: Remove the Old Brake Pads
1. Remove the old brake pads from the caliper
2. Inspect the brake rotor for any signs of wear or damage
### Step 3: Suspension Bushing Replacement
Suspension bushing replacement is critical to maintaining your Miata's handling and stability.
#### Sub-step 3.1: Remove the Control Arm
1. Remove the control arm bolts using a wrench
2. Slide the control arm off the bushing and set it aside
#### Sub-step 3.2: Remove the Old Bushing
1. Remove the old bushing from the control arm
2. Inspect the control arm for any signs of wear or damage
#### Sub-step 3.3: Install the New Bushing
1. Install the new bushing onto the control arm
2. Slide the control arm back onto the bushing and tighten the bolts

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is the recommended oil type for my Miata?**
The recommended oil type for your Miata is 0W-20 synthetic oil.
2. **How often should I rotate my tires?**
You should rotate your tires every 5,000 to 7,500 miles.
3. **What are the symptoms of worn-out brake pads?**
The symptoms of worn-out brake pads include squealing or grinding noises, vibration, and decreased braking performance.
4. **Can I replace my suspension bushings myself?**
Yes, you can replace your suspension bushings yourself, but it's recommended to seek the help of a professional mechanic if you're not experienced with suspension work.
5. **How often should I inspect my brake rotors?**
You should inspect your brake rotors every 10,000 to 15,000 miles for any signs of wear or damage.
