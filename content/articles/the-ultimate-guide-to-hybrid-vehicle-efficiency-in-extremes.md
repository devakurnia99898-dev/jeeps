## Technical Deep Dive
Hybrid vehicles use a combination of electric motors and internal combustion engines to achieve improved fuel efficiency. The electric motor is powered by a battery pack, which can be affected by temperature extremes. In extreme cold, the battery's chemical reactions slow down, reducing its ability to hold a charge. In extreme heat, the battery's chemical reactions speed up, causing it to degrade faster.

Common failure points in hybrid vehicles operating in temperature extremes include:

* The battery pack: Extreme temperatures can cause the battery pack to degrade faster, reducing its overall lifespan.
* The electric motor: Extreme temperatures can cause the electric motor to overheat or underperform, reducing its overall efficiency.
* The internal combustion engine: Extreme temperatures can cause the internal combustion engine to underperform, reducing its overall efficiency.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Check the Battery Pack
The battery pack is a critical component in hybrid vehicles. In extreme temperatures, it can be affected, reducing its overall performance. To check the battery pack:

#### Step 1.1: Locate the Battery Pack
The battery pack is usually located in the trunk or under the rear seat. Consult the vehicle's manual for specific location.

#### Step 1.2: Check the Battery Pack's State of Charge
Use a multimeter to check the battery pack's state of charge. A fully charged battery pack should read around 12.6 volts.

#### Step 1.3: Check the Battery Pack's Temperature
Use a thermometer to check the battery pack's temperature. The ideal operating temperature for most hybrid batteries is between 15°C and 35°C.

### Step 2: Check the Electric Motor
The electric motor is another critical component in hybrid vehicles. In extreme temperatures, it can be affected, reducing its overall performance. To check the electric motor:

#### Step 2.1: Locate the Electric Motor
The electric motor is usually located near the transmission. Consult the vehicle's manual for specific location.

#### Step 2.2: Check the Electric Motor's Temperature
Use a thermometer to check the electric motor's temperature. The ideal operating temperature for most electric motors is between 15°C and 35°C.

#### Step 2.3: Check the Electric Motor's Performance
Use a scan tool to check the electric motor's performance. Look for any error codes or abnormal readings.

### Step 3: Check the Internal Combustion Engine
