## The Diagnostic / The Situation
The Nissan Altima has been a staple in the mid-size sedan market for decades, known for its reliability and performance. However, like any vehicle, it requires regular maintenance to ensure it continues to run smoothly. Neglecting maintenance can lead to premature wear on engine components, decreased fuel efficiency, and even engine failure. In this guide, we'll explore the importance of maintaining your Nissan Altima's engine and provide a comprehensive checklist for DIY maintenance.

Symptoms of neglected engine maintenance may include:
- Decreased fuel efficiency
- Rough idling or stalling
- Increased emissions
- Overheating

## Technical Deep Dive
The Nissan Altima's engine is a complex system consisting of numerous components working in harmony. The 2.5L 4-cylinder engine, for example, features a dual overhead camshaft design with variable valve timing. Common failure points in this engine include:
- The timing chain tensioner: If not properly maintained, the timing chain can stretch or break, causing costly repairs.
- The water pump: The plastic impeller in the water pump can fail, leading to overheating and potential engine damage.
- The spark plugs: Worn spark plugs can cause misfires, decreased fuel efficiency, and increased emissions.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug
Find the oil drain plug located at the bottom of the engine. Use a 10mm socket to loosen the plug.
#### Step 1.2: Drain the Oil
Allow the oil to drain completely into a pan. Replace the oil drain plug and tighten to the specified torque (30 ft-lbs).
#### Step 1.3: Replace the Oil Filter
Remove the old oil filter and discard it. Install a new oil filter and hand tighten.

### Step 2: Spark Plug Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Locate the Spark Plugs
Find the spark plugs located on the side of the engine. Use a spark plug socket to remove the spark plugs.
#### Step 2.2: Inspect and Clean the Spark Plug Wells
Inspect the spark plug wells for any debris or corrosion. Clean the wells with a spark plug well cleaner.
#### Step 2.3: Install New Spark Plugs
Install new spark plugs and tighten to the specified torque (10 ft-lbs).

### Step 3: Water Pump Replacement
#### Step 3.1: Disconnect the Battery
Disconnect the negative battery cable to prevent any accidental starts.
#### Step 3.2: Drain the Coolant
Drain the coolant into a pan. Remove the water pump belt and pulley.
#### Step 3.3: Remove the Water Pump
Use a 10mm socket to remove the water pump mounting bolts. Remove the water pump and discard it.
#### Step 3.4: Install the New Water Pump
Install the new water pump and tighten the mounting bolts to the specified torque (20 ft-lbs).

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
- Don't over-tighten the spark plugs, as this can damage the threads.
- Use a torque wrench to ensure accurate torque specs.
- Always refer to the owner's manual for specific maintenance recommendations.

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **Q: How often should I change my oil?**
A: It's recommended to change your oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on your driving conditions.
2. **Q: What type of oil should I use?**
A: Use the type of oil recommended by the manufacturer, as specified in the owner's manual.
3. **Q: Can I replace my spark plugs myself?**
A: Yes, replacing spark plugs is a relatively simple DIY task. However, if you're not comfortable with the process, it's recommended to consult a professional mechanic.
4. **Q: How do I know if my water pump is failing?**
A: Signs of a failing water pump include overheating, leaks, and strange noises coming from the engine.
5. **Q: Can I use a universal oil filter?**
A: It's recommended to use an oil filter specifically designed for your vehicle's make and model to ensure proper fitment and performance.
