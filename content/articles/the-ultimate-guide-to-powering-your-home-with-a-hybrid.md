## Technical Deep Dive
Hybrid vehicles, like the Toyota RAV4 Plug-In Hybrid, use a combination of a traditional internal combustion engine and one or more electric motors. The electric motors are powered by a battery pack, which can be charged via an external power source or through regenerative braking. The 'HV power supply' mode in the Toyota RAV4 Plug-In Hybrid allows the vehicle to operate as a generator, providing up to 1.5 kW of power. This is achieved by using the vehicle's electric motor as a generator, which is then connected to an inverter to produce AC power.

Common failure points in hybrid vehicles used for powering homes include the battery pack, electric motor, and inverter. The battery pack is susceptible to degradation over time, which can reduce its capacity and overall performance. The electric motor and inverter can also be prone to overheating, which can reduce their efficiency and lifespan.
