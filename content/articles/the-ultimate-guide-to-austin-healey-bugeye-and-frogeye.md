The Bugeye Sprite, also known as the Mk I, was produced from 1958 to 1961, while the Frogeye Sprite, also known as the Mk I, was produced from 1958 to 1960. Understanding these differences is crucial for proper maintenance and repair.

## Technical Deep Dive
The Austin-Healey Bugeye and Frogeye Sprite are both equipped with a 948cc A-Series engine, which produces 43 horsepower. The engine features a single carburetor and a four-speed manual transmission. The suspension system consists of a coil spring and wishbone setup at the front, while the rear features a live axle with quarter elliptic leaf springs.

Common failure points in these vehicles include the plastic impeller in the water pump, the rubber seals in the engine, and the electrical components in the ignition system. The Bugeye Sprite is also prone to overheating issues due to its smaller radiator, while the Frogeye Sprite is more susceptible to brake failure due to its smaller brake drums.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the car. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and drain the oil into a pan.

#### Step 1.2: Replace the Oil Filter
The oil filter is located on the top of the engine. Use a filter wrench to remove the old filter and replace it with a new one.

#### Step 1.3: Refill the Oil
Refill the oil with the recommended type and amount. Check the owner's manual for specifications.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake drums. Use a lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts and then remove the wheels.

#### Step 2.2: Remove the Brake Drums
Remove the brake drums to access the brake pads. Use a screwdriver to remove the retaining screws.

#### Step 2.3: Replace the Brake Pads
Replace the old brake pads with new ones. Ensure the new pads are properly seated and secured.

### Step 3: Ignition System Repair
#### Step 3.1: Locate the Ignition Coil
The ignition coil is located near the distributor cap. Use a screwdriver to remove the retaining screws and access the coil.

#### Step 3.2: Replace the Spark Plugs
Replace the old spark plugs with new ones. Ensure the new plugs are properly gapped and secured.

#### Step 3.3: Replace the Distributor Cap
Replace the old distributor cap with a new one. Ensure the new cap is properly seated and secured.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the recommended type and amount of oil to prevent engine damage.
* Never mix old and new brake pads, as this can cause brake failure.
* Use a torque wrench to ensure proper tightening of bolts and screws.
* Don't over-tighten the oil drain plug, as this can cause damage to the engine.
