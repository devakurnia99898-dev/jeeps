## Technical Deep Dive
The Fiat Multipla features a range of advanced systems, including a turbocharged engine, automatic transmission, and advanced safety features. To understand how these systems work, let's dive deeper into the technical aspects.

* The engine: The Fiat Multipla is equipped with a 1.4L turbocharged engine, producing 95 horsepower and 130 Nm of torque. This engine features a timing belt, water pump, and oil cooler, which require regular maintenance.
* The transmission: The Fiat Multipla features a 5-speed automatic transmission, which provides smooth shifting and optimal fuel efficiency. However, this transmission requires regular fluid changes and filter replacements.
* The electrical system: The Fiat Multipla features a range of advanced electrical systems, including a start/stop system, regenerative braking, and a touchscreen infotainment system. These systems require proper diagnosis and repair techniques.

## Step-by-Step Guide
In this section, we'll cover the step-by-step guide for common maintenance tasks on the Fiat Multipla.

### Oil Change
#### Step 1: Gather materials
* Oil drain pan
* Funnel
* New oil filter
* Oil filler cap

#### Step 2: Warm up the engine
* Drive the vehicle for a few minutes to warm up the engine.

#### Step 3: Locate the oil drain plug
* The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the vehicle.

#### Step 4: Drain the oil
* Use a 10mm socket to remove the oil drain plug.
* Allow the oil to drain into the oil drain pan.

### Tire Rotation
#### Step 1: Gather materials
* Tire pressure gauge
* Lug wrench

#### Step 2: Check the tire pressure
* Use the tire pressure gauge to check the tire pressure.

#### Step 3: Loosen the lug nuts
* Use the lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts on the first wheel.

#### Step 4: Raise the vehicle
* Use a jack to raise the vehicle and remove the wheel.

### Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 1: Gather materials
* New brake pads
* Brake pad wear sensor

#### Step 2: Remove the wheel
* Use the lug wrench to remove the wheel.

#### Step 3: Remove the caliper
* Use a 12mm socket to remove the caliper bolts.

#### Step 4: Remove the old brake pads
* Use a screwdriver to remove the old brake pads.
