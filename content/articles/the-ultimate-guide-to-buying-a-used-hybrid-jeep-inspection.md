## Technical Deep Dive
The hybrid system in a Jeep consists of a combination of a conventional internal combustion engine and an electric motor. The battery pack is the heart of the hybrid system, and it's responsible for storing energy generated by the engine and the regenerative braking system. The battery pack is made up of multiple cells, and its health can be affected by various factors such as age, mileage, and usage patterns.

Common failure points in a hybrid Jeep include:

* The battery pack: This is the most expensive component to replace, and its health can be affected by age, mileage, and usage patterns.
* The electric motor: This can be prone to overheating and can be expensive to replace.
* The power control unit: This is the brain of the hybrid system, and it can be prone to software issues and hardware failures.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspecting the Battery Pack
The battery pack is the most critical component of the hybrid system, and its health can be affected by various factors such as age, mileage, and usage patterns. To inspect the battery pack, follow these steps:

#### Checking the Battery Voltage
Use a multimeter to check the voltage of the battery pack. A healthy battery pack should have a voltage of around 12.6V.

#### Checking the Battery State of Charge
Use a battery tester to check the state of charge of the battery pack. A healthy battery pack should have a state of charge of around 80%.

#### Checking the Battery Temperature
Use a thermometer to check the temperature of the battery pack. A healthy battery pack should have a temperature of around 20-30°C.

### Inspecting the Electric Motor
The electric motor is a critical component of the hybrid system, and it can be prone to overheating. To inspect the electric motor, follow these steps:

#### Checking the Motor Temperature
Use a thermometer to check the temperature of the electric motor. A healthy electric motor should have a temperature of around 20-30°C.

#### Checking the Motor Controller
Use a scan tool to check the motor controller for any error codes or issues.

### Inspecting the Power Control Unit
The power control unit is the brain of the hybrid system, and it can be prone to software issues and hardware failures. To inspect the power control unit, follow these steps:

#### Checking the Software Version
Use a scan tool to check the software version of the power control unit. A healthy power control unit should have the latest software version.

#### Checking the Hardware
Use a multimeter to check the hardware components of the power control unit. A healthy power control unit should have no issues with the hardware components.
