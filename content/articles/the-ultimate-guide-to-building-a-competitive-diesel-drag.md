## Technical Deep Dive
A diesel engine works by using the heat of compression to ignite the fuel, rather than a spark plug. This process allows for a higher compression ratio and more efficient combustion, resulting in more torque and power. The key to building a competitive diesel drag racer is to optimize the engine for maximum power and torque output.

Common failure points in a diesel engine that is used for drag racing include the turbocharger, fuel injectors, and engine bearings. The turbocharger is responsible for compressing the air and forcing it into the engine, which can lead to high temperatures and pressures. The fuel injectors are responsible for delivering the fuel to the engine, and they can become clogged or damaged due to the high pressures and temperatures. The engine bearings are responsible for supporting the engine's moving parts, and they can become worn or damaged due to the high stresses and pressures.
