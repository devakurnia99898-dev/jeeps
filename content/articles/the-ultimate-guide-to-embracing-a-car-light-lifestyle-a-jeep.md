## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Conduct a Transportation Audit
#### Step 1.1: Identify Your Daily Commute
Start by tracking your daily commute, including the distance, mode of transportation, and time spent traveling. This will help you identify areas where you can reduce your reliance on your car.

#### Step 1.2: Research Alternative Modes of Transportation
Look into alternative modes of transportation, such as public transportation, car-sharing, or ride-hailing services. You can also consider biking or walking for shorter trips.

#### Step 1.3: Plan a Car-Light Commute
Using the data from your transportation audit, plan a car-light commute that incorporates alternative modes of transportation. You can use online tools or consult with a transportation expert to help you create a personalized plan.

### Step 2: Maintain Your Jeep for Occasional Use
#### Step 2.1: Check the Tire Pressure
Ensure the tire pressure is at the recommended level, as specified in your owner's manual. Underinflated tires can decrease fuel efficiency and increase wear on your tires.

#### Step 2.2: Change the Oil
Regular oil changes are essential to maintain the health of your engine. Use a high-quality oil filter and change the oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on your vehicle's specifications.

#### Step 2.3: Inspect the Brake Pads
Worn-out brake pads can be a safety hazard. Inspect the brake pads every 10,000 to 15,000 miles and replace them as needed.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't assume that a car-light lifestyle means you'll never need to drive again. Be prepared to use your Jeep for occasional trips or off-roading adventures.
* Invest in a good bike or walking shoes to make alternative modes of transportation more enjoyable.
* Don't neglect your Jeep's maintenance. Regular upkeep will ensure your vehicle remains in good condition for when you need it.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
Maintaining your Jeep for a car-light lifestyle can be cost-effective, especially if you're willing to do some of the work yourself. Here's a breakdown of the costs:
