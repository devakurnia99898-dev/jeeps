## Technical Deep Dive
The Jaguar XJ220 is powered by a 3.5-liter twin-turbo V6 engine, producing 542 horsepower and 475 lb-ft of torque. The engine features a number of complex components, including twin turbochargers, an intercooler, and a sophisticated fuel injection system. The transmission is a 5-speed manual, with a limited-slip differential and a rear-wheel drive layout. The suspension is a double-wishbone design, with coil springs and anti-roll bars. The electrical system is equally complex, with a number of sophisticated systems, including traction control, anti-lock braking, and a advanced instrument cluster.

Common failure points on the XJ220 include the turbochargers, which can be prone to failure due to overheating or oil contamination. The fuel injection system can also be problematic, with faulty fuel injectors or a malfunctioning fuel pump. The suspension and steering systems can also be prone to wear and tear, with worn-out bushings and ball joints requiring replacement.
