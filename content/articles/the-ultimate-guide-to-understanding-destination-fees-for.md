# The Diagnostic / The Situation
Destination fees, also known as delivery fees, are charges added to the purchase price of a vehicle by the manufacturer or dealer to cover the cost of transporting the vehicle from the factory to the dealership. These fees can range from a few hundred to several thousand dollars, depending on the brand, model, and location. As a Jeep owner or enthusiast, it's essential to understand how destination fees work and how they can impact the overall cost of your vehicle.

## Technical Deep Dive
The destination fee is typically included in the vehicle's MSRP (Manufacturer's Suggested Retail Price) and is usually a fixed amount set by the manufacturer. However, some dealerships may charge additional fees, such as documentation fees, prep fees, or delivery fees, which can add to the overall cost. It's crucial to review the sales contract carefully to ensure you understand all the fees associated with your vehicle purchase.

Common failure points in navigating destination fees include:

* Not researching the destination fee for your specific vehicle model
* Not understanding the breakdown of fees included in the sales contract
* Not negotiating the destination fee with the dealer
