## Technical Deep Dive
To understand the implications of the proposed regulation, let's dive into how tires work and what factors contribute to rolling resistance. Rolling resistance is the energy lost as a tire rolls, primarily due to the deformation of the tire's sidewall and tread. Factors that affect rolling resistance include:

* Tire size and width
* Tread pattern and depth
* Sidewall stiffness
* Tire pressure

Common failure points in tires include:

* The tread, which can wear down over time, reducing traction and increasing rolling resistance
* The sidewall, which can crack or become damaged, leading to a loss of air pressure and reduced tire performance

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Choosing the Right Tires for Your Jeep
When selecting tires for your Jeep, consider the following factors:

#### Tire Size and Width
Choose a tire that is compatible with your Jeep's wheel size and suspension. A larger tire may provide more traction, but it can also increase rolling resistance and affect fuel efficiency.

#### Tread Pattern and Depth
Select a tire with a tread pattern that suits your driving style and terrain. Deeper tread patterns can provide more traction, but they can also increase rolling resistance.

#### Sidewall Stiffness
A stiffer sidewall can improve handling and stability, but it can also increase rolling resistance.

### Maintaining Your Tires
Proper tire maintenance is crucial to extending the life of your tires and ensuring optimal performance.

#### Checking Tire Pressure
Check your tire pressure at least once a month, and before long trips. Use a tire pressure gauge to ensure the pressure matches the recommended level, as specified in your Jeep's owner's manual.

#### Rotating Your Tires
Rotate your tires every 5,000 to 8,000 miles to ensure even wear and extend their lifespan.

#### Inspecting Your Tires
Regularly inspect your tires for signs of wear, damage, or uneven wear patterns. Address any issues promptly to prevent further damage.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
Don't make the mistake of underestimating the importance of proper tire maintenance. Here are some pro tips to keep in mind:

* Always check your tire pressure when the tires are cold, as driving can heat up the tires and affect the pressure reading.
* Use a tire pressure monitoring system (TPMS) to ensure your tires are always at the optimal pressure.
* Avoid overloading your Jeep, as this can put excessive stress on the tires and lead to premature wear.
