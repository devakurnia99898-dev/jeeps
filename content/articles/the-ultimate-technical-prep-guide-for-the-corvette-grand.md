## **Technical Deep Dive**
The Corvette Grand Sport features a 6.2L V8 engine, producing 460 horsepower and 465 lb-ft of torque. The engine is paired with a 7-speed manual transmission or an 8-speed automatic transmission. The Grand Sport also features a unique suspension system, including magnetic ride control and performance traction management.

Common failure points to look out for include:
* The plastic impeller in the water pump, which can fail due to high temperatures or debris
* The rear differential, which can experience excessive wear due to the high torque output of the engine
* The brake pads, which can wear down quickly due to the vehicle's high performance capabilities

## **Step-by-Step Guide**
### **Step 1: Oil Change**
#### **Step 1.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug**
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the vehicle. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.

#### **Step 1.2: Replace the Oil Filter**
The oil filter is located on the top of the engine. Use a filter wrench to remove the old filter and discard it. Install a new oil filter and hand tighten it.

#### **Step 1.3: Refill the Oil**
Use a funnel to pour in the new oil. The recommended oil capacity is 6 quarts. Replace the oil cap and start the engine to circulate the new oil.

### **Step 2: Tire Rotation**
#### **Step 2.1: Loosen the Lug Nuts**
Before raising the vehicle, loosen the lug nuts on the wheels using a torque wrench. Turn them counterclockwise until they are loose, but do not remove them yet.

#### **Step 2.2: Raise the Vehicle**
Use a jack to raise the vehicle until the wheels are off the ground. Support the vehicle with jack stands for safety.

#### **Step 2.3: Remove the Lug Nuts and Wheels**
Now that the vehicle is raised, you can remove the lug nuts and wheels. Place the wheels on the ground and use a tire pressure gauge to check the pressure.

### **Step 3: Brake Pad Replacement**
#### **Step 3.1: Remove the Caliper**
The caliper is held in place by two bolts. Use a 10mm socket to remove the bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.

#### **Step 3.2: Remove the Old Brake Pads**
The old brake pads are held in place by clips. Use a screwdriver to remove the clips and pull out the old pads.

#### **Step 3.3: Install the New Brake Pads**
Install the new brake pads and replace the clips. Make sure they are securely in place.

## **Pro Tips & Common Mistakes**
* Always use the correct type of oil for your vehicle, as specified in the owner's manual
* Never mix different types of brake pads, as this can cause inconsistent brake performance
* Use a torque wrench to tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern to ensure even tightening

A: The recommended oil type is 0W-40 synthetic oil.
2. **Q: How often should I rotate my tires?**
A: It is recommended to rotate your tires every 5,000 to 8,000 miles.
3. **Q: What are the symptoms of worn-out brake pads?**
A: Symptoms include squealing or grinding noises, vibration when braking, and decreased brake performance.
4. **Q: Can I use different types of brake pads on my Corvette Grand Sport?**
A: No, it is not recommended to mix different types of brake pads, as this can cause inconsistent brake performance.
5. **Q: How often should I check my tire pressure?**
A: It is recommended to check your tire pressure at least once a month, and before long trips.
