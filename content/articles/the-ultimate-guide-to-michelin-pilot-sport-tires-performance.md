## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspecting and Maintaining Your Michelin Pilot Sport Tires
#### Checking Tire Pressure
Check your tire pressure at least once a month, and before long trips. Use a tire pressure gauge to ensure the pressure matches the recommended level, as specified in your Jeep's owner's manual.

#### Rotating Your Tires
Rotate your tires every 5,000 to 8,000 miles to ensure even wear. Use a tire rotation pattern recommended by Michelin or your Jeep's manufacturer.

#### Inspecting for Damage
Inspect your tires regularly for signs of damage, such as uneven wear, tire punctures, or sidewall damage. Address any issues promptly to prevent further damage.

#### Balancing Your Tires
Balance your tires every 12,000 to 15,000 miles to ensure even wear and prevent vibration. Use a tire balancing machine to ensure accurate results.

### Upgrading Your Michelin Pilot Sport Tires
#### Choosing the Right Upgrade
Consider upgrading to a higher-performance tire, such as the Michelin Pilot Sport 4S, for improved handling and grip.

#### Installing the New Tires
Install the new tires according to the manufacturer's instructions, ensuring proper torque specs and wheel alignment.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Don't neglect regular tire maintenance, as it can lead to reduced performance and safety issues.
* Avoid over- or under-inflating your tires, as it can cause uneven wear and reduced traction.
* Use a tire repair kit to address punctures promptly, rather than driving on a damaged tire.

The cost of maintaining and upgrading your Michelin Pilot Sport tires can vary depending on the specific services and products required. On average, a DIY tire maintenance and upgrade can cost between $500 to $1,000, while a mechanic's labor can add an additional $200 to $500.
