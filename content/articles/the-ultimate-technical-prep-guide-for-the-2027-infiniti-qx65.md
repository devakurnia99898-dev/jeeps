## Technical Deep Dive
The variable-compression turbocharged engine in the 2027 Infiniti QX65 is a marvel of modern engineering. This engine features a unique multi-link system that allows for seamless transitions between different compression ratios, providing optimal performance and efficiency. The engine also features a turbocharger, which compresses air and forces it into the engine, resulting in a significant increase in power.

Common failure points in this engine include the turbocharger, engine bearings, and piston rings. The turbocharger can fail due to excessive heat, oil contamination, or foreign object damage. Engine bearings can wear out over time, causing vibrations and noise, while piston rings can become worn, leading to decreased engine performance and fuel efficiency.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Preparation
#### Step 1: Gather Tools and Materials
Before starting any maintenance or repair work, gather all necessary tools and materials. This includes the tools listed above, as well as any replacement parts or fluids.

#### Step 2: Locate the Engine and Components
Familiarize yourself with the engine and its components, including the turbocharger, engine bearings, and piston rings. Understand the location and function of each component to ensure proper diagnosis and repair.

### Maintenance and Repair
#### Step 3: Change the Oil and Filter
Regular oil changes are essential to maintain the health and performance of the engine. Use a high-quality oil filter and oil that meets the manufacturer's specifications.

##### Step 3.1: Locate the Oil Drain Plug
Locate the oil drain plug, usually located at the bottom of the engine. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.

##### Step 3.2: Replace the Oil Filter
Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old oil filter. Dispose of the old filter responsibly and install a new one.

##### Step 3.3: Refill the Oil
Refill the oil with the recommended type and amount. Use a funnel to prevent spills and overfilling.

#### Step 4: Inspect and Replace the Turbocharger
The turbocharger is a critical component of the engine, and regular inspection and maintenance are essential to prevent failure.

##### Step 4.1: Locate the Turbocharger
Locate the turbocharger, usually located on the side of the engine. Use a 12mm socket to remove the bolts holding the turbocharger in place.

##### Step 4.2: Inspect the Turbocharger
Inspect the turbocharger for signs of damage or wear. Check for excessive play, noise, or vibration.

##### Step 4.3: Replace the Turbocharger (if necessary)
If the turbocharger is damaged or worn, replace it with a new one. Use a torque wrench to secure the bolts, following the manufacturer's specifications.

### Final Inspection and Testing
#### Step 5: Inspect the Engine and Components
Inspect the engine and its components, including the turbocharger, engine bearings, and piston rings. Check for any signs of damage or wear.

#### Step 6: Test the Vehicle
Test the vehicle to ensure proper performance and function. Check for any unusual noises, vibrations, or decreased performance.
