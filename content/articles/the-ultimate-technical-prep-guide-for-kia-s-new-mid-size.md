## **Technical Deep Dive**
The body-on-frame architecture of Kia's new truck will likely feature a ladder-frame design, with a separate body mounted on top of the frame. This setup provides excellent strength, stability, and versatility, making it ideal for a mid-size truck. The truck's suspension system will play a critical role in its overall performance, with a likely combination of coil springs, shock absorbers, and stabilizer bars.

Common failure points in a body-on-frame architecture include:

* The frame itself, which can be prone to rust and damage from off-road use
* The suspension system, which can wear out over time, affecting the truck's ride and handling
* The steering system, which can be affected by wear and tear on the frame and suspension

## **Step-by-Step Guide**
### **Step 1: Inspecting the Frame**
#### **Step 1.1: Visual Inspection**
Inspect the frame for any signs of rust, damage, or wear, paying particular attention to areas prone to corrosion, such as the wheel wells and suspension mounts.
#### **Step 1.2: Measuring the Frame**
Use a tape measure to check the frame's dimensions and ensure it is within specifications, taking note of any deviations or irregularities.

### **Step 2: Replacing the Suspension System**
#### **Step 2.1: Removing the Old Suspension**
Use a coil spring compressor to remove the old coil springs, taking care not to damage the surrounding components, such as the shock absorbers and stabilizer bars.
#### **Step 2.2: Installing the New Suspension**
Install the new coil springs, shock absorbers, and stabilizer bars, ensuring proper torque specs are followed, such as 50 Nm (37 ft-lbs) for the coil spring mounts.

### **Step 3: Upgrading the Steering System**
#### **Step 3.1: Removing the Old Steering Components**
Remove the old steering components, including the steering gear and linkages, taking care not to damage the surrounding components, such as the frame and suspension.
#### **Step 3.2: Installing the New Steering Components**
Install the new steering components, ensuring proper torque specs are followed, such as 70 Nm (52 ft-lbs) for the steering gear mounts.

## **Pro Tips & Common Mistakes**
* Don't neglect to inspect the frame and suspension system regularly, as this can lead to premature wear and damage
* Be aware of the potential for over-tightening or under-tightening of bolts and fasteners, which can affect the truck's performance and safety
* Use proper lifting and supporting techniques when working on the truck, to avoid damage to the frame and surrounding components
