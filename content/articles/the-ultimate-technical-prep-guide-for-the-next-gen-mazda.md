## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Preparation and Inspection
Before diving into any maintenance or performance upgrades, it's crucial to inspect the vehicle and prepare the necessary tools and materials.
#### Step 1.1: Inspect the Vehicle
Perform a thorough inspection of the vehicle, paying close attention to the suspension, brakes, and engine. Check for any signs of wear or damage, and make a note of any areas that require attention.

#### Step 1.2: Gather Tools and Materials
Collect all the necessary tools and materials, including those listed in the pre-work checklist. Ensure you have a clean, well-lit workspace and a suitable jack and jack stands.

### Step 2: Suspension Upgrades
The Miata's suspension is a critical component of its handling and performance. Upgrading the suspension can significantly improve the vehicle's agility and responsiveness.
#### Step 2.1: Remove the Wheels and Suspension Components
Remove the wheels and suspension components, taking care to label and organize the various parts. Use a suspension compressor to compress the springs and remove the shock absorbers.

#### Step 2.2: Install Upgraded Suspension Components
Install the upgraded suspension components, including new springs, shock absorbers, and sway bars. Ensure all components are properly aligned and secured.

### Step 3: Engine Performance Upgrades
The Miata's engine is a vital component of its performance and character. Upgrading the engine can significantly improve the vehicle's power and responsiveness.
#### Step 3.1: Remove the Engine Covers and Intake System
Remove the engine covers and intake system, taking care to label and organize the various components. Use a torque wrench to loosen the intake manifold bolts.

#### Step 3.2: Install Upgraded Engine Components
Install the upgraded engine components, including a cold air intake, performance exhaust system, and engine tune. Ensure all components are properly aligned and secured.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When working on the Miata, it's essential to avoid common mistakes that can compromise the vehicle's performance and reliability. Some pro tips include:
* Using the correct torque specs when tightening bolts and fasteners
* Ensuring all components are properly aligned and secured
* Avoiding over-tightening or under-tightening critical components
