## Technical Deep Dive
Subaru's AWD system is based on a simple yet effective principle: power is sent to all four wheels simultaneously, with the ability to dynamically allocate torque to each wheel as needed. This is achieved through a combination of advanced differentials, transfer cases, and electronic control systems.

The system works as follows: power from the engine is sent to the transmission, which then splits the power between the front and rear differentials. The front differential sends power to the front wheels, while the rear differential sends power to the rear wheels. The transfer case, located between the transmission and the rear differential, is responsible for allocating torque to each wheel.

Common failure points in Subaru's AWD system include the viscous coupling, which can wear out over time, and the differential gears, which can become worn or damaged.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Inspect the AWD System
#### Step 1.1: Check the Viscous Coupling
The viscous coupling is a critical component of Subaru's AWD system, responsible for dynamically allocating torque to each wheel. To inspect the viscous coupling, locate it on the rear differential and check for signs of wear or damage.

#### Step 1.2: Check the Differential Gears
The differential gears are responsible for sending power to each wheel. To inspect the differential gears, remove the rear differential cover and check for signs of wear or damage.

### Step 2: Replace the Viscous Coupling
#### Step 2.1: Remove the Rear Differential Cover
To replace the viscous coupling, start by removing the rear differential cover. This will give you access to the viscous coupling and the differential gears.

#### Step 2.2: Remove the Viscous Coupling
Using a differential gear puller, remove the viscous coupling from the rear differential. Be careful not to damage the surrounding components.

#### Step 2.3: Install the New Viscous Coupling
Install the new viscous coupling, making sure it's properly seated and secured. Torque the bolts to the specified value (usually around 30-40 Nm).

### Step 3: Replace the Differential Gears
#### Step 3.1: Remove the Differential Gears
Using a gear puller, remove the differential gears from the rear differential. Be careful not to damage the surrounding components.

#### Step 3.2: Install the New Differential Gears
Install the new differential gears, making sure they're properly seated and secured. Torque the bolts to the specified value (usually around 50-60 Nm).

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use the correct torque specs when working with the AWD system to avoid damaging the components.
