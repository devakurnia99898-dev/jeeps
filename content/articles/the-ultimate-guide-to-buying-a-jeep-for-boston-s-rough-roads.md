Symptoms or signs to look for when driving on rough roads include:
- Excessive vibration or bouncing

- Reduced traction or stability

## Technical Deep Dive
Jeeps are known for their off-road capabilities, but they're also well-suited for handling rough urban roads. The key to a Jeep's success in this environment lies in its suspension and axle design. The Wrangler and Gladiator, for example, feature a robust five-link coil suspension in the front and a five-link coil or heavy-duty Dana 44 axle in the rear. This setup provides excellent articulation and stability, even on the most uneven surfaces.

Common failure points to be aware of when driving on rough roads include:
- The plastic impeller in the water pump, which can crack or break due to excessive vibration
- The control arms and track bars, which can wear out or become loose over time
- The shocks and struts, which can lose their damping ability and compromise ride quality

When working on your Jeep's suspension or axles, be sure to:
- Always support the vehicle with jack stands and blocks
- Wear protective gear, including gloves and safety glasses

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Choosing the Right Jeep Model
When selecting a Jeep for Boston's rough roads, consider the following models:
- Jeep Wrangler Sport: A great entry-point for off-road enthusiasts, with a starting price under $30,000
- Jeep Gladiator Sport: A pickup truck version of the Wrangler, with a similar price point and capabilities

#### Researching and Test-Driving
1. Research different trim levels and options to find the best fit for your budget and needs
2. Test-drive each model to get a feel for its on-road and off-road performance
3. Pay attention to the suspension and steering, noting any unusual noises or vibrations

### Preparing Your Jeep for Rough Roads
#### Upgrading the Suspension
1. Consider adding a lift kit to increase ground clearance and improve articulation
2. Upgrade to heavier-duty shocks and struts to improve damping and stability
3. Replace the control arms and track bars with heavier-duty components

#### Reinforcing the Axles
1. Upgrade to a heavier-duty axle, such as the Dana 44 or Dana 60
2. Add axle shafts and bearings to improve strength and durability
3. Consider adding a locking differential to improve traction

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
- Don't overlook the importance of proper wheel alignment and tire pressure
- Avoid over-tightening suspension components, as this can compromise articulation and stability
- Consider adding skid plates and rock rails to protect the underside of your Jeep
