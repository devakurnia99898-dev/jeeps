## The Diagnostic / The Situation
Electric vehicles (EVs) are becoming increasingly popular, and with that comes the need for efficient and fast charging solutions. The concept of a 'daisy chain' charger, as recently patented by GM, aims to address this by allowing multiple EVs to be charged simultaneously while optimizing the charging process. This technology is crucial for high-demand scenarios such as public charging stations or fleet operations. Symptoms of inefficient charging include prolonged charging times, overheating of charging equipment, and reduced battery lifespan due to improper charging practices.

## Technical Deep Dive
The 'daisy chain' charging system works by connecting multiple EVs in series, allowing them to share the available charging power. The system is designed to prioritize vehicles with the highest voltage requirements, ensuring that those needing the most power are charged first. This is achieved through advanced control units that monitor the state of charge and voltage requirements of each vehicle. Common failure points in such systems could include faulty charging cables, malfunctioning control units, or inadequate cooling systems for the charging equipment.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Setting Up the Daisy Chain Configuration
The first step involves configuring the 'daisy chain' setup by connecting the EVs in series. This requires careful planning to ensure that the total voltage and current requirements do not exceed the capabilities of the charging system.

#### Connecting the Vehicles
1. Begin by connecting the first vehicle to the charging station.
2. Ensure all safety protocols are followed, including wearing PPE.
3. Connect the subsequent vehicles in series, making sure each connection is secure and meets the manufacturer's specifications.

#### Configuring the Control Unit
1. Program the control unit to recognize the number of vehicles connected and their respective voltage requirements.
2. Set the prioritization parameters based on the vehicle's state of charge and voltage needs.
3. Test the system to ensure it is functioning as intended, with the correct vehicles being prioritized.

### Implementing the Charging Cycle
#### Initiating the Charge
1. Start the charging cycle, and the system will automatically begin charging the vehicles based on their priority.
2. Monitor the system's performance, ensuring that the charging process is efficient and safe.

#### Adjusting Charging Parameters
1. Based on the system's performance, adjust the charging parameters as necessary to optimize the charging speed and efficiency.
2. Consider factors such as ambient temperature, the state of charge of each vehicle, and the total power available.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
- **Avoid Overloading**: Ensure that the total power demand of the connected vehicles does not exceed the charging system's capacity.
- **Regular Maintenance**: Regularly inspect and maintain the charging cables and connections to prevent faults and ensure safe operation.
- **Incorrect Prioritization**: Double-check the prioritization settings to ensure that vehicles with the highest voltage needs are charged first.

## Cost Analysis: DIY vs Mechanic
The cost of implementing a 'daisy chain' charging system can vary widely, depending on the specific components and the scale of the operation. For a DIY approach, the cost could range from $500 to $1000 for a small-scale setup, not including labor. However, for a professional installation, especially in a commercial setting, the cost could be significantly higher, potentially exceeding $10,000, including both parts and labor.
