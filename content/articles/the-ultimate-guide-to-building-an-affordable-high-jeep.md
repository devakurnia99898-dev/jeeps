The key to building an affordable high-performance Jeep is to focus on modifications that provide the greatest performance gain while minimizing cost. This can include upgrading the engine, suspension, and brakes. One common area of modification is the engine, where upgrades such as a cold air intake, cat-back exhaust, and engine tune can provide significant power gains. The suspension is another critical area, where upgrades such as coilovers, sway bars, and control arms can improve handling and stability.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Upgrading the Engine
The engine is the heart of any high-performance vehicle. Upgrading the engine can provide significant power gains, but it's essential to approach this modification with caution. Here are the steps to follow:
#### Step 1: Install a Cold Air Intake
A cold air intake is a simple yet effective modification that can provide a small power gain. To install a cold air intake, follow these steps:
1. Locate the stock air intake and remove the screws holding it in place.
2. Carefully pull the stock air intake out of the engine compartment.
3. Install the cold air intake, ensuring it's securely fastened with the provided screws.
4. Connect the cold air intake to the engine's throttle body.

#### Step 2: Install a Cat-Back Exhaust
A cat-back exhaust is a more significant modification that can provide a noticeable power gain. To install a cat-back exhaust, follow these steps:
1. Locate the stock exhaust system and remove the screws holding it in place.
2. Carefully pull the stock exhaust system out of the vehicle.
3. Install the cat-back exhaust, ensuring it's securely fastened with the provided screws.
4. Connect the cat-back exhaust to the engine's exhaust manifold.

### Upgrading the Suspension
The suspension is critical to handling and stability. Upgrading the suspension can provide a significant improvement in performance, but it's essential to approach this modification with caution. Here are the steps to follow:
#### Step 1: Install Coilovers
Coilovers are a popular suspension upgrade that can provide a significant improvement in handling and stability. To install coilovers, follow these steps:
1. Locate the stock suspension and remove the screws holding it in place.
2. Carefully pull the stock suspension out of the vehicle.
3. Install the coilovers, ensuring they're securely fastened with the provided screws.
4. Adjust the coilovers to the desired height and damping setting.

#### Step 2: Install Sway Bars
Sway bars are a simple yet effective modification that can provide a small improvement in handling and stability. To install sway bars, follow these steps:
1. Locate the stock sway bars and remove the screws holding them in place.
2. Carefully pull the stock sway bars out of the vehicle.
3. Install the new sway bars, ensuring they're securely fastened with the provided screws.
4. Adjust the sway bars to the desired stiffness setting.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
