## Step-by-Step Guide
### Pre-Delivery Inspection
Before taking delivery of your new Audi Q7, it's essential to conduct a thorough inspection to ensure everything is in working order.
#### Checking the Exterior
Inspect the vehicle's exterior for any signs of damage or imperfections. Check the paintwork, trim, and wheels for any scratches or dents.
#### Checking the Interior
Inspect the interior for any signs of wear or damage. Check the seats, dashboard, and carpets for any stains or tears.
#### Checking the Electrical Systems
Test all electrical systems, including the lights, wipers, and infotainment system.

### Routine Maintenance
Regular maintenance is essential to keep your Audi Q7 running smoothly. This includes regular oil changes, tire rotations, and brake pad replacements.
#### Oil Change
Change the oil every 5,000 to 7,500 miles, depending on the manufacturer's recommendations. Use a high-quality oil filter and ensure the oil is disposed of properly.
#### Tire Rotation
Rotate the tires every 5,000 to 8,000 miles to ensure even wear and prolong their lifespan.

### Advanced Maintenance
For more advanced maintenance tasks, it's recommended to consult a professional mechanic.
#### Suspension Inspection
Inspect the suspension system every 12,000 to 15,000 miles to ensure it's in good working order. Check for any signs of wear or damage on the shocks, struts, and springs.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When working on your Audi Q7, it's essential to avoid common mistakes that can cause damage to the vehicle.
* Never over-tighten bolts or screws, as this can cause damage to the threads or surrounding material.
* Always use the correct tools for the job, as using the wrong tools can cause damage to the vehicle or injury to yourself.
* Keep track of maintenance records to ensure you stay on top of routine maintenance tasks.
