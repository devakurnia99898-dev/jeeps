The diesel engine is a type of internal combustion engine that uses diesel fuel to generate power. It operates on the principle of compression ignition, where the fuel is injected into the cylinder and ignited by the heat generated by the compression of the air. This is different from a gasoline engine, which uses spark ignition to ignite the fuel.
