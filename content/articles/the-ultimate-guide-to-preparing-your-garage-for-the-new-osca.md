## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Prepare Your Garage
#### Step 1.1: Clear the Workspace
Clear a large area in your garage to accommodate the Osca sports car. Remove any obstacles, such as tools, equipment, or storage containers, to create a safe and efficient workspace.

#### Step 1.2: Install a Lift
Consider installing a lift in your garage to make it easier to work on the Osca sports car. A lift will give you better access to the underside of the vehicle and make it easier to perform tasks such as oil changes and brake pad replacements.

#### Step 1.3: Organize Your Tools
Organize your tools and equipment in a logical and accessible manner. This will save you time and frustration when working on the Osca sports car.

### Step 2: Familiarize Yourself with the Osca Sports Car's Systems
#### Step 2.1: Study the Owner's Manual
Read the owner's manual to learn about the Osca sports car's systems, including the engine, transmission, suspension, and brakes.

#### Step 2.2: Research Online
Research online to learn more about the Osca sports car's systems and how to maintain and repair them.

#### Step 2.3: Join a Community
Join a community of Osca sports car owners to learn from their experiences and gain valuable insights into maintaining and repairing the vehicle.

### Step 3: Perform Regular Maintenance
#### Step 3.1: Oil Changes
Perform regular oil changes to keep the engine well-lubricated and running smoothly. Use a high-quality oil filter and oil that meets the manufacturer's specifications.

#### Step 3.2: Brake Pad Replacements
Replace the brake pads regularly to ensure optimal braking performance. Use high-quality brake pads that meet the manufacturer's specifications.

#### Step 3.3: Suspension Inspections
Inspect the suspension system regularly to identify any wear and tear. Replace any worn-out components, such as shocks, struts, and bushings, to maintain optimal handling and stability.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
