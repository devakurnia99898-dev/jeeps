## Technical Deep Dive
The aerodynamic system in Jeeps works by manipulating the flow of air around the vehicle to minimize resistance and maximize efficiency. This is achieved through the strategic design of various components, including the front grille, hood, windshield, and rear spoiler. Common failure points in aerodynamic design include:

* Inadequate front grille design, leading to increased air resistance
* Insufficient hood design, resulting in turbulence and reduced airflow
* Poorly designed windshield, causing wind noise and reduced visibility
* Ineffective rear spoiler design, compromising downforce and stability
