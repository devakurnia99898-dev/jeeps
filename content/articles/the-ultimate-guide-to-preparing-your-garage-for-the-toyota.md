## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Pre-Maintenance Inspection
Before performing any maintenance or upgrades on the GRMN Corolla, it's crucial to conduct a thorough inspection of the vehicle. This includes checking the oil and fluid levels, inspecting the brake pads and rotors, and examining the suspension and steering components for any signs of wear or damage.

#### Step 1.1: Check the Oil and Fluid Levels
Locate the oil dipstick and check the oil level, adding oil as necessary. Also, inspect the coolant, brake, and transmission fluid levels, topping them off as required.

#### Step 1.2: Inspect the Brake Pads and Rotors
Remove the wheel and inspect the brake pads and rotors, looking for signs of wear or damage. Replace the brake pads if necessary, and resurface or replace the rotors if they are excessively worn.

### Step 2: Suspension and Steering Upgrades
The GRMN Corolla's sport-tuned suspension is designed to deliver exceptional handling and agility. However, to further enhance the vehicle's performance, it's possible to upgrade the suspension and steering components.

#### Step 2.1: Install Upgraded Coilovers
Remove the factory coilovers and replace them with upgraded units, taking care to properly align and secure the new components.

#### Step 2.2: Upgrade the Steering Components
Install a upgraded steering rack or steering gear, and replace the factory steering bushings with high-performance alternatives.

### Step 3: Engine and Transmission Upgrades
The GRMN Corolla's turbocharged engine and manual transmission offer a range of opportunities for upgrades and modifications.

#### Step 3.1: Install a Performance Air Filter
Replace the factory air filter with a high-performance alternative, designed to improve airflow and increase power output.

#### Step 3.2: Upgrade the Exhaust System
Install a high-performance exhaust system, including a cat-back exhaust and a high-flow catalytic converter.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When working with the GRMN Corolla, it's essential to avoid common mistakes that can compromise the vehicle's performance and safety.

* Don't over-tighten the suspension components, as this can cause premature wear and damage.
* Avoid using low-quality or counterfeit parts, as these can compromise the vehicle's performance and safety.
* Always follow proper torque specifications when tightening bolts and fasteners.
