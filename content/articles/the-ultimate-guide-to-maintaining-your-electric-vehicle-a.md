## Step-by-Step Guide
### Checking and Maintaining Tire Pressure
Tire pressure is crucial for electric vehicles, as it affects range and efficiency. To check tire pressure, follow these steps:
#### Step 1: Locate the Tire Information Placard
The tire information placard can be found on the driver's side doorjamb or inside the fuel door.
#### Step 2: Check the Recommended Tire Pressure
The recommended tire pressure for the Fisker Ocean is 35 psi for the front tires and 38 psi for the rear tires.
#### Step 3: Use a Tire Pressure Gauge to Check the Current Pressure
Use a tire pressure gauge to check the current pressure and compare it to the recommended pressure.

### Rotating Tires
Tire rotation is essential to ensure even wear and extend the life of the tires. To rotate the tires, follow these steps:
#### Step 1: Jack Up the Vehicle
Use a jack to lift the vehicle and remove the wheels.
#### Step 2: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels and rotate them according to the recommended pattern.
#### Step 3: Tighten the Lug Nuts
Tighten the lug nuts in a star pattern to ensure even tightening.

### Checking and Maintaining Brake Pads
Brake pads are a critical component of the braking system. To check and maintain brake pads, follow these steps:
#### Step 1: Remove the Wheels
Remove the wheels to access the brake pads.
#### Step 2: Inspect the Brake Pads
Inspect the brake pads for wear and damage.
#### Step 3: Replace the Brake Pads (if necessary)
Replace the brake pads if they are worn or damaged.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When maintaining your electric vehicle, it's essential to avoid common mistakes that can affect performance and longevity. Some common mistakes include:
* Not checking tire pressure regularly
* Not rotating tires regularly
* Not inspecting brake pads regularly
Some pro tips include:
* Using a tire pressure gauge with a high level of accuracy
* Rotating tires every 5,000 to 7,000 miles
* Inspecting brake pads every 10,000 to 15,000 miles

## Frequently Asked Questions
1. **What is the recommended tire pressure for the Fisker Ocean?**
The recommended tire pressure for the Fisker Ocean is 35 psi for the front tires and 38 psi for the rear tires.

Tires should be rotated every 5,000 to 7,000 miles to ensure even wear and extend the life of the tires.
