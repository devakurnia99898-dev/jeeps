One of the most common issues with the Cutlass Supreme is the deterioration of the exterior and interior components due to age and wear. In this guide, we will focus on the restoration of the Cutlass Supreme, including the engine, suspension, and electrical systems.

Symptoms or signs to look for when restoring a Cutlass Supreme include:
* Rust and corrosion on the body and chassis
* Worn-out suspension and steering components
* Faulty electrical systems, including the ignition and lighting
* Engine problems, such as overheating and oil leaks

## Technical Deep Dive
The Cutlass Supreme has a 3.1L V6 engine, which is known for its reliability and performance. However, like any engine, it can be prone to problems if not properly maintained. The suspension system is a MacPherson strut design, which provides a smooth ride and stable handling. The electrical system is a complex network of wires and components, including the ignition, lighting, and accessories.

Common failure points on the Cutlass Supreme include:
* The plastic impeller in the water pump, which can crack and cause coolant leaks
* The ball joints and tie rod ends, which can wear out and cause steering problems
* The ignition coil and spark plugs, which can fail and cause engine misfires
* The alternator and battery, which can fail and cause electrical system problems

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Engine Overhaul
The engine overhaul is a major task that requires specialized tools and expertise. However, it can be done with the right guidance and precautions.
#### Step 1.1: Remove the engine
Remove the engine from the car and place it on a engine stand. Make sure to disconnect all the hoses and wires before removing the engine.
#### Step 1.2: Disassemble the engine
Disassemble the engine and remove all the components, including the cylinder head, pistons, and crankshaft. Make sure to label all the components and store them in a safe place.
#### Step 1.3: Inspect and clean the components
Inspect all the components and clean them thoroughly. Replace any worn-out or damaged components, including the piston rings and bearings.
#### Step 1.4: Reassemble the engine
Reassemble the engine and make sure all the components are properly aligned and secured. Use a torque wrench to tighten all the bolts and nuts.

### Step 2: Suspension Repair
The suspension repair is a critical task that requires attention to detail and precision.
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheels
Remove the wheels and place the car on a lift. Make sure to support the car with jack stands and apply the parking brake.
#### Step 2.2: Inspect the suspension components
Inspect all the suspension components, including the ball joints, tie rod ends, and shocks. Replace any worn-out or damaged components.
#### Step 2.3: Replace the ball joints and tie rod ends
Replace the ball joints and tie rod ends with new ones. Make sure to tighten all the bolts and nuts properly.
#### Step 2.4: Align the wheels
Align the wheels and make sure the car is properly balanced. Use a wheel alignment tool to ensure accurate alignment.

### Step 3: Electrical System Repair
The electrical system repair is a complex task that requires specialized tools and expertise.
#### Step 3.1: Disconnect the battery
Disconnect the battery and make sure all the electrical systems are turned off.
#### Step 3.2: Inspect the wiring and components
Inspect all the wiring and components, including the ignition coil, spark plugs, and alternator. Replace any worn-out or damaged components.
#### Step 3.3: Replace the ignition coil and spark plugs
Replace the ignition coil and spark plugs with new ones. Make sure to tighten all the bolts and nuts properly.
#### Step 3.4: Test the electrical system
Test the electrical system and make sure all the components are working properly. Use a multimeter and test light to diagnose any problems.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes

* Make sure to test all the components and systems before completing the repair
* Don't overtighten any bolts or nuts, as this can cause damage to the components
