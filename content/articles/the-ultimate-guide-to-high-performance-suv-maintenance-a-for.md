# Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
#### Step 1.1: Locate the oil drain plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the vehicle. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug.
#### Step 1.2: Drain the oil
Allow the oil to drain completely before replacing the plug. Use a torque wrench to tighten the plug to the specified torque (30 Nm).
#### Step 1.3: Replace the oil filter
Use a filter wrench to remove the oil filter. Dispose of the old filter responsibly.
#### Step 1.4: Refill the oil
Use a high-quality oil that meets the manufacturer's specifications. Fill the engine to the recommended level.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheels
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheels. Set the parking brake and place the vehicle in park.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the caliper
Use a 10mm socket to remove the caliper bolts. Slide the caliper off the rotor.
#### Step 2.3: Remove the old brake pads
Pull the old brake pads out of the caliper. Take note of the pad wear sensor wire.
#### Step 2.4: Install the new brake pads
Insert the new brake pads into the caliper. Ensure the pad wear sensor wire is securely connected.

### Step 3: Suspension Inspection
#### Step 3.1: Raise the vehicle
Use a lift or jack to raise the vehicle. Support the vehicle with jack stands.
#### Step 3.2: Inspect the adaptive dampers
Use a multimeter to test the adaptive dampers' electrical connections. Check for any signs of leakage or damage.
#### Step 3.3: Inspect the air springs
Use a suspension inspection tool to check the air springs' pressure and condition.
