## Technical Deep Dive
Corvette engines are renowned for their high-performance capabilities, thanks to advanced technologies like direct fuel injection, variable valve timing, and advanced materials. The most common Corvette engines used in non-Corvette models include the LS1, LS2, LS3, and LT1.

The LS1, for example, is a 5.7L V8 engine that produces 350 horsepower and 370 lb-ft of torque. It features a cast aluminum block, cast iron cylinder heads, and a hydraulic roller camshaft. The LS2, on the other hand, is a 6.0L V8 engine that produces 400 horsepower and 400 lb-ft of torque. It features a cast aluminum block, cast aluminum cylinder heads, and a hydraulic roller camshaft.

Common failure points in Corvette engines include the plastic impeller in the water pump, the valve stem seals, and the piston rings. Regular maintenance, such as oil changes and spark plug replacements, can help prevent these issues.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When working with Corvette engines, it's essential to avoid common mistakes, such as over-tightening the oil drain plug or spark plug. Always use a torque wrench to ensure the correct torque specification is met.

Pro tips include using a funnel to prevent spills when adding new oil, and using a spark plug socket with a rubber insert to prevent damaging the spark plug.
