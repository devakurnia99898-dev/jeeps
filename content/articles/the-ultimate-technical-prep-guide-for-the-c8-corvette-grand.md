## Technical Deep Dive
The C8 Corvette Grand Sport features a rear-mid engine layout, with the engine located behind the driver and passenger seats. This design provides optimal weight distribution, allowing for improved handling and performance. The vehicle's suspension system is equipped with magnetic ride control, which adjusts to different driving conditions to provide a smooth ride.

Common failure points in the C8 Corvette Grand Sport include the engine's oil cooler lines, which can leak over time, and the vehicle's differential, which can wear out due to excessive wear and tear. Regular maintenance is essential to prevent these issues and ensure the vehicle runs smoothly.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Step 1: Oil Change
The C8 Corvette Grand Sport requires regular oil changes to maintain the engine's performance and longevity. To perform an oil change, follow these steps:
#### Step 1.1: Locate the oil drain plug
The oil drain plug is located at the bottom of the engine, near the front of the vehicle. Use a 10mm socket to remove the plug and allow the oil to drain into a pan.
#### Step 1.2: Replace the oil filter
The oil filter is located on top of the engine, near the oil drain plug. Use a filter wrench to remove the filter and replace it with a new one.
#### Step 1.3: Refill the oil
Use a funnel to pour in the new oil, making sure to use the correct type and amount specified in the owner's manual.

### Step 2: Brake Pad Replacement
The C8 Corvette Grand Sport's brake pads should be replaced every 10,000 to 15,000 miles to ensure optimal braking performance. To replace the brake pads, follow these steps:
#### Step 2.1: Remove the wheel
Use a lug wrench to remove the wheel and access the brake caliper.
#### Step 2.2: Remove the caliper
Use a 10mm socket to remove the caliper bolts and slide the caliper off the rotor.
#### Step 2.3: Remove the old brake pads
Use pliers to remove the old brake pads from the caliper.
#### Step 2.4: Install the new brake pads
Use pliers to install the new brake pads into the caliper, making sure they are properly seated.

### Step 3: Suspension Adjustment
The C8 Corvette Grand Sport's suspension system can be adjusted to optimize handling and performance. To adjust the suspension, follow these steps:
#### Step 3.1: Locate the suspension adjustment bolts
The suspension adjustment bolts are located near the shock absorbers, near the rear of the vehicle. Use a 10mm socket to loosen the bolts.
#### Step 3.2: Adjust the suspension
Use a wrench to adjust the suspension, making sure to follow the manufacturer's specifications.
#### Step 3.3: Tighten the bolts
Use a 10mm socket to tighten the bolts, making sure they are securely tightened.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
When working on the C8 Corvette Grand Sport, it's essential to avoid common mistakes that can cause damage to the vehicle. One common mistake is over-tightening the suspension adjustment bolts, which can cause the suspension to become too stiff. Another mistake is using the wrong type of oil, which can cause engine damage.

To avoid these mistakes, make sure to follow the manufacturer's specifications and use the correct tools and materials. It's also essential to consult the owner's manual and seek professional help if you're unsure about any aspect of the maintenance process.
