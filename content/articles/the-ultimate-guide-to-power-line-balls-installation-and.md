## The Diagnostic / The Situation
Power line balls, also known as visibility balls or marker balls, are an essential component of power line systems. They are installed on power lines to increase visibility, particularly in low-light conditions or areas with heavy tree coverage. The primary purpose of these balls is to alert aircraft pilots, crane operators, and other individuals of the presence of power lines, reducing the risk of accidents and fatalities. 
The balls are typically made of durable materials, such as plastic or fiberglass, and come in various colors, including orange, white, and yellow. Orange is the most common color used, as it provides the highest visibility. 
Symptoms or signs to look for include damaged or missing balls, which can compromise the safety of the power line system.

## Technical Deep Dive
The installation of power line balls involves a complex process that requires careful planning and execution. The system consists of a series of balls attached to the power line at regular intervals, typically using a specialized clamp or bracket. The balls are designed to be aerodynamically stable, ensuring they remain securely attached to the power line even in strong winds.
Common failure points include the clamp or bracket, which can corrode or become damaged over time, causing the ball to become dislodged. Regular maintenance is essential to ensure the integrity of the power line system and prevent accidents.
