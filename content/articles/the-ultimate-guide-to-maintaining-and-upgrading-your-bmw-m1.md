# The Diagnostic / The Situation
The BMW M1 is a rare and iconic supercar that has been out of production for decades. As a result, maintaining and upgrading its suspension system can be a challenging task. The M1's suspension system is a complex network of components that work together to provide a smooth ride and responsive handling. However, over time, these components can wear out, leading to a decrease in performance and an increase in maintenance costs.

Symptoms of a worn-out suspension system in the BMW M1 include uneven tire wear, vibration, and a decrease in handling precision. If left unchecked, these issues can lead to more serious problems, such as damage to the steering system, brakes, and chassis.

## Technical Deep Dive
The BMW M1's suspension system is a MacPherson strut design with a coil spring and shock absorber at each corner. The system is designed to provide a high level of stability and control, while also absorbing bumps and vibrations from the road.

Common failure points in the M1's suspension system include the plastic impeller in the power steering pump, the rubber bushings in the control arms, and the seals in the shock absorbers. These components can wear out over time, leading to a decrease in performance and an increase in maintenance costs.

## Step-by-Step Guide
### Inspect the Suspension System
The first step in maintaining and upgrading the BMW M1's suspension system is to inspect the components for wear and damage. This includes checking the condition of the shock absorbers, coil springs, and control arms.

#### Check the Shock Absorbers
The shock absorbers in the M1's suspension system are designed to absorb bumps and vibrations from the road. Over time, these components can wear out, leading to a decrease in performance and an increase in maintenance costs.

To check the shock absorbers, start by jacking up the vehicle and supporting it with jack stands. Then, remove the wheel and inspect the shock absorber for signs of wear and damage. Check the seals for leaks and the piston rod for scoring.

#### Check the Coil Springs
The coil springs in the M1's suspension system are designed to provide a high level of stability and control. Over time, these components can sag, leading to a decrease in performance and an increase in maintenance costs.

To check the coil springs, start by jacking up the vehicle and supporting it with jack stands. Then, remove the wheel and inspect the coil spring for signs of wear and damage. Check the spring for sag and the spring seats for wear.

#### Check the Control Arms
The control arms in the M1's suspension system are designed to provide a high level of stability and control. Over time, these components can wear out, leading to a decrease in performance and an increase in maintenance costs.

To check the control arms, start by jacking up the vehicle and supporting it with jack stands. Then, remove the wheel and inspect the control arm for signs of wear and damage. Check the bushings for wear and the arm for cracks.

### Replace the Shock Absorbers
If the inspection reveals that the shock absorbers are worn out, they will need to be replaced. This is a complex task that requires specialized tools and expertise.

#### Remove the Wheel and Suspension Components
To replace the shock absorbers, start by jacking up the vehicle and supporting it with jack stands. Then, remove the wheel and suspension components, including the coil spring and control arm.

#### Compress the Coil Spring
To remove the shock absorber, the coil spring will need to be compressed. This can be done using a spring compressor.

#### Remove the Shock Absorber
Once the coil spring is compressed, the shock absorber can be removed. This is typically done by removing the retaining ring and pulling the shock absorber out of the suspension system.

#### Install the New Shock Absorber
To install the new shock absorber, start by inserting it into the suspension system. Then, reinstall the retaining ring and compress the coil spring.

### Replace the Control Arm Bushings
If the inspection reveals that the control arm bushings are worn out, they will need to be replaced. This is a complex task that requires specialized tools and expertise.

#### Remove the Control Arm
To replace the control arm bushings, start by jacking up the vehicle and supporting it with jack stands. Then, remove the control arm by removing the retaining bolts.

#### Remove the Old Bushings
Once the control arm is removed, the old bushings can be removed. This is typically done by pressing them out of the control arm.

#### Install the New Bushings
To install the new bushings, start by pressing them into the control arm. Then, reinstall the control arm and retaining bolts.

## Pro Tips & Common Mistakes
* Always use a torque wrench when tightening suspension components to avoid over-tightening.
* Never use a impact wrench to tighten suspension components, as this can damage the threads.
* Always follow proper lifting and lowering procedures when working with a vehicle.
