## Technical Deep Dive
To understand how vehicle surveillance systems work, let's take a closer look at the technologies involved. Flock cameras, for example, use a combination of cameras, sensors, and artificial intelligence to detect and track vehicles. The cameras capture images of license plates, which are then processed using optical character recognition (OCR) software to extract the plate number and other relevant information.

The collected data is stored in a database and can be accessed by law enforcement agencies and other authorized parties. The FreeForm search tool, mentioned in the source news headline, allows users to search for vehicles and individuals based on various visual characteristics, such as license plate number, color, make, and model.

Common failure points in vehicle surveillance systems include:

* Poor camera quality or positioning, which can lead to inaccurate or incomplete data collection
* Inadequate data storage and security measures, which can compromise the integrity of the collected data
* Insufficient training and calibration of the AI algorithms used for object detection and recognition
